# Boston Medical Center - Geriatrics & Home Care Referrals

Boston Medical Center (BMC) is the primary teaching affiliate of Boston University School of Medicine and the largest safety-net hospital in New England. Its robust geriatrics program makes it a significant referral source for home care agencies.

## Overview

**Location:** Boston, MA (South End)
**System:** Boston Medical Center Health System
**Specialty:** Safety-net hospital with strong geriatrics focus
**Annual Elder Patients:** 50,000+ visits to geriatric services

## Geriatric Services at BMC

### Section of Geriatrics
BMC's Section of Geriatrics provides comprehensive care for older adults:

- **Outpatient Geriatric Clinic** - Primary care focused on complex older adults
- **Geriatric Inpatient Service** - Dedicated unit for hospitalized elders
- **Geriatric Emergency Department** - Specialized ER for older adults
- **Memory Clinic** - Evaluation and treatment for dementia
- **Falls Prevention Clinic** - Assessment and intervention for fall risks
- **Palliative Care** - Symptom management and advance care planning

### Key Departments for Home Care Referrals

#### 1. **Social Work Department**
- Coordinates discharge planning
- Assesses post-acute care needs
- Makes referrals to home care agencies
- Helps families navigate insurance and benefits

**Key Contact:** Hospital Social Work Department (617-414-5648)

#### 2. **Case Management**
- Works with insurers to authorize services
- Coordinates complex discharges
- Connects patients with community resources
- Facilitates transitions from hospital to home

#### 3. **Geriatric Care Coordination**
- Manages care for high-risk older adults
- Coordinates with primary care physicians
- Links patients to home-based services
- Provides follow-up post-discharge

## Why BMC is Important for Home Care Agencies

### High-Volume Safety-Net Hospital
BMC serves a diverse, medically complex population:
- **75%** of patients are racial/ethnic minorities
- **70%** rely on Medicaid or are uninsured
- High prevalence of chronic conditions
- Many patients need home care post-discharge

### Strong Geriatric Focus
- Dedicated geriatric specialists understand home care value
- Culture of preventing nursing home placements
- Emphasis on aging in place
- Active partnerships with community providers

### Teaching Hospital
- Medical residents trained in comprehensive discharge planning
- Evidence-based approach to transitions of care
- Research collaborations with home care agencies
- Innovation in care coordination models

## Building Relationships with BMC

### Step 1: Register as a Preferred Provider
- Contact BMC's Vendor Management Office
- Complete vendor registration forms
- Provide proof of licensure and insurance
- Submit service rates and capabilities

**Contact:** Vendor Relations (617-414-4444)

### Step 2: Connect with Social Work
- Introduce your agency to the Social Work Department
- Provide business cards and agency information
- Offer to conduct in-service training
- Be responsive to referrals (call back within 2-4 hours)

### Step 3: Attend Provider Events
BMC occasionally hosts provider fairs and networking events:
- Community Provider Networking Sessions
- Discharge Planning Symposiums
- Quality Improvement Collaboratives

### Step 4: Demonstrate Quality
- Accept challenging cases (complex medical needs)
- Provide excellent communication
- Submit documentation promptly
- Report patient outcomes
- Maintain high satisfaction scores

## Referral Process

### How Referrals Work
1. **Patient identified** - Social worker or case manager identifies need for home care
2. **Insurance verification** - Case manager verifies coverage and benefits
3. **Referrals placed** - Social worker contacts 2-3 home care agencies
4. **Agency response** - Agencies assess and provide availability
5. **Family choice** - Patient/family select preferred agency
6. **Start of care** - Agency begins services post-discharge

### What BMC Looks For in Home Care Partners
- **Responsiveness** - Quick turnaround on referrals (same day preferred)
- **Flexibility** - Ability to accommodate urgent discharges
- **Cultural competence** - Staff who reflect BMC's diverse patient population
- **Clinical expertise** - Experience with complex medical conditions
- **Care coordination** - Strong communication with BMC providers
- **Technology** - EMR/EHR capabilities for information sharing

## Insurance & Payer Mix

BMC patients typically have:

### Public Insurance
- **MassHealth (Medicaid)** - 40-50% of patients
- **Medicare** - 20-30% of elderly patients
- **Dual eligible** (Medicare + Medicaid) - Significant percentage

### Private Insurance
- Commercial insurance - 20-30%
- Managed Care plans
- Accountable Care Organization (ACO) arrangements

### Uninsured/Self-Pay
- BMC has programs to help uninsured access home care
- Partnership with community organizations
- Charitable care funding available

## Specialized Programs

### Hospital at Home
BMC operates a Hospital at Home program:
- Acute hospital-level care delivered at home
- Home care agencies provide support services
- Opportunity for partnership and referrals

### Geriatric Emergency Department
Specialized ER for adults 65+:
- Designed to prevent unnecessary hospitalizations
- Strong connection to home-based services
- Direct referral pathway to home care

### Memory Care Program
Comprehensive dementia services:
- Early-stage interventions
- Caregiver support
- Home safety assessments
- Referrals for in-home support

## Key Statistics

- **500+** bed acute care hospital
- **50,000+** annual geriatric visits
- **70%** of patients covered by public insurance
- **20+** primary care locations across Greater Boston
- **Major** referral source for home care services in Boston

## Tips for Success

1. **Be culturally responsive** - BMC serves diverse communities; multilingual staff is a plus
2. **Accept MassHealth** - Many BMC patients have Medicaid coverage
3. **Respond urgently** - Hospital discharge timelines are tight; same-day response is ideal
4. **Build trust** - Consistent quality leads to repeat referrals
5. **Communicate proactively** - Update social workers on patient status and any concerns
