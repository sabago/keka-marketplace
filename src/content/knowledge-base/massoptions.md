# MassOptions: Massachusetts Aging & Disability Resource Center

MassOptions serves as the front door to long-term services and supports in Massachusetts. It's a free, statewide service that helps individuals age 60+ and adults with disabilities access community-based services, including home care.

## What is MassOptions?

MassOptions is Massachusetts' Aging and Disability Resource Center (ADRC), part of a national initiative to streamline access to long-term supports and services. The system is administered through the state's network of Aging Services Access Points (ASAPs).

## How It Works

### For Consumers
1. **Single Point of Contact** - Seniors and families can call **1-844-422-6277** (1-844-4ACHOICE) to reach their local ASAP
2. **Comprehensive Information** - Information specialists provide guidance on available services
3. **Neutral Referrals** - Consumers receive unbiased information about home care providers in their area
4. **No Cost** - All services are free to consumers

### For Home Care Agencies
MassOptions represents a significant referral source because:
- High-intent leads - families calling have immediate needs
- Trusted source - consumers trust state-sponsored referrals
- Active outreach - ASAPs conduct community education and outreach
- MassHealth connection - many callers are eligible for state-funded services

## Getting Listed

To receive referrals from MassOptions:

1. **Register with your regional ASAP** - Each ASAP maintains a provider directory
2. **Meet quality standards** - Must be licensed and certified as required by state regulations
3. **Maintain good standing** - Keep licenses current and address any complaints
4. **Stay engaged** - Participate in ASAP provider meetings and training sessions

## Regional ASAPs

Massachusetts is divided into 23 ASAP service areas. Find your regional ASAP at: **www.mass.gov/asaps**

Key ASAP regions include:
- **Greater Boston** - Boston, Brookline, Chelsea, Revere, Winthrop
- **Cape Cod** - Barnstable County
- **Berkshire County** - Western MA
- **Central MA** - Worcester area
- **Metro North** - Malden, Medford, Everett, etc.

## Key Statistics

- **300,000+** - Massachusetts residents age 60+ served annually
- **23** - Regional ASAPs across the state
- **Free** - No cost to consumers or providers for referrals
- **Trusted** - State-sponsored and neutral

## Best Practices

To maximize MassOptions referrals:

1. **Build relationships** - Get to know your ASAP staff personally
2. **Respond quickly** - Return calls from referred consumers within 24 hours
3. **Provide feedback** - Let ASAPs know when you successfully serve a referral
4. **Attend meetings** - Participate in quarterly provider forums
5. **Stay compliant** - Maintain all required licenses and certifications
