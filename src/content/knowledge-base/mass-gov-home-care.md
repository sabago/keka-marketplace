# Mass.gov Home Care Services & Resources

The Commonwealth of Massachusetts provides extensive online resources about home care services through Mass.gov, the official state website. These resources cover everything from finding providers to understanding MassHealth coverage.

## Key State Agencies

### 1. Executive Office of Elder Affairs (EOEA)
**Website:** www.mass.gov/elders

EOEA is the primary state agency serving Massachusetts residents age 60 and older. It oversees:
- ASAPs (Aging Services Access Points)
- MassOptions information system
- Home care programs
- Elder protective services
- Senior nutrition programs

**Contact:**
- Phone: 1-800-AGE-INFO (1-800-243-4636)
- TTY: 1-800-872-0166
- Address: One Ashburton Place, 5th Floor, Boston, MA 02108

### 2. MassHealth (Massachusetts Medicaid)
**Website:** www.mass.gov/masshealth

MassHealth provides health coverage for eligible low-income individuals, including comprehensive home care benefits.

**Key Home Care Programs:**
- Personal Care Attendant (PCA) Program
- Home and Community-Based Services Waivers
- MassHealth Home Care
- Community Choices
- Money Follows the Person

**Contact:**
- Member Services: 1-800-841-2900
- TTY: 1-800-497-4648
- Provider Services: 1-800-325-5231

### 3. Department of Public Health (DPH)
**Website:** www.mass.gov/dph

DPH licenses and regulates home health agencies in Massachusetts, ensuring quality and safety standards.

**Licensing Division:**
- Oversees home health agency licenses
- Conducts inspections and investigations
- Maintains public records of licensed agencies
- Handles complaints and enforcement

## Home Care Programs for Consumers

### State-Funded Programs

#### Home Care Program
Provides in-home services to elders to help them remain independent:

**Eligibility:**
- Age 60 or older
- Massachusetts resident
- Income below program limits
- Need for assistance with daily activities

**Services Covered:**
- Personal care (bathing, dressing, grooming)
- Homemaking (cleaning, laundry, shopping)
- Respite care for family caregivers
- Home-delivered meals
- Transportation

**How to Apply:**
Call your local ASAP (find via MassOptions: 1-844-422-6277)

#### Enhanced Community Options Program (ECOP)
For elders at risk of nursing home placement who need intensive services:

**Features:**
- Higher service hours than standard Home Care Program
- Intensive care management
- 24/7 emergency response capability
- Coordination with healthcare providers

**Eligibility:**
- Nursing facility level of care needed
- Preference to remain at home
- Can be safely served at home with supports

### MassHealth (Medicaid) Programs

#### Personal Care Attendant (PCA) Program
Provides personal care services to MassHealth members with disabilities:

**Eligible Members:**
- All ages (adults and children)
- Have MassHealth coverage
- Clinical need for personal care assistance
- Can self-direct care or have surrogate

**Services:**
- Assistance with activities of daily living
- Consumer-directed model (member hires/fires PCAs)
- Agency-based model also available

**Website:** www.mass.gov/pca

#### Community Choices Program
Serves adults with disabilities who need long-term services and supports:

**Services:**
- Personal care
- Homemaking
- Adult day health
- Respite care
- Care coordination

#### Home and Community-Based Services (HCBS) Waivers
MassHealth offers several HCBS waivers:

1. **Acquired Brain Injury (ABI) Waiver** - For individuals with acquired brain injuries
2. **MFP Residential Supports Waiver** - For individuals transitioning from institutions
3. **MFP Community Living Waiver** - Comprehensive community services

## Resources for Home Care Agencies

### Licensing & Certification

#### Home Health Agency License
Required to operate a home health agency in Massachusetts.

**Requirements:**
- Administrator with appropriate qualifications
- Written policies and procedures
- Quality assurance program
- Adequate insurance coverage
- Compliance with all DPH regulations

**Application Process:**
www.mass.gov/how-to/apply-for-a-home-health-agency-license

#### Provider Enrollment
To serve MassHealth members:

**Steps:**
1. Obtain home health agency license from DPH
2. Complete MassHealth provider enrollment
3. Sign provider agreements
4. Complete required training

**Website:** www.mass.gov/how-to/enroll-as-a-masshealth-provider

### Regulations & Compliance

#### 105 CMR 210.000: Licensing of Home Health Agencies
The primary regulation governing home health agencies in Massachusetts.

**Key Requirements:**
- Patient rights and protections
- Personnel qualifications
- Infection control
- Emergency preparedness
- Quality assessment and performance improvement

**Full Text:** www.mass.gov/regulations/105-CMR-210000-licensing-of-home-health-agencies
