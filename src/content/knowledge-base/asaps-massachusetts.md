# ASAPs: Aging Services Access Points in Massachusetts

Aging Services Access Points (ASAPs) are nonprofit agencies designated by the Massachusetts Executive Office of Elder Affairs to coordinate and provide services to elders and adults with disabilities in specific geographic regions.

## What Are ASAPs?

ASAPs serve as regional "one-stop shops" for aging and disability services in Massachusetts. They function as:

- **Information centers** - Providing guidance on available services and supports
- **Referral sources** - Connecting consumers to home care providers
- **Care coordinators** - Assessing needs and developing care plans
- **Program administrators** - Managing state-funded programs like home care services
- **Community advocates** - Representing elder interests at the local level

## Services Provided by ASAPs

### Core Services
- **Information & Referral** - Comprehensive information about aging services
- **Options Counseling** - One-on-one assistance exploring long-term care options
- **Home Care Services** - Care management for state-funded home care programs
- **Protective Services** - Investigation and intervention for elder abuse cases
- **Medicare/Medicaid Assistance** (SHINE Program) - Free health insurance counseling

### Referral Services
- Private home care agencies
- Adult day health programs
- Transportation services
- Meal programs (home-delivered and congregate)
- Housing options
- Legal services
- Support groups

## Why ASAPs Matter for Home Care Agencies

ASAPs are crucial referral partners because:

1. **High Volume** - ASAPs field thousands of calls monthly from families seeking home care
2. **Qualified Leads** - Callers have immediate, assessed needs
3. **Trusted Source** - Families trust state-designated agencies
4. **Purchasing Power** - ASAPs administer state-funded home care programs (they can directly hire your agency)
5. **Community Presence** - ASAPs have strong relationships with hospitals, doctors, and community organizations

## Getting on ASAP Provider Lists

Each ASAP maintains lists of approved home care providers. To get listed:

### Step 1: Verify Licensure
Ensure your agency has:
- Valid Massachusetts home care license
- Current liability insurance
- Workers' compensation insurance
- Required certifications

### Step 2: Contact Your Regional ASAP
- Call the ASAP serving your geographic area
- Ask for the Provider Relations or Vendor Management department
- Request information about provider enrollment

### Step 3: Complete Registration
Requirements vary by ASAP but typically include:
- Completed vendor application
- Proof of licensure and insurance
- W-9 tax form
- Service rate sheets
- References

### Step 4: Maintain Your Status
- Keep all licenses and certifications current
- Respond to referrals promptly (within 24 hours)
- Attend provider meetings and training sessions
- Submit any required reports or documentation
- Maintain quality standards

## Working with ASAPs

### Best Practices
1. **Build relationships** - Meet ASAP care managers in person
2. **Be responsive** - Return calls from ASAPs immediately
3. **Communicate proactively** - Update ASAPs on your capacity and service offerings
4. **Provide feedback** - Report successful placements and thank them for referrals
5. **Participate** - Attend ASAP-sponsored events and trainings
6. **Be reliable** - If you commit to taking a client, follow through

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Not responding to referrals quickly enough
- Being selective about which referrals to accept (this damages your reputation)
- Failing to update contact information
- Not attending provider meetings
- Letting licenses or insurance lapse

## ASAP Funding & Programs

ASAPs administer several state-funded programs that purchase home care services:

### State Programs
- **Home Care Program** - State-funded home care for eligible elders
- **Enhanced Community Options Program (ECOP)** - Higher-acuity home care to prevent nursing home placement
- **Community Choices Program** - Personal care services for adults with disabilities
- **MassHealth HCBS Waivers** - Medicaid-funded home and community-based services

### Eligibility
Generally requires:
- Age 60+ (or adult with disability for some programs)
- Income below certain thresholds
- Clinical need for services
- Residency in Massachusetts
