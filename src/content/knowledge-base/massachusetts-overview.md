# Massachusetts Home Care Referral Landscape

Massachusetts offers a robust ecosystem of referral sources for home care agencies. Understanding the landscape can help you maximize quality leads and grow your business effectively.

## State-Level Resources

Massachusetts provides several state-funded and state-managed resources that connect seniors and their families with home care services:

### MassOptions
MassOptions is the state's primary information and referral system for aging and disability resources. It's operated through Aging Services Access Points (ASAPs) located throughout the state.

### ASAPs (Aging Services Access Points)
Massachusetts has 23 ASAPs that serve as regional one-stop shops for information, referrals, and access to services for elders and individuals with disabilities. These are critical referral partners for home care agencies.

## Hospital Systems

Major hospital systems in Massachusetts often have dedicated geriatric departments and social workers who make home care referrals:

- **Boston Medical Center** - Large geriatrics program with active discharge planning
- **Massachusetts General Hospital** - Strong elder care focus
- **Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center** - Comprehensive aging services
- **Brigham and Women's Hospital** - Geriatric medicine specialists

## Consumer Platforms

Several online platforms help families find home care services:

- **Caring.com** - National platform with MA presence
- **A Place for Mom** - Senior housing and home care referrals
- **Home Care Assistance** - National franchise with MA locations
- **Honor Technology** - Tech-enabled home care platform

## Tips for Success

1. **Register with ASAPs** - Each ASAP maintains lists of approved home care providers
2. **Build hospital relationships** - Social workers and discharge planners are key referral sources
3. **Maintain your online presence** - Families increasingly search online first
4. **Understand Medicaid** - MassHealth (MA Medicaid) covers home care for eligible individuals
