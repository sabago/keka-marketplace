## Overview

Fallon Medicare Plus is a **regional Medicare Advantage plan** serving 25,000+ Medicare beneficiaries with deep roots in Central Massachusetts. Founded in 1977, Fallon Health is a non-profit, community-based health plan known for personalized care management and strong relationships with Worcester-area hospitals and physician practices, making it a valuable referral source for home care agencies serving Central MA.

**Why Fallon Medicare Plus matters for home care agencies:**
- **25,000+ Medicare members:** Dominant Medicare Advantage plan in Central Massachusetts
- **Geographic concentration:** 65% of members in Worcester County (high referral density)
- **Community-based:** 45+ year presence in Central MA, deep provider relationships
- **Care coordination excellence:** 60+ care coordinators (many based in Worcester) actively managing home care referrals
- **Supplemental benefits:** Generous home care benefits in most plans (personal care, homemaker, companion, respite)
- **Member loyalty:** Average 6+ years with plan (strong brand loyalty in Central MA)
- **Hospital partnerships:** Close relationships with UMass Memorial, Saint Vincent Hospital, Harrington Hospital
- **Non-profit mission:** Community-focused, values long-term provider partnerships
- **Low competition:** Fewer agencies compete for Fallon referrals vs. Boston-area plans

**Investment:** FREE credentialing (no enrollment fees)

**Timeline:** 6-10 weeks for provider approval (efficient credentialing for Central MA providers)

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from Fallon Medicare Plus

**Fallon Medicare Plus creates FIVE referral pathways:**

### **Pathway 1: Care Coordinator Direct Referrals (PRIMARY - 75%)**

**How it works:**
- Fallon's 60+ care coordinators conduct health risk assessments with members
- Care coordinator identifies member needing home care (ADL decline, post-hospitalization, chronic disease support, family caregiver burnout)
- Care coordinator searches provider directory for credentialed Central MA agencies
- Care coordinator calls 2-3 agencies with member details
- Your agency responds within 4-24 hours
- Complete in-home assessment within 48-72 hours (24 hours for hospital discharges)
- Submit care plan to care coordinator via portal or fax
- Care coordinator reviews, authorizes services (60-90 day initial authorization)
- Begin services, document, submit claims

**Conversion rate:** 85-92% (Fallon care coordinators build strong relationships with members, endorsement highly trusted)

**Volume potential:** Established agencies receive 3-7 care coordinator referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Fallon care coordinators are community-based (many work in Worcester office). Build local relationships, attend Fallon provider events in Worcester.

### **Pathway 2: Hospital/SNF Discharge Referrals (18%)**

**How it works:**
- Fallon member hospitalized (UMass Memorial, Saint Vincent, Harrington, Worcester Recovery Center)
- Hospital discharge planner contacts Fallon utilization management
- Fallon care coordinator coordinates discharge planning (often participates in person at UMass Memorial)
- Care coordinator arranges home care for safe transition
- In-network agencies contacted for rapid response
- First agency to complete assessment typically wins

**Conversion rate:** 70-85% (less competitive than Boston; fewer agencies in Central MA)

**Volume potential:** 2-5 hospital discharge referrals monthly (higher if strong UMass Memorial relationship)

**Pro tip:** UMass Memorial is Fallon's primary hospital partner. Build direct relationships with discharge planners at UMass Memorial Worcester, UMass Memorial Leominster, UMass Memorial Marlborough.

### **Pathway 3: Member Self-Referral (5%)**

**How it works:**
- Member finds your agency via Google, referral from friend/family, Council on Aging
- Member or family calls your agency
- Verify Fallon Medicare Plus coverage
- Contact Fallon Provider Services (888-566-0008) to verify benefits, authorization
- Complete assessment, submit care plan to care coordinator
- Care coordinator authorizes
- Begin care

**Conversion rate:** 85-90%

**Volume potential:** 1-2 member-initiated referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Optimize Google Business Profile for "Fallon Medicare home care Worcester," "Fallon Medicare Plus home care Leominster."

### **Pathway 4: Primary Care Physician Referrals (2%)**

**How it works:**
- Member visits PCP
- PCP identifies home care need
- PCP notifies Fallon care coordinator
- Care coordinator contacts member, then agencies
- You complete assessment, care coordinator authorizes

**Conversion rate:** 75-85%

**Volume potential:** 0-2 PCP referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Focus on high-volume Fallon primary care practices (UMass Memorial primary care practices, Reliant Medical Group, Edward M. Kennedy Community Health Center Worcester).

### **Pathway 5: Supplemental Benefits Referrals (<1%)**

**How it works:**
- Member inquires about supplemental home care benefits
- Directed to care coordinator or vendor
- Agencies contacted for coordination
- Complete assessment, begin services

**Conversion rate:** 60-75%

**Volume potential:** 0-1 supplemental benefit referrals monthly

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "Geographic limitation - only valuable for Central MA agencies"**

**Reality:** 65% of members in Worcester County. Limited value for Boston, North Shore, Western MA agencies.

**Solution:**
- If you serve Worcester County: Fallon is MUST-HAVE payer (dominant in region)
- If you don't serve Worcester County: Focus on other Medicare Advantage plans (Tufts, BCBSMA, Harvard Pilgrim)
- Consider expanding service area to Worcester County (Fallon + Commonwealth Care Alliance + UMass Memorial discharge referrals make Worcester market attractive)

### **Challenge 2: "Smaller member base than Tufts/BCBSMA"**

**Reality:** 25,000 members vs. 100,000 (Tufts) or 80,000 (BCBSMA).

**Solution:**
- Geographic concentration offsets smaller size (Worcester County agencies see high referral volume)
- Less competition (fewer agencies compete for Fallon referrals)
- Community relationships easier to build (smaller care coordinator team, local focus)
- Complement Fallon with other payers (diversified payer mix)

### **Challenge 3: "Credentialing delays"**

**Why delays happen:**
- Incomplete application
- Insurance gaps
- Reference delays

**Solution:**
- Submit complete application
- Respond to requests within 24-48 hours
- Email provider.services@fallonhealth.org weekly for status
- Escalate if stalled beyond 12 weeks

**Note:** Fallon credentialing generally efficient for Central MA providers (6-10 weeks typical).

### **Challenge 4: "I'm credentialed but not receiving referrals"**

**Solution:**
- Introduce yourself to care coordinators (emphasize Worcester presence)
- Attend Fallon Worcester provider events (visibility critical)
- Respond rapidly to first referrals (prove reliability)
- Update provider profile (services, languages, specialties)
- Market directly to Worcester County members
- Build UMass Memorial relationships

**Timeframe:** 2-4 month ramp-up period

### **Challenge 5: "Need Polish-speaking caregivers"**

**Reality:** Worcester has large Polish population, but few agencies have Polish-speaking caregivers.

**Solution:**
- Recruit Polish-speaking caregivers (Worcester has large Polish community)
- Partner with Polish organizations (Polish Center of Discovery and Learning Worcester)
- Advertise in Polish-language media
- Contact Fallon care coordinators when you add Polish caregivers (major network gap)

**Opportunity:** Polish language care is significant differentiator for Fallon referrals.
