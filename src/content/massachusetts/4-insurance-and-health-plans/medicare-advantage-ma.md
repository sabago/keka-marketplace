## Overview

Massachusetts has **103+ Medicare Advantage plans** available in 2026, providing alternatives to traditional Medicare for 65+ beneficiaries. Major MA plans in Massachusetts include:
- Mass General Brigham Health Plan (MGB Advantage)
- Tufts Health Plan Medicare Preferred
- Blue Cross Blue Shield Medicare Advantage
- Fallon Health Medicare plans
- UnitedHealthcare MA plans
- WellCare/Centene MA plans

**For home care agencies:** MA plans cover Medicare home health benefits, but NOT non-medical companion/personal care—creating private-pay opportunities for well-insured seniors.

## Who Sends Referrals

- MA plan care coordinators
- Network PCPs and specialists
- Hospital discharge planners
- Members self-referring

## Who Can Receive Referrals

- Medicare-certified home health (covered benefit)
- Private-pay home care (non-covered services)

## How It Works

1. **Enroll with MA plan provider networks** (each plan separately)
2. **Complete credentialing**
3. **Receive referrals** for skilled home health (covered) and private-pay companion care (not covered)
4. **Position private-pay services:** "Your plan covers medical care; we provide companionship and daily living support"

## Cost and Lead Quality

**Cost:** FREE credentialing

**Lead Quality:** Excellent—MA members tend to be educated, engaged, financially stable

## Best Practices

1. **Prioritize largest MA plans** (MGB, Tufts, BCBS)
2. **Educate members on coverage gaps:** Medical vs. non-medical services
3. **Build care coordinator relationships**
4. **Position as premium private-pay service**
