## Overview

Senior Care Options (SCO) is a **managed care program for dual-eligible seniors** (65+) who have both Medicare and MassHealth (Medicaid). SCO plans integrate all medical, hospital, and long-term services into a single coordinated program with zero or low premiums. For Massachusetts home care agencies, SCO represents access to **thousands of medically complex seniors** who often need both skilled home health and non-medical supportive care.

**Key plans operating in MA:**
- Commonwealth Care Alliance (CCA) SCO - Largest, 10 counties
- Tufts Health Plan SCO
- Senior Whole Health (part of Care New England)

SCO members get comprehensive care coordination through dedicated care teams, making these plans valuable partners for home care agencies focused on serving dual-eligible populations.

## Who Sends Referrals Through This Source

- **SCO care coordinators** managing member care plans
- **Primary care physicians** within SCO networks
- **Hospital discharge planners** coordinating with SCO teams
- **Nurse practitioners and case managers** within integrated care teams
- **Social workers** addressing social determinants of health

## Who Can Receive Referrals

- Medicare-certified home health agencies (skilled services covered by Medicare portion)
- Private-pay home care agencies (non-medical services, supplemental to covered benefits)
- Adult day health programs (covered benefit)
- Hospice providers
- DME suppliers

**For private-pay agencies:** SCO plans cover limited home care hours; families often need supplemental private-pay services for additional support beyond plan benefits.

## How It Works

1. **Enroll in SCO plan provider network** (contact each plan's provider relations)
2. **Complete credentialing** (license, insurance, quality metrics)
3. **Receive referrals from care coordinators** when members need services
4. **Coordinate with SCO care team** on care plans and authorizations
5. **Bill per contract terms** (covered services) or offer private-pay supplements

**Typical referral:** Care coordinator calls: "We have a member in [city] who needs companion care and meal preparation. Can you assist?"

## How to Get Listed / Enrolled

**Step 1:** Contact SCO plan provider relations
- **Commonwealth Care Alliance:** (866) 420-9332 | commonwealthcarealliance.org/providers
- **Tufts Health Plan SCO:** point32health.org/provider
- **Senior Whole Health:** seniorwholehealth.com/providers

**Step 2:** Complete provider application
- Business information and tax ID
- Liability and workers' comp insurance
- License (if applicable)
- Quality metrics and references

**Step 3:** Negotiate contract
- Understand covered vs. non-covered services
- Review rates and billing procedures
- Clarify care coordination expectations

**Step 4:** Training and onboarding
- Learn plan benefits, authorization processes
- Meet care coordination teams

## Cost and Lead Quality

**Cost:** FREE enrollment; contracted rates for covered services

**Lead Quality:** Excellent
- Pre-qualified by care coordinators
- Medically complex, high-need members
- Long-term relationships (members stay enrolled for years)
- Mix of covered services + private-pay supplements

**Volume:** Varies by plan; CCA alone serves 75,000+ dual-eligible members

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- Large member populations across MA
- Comprehensive care coordination support
- Mix of covered + private-pay revenue
- Long-term client relationships
- Professional credibility

**Cons:**
- Credentialing requirements
- Must coordinate authorizations for covered services
- Lower rates for covered services vs. private-pay
- Care plan constraints (covered hours limits)

## How This Fits Mastering Home Care

**Ingest paths:** Portal, phone, email from care coordinators

**Automation:**
- **SCO member flag:** Track members with plan coverage + private-pay supplements
- **Authorization tracking:** Monitor covered hours vs. private-pay hours
- **Care coordinator contacts:** Automate communication with SCO teams
- **Billing split:** Separate billing for covered vs. private-pay services

## Best Practices

1. **Build care coordinator relationships:** Personal connections drive referrals
2. **Offer private-pay supplements:** "Your plan covers 10 hours; let's add 10 private-pay hours"
3. **Communicate proactively:** Update care coordinators on member status regularly
4. **Specialize in dual-eligible populations:** Cultural competency, social services navigation
5. **Track outcomes:** Hospital readmission prevention, medication adherence
