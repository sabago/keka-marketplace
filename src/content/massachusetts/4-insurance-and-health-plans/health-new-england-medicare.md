## Overview

Health New England (HNE) Medicare is the **dominant Medicare Advantage plan in Western Massachusetts**, serving 18,000+ Medicare beneficiaries across Hampden, Hampshire, Franklin, and Berkshire counties. Founded in 1985 and headquartered in Springfield, HNE is a non-profit, community-based health plan with deep roots in Western MA, making it an essential referral source for home care agencies serving the region.

**Why HNE Medicare matters for home care agencies in Western MA:**
- **18,000+ Medicare members:** Largest Medicare Advantage plan in Western Massachusetts
- **Geographic dominance:** 70%+ market share in Springfield/Holyoke area
- **Community-based:** 40-year presence in Western MA, strong provider relationships
- **Care management team:** 45+ care managers (Springfield-based) actively coordinating home care referrals
- **Supplemental benefits:** Home care services included in most plans (personal care, homemaker, respite)
- **Member loyalty:** Average 6+ years with plan (strong regional brand loyalty)
- **Hospital partnerships:** Close relationships with Baystate Health, Cooley Dickinson, Berkshire Health Systems
- **Non-profit mission:** Community-first approach, values long-term provider partnerships
- **Low competition:** Fewer home care agencies in Western MA = less referral competition than Boston

**Investment:** FREE credentialing (no enrollment fees)

**Timeline:** 6-10 weeks for provider approval (efficient for Western MA agencies)

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from Health New England Medicare

**HNE Medicare creates FIVE referral pathways:**

### **Pathway 1: Care Manager Direct Referrals (PRIMARY - 80%)**

**How it works:**
- HNE's 45+ care managers (Springfield-based) conduct health risk assessments
- Care manager identifies member needing home care (ADL decline, post-hospitalization, chronic disease support, caregiver burnout)
- Care manager searches provider directory for Western MA agencies
- Care manager calls 2-3 agencies with member information
- Your agency responds within 4-24 hours
- Complete in-home assessment within 48-72 hours (24 hours for hospital discharges)
- Submit care plan to care manager via portal or fax
- Care manager reviews, authorizes services (60-90 day initial authorization)
- Begin services, document, submit claims

**Conversion rate:** 88-95% (HNE care managers have close relationships with members, high trust)

**Volume potential:** Established Western MA agencies receive 4-9 care manager referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** HNE care managers are locally based in Springfield. Build face-to-face relationships, attend HNE Springfield provider events.

### **Pathway 2: Hospital/SNF Discharge Referrals (15%)**

**How it works:**
- HNE member hospitalized (Baystate Medical Center, Baystate Franklin, Cooley Dickinson, Berkshire Medical Center)
- Hospital discharge planner contacts HNE utilization management
- HNE care manager coordinates discharge planning (often participates in person at Baystate)
- Care manager arranges home care for safe transition
- In-network agencies contacted for rapid assessment
- First agency to respond typically wins

**Conversion rate:** 75-88% (less competitive than Boston - fewer Western MA agencies)

**Volume potential:** 2-6 hospital discharge referrals monthly (higher if strong Baystate relationship)

**Pro tip:** Baystate Health is HNE's largest hospital partner. Build relationships with discharge planners at Baystate Medical Center (Springfield), Baystate Franklin Medical Center (Greenfield), Baystate Noble Hospital (Westfield).

### **Pathway 3: Member Self-Referral (4%)**

**How it works:**
- Member finds your agency via Google, referral, Council on Aging
- Verify HNE Medicare coverage
- Contact HNE Provider Services (800-310-2835) to verify benefits, authorization
- Complete assessment, submit care plan to care manager
- Care manager authorizes
- Begin care

**Conversion rate:** 85-92%

**Volume potential:** 1-2 member-initiated referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Optimize Google Business Profile for "Health New England home care Springfield," "HNE Medicare home care Holyoke."

### **Pathway 4: Primary Care Physician Referrals (1%)**

**How it works:**
- Member visits PCP
- PCP identifies home care need
- PCP notifies HNE care manager
- Care manager contacts member, then agencies
- You complete assessment, care manager authorizes

**Conversion rate:** 75-85%

**Volume potential:** 0-1 PCP referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Focus on high-volume HNE practices (Baystate Medical Practices, Community Health Center of Franklin County, Caring Health Center Springfield).

### **Pathway 5: Supplemental Benefits Referrals (<1%)**

**How it works:**
- Member inquires about supplemental home care benefits
- Directed to care coordinator
- Agencies contacted
- Complete assessment, begin services

**Conversion rate:** 65-80%

**Volume potential:** 0-1 supplemental benefit referrals monthly

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "Geographic limitation - only valuable for Western MA agencies"**

**Reality:** Must serve Western MA to benefit from HNE.

**Solution:**
- If you serve Western MA: HNE is ESSENTIAL payer (dominant in region)
- If you don't serve Western MA: Focus on other Medicare plans
- Consider expanding to Western MA (HNE + Baystate + lower competition make attractive market)

### **Challenge 2: "Smaller member base than Tufts/BCBSMA"**

**Reality:** 18,000 members vs. 100,000 (Tufts) or 80,000 (BCBSMA).

**Solution:**
- Geographic concentration offsets smaller size
- Western MA market dominance (70%+ market share in Springfield)
- Less competition (fewer agencies)
- Community relationships easier
- Complement with other payers

### **Challenge 3: "Need Spanish-speaking caregivers"**

**Reality:** Springfield/Holyoke have large Puerto Rican populations.

**Solution:**
- Recruit Spanish-speaking caregivers (large available workforce in Springfield/Holyoke)
- Partner with Latino organizations (Nueva Esperanza, Enlace de Familias)
- Advertise in Spanish media
- Contact HNE care managers when you add Spanish caregivers (major need)

**Opportunity:** Spanish language care is CRITICAL differentiator for HNE referrals.

### **Challenge 4: "Credentialing delays"**

**Solution:**
- Submit complete application
- Respond within 24-48 hours
- Email provider.services@hne.com weekly for status
- Escalate if stalled beyond 12 weeks

**Note:** HNE credentialing efficient for Western MA providers (6-10 weeks typical).

### **Challenge 5: "I'm credentialed but not receiving referrals"**

**Solution:**
- Introduce yourself to care managers (emphasize Western MA presence)
- Attend HNE Springfield events
- Respond rapidly to first referrals
- Update provider profile

- Build Baystate relationships

**Timeframe:** 2-4 month ramp-up
