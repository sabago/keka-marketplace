## Overview

MassHealth Long-Term Services and Supports (LTSS) is **the essential enrollment** for Massachusetts home care agencies serving Medicaid-eligible clients. With 1.9 million MassHealth members, enrolling as an LTSS provider opens access to the state's largest payer network, including the Personal Care Attendant (PCA) program and Frail Elder Waiver.

**Why MassHealth LTSS enrollment matters:**
- **1.9 million members:** Largest potential client base in Massachusetts
- **Guaranteed payment:** State reimbursement is reliable and predictable
- **Multiple programs:** PCA, Frail Elder Waiver, Community First, Adult Day Health
- **Stable relationships:** Long-term clients with ongoing care needs
- **Required for SCO plans:** Many Senior Care Options plans require MassHealth enrollment first
- **Union rates:** PCA program rates ~$21-24/hour per collective bargaining agreements

**Investment:** 100% FREE (no enrollment fees, only administrative time)

**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks for approval after complete application submission

## Who This Referral Source Serves

**Primary populations:**
- **Low-income seniors (65+):** Income ≤138% federal poverty level
- **Adults with disabilities:** Physical, cognitive, developmental disabilities
- **Frail elders:** MassHealth Standard members needing LTSS
- **Dual-eligible beneficiaries:** Medicare + MassHealth coverage
- **LTSS-eligible children:** Pediatric home care through Katie Beckett Waiver

**Geographic coverage:** All Massachusetts residents (statewide)

**Program breakdown:**

**1. Personal Care Attendant (PCA) Program**
- Consumer-directed model for personal care services
- Member controls hiring, scheduling, supervision of PCA
- Agency serves as PCM (Personal Care Management) entity
- 60,000+ active PCA users

**2. Frail Elder Waiver**
- Alternative to nursing facility care for seniors 65+
- Comprehensive home and community-based services
- Care coordination, adult day health, homemaker, personal care
- 8,000+ members annually

**3. Community First LTSS**
- MassHealth Standard members needing LTSS
- Personal care, adult day health, homemaker services
- Not waiver-based (entitlement program)

**4. Adult Foster Care**
- Residential care in community homes
- Alternative to nursing facilities
- Smaller program but specialized need

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Use This as a Referral Source

**MassHealth LTSS creates THREE referral pathways:**

### **Pathway 1: Direct Member Referrals**

**How it works:**
- MassHealth member or family contacts your agency directly
- You verify MassHealth eligibility through LTSS portal
- Complete assessment, submit care plan for authorization
- MassHealth approves services, you begin care
- Submit claims for reimbursement

**Conversion rate:** 60-75% (members seeking home care are highly motivated)

### **Pathway 2: Hospital Discharge Referrals**

**How it works:**
- Hospital discharge planner identifies MassHealth patient needing home care
- Discharge planner refers to LTSS provider network
- Your agency (if enrolled) is included in referral list
- You complete assessment within 24-48 hours of discharge
- MassHealth authorizes services for smooth transition home

**Conversion rate:** 50-65% (competitive with multiple agencies responding)

### **Pathway 3: MassHealth Care Coordinator Referrals**

**How it works:**
- MassHealth Enrollment Centers have care coordinators
- Care coordinators identify members needing LTSS
- They refer members to enrolled LTSS provider agencies
- You complete assessment, submit authorization request
- MassHealth approves, you start services

**Conversion rate:** 70-85% (care coordinator endorsement increases trust)

## Provider Network Credentialing Process

**Step-by-step enrollment as MassHealth LTSS provider:**

### **Step 1: Determine Provider Type (Week 1)**

**Choose your provider category:**

**Option A: Personal Care Management (PCM) Agency**
- Manage PCA program services (consumer-directed model)
- Coordinate with Tempus Unlimited (fiscal intermediary)
- Help members hire, train, supervise PCAs
- Most common for non-medical home care agencies

**Option B: Home Health Agency**
- Skilled nursing, therapy services
- Requires professional licensure (RN, PT, OT, etc.)
- Medical model (not consumer-directed)

**Option C: Homemaker Agency**
- Light housekeeping, meal preparation
- No personal care services
- Smaller scope than PCM agency

**Most agencies choose PCM agency enrollment** for maximum flexibility

### **Step 2: Gather Required Documentation (Weeks 1-2)**

**Required documents:**
- [ ] **Business documentation:** Articles of incorporation, operating agreement, DBA certificate
- [ ] **Tax documents:** W-9, EIN verification, Tax ID
- [ ] **Licenses/permits:** Massachusetts business registration, local permits (if applicable)
- [ ] **Insurance certificates:**
  - General liability: $1-2 million per occurrence
  - Professional liability: $1 million (recommended)
  - Workers' compensation: Required if you have employees
- [ ] **Background check policy:** Written CORI/SORI screening procedures
- [ ] **Policies and procedures:**
  - Client rights and responsibilities
  - Service delivery standards
  - Complaint/grievance process
  - Emergency procedures
  - Confidentiality/HIPAA compliance
- [ ] **References:** 3-5 professional references (clients, partners, healthcare providers)
- [ ] **Organizational chart:** Agency leadership, reporting structure
- [ ] **Staff qualifications:** Training requirements, caregiver credentials

### **Step 3: Complete Online Application (Weeks 2-3)**

**Application portal:** masshealthltss.com/provider-enrollment

**Application sections:**
1. **Agency information:** Legal name, DBA, address, contact information
2. **Ownership structure:** Owners, officers, directors (background checks required)
3. **Service capabilities:** Services offered, geographic coverage area
4. **Staffing:** Number of employees, caregiver qualifications
5. **Compliance attestations:** HIPAA, fraud/abuse, MassHealth policies
6. **Upload documentation:** All documents from Step 2

**Pro tip:** Save application progress frequently. Portal times out after 30 minutes of inactivity.

### **Step 4: Background Checks for All Owners/Officers (Weeks 3-4)**

**Required screenings:**
- **CORI (Criminal Offender Record Information):** Massachusetts criminal history
- **SORI (Sex Offender Registry Information):** MA sex offender database
- **OIG exclusion check:** Federal Office of Inspector General exclusion list
- **SAM exclusion check:** System for Award Management (federal contracts)

**MassHealth conducts these checks automatically** after application submission. No action required from agency (besides disclosing any prior convictions).

### **Step 5: Site Visit (Optional - Weeks 4-6)**

**Some provider types require on-site review:**
- MassHealth surveyor visits agency office
- Reviews physical space, records, policies
- Interviews staff (if applicable)
- Ensures compliance with LTSS standards

**PCM agencies:** Site visit not always required (MassHealth decides case-by-case)

### **Step 6: Application Review (Weeks 4-8)**

**MassHealth LTSS provider enrollment team reviews:**
- Application completeness
- Background check results
- Documentation verification
- Compliance with LTSS provider standards

**Common delays:**
- Missing documentation (always submit complete application)
- Background check issues (disclose any convictions upfront)
- Insurance certificate gaps (ensure continuous coverage)
- Incomplete policies/procedures (have attorney or consultant review)

**Pro tip:** Email provider@masshealthquestions.com weekly for status updates. Squeaky wheel gets processed faster.

### **Step 7: Approval & Provider Agreement (Week 8+)**

**Upon approval:**
- Receive MassHealth provider ID number
- Sign provider service agreement (contract with MassHealth)
- Complete W-9 for payment setup
- Gain access to LTSS portal for authorizations, claims, member verification

**Effective date:** Typically first day of month following approval

## Tips to Get More Referrals from This Source

### **Strategy 1: Build Relationships with Hospital Discharge Planners**

**Action steps:**
- Identify 5-10 hospitals in your service area with high MassHealth volume
- Contact discharge planning/case management departments
- Introduce your agency, share MassHealth LTSS provider ID
- Ask to be added to preferred referral list for MassHealth patients
- Visit hospitals quarterly to maintain relationship

**Hospitals to prioritize:**
- Boston Medical Center (highest MassHealth volume in state)
- Cambridge Health Alliance
- Lowell General Hospital
- Mercy Medical Center (Springfield)
- Signature Healthcare Brockton Hospital

### **Strategy 2: Partner with MassHealth Enrollment Centers**

**MassHealth Enrollment Centers (MECs) help members access LTSS:**
- 27 locations statewide
- Care coordinators assess members for LTSS eligibility
- MECs maintain preferred provider lists

**Action steps:**
- Locate MECs in your service area: mass.gov/masshealth-enrollment-centers
- Email care coordination supervisor, introduce your agency
- Drop off agency brochures, service descriptions
- Attend MEC provider networking events (quarterly)

### **Strategy 3: Market Directly to MassHealth Members**

**Target channels:**
- **Council on Aging partnerships:** Present at COA health fairs, senior centers
- **Community health centers:** Post flyers, leave brochures in waiting rooms
- **Housing authorities:** Senior housing complexes have high MassHealth concentration
- **Church/faith communities:** Gateway cities (Springfield, Worcester, Brockton, Lawrence, Lowell)
- **Google Ads:** Target "MassHealth home care," "PCA agency near me"

**Pro tip:** Many MassHealth members lack internet access. Print materials, in-person outreach, and phone contact are essential.

### **Strategy 4: Specialize in PCA Program (Consumer-Directed Model)**

**Why PCA program drives volume:**
- 60,000+ MassHealth members use PCA program
- Consumer-directed model = member controls hiring (less competitive than agency-directed)
- PCM agencies provide administrative support (payroll, training, compliance)
- Members seek PCM agencies with responsive service, bilingual staff, cultural competency

**Action steps:**
- Register as PCM agency (separate from general LTSS enrollment)
- Partner with Tempus Unlimited (fiscal intermediary): tempusunlimited.org
- List agency on Mass PCA Directory: masspcadirectory.org
- Train staff on consumer-directed model (different from traditional home care)

### **Strategy 5: Respond to Referrals FAST**

**MassHealth members often contact multiple agencies:**
- First agency to respond usually gets the client
- Respond within 4 hours (during business hours)
- Offer assessment within 24-48 hours
- Confirm MassHealth eligibility immediately

**Tool recommendation:** Mastering Home Care's intake module helps track referrals, set response time alerts, ensure no lead falls through cracks.

### **Strategy 6: Maintain High Quality Ratings**

**MassHealth tracks provider performance:**
- Member satisfaction surveys
- Service authorization compliance
- Claims accuracy and timeliness
- Complaint/grievance rates

**High-performing providers get prioritized referrals from care coordinators**

**Action steps:**
- Survey clients quarterly (identify issues proactively)
- Submit authorizations on time (don't wait until last minute)
- File clean claims (reduce denials, delays)
- Resolve complaints quickly, document resolutions

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "Application process takes 6+ months"**

**Reality:** Average approval time is 6-8 weeks for complete applications, but many agencies experience 3-6 month delays.

**Why delays happen:**
- Incomplete documentation (most common cause)
- Background check issues (convictions, OIG exclusions)
- Missing insurance certificates
- Policy/procedure documentation insufficient
- Application errors or inconsistencies

**Solution:**
- Submit 100% complete application first time (don't submit partial, plan to complete later)
- Hire consultant or attorney to review application before submission
- Ensure insurance certificates show adequate coverage, continuous dates
- Follow up weekly via email/phone for status updates
- Escalate to MassHealth Ombudsman if stalled beyond 90 days: mass.gov/masshealth-ombudsman

### **Challenge 2: "I'm enrolled but haven't received any MassHealth referrals"**

**Reality:** LTSS enrollment alone doesn't generate referrals. You must actively market.

**Solution:**
- List agency on Mass PCA Directory: masspcadirectory.org (free, searchable by members)
- Build hospital discharge planner relationships (see Strategy 1 above)
- Partner with MassHealth Enrollment Centers (see Strategy 2)
- Market directly to MassHealth members via COAs, community health centers, housing authorities
- Register agency with Google Business Profile, optimize for "MassHealth home care" searches
- Join MassHealth provider webinars, networking events to build visibility

### **Challenge 3: "Authorization requests take too long"**

**Typical timeline:** 3-5 business days for routine authorizations, 24 hours for urgent/hospital discharge

**Delays happen when:**
- Incomplete assessment documentation
- Missing physician orders (required for some LTSS services)
- Care plan doesn't meet medical necessity criteria
- Member eligibility issues (not enrolled in correct MassHealth plan)

**Solution:**
- Verify member eligibility BEFORE assessment (use LTSS portal)
- Ensure assessment is comprehensive (ADLs, IADLs, functional limitations, medical conditions)
- Obtain physician orders before submitting authorization (if required)
- Call MassHealth provider line 800-841-2900 if authorization pending beyond 5 days
- Build relationships with specific MassHealth authorization staff (they can expedite review)

### **Challenge 4: "Claims denials or payment delays"**

**Common denial reasons:**
- Services provided before authorization approved
- Services exceed authorized hours/frequency
- Claims submitted beyond filing deadline (180 days from service date)
- Member not eligible on date of service
- Incorrect billing codes or modifiers

**Solution:**
- NEVER start services before authorization approval (wait for approval notice)
- Track authorized hours carefully, request increase before exceeding limit
- Submit claims within 30 days of service (don't wait until 180-day deadline)
- Verify member eligibility monthly (members can lose coverage if they don't recertify)
- Invest in billing software compatible with MassHealth LTSS (reduces coding errors)
- Appeal denied claims within 60 days (often overturned on appeal)

### **Challenge 5: "Consumer-directed PCA model is unfamiliar"**

**Traditional home care:** Agency hires, trains, supervises, schedules caregivers

**PCA consumer-directed model:** Member is employer (hires, trains, supervises, schedules PCA). Agency (PCM) provides administrative support only.

**Challenges:**
- Members sometimes struggle to manage PCAs (no-shows, performance issues)
- PCM agency has limited control over caregiver quality
- Member complaints about PCAs reflect on PCM agency

**Solution:**
- Train staff on consumer-directed philosophy (member empowerment, choice, control)
- Provide robust PCA training resources (online courses, in-person training, mentorship)
- Connect members with PCA support groups, peer mentors
- Partner closely with Tempus Unlimited (they handle payroll, tax, workers' comp)
- Set clear expectations with members upfront about PCM vs. PCA roles
- Document all training, support provided to members (demonstrates PCM agency fulfilled responsibilities)
