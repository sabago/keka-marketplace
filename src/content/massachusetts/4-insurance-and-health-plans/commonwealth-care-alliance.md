## Overview

Commonwealth Care Alliance (CCA) is **Massachusetts' largest dual-eligible health plan**, serving over 100,000 members through its Senior Care Options (SCO) program and Medicare Advantage Special Needs Plans. CCA provides integrated Medicare and MassHealth coverage for seniors and adults with disabilities, making it a high-value referral source for home care agencies.

**Why CCA matters for home care agencies:**
- **100,000+ members:** Largest member base of any dual-eligible plan in MA
- **Mission-driven:** Non-profit, member-centered care philosophy (easier to partner with than for-profit plans)
- **Care coordinator model:** 200+ care coordinators actively connecting members with home care agencies
- **Comprehensive coverage:** Medical + social services, home care fully integrated into benefit design
- **CareSource acquisition (April 2025):** Merger brings additional resources, maintains CCA's member-first approach
- **Long-term relationships:** Members stay with CCA average 4-6 years (chronic condition management, aging in place)

**Investment:** FREE credentialing (no enrollment fees)

**Timeline:** 6-10 weeks for provider approval

## Who This Referral Source Serves

**CCA member profile:**

### **Primary populations:**
- **Dual-eligible seniors (65+):** Medicare + MassHealth coverage (low-income seniors)
- **Adults under 65 with disabilities:** Physical disabilities, chronic conditions, intellectual disabilities
- **Frail elders:** Complex medical needs, high care coordination requirements
- **Post-hospitalization:** Members transitioning home after hospital/SNF stays

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Enrolled in both Medicare Parts A & B
- Eligible for MassHealth (income ≤138% federal poverty level)
- Live in CCA service area (most of Massachusetts)
- Choose CCA as their health plan

### **CCA Programs:**

**1. CCA Senior Care Options (SCO)**
- Largest SCO plan in Massachusetts (75,000+ members)
- Integrated Medicare + MassHealth coverage
- All services coordinated through CCA network
- Care coordinators manage home care referrals

**2. CCA Medicare Advantage D-SNP (Dual-Eligible Special Needs Plan)**
- Medicare Advantage plan for dual-eligible adults under 65
- Similar benefits to SCO, younger population
- 15,000+ members

**3. CCA One Care (Adults with Disabilities)**
- Integrated Medicare + MassHealth for adults 21-64 with disabilities
- Smaller program (~10,000 members)
- High home care utilization rate

**Geographic coverage:** Most of Massachusetts (excludes Berkshire, Franklin, Hampden, Hampshire counties in western MA)

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Use This as a Referral Source

**CCA creates FOUR referral pathways:**

### **Pathway 1: Care Coordinator Direct Referrals (PRIMARY)**

**How it works:**
- CCA's 200+ care coordinators assess members' needs
- Care coordinator identifies member needing home care services
- Care coordinator contacts 2-3 in-network home care agencies
- Your agency responds within 4-24 hours
- Complete assessment, submit care plan to care coordinator for approval
- CCA authorizes services, you begin care

**Conversion rate:** 70-85% (care coordinator endorsement builds trust)

**Volume potential:** Established agencies receive 2-5 care coordinator referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Build personal relationships with care coordinators. They refer to agencies they know and trust.

### **Pathway 2: Hospital/SNF Discharge Referrals**

**How it works:**
- CCA member hospitalized or in skilled nursing facility
- CCA care coordinator coordinates discharge planning with hospital team
- Care coordinator arranges home care for safe transition home
- In-network agencies contacted for rapid response (assessment within 24 hours of discharge)

**Conversion rate:** 60-75% (competitive, multiple agencies responding)

**Volume potential:** 1-3 hospital discharge referrals monthly (higher if you build hospital relationships)

**Pro tip:** Respond FAST. First agency to complete assessment usually wins.

### **Pathway 3: Member Self-Referral**

**How it works:**
- CCA member finds your agency via Google, COA, friend referral
- During intake, you verify CCA coverage
- You contact member's care coordinator for authorization
- Care coordinator approves, you start services

**Conversion rate:** 80-90% (member already chose your agency)

**Volume potential:** 1-2 member-initiated referrals monthly (varies by marketing efforts)

**Pro tip:** Optimize Google Business Profile for "CCA home care," "Senior Care Options home care near me"

### **Pathway 4: Primary Care Physician Referrals**

**How it works:**
- CCA member visits PCP (primary care physician)
- PCP identifies need for home care (post-hospitalization, declining function, family caregiver burnout)
- PCP notifies CCA care coordinator
- Care coordinator contacts in-network home care agencies
- You complete assessment, care coordinator approves

**Conversion rate:** 75-85% (physician referral = medical necessity documented)

**Volume potential:** 1-2 PCP referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Build relationships with high-volume CCA primary care practices (CCA owns several health centers, also contracts with community health centers)

## Provider Network Credentialing Process

**Step-by-step credentialing with CCA:**

### **Step 1: Verify Prerequisites (Week 1)**

**Before applying to CCA, ensure you have:**
- [ ] **MassHealth LTSS provider enrollment** (required for CCA network participation)
- [ ] **Massachusetts business registration** (DBA, articles of incorporation)
- [ ] **Liability insurance:** General liability ($1-2M), professional liability ($1M)
- [ ] **Workers' compensation insurance** (if you have employees)
- [ ] **Background check policies:** CORI/SORI screening procedures

**If not yet MassHealth enrolled:** See [MassHealth LTSS Provider Enrollment Guide](/massachusetts/4-insurance-and-health-plans/masshealth-ltss-provider-enrollment) (complete this first, 4-8 week process)

### **Step 2: Submit Initial Application (Weeks 1-2)**

**Application methods:**

**Option A: Online application (preferred)**
- Website: commonwealthcarealliance.org/providers
- Navigate to "Join Our Network"
- Complete Provider Enrollment Application
- Upload required documents (see list below)

**Option B: Paper application**
- Download application: commonwealthcarealliance.org/provider-forms
- Mail completed application to:
  Commonwealth Care Alliance
  Provider Network Development
  30 Winter Street, Suite 100
  Boston, MA 02108

**Required documents:**
- [ ] W-9 tax form
- [ ] MassHealth LTSS provider ID
- [ ] Copy of Massachusetts business registration
- [ ] Certificate of liability insurance (COI)
- [ ] Certificate of workers' compensation insurance
- [ ] Background check policy (written procedure)
- [ ] Client rights and responsibilities policy
- [ ] Complaint/grievance policy
- [ ] Emergency procedures
- [ ] Services description (homemaker, personal care, companion care, etc.)
- [ ] Staff qualifications summary
- [ ] References (3-5 professional references)

**Timeline:** Application processing begins within 5 business days of submission

### **Step 3: Credentialing Review (Weeks 2-6)**

**CCA Provider Network team reviews:**
- Application completeness
- MassHealth LTSS enrollment verification
- Insurance coverage adequacy
- Background checks for owners/officers (CCA conducts independently)
- References (CCA contacts 2-3 references)
- Compliance with CCA network standards

**Common follow-up requests:**
- Additional insurance documentation (ensure no coverage gaps)
- Clarification on service capabilities (which services you provide in-home)
- Updated policies (CCA may request specific language in client rights policy)

**Pro tip:** Respond to follow-up requests within 24-48 hours. Delays extend credentialing timeline.

### **Step 4: Site Visit (Optional - Weeks 4-6)**

**CCA may conduct on-site visit:**
- CCA network development representative visits agency office
- Reviews physical space, record-keeping systems
- Interviews agency leadership about service model
- Ensures compliance with regulatory standards

**Not all applicants receive site visit** (CCA prioritizes new agencies, higher-risk service types)

**Preparation:**
- Ensure office is professional, organized
- Have policies/procedures easily accessible
- Prepare 1-page agency overview (history, specialties, outcomes)

### **Step 5: Contract Execution (Weeks 6-10)**

**Upon approval:**
- CCA sends Provider Service Agreement (contract)
- Review contract terms carefully (reimbursement rates, authorization requirements, termination clauses)
- Sign and return within 10 business days
- CCA countersigns, returns executed copy

**Contract highlights:**
- **Reimbursement rates:** Negotiated based on service type (homemaker, personal care, companion care)
- **Authorization requirements:** Pre-authorization required for all services
- **Claims submission:** 60 days from service date
- **Quality metrics:** Member satisfaction, service plan compliance
- **Term:** Typically 3-year initial term, auto-renews

**Effective date:** First day of month following contract execution

### **Step 6: Provider Portal Training (Week 10+)**

**CCA provides access to Provider Portal:**
- Member eligibility verification
- Authorization requests and status
- Care coordination notes
- Claims submission
- Payment status and remittance

**Training offered:**
- Live webinar (2 hours, offered monthly)
- Recorded training videos (available 24/7)
- User manual (PDF download)
- Technical support: 866-420-9332

**Pro tip:** Assign 1-2 staff members to complete training, become portal "super users"

## Tips to Get More Referrals from This Source

### **Strategy 1: Build Personal Relationships with Care Coordinators**

**Care coordinators control referral flow:**

**Action steps:**
- Request care coordinator contact list from Provider Services (organized by region)
- Email 5-10 care coordinators in your service area, introduce your agency
- Highlight differentiators: bilingual staff, weekend availability, specialized training (dementia, Parkinson's, stroke recovery)
- Offer to meet for coffee, virtual intro call
- Attend CCA provider networking events (quarterly lunch-and-learns)
- When care coordinator sends referral, follow up personally to thank them (builds relationship)

**Pro tip:** Care coordinators remember agencies that are responsive, reliable, communicative. Be the agency they can count on.

### **Strategy 2: Respond to Referrals FAST**

**CCA care coordinators contact 2-3 agencies per referral:**

**Speed wins:**
- Answer Provider Services calls immediately (display 866-420-9332 in phone system)
- Check email every 2-4 hours during business hours
- Return care coordinator voicemails within 1 hour
- Offer assessment same-day or next-day (especially hospital discharges)

**Data:** Agencies responding within 4 hours win 80% of competitive referrals vs. 30% for next-day response.

**Tool recommendation:** Mastering Home Care's referral tracking alerts staff instantly when CCA referrals arrive, ensuring rapid response.

### **Strategy 3: Attend CCA Provider Events**

**CCA hosts quarterly provider networking events:**
- Provider appreciation lunches
- Clinical training webinars
- Policy update sessions
- Annual provider summit

**Benefits:**
- Meet care coordinators face-to-face
- Learn about CCA initiatives, priorities
- Network with other providers (learn best practices)
- Demonstrate your engagement (care coordinators remember agencies that show up)

**Access:** After credentialing, receive email invitations. RSVP promptly (popular events fill up).

### **Strategy 4: Specialize in High-Need Services**

**CCA has network gaps in these areas:**

**Dementia/Alzheimer's care:**
- 40%+ of CCA members have cognitive impairment
- Few agencies have certified dementia care specialists
- Training: Alzheimer's Association offers certification programs

**Non-English languages:**
- Spanish (largest non-English group)
- Portuguese (New Bedford, Fall River)
- Haitian Creole (Boston, Brockton)
- Cape Verdean Creole (Boston)
- Mandarin (Boston, Quincy)
- Vietnamese (Worcester, Lowell)

**Complex medical needs:**
- Hoyer lift transfers
- Feeding tube care
- Oxygen management
- Post-stroke rehabilitation support

**Action steps:**
- Train staff in specialty areas
- Update CCA provider profile to highlight specialties
- Email care coordinators when you add new specialty service
- Market specialty services to CCA members directly (Google Ads, COA presentations)

### **Strategy 5: Maintain Excellent Quality Metrics**

**CCA tracks provider performance:**
- Member satisfaction surveys
- Service plan compliance
- No-show rates
- Complaint resolution

**High-performing providers receive preferential referrals**

**Action steps:**
- Survey CCA clients quarterly (identify issues before they become complaints)
- Respond to member concerns within 24 hours
- Submit care plan updates proactively (don't wait for care coordinator to request)
- Reduce no-shows: Confirm visits 24 hours in advance, have backup caregivers available
- Attend CCA quality improvement webinars (demonstrates commitment)

### **Strategy 6: Partner with CCA-Affiliated Primary Care Practices**

**CCA owns several primary care practices:**
- CCA Health Centers (Boston, Lynn, Brockton)
- Multiple contracted community health centers

**Action steps:**
- Identify CCA-affiliated practices in your area (ask Provider Services for list)
- Contact practice care coordinators, introduce your agency
- Offer to present at practice team meetings (educate about home care services)
- When CCA member referred by PCP, call PCP office to thank for referral (builds relationship)

**Pro tip:** PCPs refer to home care agencies they know. Visibility = referrals.

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "Credentialing takes 4-6 months"**

**Reality:** Target timeline is 6-10 weeks, but delays extend to 4-6 months if application incomplete or follow-up slow.

**Why delays happen:**
- Missing MassHealth LTSS enrollment (prerequisite)
- Incomplete documentation
- Insurance certificate gaps
- Background check issues
- Slow response to CCA follow-up requests

**Solution:**
- Complete MassHealth LTSS enrollment FIRST (don't apply to CCA until LTSS approved)
- Submit 100% complete application (checklist every document)
- Respond to CCA requests within 24-48 hours
- Email networkdevelopment@commonwealthcarealliance.org weekly for status updates
- Escalate if stalled beyond 12 weeks (contact Provider Services supervisor)

### **Challenge 2: "I'm credentialed but haven't received referrals"**

**Reality:** Credentialing doesn't automatically generate referrals. Care coordinators refer to agencies they know and trust.

**Solution:**
- Introduce yourself to care coordinators (email, phone, in-person meetings)
- Attend CCA provider events (visibility = referrals)
- Respond rapidly to first few referrals (prove reliability)
- Market directly to CCA members (Google, COA, community health centers)
- Update CCA provider profile with specialties, languages, service area

**Timeframe:** Expect 2-4 month ramp-up period as care coordinators learn about your agency.

### **Challenge 3: "Authorization requests take too long"**

**Typical timeline:** 2-3 business days for routine authorizations, 24 hours for urgent (hospital discharge)

**Delays happen when:**
- Incomplete assessment documentation
- Care plan doesn't align with member's CCA care plan
- Member not eligible for requested services
- Authorization request submitted to wrong care coordinator

**Solution:**
- Submit comprehensive assessment (ADLs, IADLs, functional limitations, safety concerns, family caregiver status)
- Review member's CCA care plan before submitting authorization (available in Provider Portal)
- Verify member eligibility day of assessment (coverage can change)
- Contact member's assigned care coordinator directly (faster than submitting via portal)
- For urgent needs: Call Provider Services 866-420-9332, request expedited review

### **Challenge 4: "Care coordinators micromanage service delivery"**

**Some agencies find CCA care coordinators highly involved:**
- Frequent check-ins on member status
- Requests to adjust care plans
- Monitoring visit compliance closely

**Why this happens:** CCA's model is intensive care coordination (especially for high-risk members). Care coordinators are accountable for member outcomes.

**Solution:**
- Embrace partnership approach (care coordinators want members to succeed, same as you)
- Communicate proactively (don't wait for care coordinator to ask for updates)
- Alert care coordinator immediately if concerns arise (member declining, family caregiver burnout, safety issues)
- View care coordinator as resource (they can authorize additional services, connect member with other supports)

**Mindset shift:** CCA care coordinators are partners, not adversaries.

### **Challenge 5: "Reimbursement rates are lower than private pay"**

**Reality:** CCA rates are typically 20-30% lower than private pay rates.

**Trade-off:**
- Lower rates BUT higher volume, longer client relationships, guaranteed payment

**Solution:**
- Treat CCA as stable revenue base (diversify with private pay, other insurers)
- Negotiate rates during contract renewal (demonstrate value, outcomes)
- Leverage CCA volume to reduce per-client acquisition costs
- Factor in payment reliability (CCA pays on time, private pay clients may default)

**ROI calculation:** CCA member at $20/hour for 20 hours/week = $20,800/year vs. private pay at $28/hour for 10 hours/week = $14,560/year (CCA wins on volume)
