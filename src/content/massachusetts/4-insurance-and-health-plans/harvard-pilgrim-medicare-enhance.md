## Overview

Harvard Pilgrim Medicare Enhance is a **regional Medicare Advantage plan** serving 35,000+ Medicare beneficiaries across Massachusetts and southern New Hampshire. As part of Point32Health (formed from the 2021 merger of Harvard Pilgrim Health Care and Tufts Health Plan), Medicare Enhance offers comprehensive Medicare Advantage coverage with strong supplemental benefits and a reputation for quality and member satisfaction.

**Why Harvard Pilgrim Medicare Enhance matters for home care agencies:**
- **35,000+ Medicare members:** Growing Medicare Advantage plan in MA/NH
- **Point32Health resources:** Access to combined infrastructure of Harvard Pilgrim + Tufts Health Plan
- **Quality focus:** Consistently 4+ star Medicare rating (quality-driven, values strong provider partnerships)
- **Care coordination model:** 80+ care coordinators actively managing home care referrals
- **Supplemental benefits:** Home care services included in most plans (personal care, homemaker, companion)
- **Member demographics:** Educated, engaged seniors (historically strong in Boston suburbs, North Shore, MetroWest)
- **Long member tenure:** Average 5+ years (high retention, aging in place support)
- **Non-profit mission:** Member-centered care philosophy (easier partnerships than for-profit plans)

**Investment:** FREE credentialing (no enrollment fees)

**Timeline:** 8-12 weeks for provider approval

## Who This Referral Source Serves

**Harvard Pilgrim Medicare Enhance member profile:**

### **Primary populations:**
- **Medicare Advantage members (65+):** Traditional seniors with Medicare Parts A & B
- **Early retirees (under 65 with disabilities):** Long-term disability, qualifying conditions
- **Dual-eligible members:** Medicare + MassHealth (lower-income seniors)
- **Chronic condition members:** Diabetes, heart disease, COPD, arthritis (65%+ of members)
- **Post-acute care:** Hospital discharge, surgery recovery, rehabilitation support
- **Highly educated seniors:** College-educated professionals, engaged in healthcare decisions

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Age 65+ OR under 65 with Medicare-qualifying disability
- Enrolled in Medicare Parts A & B
- Live in Harvard Pilgrim service area (specific MA/NH counties)
- Not have End-Stage Renal Disease (ESRD) unless qualifying exception
- Choose Harvard Pilgrim during enrollment periods

### **Harvard Pilgrim Medicare Enhance Plans:**

**1. Medicare Enhance HMO**
- Largest plan (~20,000 members)
- Primary care physician (PCP) coordination required
- Specialist referrals needed
- Lower premiums ($0-60/month depending on county)
- Comprehensive supplemental benefits including home care
- Strong in Boston suburbs (Newton, Wellesley, Lexington, Brookline)

**2. Medicare Enhance HMO-POS (Point of Service)**
- Flexibility plan (~8,000 members)
- PCP for in-network coordination
- Can see out-of-network providers (higher cost-sharing)
- Moderate premiums ($50-100/month)
- Popular with members who travel, have out-of-state family

**3. Medicare Enhance D-SNP (Dual-Eligible Special Needs)**
- Dual-eligible plan (~5,000 members)
- Medicare + MassHealth integrated coverage
- $0 premium for most members
- Enhanced home care benefits (high utilization: 75%+ use home care)
- Intensive care management

**4. Medicare Enhance PPO**
- National network (~2,000 members in MA)
- No PCP requirement, no referrals needed
- See any provider nationwide
- Higher premiums ($100-175/month)
- Smaller plan but high per-member revenue potential

**Geographic coverage (Massachusetts):**

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from Harvard Pilgrim Medicare Enhance

**Harvard Pilgrim creates FIVE referral pathways:**

### **Pathway 1: Care Coordinator Direct Referrals (PRIMARY - 70%)**

**How it works:**
- Harvard Pilgrim's 80+ care coordinators conduct health risk assessments with members
- Care coordinator identifies member needing home care (ADL limitations, post-hospitalization, chronic disease support, family caregiver burnout)
- Care coordinator searches provider directory for credentialed agencies
- Care coordinator calls 2-3 agencies with member information
- Your agency responds within 4-24 hours
- Complete in-home assessment within 48-72 hours
- Submit care plan to care coordinator for review/approval
- Care coordinator authorizes services (typically 60-90 days initially)
- Begin services, document, submit claims

**Conversion rate:** 80-90% (care coordinator endorsement builds trust)

**Volume potential:** Established agencies receive 2-5 care coordinator referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Harvard Pilgrim care coordinators value education, outcomes data, evidence-based approaches. Emphasize quality, credentials, clinical expertise.

### **Pathway 2: Hospital/SNF Discharge Referrals (18%)**

**How it works:**
- Harvard Pilgrim member hospitalized (Mass General, Brigham & Women's, Beth Israel, Newton-Wellesley, North Shore Medical, Lahey)
- Hospital discharge planner contacts Harvard Pilgrim utilization management
- Care coordinator joins discharge planning (coordinates post-acute services)
- Care coordinator arranges home care for safe transition home
- In-network agencies contacted for rapid assessment (within 24 hours of discharge)
- First agency to respond and complete assessment typically selected

**Conversion rate:** 65-80% (competitive, multiple agencies responding)

**Volume potential:** 1-3 hospital discharge referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Build relationships with discharge planners at hospitals in Harvard Pilgrim's strong markets (Newton-Wellesley, North Shore Medical Center, Beverly Hospital, Lahey).

### **Pathway 3: Member Self-Referral (8%)**

**How it works:**
- Harvard Pilgrim member finds your agency via Google, referral from friend, Council on Aging
- Member or family calls your agency
- During intake, you verify Harvard Pilgrim coverage
- Contact Harvard Pilgrim Provider Services (888-333-4742) to verify benefits, authorization requirements
- Complete assessment, submit care plan to member's care coordinator
- Care coordinator reviews, authorizes services
- Begin care

**Conversion rate:** 85-90% (member already selected your agency)

**Volume potential:** 1-2 member-initiated referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Harvard Pilgrim members are educated, research-oriented. Optimize Google presence, emphasize credentials, share testimonials.

### **Pathway 4: Primary Care Physician Referrals (3%)**

**How it works:**
- Member visits PCP for routine or illness care
- PCP identifies need for home care (declining function, medication issues, post-hospitalization)
- PCP notifies Harvard Pilgrim care coordinator
- Care coordinator contacts member, discusses home care options
- Care coordinator contacts in-network agencies
- You complete assessment, care coordinator authorizes

**Conversion rate:** 75-85% (physician documentation supports authorization)

**Volume potential:** 0-2 PCP referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Focus on Harvard Vanguard Medical Associates (Atrius Health) practices - Harvard Pilgrim has strong relationship with Atrius.

### **Pathway 5: Supplemental Benefits Referrals (1%)**

**How it works:**
- Harvard Pilgrim plans include supplemental home care benefits
- Member calls Member Services about benefits
- Directed to supplemental benefits vendor or care coordinator
- Agencies contacted for service coordination
- Complete assessment, begin services (typically lower hours)

**Conversion rate:** 60-75%

**Volume potential:** 0-1 supplemental benefit referrals monthly

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "Smaller member base than Tufts/BCBSMA"**

**Reality:** 35,000 members vs. 100,000 (Tufts) or 80,000 (BCBSMA).

**Solution:**
- Focus on Harvard Pilgrim's geographic strongholds (Boston suburbs, North Shore, MetroWest)
- Leverage Point32Health connection (credential with both Harvard Pilgrim AND Tufts)
- Market directly to Harvard Pilgrim members (supplement care coordinator referrals)
- Build strong care coordinator relationships (smaller network = easier to stand out)

### **Challenge 2: "Credentialing takes 4-5 months"**

**Why delays happen:**
- Incomplete application
- Insurance gaps
- Reference delays
- Peak periods

**Solution:**
- Submit complete application
- Respond to requests within 24-48 hours
- Alert references in advance
- Email provider.services@harvardpilgrim.org weekly for status
- Escalate if stalled beyond 14 weeks

### **Challenge 3: "Geographic coverage limited"**

**Harvard Pilgrim strongest in specific areas:**

**Solution:**
- If you serve Boston suburbs, North Shore, MetroWest: Harvard Pilgrim excellent fit
- If you serve other regions: Consider Tufts, BCBSMA instead (broader coverage)
- Focus marketing on Harvard Pilgrim's strong markets

### **Challenge 4: "I'm credentialed but not receiving referrals"**

**Solution:**
- Introduce yourself to care coordinators (smaller team = easier to connect)
- Attend provider events
- Respond rapidly to first referrals
- Update provider profile
- Market directly to members

**Timeframe:** 2-4 month ramp-up period

### **Challenge 5: "Competing with established agencies"**

**Solution:**
- Specialize in underserved needs (non-English languages, specialty care)
- Response speed (be fastest to respond)
- Quality outcomes (prove value)
- Relationship building (personal connections overcome incumbency)
