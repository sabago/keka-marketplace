**Investment:** FREE credentialing (no enrollment fees)

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from Tufts Medicare Preferred

**Tufts Medicare Preferred creates FIVE referral pathways:**

### **Pathway 1: Care Manager Direct Referrals (PRIMARY - 60%)**

**How it works:**
- Tufts care managers (150+ statewide) assess member needs during health risk assessments
- Care manager identifies member needing home care (ADL limitations, post-hospitalization, caregiver support)
- Care manager accesses provider directory, identifies 2-3 credentialed agencies in member's area
- Care manager calls agencies, provides member info (needs, preferences, insurance details)
- Your agency responds within 4-24 hours, confirms availability
- Complete in-home assessment within 48-72 hours
- Submit care plan to care manager for approval
- Tufts authorizes services (typically 30-90 days initial authorization)
- Begin services, submit claims through Tufts provider portal

**Conversion rate:** 75-85% (care manager endorsement builds member trust)

**Volume potential:** Established agencies receive 3-6 care manager referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Care managers refer to agencies they know personally. Introduce yourself, attend Tufts provider events, respond rapidly to build relationships.

### **Pathway 2: Hospital/SNF Discharge Referrals (25%)**

**How it works:**
- Tufts member hospitalized or in skilled nursing facility
- Hospital discharge planner contacts Tufts utilization management team
- Tufts care manager joins discharge planning (coordinates post-acute care)
- Care manager arranges home care for safe transition (medication management, wound care support, fall prevention)
- In-network agencies contacted for rapid assessment (within 24 hours of discharge)
- First agency to respond and complete assessment typically wins referral

**Conversion rate:** 60-75% (competitive, multiple agencies responding)

**Volume potential:** 2-4 hospital discharge referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Build relationships with hospital discharge planners at major Tufts partner hospitals (Mass General, Brigham & Women's, Beth Israel, Tufts Medical Center, Boston Medical Center). Attend discharge planning meetings.

### **Pathway 3: Member Self-Referral (10%)**

**How it works:**
- Tufts member finds your agency via Google, referral from friend/family, Council on Aging
- Member or family calls your agency inquiring about services
- During intake, you verify Tufts Medicare Preferred coverage
- You call Tufts Provider Services (866-783-1444) to verify benefits, authorization requirements
- You complete assessment, submit care plan to member's care manager
- Care manager reviews, authorizes services
- Begin care

**Conversion rate:** 85-90% (member already chose your agency)

**Volume potential:** 1-3 member-initiated referrals monthly (varies by marketing efforts)

**Pro tip:** Optimize Google Business Profile for "Tufts Medicare home care," "Tufts Medicare Preferred home care near me." Many members search online before calling care manager.

### **Pathway 4: Primary Care Physician Referrals (4%)**

**How it works:**
- Tufts member visits primary care physician (PCP)
- PCP identifies need for home care (declining function, medication non-adherence, recent hospitalization, family caregiver burnout)
- PCP documents need in medical record, notifies Tufts care manager
- Care manager contacts member, discusses home care options
- Care manager contacts in-network home care agencies
- You complete assessment, care manager approves

**Conversion rate:** 70-80% (physician referral = medical necessity documented)

**Volume potential:** 1-2 PCP referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Build relationships with high-volume Tufts primary care practices. Offer to present at practice care team meetings about home care services, when to refer.

### **Pathway 5: Supplemental Benefits Coordination (1%)**

**How it works:**
- Tufts Medicare Preferred includes supplemental benefits (personal care, homemaker services) in many plans
- Member calls Tufts Member Services about supplemental benefits
- Tufts refers member to vendor managing supplemental benefits (often Papa, Honor, or direct to in-network agencies)
- Vendor or care manager contacts agencies for service coordination
- You complete assessment, begin services (typically shorter-term, lower hours than care manager referrals)

**Conversion rate:** 50-65% (members may not fully understand benefits, may decline after learning limitations)

**Volume potential:** 1-2 supplemental benefit referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Understand which Tufts plans include supplemental home care benefits. D-SNP and C-SNP plans have most generous benefits.

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "Credentialing takes 6+ months"**

**Reality:** Target timeline is 8-12 weeks, but delays can extend to 6 months if application incomplete or responsiveness slow.

**Why delays happen:**
- Incomplete application (missing documents, unclear service descriptions)
- Insurance certificate issues (coverage gaps, insufficient limits)
- Background check delays (CORI/SORI processing, out-of-state providers)
- Reference unresponsive (Tufts contacts 3-5 references, slow responses delay process)
- Site visit scheduling conflicts
- Peak credentialing periods (January, July when many providers apply)

**Solution:**
- Submit 100% complete application (checklist every required document)
- Ensure insurance certificates current, adequate limits ($1M professional liability, $2M general liability minimum)
- Alert references you've provided their contact info (encourage prompt response)
- Respond to Tufts requests within 24-48 hours
- Email provider.relations@tuftshealthplan.com weekly for status updates
- Escalate if stalled beyond 14 weeks (request supervisor review)

**Pro tip:** Apply during slower credentialing periods (September-November) for faster processing.

### **Challenge 2: "I'm credentialed but not receiving referrals"**

**Reality:** Credentialing adds you to provider directory, but doesn't automatically generate referrals. Care managers refer to agencies they know and trust.

**Why this happens:**
- Care managers don't know your agency exists (large provider network)
- Other agencies have established relationships
- Your agency not appearing in care manager searches (profile incomplete or outdated)
- Service area mismatch (care manager seeking agency in location you don't serve)

**Solution:**
- Proactively introduce yourself to care managers (email, phone, in-person)
- Update provider profile with complete information (services, languages, specialties, availability)
- Attend Tufts provider events (visibility critical)
- Respond rapidly to first 2-3 referrals (prove reliability)
- Ask satisfied Tufts members to mention your agency to care managers
- Market directly to Tufts members (Google, COA, senior housing)

**Timeframe:** Expect 2-4 month ramp-up period after credentialing before consistent referrals flow.

**Pro tip:** Persistence pays off. Keep introducing yourself to care managers until referrals start flowing.

### **Challenge 3: "Authorization process is confusing"**

**Tufts authorization requirements vary by service type:**

**Services requiring prior authorization:**
- Personal care/homemaker services (ongoing)
- Skilled nursing visits
- Physical therapy (home-based)
- Occupational therapy (home-based)
- Medical equipment (hospital beds, wheelchairs, etc.)

**Services NOT requiring prior authorization:**
- Companion care (non-medical)
- Respite care (short-term)
- Some supplemental benefits (varies by plan)

**Authorization process:**
1. Complete comprehensive assessment (ADLs, IADLs, safety, caregiver status)
2. Submit authorization request via provider portal OR fax (866-609-0126)
3. Include: Assessment, care plan, physician orders (if applicable), member consent
4. Tufts reviews within 2-3 business days (urgent requests within 24 hours)
5. Approval received via portal/phone/fax
6. Begin services (within approved hours/timeframe)

**Solution:**
- Attend Tufts authorization training webinar (offered quarterly)
- Create authorization checklist (ensure complete submissions)
- Submit via portal (faster than fax)
- Call Provider Services for urgent authorizations (hospital discharges)
- Verify authorization status before starting services

**Pro tip:** Incomplete authorization requests main cause of delays. Submit comprehensive documentation upfront.

### **Challenge 4: "Care managers request frequent updates"**

**Some agencies find Tufts care managers request frequent status updates:**
- Monthly check-ins on member progress
- Updates after care plan changes
- Notification of any member concerns (health changes, family issues, medication problems)

**Why this happens:** Tufts care coordination model is intensive, especially for high-risk members (multiple chronic conditions, recent hospitalizations, frail elderly). Care managers accountable for member outcomes, HEDIS measures.

**Solution:**
- Embrace partnership mindset (care managers and agencies share goal: member success)
- Communicate proactively (don't wait for care manager to request updates)
- Alert care manager immediately if concerns arise (member declining, safety issues, family conflicts)
- Submit care plan updates via portal (care managers can access without calling you)
- View care manager requests as opportunity (they can authorize additional services, connect member with resources)

**Mindset shift:** Care managers are partners, not micromanagers. They want you to succeed because member success is their success.

### **Challenge 5: "Reimbursement rates below private pay"**

**Reality:** Tufts Medicare rates typically 15-25% lower than private pay rates.

**Typical Tufts rates:**
- Personal care/homemaker: $20-$26/hour (varies by region, service type)
- Companion care: $18-$24/hour
- Overnight care: $150-$200/night

**Private pay rates:**
- Personal care/homemaker: $28-$38/hour
- Companion care: $25-$35/hour
- Overnight care: $225-$300/night

**Trade-off analysis:**
- **Lower rates** BUT higher volume, longer relationships, guaranteed payment, lower marketing costs
- **Tufts member:** $20/hour x 20 hours/week x 52 weeks = $20,800/year
- **Private pay client:** $30/hour x 12 hours/week x 52 weeks = $18,720/year (lower volume, higher churn)

**Solution:**
- Diversify payer mix (Tufts + private pay + other insurers)
- Treat Tufts as stable revenue base (consistent cash flow)
- Factor in payment reliability (Tufts pays on time, private pay clients may default)
- Negotiate rates during contract renewal (after proving value)
- Leverage Tufts volume to reduce per-client acquisition costs (care manager referrals cheaper than marketing)

**ROI calculation:** Tufts may pay less per hour, but total revenue per client often higher due to volume and longevity.

### **Challenge 6: "Competing with established agencies"**

**Reality:** Tufts network includes 200+ home care agencies statewide. Care managers often have go-to agencies they've worked with for years.

**How to compete:**
- **Specialize:** Offer services established agencies don't (non-English languages, specialty care)
- **Response speed:** Be fastest to respond to referrals (beat established agencies on speed)
- **Quality:** Deliver exceptional service to first few Tufts members (word spreads among care managers)
- **Availability:** Offer evening/weekend service (many agencies 9-5 Monday-Friday only)
- **Geographic coverage:** Serve areas established agencies don't (rural areas, Cape Cod, Western MA)
- **Relationship building:** Invest time building care manager relationships (personal connections overcome incumbency)

**Pro tip:** New agencies can win significant Tufts volume within 2-3 years by outperforming established competitors on speed, quality, specialization.
