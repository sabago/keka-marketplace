## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from BCBSMA Medicare Advantage

**BCBSMA Medicare Advantage creates FIVE referral pathways:**

### **Pathway 1: Nurse Care Manager Direct Referrals (PRIMARY - 65%)**

**How it works:**
- BCBSMA's 120+ nurse care managers conduct health risk assessments with high-risk members
- Care manager identifies member needing home care (ADL decline, post-hospitalization, chronic disease management, family caregiver burnout)
- Care manager searches provider directory for credentialed agencies in member's service area
- Care manager calls 2-3 agencies, provides member information
- Your agency responds within 4-24 hours
- Complete in-home assessment within 48-72 hours (within 24 hours for hospital discharges)
- Submit comprehensive care plan to care manager via secure fax or provider portal
- Care manager reviews, authorizes services (initial authorization typically 60-90 days)
- Begin services, document visits, submit claims electronically

**Conversion rate:** 80-90% (nurse care manager endorsement + BCBSMA brand trust = high acceptance)

**Volume potential:** Established agencies receive 4-8 care manager referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** BCBSMA nurse care managers are registered nurses with clinical expertise. Build relationships by demonstrating clinical knowledge, quality outcomes, proactive communication.

### **Pathway 2: Hospital/SNF Discharge Referrals (20%)**

**How it works:**
- BCBSMA member hospitalized (Mass General, Brigham & Women's, Beth Israel, Lahey, etc.)
- Hospital discharge planner notifies BCBSMA utilization management team
- BCBSMA nurse care manager coordinates discharge planning (participates in hospital discharge meetings)
- Care manager arranges home care for safe transition (medication reconciliation support, wound care, mobility assistance, fall prevention)
- In-network agencies contacted for rapid response (assessment within 24 hours of discharge)
- First agency to complete assessment and submit care plan typically wins referral

**Conversion rate:** 65-80% (competitive, multiple agencies responding)

**Volume potential:** 2-5 hospital discharge referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** BCBSMA has strong partnerships with major Boston hospitals. Build direct relationships with hospital discharge planners at Mass General, Brigham & Women's, Beth Israel. Offer 24/7 availability for urgent discharges.

### **Pathway 3: Member Self-Referral (10%)**

**How it works:**
- BCBSMA member finds your agency via Google search, referral from friend/family, Council on Aging
- Member or family member calls your agency
- During intake, you verify BCBSMA Medicare Advantage coverage
- You contact BCBSMA Provider Services (800-262-2583) to verify home care benefits, authorization requirements
- Complete in-home assessment, submit care plan to member's assigned care manager
- Care manager reviews, authorizes services
- Begin care

**Conversion rate:** 85-95% (member already chose your agency, high intent)

**Volume potential:** 2-4 member-initiated referrals monthly (varies by marketing investment)

**Pro tip:** BCBSMA members are brand-conscious, quality-focused. Optimize Google Business Profile for "Blue Cross Blue Shield home care," emphasize quality credentials (Joint Commission, ACHC accreditation if applicable).

### **Pathway 4: Primary Care Physician Referrals (4%)**

**How it works:**
- BCBSMA member visits PCP for routine care or illness
- PCP identifies need for home care (declining function, medication non-adherence, chronic disease exacerbation, post-hospitalization follow-up)
- PCP documents need in medical record, contacts BCBSMA care manager
- Care manager calls member, discusses home care options
- Care manager contacts in-network home care agencies
- You complete assessment, care manager authorizes services

**Conversion rate:** 75-85% (physician referral = medical necessity documented, higher authorization approval rate)

**Volume potential:** 1-3 PCP referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** BCBSMA contracts with 5,000+ primary care physicians. Focus on high-volume practices affiliated with major hospital systems (Mass General Brigham, Beth Israel Lahey, Tufts Medicine).

### **Pathway 5: Supplemental Benefits Vendor Referrals (1%)**

**How it works:**
- BCBSMA Medicare plans include supplemental home care benefits (companion care, homemaker services, personal care)
- Member calls BCBSMA Member Services (800-678-2265) inquiring about benefits
- BCBSMA directs member to supplemental benefits vendor (often Papa, Honor, or direct to network agencies)
- Vendor or care manager contacts network agencies for coordination
- You complete assessment, begin services (typically lower hours than care manager referrals)

**Conversion rate:** 55-70% (members may not fully understand benefit limits, some decline after learning scope)

**Volume potential:** 1-2 supplemental benefit referrals monthly

**Pro tip:** Understand which BCBSMA plans include supplemental home care benefits. Blue Medicare HMO and Dual Care (D-SNP) have most generous benefits.

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "Credentialing takes 6-8 months"**

**Reality:** Target timeline is 10-14 weeks, but thorough credentialing process can extend to 6-8 months if issues arise.

**Why BCBSMA credentialing is thorough:**
- Comprehensive background checks (owners, officers, key staff)
- Detailed policy review (BCBSMA expects evidence-based care policies)
- Site visits (nearly all applicants receive site visit)
- Reference checks (BCBSMA contacts 4-5 references)
- Quality verification (accreditation status, quality improvement processes)

**Why delays happen:**
- Incomplete application (missing documents, unclear service descriptions)
- Policy deficiencies (BCBSMA expects comprehensive, professional policies)
- Insurance gaps (ensure no coverage gaps, adequate limits)
- Reference delays (unresponsive references slow process)
- Site visit scheduling challenges
- Peak periods (January, July when many providers apply)

**Solution:**
- Submit 100% complete application (triple-check document checklist)
- Invest in professional policies (consider consultant if needed)
- Ensure insurance certificates current, adequate limits ($1M professional liability, $2M general liability minimum)
- Alert references in advance (encourage prompt response to BCBSMA)
- Respond to BCBSMA requests within 24-48 hours
- Email provider.relations@bluecrossma.org weekly for status updates
- Escalate if stalled beyond 16 weeks (request supervisor review)
- Consider obtaining accreditation (Joint Commission, ACHC) - expedites credentialing

**Pro tip:** BCBSMA credentialing is thorough but worth it. Quality standards ensure reputable network, which benefits credentialed providers.

### **Challenge 2: "Site visit requirements are extensive"**

**BCBSMA conducts site visits for nearly all home care applicants:**

**What BCBSMA reviews:**
- Office space (professional, organized, secure records storage)
- Policies and procedures (clinical, administrative, compliance)
- Staff qualifications (certifications, training records, background checks)
- Quality improvement processes (satisfaction surveys, outcome tracking)
- Emergency procedures (backup staffing, on-call systems)
- Technology systems (EHR, scheduling, communication)

**Solution:**
- Prepare thoroughly (BCBSMA provides site visit checklist in advance)
- Organize policies in binders (easy reference during visit)
- Prepare 1-2 page agency overview (history, mission, specialties, outcomes)
- Have sample caregiver files ready (demonstrate thorough hiring, training)
- Showcase quality initiatives (satisfaction data, outcome improvements)
- Treat as opportunity to impress (professional presentation reflects well on agency)

**Pro tip:** Agencies that excel in site visit often fast-tracked for approval. Preparation pays off.

### **Challenge 3: "I'm credentialed but not receiving referrals"**

**Reality:** Credentialing doesn't automatically generate referrals. Nurse care managers refer to agencies they know and trust.

**Why referrals may be slow:**
- Large provider network (200+ agencies compete for referrals)
- Care managers don't know your agency exists
- Established agencies have long-standing relationships
- Provider profile incomplete or outdated
- Service area mismatch

**Solution:**
- Proactively introduce yourself to nurse care managers (email, phone, meetings)
- Attend BCBSMA provider events (essential for visibility)
- Update provider profile comprehensively (services, specialties, languages)
- Respond rapidly to first 3-5 referrals (prove reliability, build reputation)
- Ask satisfied BCBSMA clients to share positive feedback with care managers
- Market directly to BCBSMA members (supplement care manager referrals)
- Consider specializing in underserved needs (non-English languages, rural areas, specialty care)

**Timeframe:** Expect 3-5 month ramp-up period after credentialing. Persistence and relationship-building essential.

### **Challenge 4: "Authorization denials for medical necessity"**

**BCBSMA applies clinical standards for authorization approval:**

**Common denial reasons:**
- Insufficient documentation of ADL limitations
- Lack of clinical rationale (why home care needed vs. member self-care)
- Services not matching benefit design (requesting services not covered)
- Inadequate safety concerns documented
- No physician involvement (for some services, physician orders required)

**Solution:**
- Complete comprehensive assessments (detailed ADL/IADL scores, functional limitations)
- Document safety concerns (fall risk, medication errors, unsafe home environment)
- Include clinical rationale (why member needs assistance, what happens without care)
- Obtain physician orders when beneficial (strengthens medical necessity)
- Review BCBSMA medical necessity criteria (available in provider manual)
- If denied, appeal with additional documentation (appeals often successful with more detail)
- Consult with nurse care manager before submitting (they can advise on authorization strategy)

**Pro tip:** BCBSMA values clinical quality. Strong clinical documentation = higher authorization approval rate.

### **Challenge 5: "Care managers request frequent clinical updates"**

**BCBSMA nurse care managers are clinically engaged:**

**What they request:**
- Monthly progress updates (member status, goal progress, concerns)
- Notification of health changes (hospitalization, falls, medication issues)
- Care plan updates when needs change
- Coordination with other providers (home health, physicians, specialists)

**Why this happens:** BCBSMA's care coordination model is intensive, especially for high-risk members. Nurse care managers are RNs accountable for clinical outcomes.

**Solution:**
- Embrace partnership approach (shared goal: member health and safety)
- Communicate proactively (don't wait for care manager to request updates)
- Submit monthly progress notes via portal (care managers can access without calling)
- Alert care manager immediately if concerns arise (hospitalization, falls, decline)
- View care manager as clinical resource (they can authorize additional services, coordinate with physicians, connect member with resources)
- Use clinical language in communications (care managers are nurses, appreciate clinical detail)

**Mindset shift:** BCBSMA nurse care managers are clinical partners. Collaboration improves member outcomes and strengthens referral relationship.

### **Challenge 6: "Reimbursement rates below competitors"**

**Reality:** BCBSMA Medicare rates are competitive but may be 10-20% lower than private pay.

**Typical BCBSMA rates:**
- Personal care/homemaker: $22-$28/hour (varies by region, service)

- Overnight care: $160-$220/night

**Trade-off analysis:**
- Lower rates BUT higher volume, longer relationships, premium payment reliability, lower marketing costs

- Member loyalty (6+ year average tenure = long-term revenue)

**Solution:**
- Diversify payer mix (BCBSMA + private pay + other Medicare Advantage + MassHealth)
- Treat BCBSMA as premium stable revenue base
- Factor payment reliability into ROI (BCBSMA pays on time, private pay may default)
