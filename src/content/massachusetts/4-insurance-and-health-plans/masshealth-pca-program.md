## Overview

The MassHealth Personal Care Attendant (PCA) Program is a **consumer-directed model** where members hire, train, supervise, and schedule their own personal care attendants. Home care agencies serve as **Personal Care Management (PCM) agencies**, providing administrative support while the member maintains employer control.

**Why the PCA Program matters for home care agencies:**
- **60,000+ active PCA users:** Large, stable client base
- **Consumer-directed = less competition:** Members choose their own PCAs, not competing with other agencies for caregivers
- **Union rates:** $21-24/hour PCA compensation (SEIU 2020 collective bargaining agreement)
- **Administrative revenue:** PCM agencies receive administrative fee for supporting members
- **Long-term relationships:** PCA users typically remain in program for years
- **Partnership with Tempus Unlimited:** Fiscal intermediary handles payroll, taxes, workers' comp (PCM agencies focus on member support)

**Investment:** FREE to enroll (requires MassHealth LTSS provider enrollment first)

**Revenue model:** PCM administrative fee (~10-15% of PCA wages) + ability to serve as PCA for your own clients if member prefers agency model

## Who This Referral Source Serves

**Eligible MassHealth members:**
- **Adults with physical disabilities:** Limited mobility, chronic conditions requiring ADL assistance
- **Seniors (65+):** Frail elders needing personal care (bathing, dressing, toileting, transferring)
- **Individuals with intellectual/developmental disabilities:** Support for daily living activities
- **People with traumatic brain injury, spinal cord injury:** Complex personal care needs
- **Chronic illness management:** Diabetes, COPD, heart failure requiring monitoring, medication reminders

**Eligibility requirements:**
- Enrolled in MassHealth Standard or CommonHealth
- Clinical assessment determines need for personal care services
- Able to direct own care (or have surrogate/representative)
- Not eligible for nursing facility care (PCA is community-based alternative)

**Geographic coverage:** Statewide (all Massachusetts cities/towns)

**Service limitations:**
- **NOT medical home health:** PCAs provide ADL assistance only (not skilled nursing, therapy)
- **Consumer-directed only:** Member must be willing/able to be employer (hiring, training, managing PCA)
- **Backup plan required:** Member must have plan if PCA calls out sick

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Use This as a Referral Source

**The PCA Program creates THREE agency opportunities:**

### **Opportunity 1: Become a PCM (Personal Care Management) Agency**

**What PCM agencies do:**
- Help members recruit, hire PCAs (advertising, interviewing, background checks)
- Train PCAs on member's specific care needs
- Monitor quality of care (check-ins with member, address concerns)
- Coordinate with Tempus Unlimited for payroll, timesheets
- Submit authorizations, service plans to MassHealth
- Provide backup planning, emergency coverage support

**What PCM agencies DON'T do:**
- Supervise PCAs day-to-day (member is employer)
- Schedule PCA shifts (member controls schedule)
- Fire PCAs (member makes employment decisions)

**Revenue:** PCM administrative fee (negotiated with MassHealth, typically 10-15% of PCA wages)

**Example:** Member authorized for 40 hours/week PCA services at $22/hour = $880/week PCA wages. PCM fee at 12% = $105.60/week administrative revenue = ~$5,500/year per member.

**Volume potential:** PCM agencies typically support 30-100 members simultaneously.

### **Opportunity 2: Serve as PCA for Your Own PCM Clients**

**Hybrid model:**
- Agency acts as PCM (administrative support)
- Member chooses to hire agency's caregivers as their PCAs
- Agency receives PCA wages + PCM administrative fee
- More control over caregiver quality than pure consumer-directed model

**Why members choose this:**
- Don't want burden of recruiting, hiring individual PCAs
- Prefer agency's established caregiver pool
- Need backup coverage (agency can provide substitute PCAs)
- Want training, supervision from agency professionals

**Revenue:** PCA wages + PCM administrative fee (double revenue stream)

**Caution:** Member must still maintain "employer" role (final say on hiring, scheduling, performance management). Agency can't override member's choices.

### **Opportunity 3: Connect Private Pay Clients to PCA Program**

**Many home care agencies serve private pay clients who are eligible for MassHealth but don't know about PCA program:**

**Action steps:**
1. Screen private pay clients for MassHealth eligibility (income, assets)
2. Refer eligible clients to MassHealth enrollment
3. Once enrolled, help client apply for PCA program
4. Transition client from private pay to MassHealth PCA (with your agency as PCM)

**Benefit:** Client gets free/subsidized care, agency converts to sustainable MassHealth revenue

## Provider Network Credentialing Process

### **Step 1: Complete MassHealth LTSS Provider Enrollment (PREREQUISITE)**

**You must be enrolled MassHealth LTSS provider before registering as PCM agency.**

**Process:** See [MassHealth LTSS Provider Enrollment Guide](/massachusetts/4-insurance-and-health-plans/masshealth-ltss-provider-enrollment)

**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks for LTSS approval

### **Step 2: Register as PCM Agency with MassHealth**

**After LTSS enrollment approved:**

**Online application:** masshealthltss.com/pca-pcm-enrollment

**Application requirements:**
- MassHealth LTSS provider ID number
- Specify PCM service area (cities/towns you'll serve)
- Staff qualifications (PCM coordinators must have training in disability services, care coordination)
- PCA training curriculum (how you'll train PCAs on member-specific care needs)
- Quality assurance procedures (how you'll monitor member satisfaction, care quality)
- Emergency backup procedures (plan if PCA calls out sick)

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks for PCM approval (after LTSS enrollment complete)

### **Step 3: Partner with Tempus Unlimited (Fiscal Intermediary)**

**Tempus Unlimited handles PCA payroll functions:**
- Process PCA timesheets
- Calculate wages, taxes, withholdings
- Issue PCA paychecks
- Workers' compensation insurance for PCAs
- Unemployment insurance
- Tax reporting (W-2s, 1099s, quarterly filings)

**PCM agency responsibilities:**
- Submit PCA hire packets to Tempus (I-9, W-4, direct deposit)
- Approve PCA timesheets (verify hours worked)
- Coordinate with Tempus on payroll issues

**Registration:** tempusunlimited.org/pcm-agency-registration

**Cost:** FREE (Tempus compensated by MassHealth directly)

**Timeline:** 1-2 weeks to establish agency account

### **Step 4: List Agency on Mass PCA Directory**

**Mass PCA Directory is official searchable database of PCM agencies:**

**Website:** masspcadirectory.org

**Listing includes:**
- Agency name, contact information
- Service area (cities/towns)
- Languages spoken
- Specialty populations (seniors, physical disabilities, developmental disabilities, etc.)
- Consumer-directed philosophy statement

**Why this matters:** MassHealth members searching for PCM agencies use this directory. If you're not listed, you're invisible.

**Cost:** FREE

**Update frequency:** Quarterly (ensure contact info, service area current)

### **Step 5: Complete PCM Agency Training**

**MassHealth requires PCM agencies complete initial training:**

**Topics covered:**
- Consumer-directed model philosophy
- Member rights and responsibilities
- PCM agency roles and boundaries
- PCA training requirements
- Quality assurance and monitoring
- Conflict resolution
- Regulatory compliance

**Format:** Online module (3-4 hours) + quarterly webinars

**Access:** After PCM approval, MassHealth sends training link

**Ongoing education:** 6 hours/year continuing education (MassHealth offers free webinars)

- **6-12 active members** (60% conversion rate)
- **Revenue:** $30,000-$65,000 in PCM administrative fees

**Where referrals come from:**
- **Mass PCA Directory:** 40-50% (members search directory proactively)
- **MassHealth Enrollment Centers:** 20-30% (care coordinators refer newly eligible members)
- **Word of mouth:** 15-25% (satisfied members refer friends/family)
- **Hospital discharge planners:** 10-15% (hospitals discharge MassHealth patients needing PCA)

- **25-50 member referrals**
- **18-35 active members** (70% conversion rate)
- **Revenue:** $95,000-$190,000

### **Year 3+:**
- **50-100 member referrals annually**
- **40-80 active members** (stable roster with attrition replaced)
- **Revenue:** $200,000-$400,000

**Growth factors:**
- **Specialization:** Agencies focusing on specific populations (developmental disabilities, brain injury, non-English speakers) find loyal niche markets

- If 50% of your PCM members also hire your agency's caregivers as their PCAs
- Add PCA wages to PCM administrative fees

## Tips to Get More Referrals from This Source

### **Strategy 1: Optimize Your Mass PCA Directory Listing**

**Most members find PCM agencies via directory search:**

**Action steps:**
- Update listing quarterly (ensure accurate contact info, service area)
- List ALL languages spoken by staff (Spanish, Portuguese, Haitian Creole, Cape Verdean Creole, Mandarin, Vietnamese critical in gateway cities)
- Specify specialty populations (seniors, physical disabilities, developmental disabilities, brain injury, autism, cerebral palsy)
- Write compelling philosophy statement (emphasize member empowerment, choice, respect, cultural competency)
- Include photos of diverse staff members (humanizes agency)

**Pro tip:** Members often search by language first, then location. Bilingual agencies get 2-3x more inquiries.

### **Strategy 2: Build Relationships with MassHealth Enrollment Centers**

**MECs help newly eligible members connect with PCM agencies:**

**Action steps:**
- Identify MECs in your service area: mass.gov/masshealth-enrollment-centers
- Email care coordination supervisor, introduce your agency as PCM provider
- Drop off brochures emphasizing consumer-directed model, member testimonials
- Offer to co-present at MEC member orientation sessions (educate members about PCA program)
- Attend quarterly MEC provider networking events

**MECs with high PCA volume:**
- Boston MEC (100+ Tremont Street)
- Springfield MEC (436 Dwight Street)
- Worcester MEC (340 Main Street)
- Cambridge MEC (362 Green Street)
- Brockton MEC (110 Pearl Street)

### **Strategy 3: Partner with Independent Living Centers**

**Independent Living Centers (ILCs) serve people with disabilities, major PCA users:**

**Massachusetts ILCs:**
- Boston Center for Independent Living
- Cape Organization for Rights of the Disabled (CORD)
- Independence Associates (central MA)
- metroWest Center for Independent Living
- Northeast Independent Living Program
- Stavros Center for Independent Living (western MA)

**Action steps:**
- Contact ILC peer advocates, explain your PCM services
- Offer to present at ILC support groups, workshops
- Partner on ILC events (disability pride, ADA celebrations)
- Ask ILC to include your agency in resource directory

**Why this works:** ILC members are highly engaged in disability community, trust peer recommendations from ILC advocates.

### **Strategy 4: Specialize in Underserved Populations**

**Less competitive niches:**

**Developmental disabilities:**
- Agencies with expertise in autism, cerebral palsy, intellectual disabilities
- Fewer PCM agencies specialize in this population
- Strong referrals from DDS (Department of Developmental Services) care coordinators

**Non-English speakers:**
- Spanish (largest non-English group in MA)
- Portuguese (New Bedford, Fall River)
- Haitian Creole (Boston, Brockton, Lynn)
- Cape Verdean Creole (Boston, Brockton)
- Mandarin, Cantonese (Boston, Quincy)
- Vietnamese (Worcester, Lowell)

**Brain injury/spinal cord injury:**
- Complex personal care needs
- Fewer agencies comfortable serving this population
- Strong referrals from Acquired Brain Injury Network, neuro rehab hospitals

**Action steps:**
- Train staff on specialized population needs
- Hire bilingual coordinators (Spanish, Portuguese, Haitian Creole)
- Market to specialty organizations (brain injury associations, autism support groups, cultural community centers)
- Update Mass PCA Directory listing to highlight specialization

### **Strategy 5: Offer Hybrid Model (PCM + Agency PCAs)**

**Many members want consumer-directed control BUT don't want to recruit/manage individual PCAs:**

**Solution:** Agency serves as PCM (administrative support) AND provides caregivers as PCAs.

**Member benefits:**
- Access to agency's caregiver pool (no need to recruit strangers)
- Backup coverage (agency can send substitute PCA if regular calls out)
- Training/supervision from agency professionals
- Still maintains employer role (final say on scheduling, performance)

**Agency benefits:**
- Double revenue stream (PCM fee + PCA wages)
- More control over caregiver quality
- Easier retention (agency can replace underperforming PCAs without losing member)

**Marketing message:** "We support your independence while providing reliable, trained caregivers you can trust."

### **Strategy 6: Respond FAST to Directory Inquiries**

**Members contacting PCM agencies via Mass PCA Directory often reach out to 3-5 agencies:**

**Speed wins:**
- Respond to inquiries within 4 hours during business hours
- Offer assessment within 24-48 hours
- Have bilingual staff answer phones (don't rely on translation services)
- Evenings/weekends: Use voicemail with 24-hour callback promise, actually follow up

**Tool recommendation:** Mastering Home Care's intake tracking helps ensure no inquiry falls through cracks. Set alerts for PCA Directory leads.

- Word-of-mouth referrals reduce marketing costs over time

**ROI verdict: High-value addition for agencies serving MassHealth population**

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "Consumer-directed model is confusing (who's really the employer?)"**

**Traditional home care:** Agency is employer (hires, trains, supervises, schedules caregivers)

**PCA consumer-directed model:** Member is employer legally (IRS treats PCA as member's employee, not agency's). PCM agency provides administrative support only.

**Confusion arises when:**
- Members want agency to make all employment decisions (defeats consumer-directed purpose)
- PCM agencies try to control PCA's work (violates consumer-directed model)
- Members don't understand employer responsibilities (taxes, workers' comp, unemployment)

**Solution:**
- **Educate members upfront:** Explain employer role, responsibilities, rights during initial assessment
- **Set clear boundaries:** PCM agency advises, supports, coordinates BUT member makes final decisions
- **Tempus Unlimited handles legal employer functions:** Payroll taxes, workers' comp, unemployment (removes burden from member and PCM agency)
- **Document member's employer choices:** If PCA isn't working out, document that member chose to continue employment (protects PCM agency from liability)

### **Challenge 2: "Members struggle to find, hire qualified PCAs"**

**PCA shortage is real:**
- $21-24/hour wages competitive but many workers prefer agency employment (benefits, stability)
- Background check requirements eliminate some candidates
- Members intimidated by hiring process (writing ads, conducting interviews, checking references)

**Solution:**
- **Offer hiring support:** PCM agency can place ads, screen candidates, conduct background checks (member makes final hiring decision)
- **Hybrid model:** Suggest members hire from your agency's caregiver pool (they're already trained, background-checked)
- **Connect members with each other:** PCA user support groups share successful recruitment strategies
- **Advertise in diverse locations:** Community centers, cultural organizations, workforce development programs, refugee resettlement agencies

### **Challenge 3: "PCAs call out sick, member has no backup"**

**Consumer-directed model doesn't guarantee backup coverage (unlike agency model):**

**MassHealth requires members have backup plan:**
- Family member or friend willing to provide emergency care
- Second PCA on standby
- Agency willing to send substitute caregiver short-term

**Solution:**
- **Assess backup plan during initial evaluation:** Don't accept members without viable backup (sets everyone up for failure)
- **Offer backup services:** PCM agency maintains pool of "float" PCAs willing to cover emergencies (charge slightly higher rate for backup shifts)
- **Encourage members to hire 2-3 part-time PCAs:** Reduces dependence on single caregiver
- **Coordinate with member's informal supports:** Family, friends, neighbors who can provide basic assistance short-term

### **Challenge 4: "Member complains about PCA performance but won't fire them"**

**Common scenario:**
- Member reports PCA is late, not performing tasks, unprofessional
- PCM agency investigates, confirms issues
- PCM agency recommends terminating PCA
- Member refuses (doesn't want confrontation, worried about not finding replacement)

**Why this is challenging:**
- PCM agency accountable to MassHealth for quality of care
- If MassHealth investigates complaint, PCM agency can be sanctioned
- But member is employer (PCM can't fire PCA without member's consent)

**Solution:**
- **Document everything:** Member's complaints, your investigation findings, recommendations to terminate, member's decision to continue employment
- **Provide coaching:** Help member develop skills to manage difficult conversations with PCA
- **Offer to facilitate termination:** PCM agency can be present during termination conversation (emotional support for member)
- **Escalate to MassHealth if safety issue:** If PCA poses immediate risk, PCM agency must report to MassHealth (overrides member's employer control)

### **Challenge 5: "Timesheet approval is time-consuming"**

**PCM agencies approve PCA timesheets in Tempus Unlimited portal:**
- Each member may have 1-3 PCAs
- Each PCA submits timesheet biweekly
- PCM coordinator verifies hours worked, approves for payroll

**For 40 members with average 2 PCAs each = 80 timesheets biweekly**

**Solution:**
- **Implement electronic timekeeping:** Tempus Unlimited offers mobile app for PCAs to clock in/out (reduces manual timesheet entry)
- **Batch timesheet review:** Set aside specific time blocks for approvals (don't interrupt throughout day)
- **Train members to review timesheets first:** Member verifies PCA worked hours claimed, notifies PCM if discrepancy (reduces PCM's investigation time)
- **Delegate to PCM coordinator:** Timesheet approval doesn't require agency owner, train coordinator to manage
