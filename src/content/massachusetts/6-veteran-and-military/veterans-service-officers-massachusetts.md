## Overview

**Veterans Service Officers (VSOs)** are the most underutilized yet most valuable veteran referral source for Massachusetts home care agencies. VSOs are county and city government employees whose sole job is to help veterans access federal VA benefits, state veteran benefits, and community resources - including home care services. Every Massachusetts county and most cities have dedicated VSO offices, collectively serving all **380,000+ veterans** across the Commonwealth.

**Why VSOs Matter for Home Care Agencies:**
- **Trusted advisors:** Veterans trust VSOs more than almost any other resource (VSOs are veterans' advocates, not salespeople)
- **First point of contact:** Veterans often contact VSO before VA, healthcare providers, or community agencies when they need help
- **Benefits expertise:** VSOs help veterans access Aid & Attendance benefit (cash payment for home care), VA healthcare, state benefits that fund home care
- **Local connections:** VSOs are embedded in local communities, know veterans personally, make warm referrals (not cold handoffs)
- **No competition:** Most home care agencies don't know VSOs exist, so little competition for VSO referrals
- **Volume potential:** Each VSO serves hundreds to thousands of veterans annually - significant referral pipeline

**Key Statistics:**
- **14 County Veterans Service Offices** (one per county)
- **40+ City Veterans Service Offices** (major cities have dedicated VSOs)
- **170+ VSO staff** statewide (directors, assistant VSOs, benefits counselors)
- **100,000+ veteran interactions** annually across all VSO offices
- **$50+ million** in benefits secured for MA veterans annually by VSOs

**Unique Opportunity:**
VSOs are critically underutilized by home care agencies. Most agencies focus on healthcare referrals (hospitals, ASAPs, PCPs) and overlook VSOs. Agencies that build VSO relationships gain access to veteran referral pipeline with almost no competition.

## Who This Referral Source Serves

### **All Massachusetts Veterans**

**VSO services are free and available to:**
- **All honorably discharged veterans** (regardless of era, service-connected disability status, or income)
- **Veteran spouses and dependents** (VSOs help surviving spouses access benefits)
- **Veterans at any stage** (newly separated military, mid-life veterans, elderly veterans, end-of-life)

**No eligibility restrictions:**
Unlike VA healthcare (which requires enrollment) or MassHealth (which has income limits), VSO services are available to ALL veterans free of charge.

### **Veterans Who Need Home Care (VSO Can Connect to Your Agency)**

**1. Elderly Veterans Seeking Aid & Attendance Benefit**
- Veterans age 65+ who need help with activities of daily living (bathing, dressing, etc.)
- **Aid & Attendance** is VA cash benefit ($2,431/month for veteran, $2,846/month for veteran+spouse as of 2024)
- Veterans use this cash to pay for home care privately
- **VSO's role:** Helps veteran apply for Aid & Attendance, can refer veteran to your agency to provide the care
- **Your opportunity:** Private-pay home care funded by VA benefit (higher rates than VA contracts)

**2. Veterans Enrolled in VA Healthcare Needing Community Support**
- Veterans using VA Boston, VA Bedford, VA Brockton, or VA Central Western MA for medical care
- Need home care to supplement VA services (or while waiting for VA home care authorization)
- **VSO's role:** Advocates for veteran to receive VA home care services, coordinates with VA social workers
- **Your opportunity:** VSO can refer veteran to your agency either for private pay (while waiting for VA) or as recommended community provider to VA

**3. Veterans with Service-Connected Disabilities**
- Veterans with disabilities rated by VA (Agent Orange, combat injuries, PTSD, hearing loss, etc.)
- May qualify for VA home care, Aid & Attendance, or state veteran benefits
- **VSO's role:** Ensures veteran receiving all benefits they're entitled to, including home care services
- **Your opportunity:** VSO identifies veteran's home care need and refers to your agency

**4. Low-Income Veterans**
- Veterans who qualify for MassHealth or state veteran benefits
- May not know they're eligible for home care through these programs
- **VSO's role:** Helps veteran apply for MassHealth, state benefits that cover home care
- **Your opportunity:** VSO refers veteran to your agency as MassHealth or state-funded client

**5. Homeless or At-Risk Veterans**
- Veterans experiencing homelessness or housing instability
- Transitioning from VA homeless programs (Domiciliary, HUD-VASH) into community
- **VSO's role:** Connects veteran to housing, benefits, services (including home care for housing stabilization)
- **Your opportunity:** VSO refers veteran needing home support to maintain housing

**6. Veterans' Surviving Spouses**
- Widows/widowers of veterans who qualify for Death Pension with Aid & Attendance
- Often elderly, need home care, eligible for VA benefit up to $1,384/month
- **VSO's role:** Helps surviving spouse access benefits
- **Your opportunity:** Private-pay home care for surviving spouse funded by VA benefit

**7. Veterans Not Eligible for VA Healthcare**
- Veterans with other-than-honorable discharge, higher income, or other barriers to VA healthcare
- Still eligible for state veteran benefits, Aid & Attendance (if age 65+), VSO assistance
- **VSO's role:** Connects veteran to non-VA resources, benefits
- **Your opportunity:** VSO may refer to your agency for private-pay or state-funded home care

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from VSOs

### **The VSO Referral Process**

**How it typically works:**

1. **Veteran contacts VSO** (walk-in, phone call, referral from family/friend/community org)
2. **VSO assesses veteran's needs** (income, medical conditions, disabilities, living situation, family support)
3. **VSO identifies benefits veteran qualifies for** (VA healthcare, Aid & Attendance, state benefits, etc.)
4. **VSO helps veteran apply for benefits** (completes paperwork, gathers medical evidence, submits claims)
5. **VSO identifies need for home care** (veteran needs ADL assistance, family caregiver overwhelmed, unsafe living alone)
6. **VSO refers veteran to home care agency** (provides veteran with agency contact information OR calls agency on veteran's behalf for warm handoff)

**Key insight:**
VSOs make QUALITY referrals. They know the veteran personally, understand their needs, have vetted that veteran has funding or benefits to pay for care. These aren't cold leads - they're warm, qualified referrals.

### **Three Ways to Receive VSO Referrals**

**1. VSO Keeps Your Materials and Refers Veterans to You**
- You provide VSO with brochures, business cards, agency information
- When VSO identifies veteran needing home care, VSO gives veteran your information
- Veteran calls your agency (VSO has already explained who you are, built trust)
- **Best for:** Agencies wanting to be on VSO's "recommended provider" list

**2. VSO Calls You Directly for Warm Handoff**
- VSO identifies veteran needing home care
- VSO calls your agency: "I have a veteran who needs home care. Can I give them your number? Can you call them today?"
- You call veteran with VSO's introduction/referral
- **Best for:** Agencies that want high-touch, immediate referrals

**3. VSO Includes Your Agency in Benefits Counseling**
- VSO is helping veteran apply for Aid & Attendance or other benefit
- VSO tells veteran: "This benefit will give you $2,400/month to pay for home care. I recommend [your agency name] - they specialize in veteran care."
- Veteran expects your call or contacts you directly
- **Best for:** Agencies specializing in Aid & Attendance benefit cases

### **What VSOs Need from Home Care Agencies**

**VSOs will refer to your agency IF:**

**1. You understand veteran benefits (especially Aid & Attendance)**
- VSOs spend significant time helping veterans access Aid & Attendance benefit
- If your agency doesn't understand this benefit, VSO won't refer (they don't want to confuse veteran)
- **Action:** Learn Aid & Attendance benefit thoroughly (see related article)

**2. You're veteran-friendly**
- Hire veteran caregivers, train staff in military culture, respect veterans' service
- VSOs are veterans themselves (nearly all VSOs are veterans) - they want agencies that honor veterans
- **Action:** Develop veteran-friendly culture, market it clearly

**3. You're responsive and reliable**
- VSOs put their reputation on the line when referring veterans to your agency
- If you don't return calls, don't start care promptly, provide poor care, VSO looks bad to veteran
- **Action:** Respond within 24 hours to VSO referrals, provide excellent care, report back to VSO on veteran's status

**4. You communicate with VSO**
- VSO wants to know: Did veteran call you? Did you start care? How is veteran doing?
- Closes the loop, allows VSO to follow up with veteran, builds trust for future referrals
- **Action:** Report back to VSO after each referral (respecting veteran's privacy/HIPAA)

**5. You accept multiple payer sources**
- Veterans VSOs serve have diverse funding (private pay, Aid & Attendance, VA contracts, MassHealth, long-term care insurance)
- VSOs prefer agencies that accept multiple payers (simplifies referrals)
- **Action:** Accept Aid & Attendance, private pay, and if possible VA Community Care contracts, MassHealth

**6. You're local to the veterans VSO serves**
- County/city VSOs serve specific geographic areas
- They refer to agencies serving those areas
- **Action:** Build relationships with VSOs in your service area

## How to Access or Get Connected (Step-by-Step)

### **Step 1: Identify VSOs in Your Service Area**

**Find VSOs you should connect with:**

**County VSOs:**
Every Massachusetts county has Veterans Service Office. Identify the county VSO(s) for counties where you provide home care.

**City VSOs:**
Major cities have dedicated VSOs (Boston, Worcester, Springfield, Lowell, Cambridge, Quincy, Lynn, etc.). Identify city VSOs in your service area.

**Geographic strategy:**
- **Urban agencies:** Connect with city VSO(s) in your city plus county VSO
- **Suburban agencies:** Connect with county VSO plus any town VSOs in your service area
- **Multi-region agencies:** Connect with VSOs in all counties/cities you serve (e.g., if you serve MetroWest, connect with Middlesex County VSO, Worcester County VSO, and city VSOs in Framingham, Marlborough, etc.)

**Resource:**
Complete list of Massachusetts VSOs at www.mass.gov/county-veterans-service-officers

---

### **Step 2: Research Each VSO Office Before Contact**

**Know before you call:**

**VSO office details:**
- Director name
- Office location (usually at county courthouse or city hall)
- Phone number
- Office hours (many VSOs have limited hours - call ahead)
- Population served (how many veterans in that county/city)

**VSO's role in that community:**
- Some VSOs very well-known in veteran community (frequent events, active outreach)
- Some VSOs quieter (office-based, veterans come to them)
- Check VSO's website, social media to understand their approach

**Current needs:**
- Some VSOs already have relationships with home care agencies (ask what's working, what gaps exist)
- Some VSOs have never worked with home care agencies (opportunity to educate)

---

### **Step 3: Make Initial Contact**

**Best approach: In-person visit**

**Why in-person:**
VSOs are relationship-oriented (they work one-on-one with veterans). Phone/email feels transactional. In-person visit shows commitment and respect.

**How to schedule:**
- Call VSO office: "I'm the [owner/director] of [agency name], a home care agency serving [area]. We specialize in serving veterans and would love to introduce our services to your office. Would [VSO name] be available for a 15-minute meeting this week or next?"
- Most VSOs will say yes (they welcome community partners who serve veterans)

**What to bring:**
- **Agency brochure/flyer** (emphasizing veteran services)
- **Business cards** (leave stack VSO can give to veterans)
- **Aid & Attendance information sheet** (demonstrates you understand this critical benefit)
- **Veteran testimonials** (if you have them, with appropriate privacy protections)
- **Leave-behind folder:** Create simple folder with your materials, Aid & Attendance info sheet, your card stapled to folder

**What to say (first meeting):**
- Introduce yourself and agency briefly
- "We serve veterans and understand the unique needs of the veteran community"
- "We know you help veterans access Aid & Attendance and other benefits. We'd like to be a resource for veterans who need home care once they receive those benefits."
- Ask: "What types of situations do you typically see where veterans need home care?"
- Ask: "How do you currently connect veterans to home care agencies?"
- Ask: "What would make you comfortable referring veterans to us?"
- **Listen more than talk:** VSO will tell you exactly what they need

**Timeline:** 15-20 minutes. Don't overstay. VSOs are busy serving veterans.

---

### **Step 4: Provide VSO with Referral-Ready Materials**

**Leave materials that make referring easy:**

**Business cards (leave stack of 20-30):**
VSO can hand to veterans on the spot

**Brochure or one-page flyer:**
- Your services (personal care, homemaker, companion)
- Veteran focus (veteran caregivers, military culture training, "proud to serve those who served")
- Payers accepted (Aid & Attendance, private pay, VA contracts, insurance)
- Contact information (phone, email, website)
- **Keep it simple:** VSOs deal with complex benefits paperwork - your materials should be clear and concise

**Aid & Attendance benefit information:**
Create one-page sheet explaining:
- What Aid & Attendance is ($2,431/month for veteran, $2,846 for vet+spouse)
- Who qualifies (age 65+, need ADL assistance)
- How veteran uses benefit to pay for home care
- Your agency accepts Aid & Attendance payment
- **Why this matters:** Shows VSO you understand their primary home care-related benefit

**Veteran-specific resources:**
Include helpful veteran information (not just sales material):
- VA contact numbers (shows you're knowledgeable partner, not just vendor)
- County/state veteran benefit information
- Community veteran resources
- **Positions you as resource, not just service provider**

---

### **Step 5: Offer to Present at VSO Events**

**VSOs host/attend veteran events:**
- Veteran benefits seminars
- Stand Down events (serving homeless/at-risk veterans)
- Veterans Day/Memorial Day ceremonies
- Monthly veteran coffee hours
- Senior center presentations (many elderly veterans attend senior centers)

**Your offer:**
"If you ever host events for veterans or present at senior centers, I'd be happy to attend and provide information about home care resources for veterans. I can staff a table or co-present with you about Aid & Attendance and how veterans can use it for home care."

**Why VSOs appreciate this:**
- Helps them provide better service to veterans (more resources)
- You're not asking VSO to do extra work (you're offering to help)
- Positions you as partner, not vendor

**At events:**
- Don't hard-sell; provide information and resources
- Network with veterans and families
- Support VSO's mission (you're there to help, not just get clients)

---

### **Step 6: Maintain Relationship Over Time**

**One meeting isn't enough:**

**Quarterly check-ins:**
- Call or visit VSO office every 3 months
- "Just checking in. Do you have any veterans who might benefit from our services? Any questions about what we offer?"
- Restock your brochures/business cards if VSO has distributed them

**Report outcomes:**
- When VSO refers veteran to you, circle back after veteran starts care
- "Thank you for referring Mr. Jones to us. He's receiving care 3x/week and doing well. He appreciates the referral."
- **Respects veteran privacy (don't share details without veteran consent) but closes loop with VSO**

**Holiday/Veterans Day acknowledgment:**
- Send card on Veterans Day thanking VSO for their service (most VSOs are veterans themselves)
- Acknowledge their work helping veterans
- **Builds goodwill, keeps your agency top of mind**

**Share resources:**
- If you come across helpful veteran resources, benefits updates, community events, share with VSO
- Positions you as collaborative partner

## Expected Referral Volume & Conversion Rates

### **Realistic Expectations**

**Referral volume varies significantly by:**
- Size of VSO's veteran population (Boston VSO serves 20,000+ veterans; small town VSO may serve 500)
- VSO's awareness of home care (some VSOs very knowledgeable about home care, others less so)
- Strength of your relationship with VSO (first referral may take 6-12 months; after that, more frequent)
- Your agency's responsiveness and quality (VSOs refer more to agencies that perform well)

**Typical referral volume (per VSO relationship):**

**Year 1 (relationship building):**
- 1-3 referrals from each VSO (VSO is testing you out)

**Year 2+ (established relationship):**
- Small town/county VSO: 2-5 referrals per year
- Mid-size city/county VSO: 5-15 referrals per year
- Large city VSO (Boston, Worcester, Springfield): 10-30 referrals per year

**Scaling referrals:**
If you build relationships with multiple VSOs (e.g., 5-10 VSO offices across your service area), referrals add up quickly:
- 5 VSO relationships × 5 referrals each = 25 referrals/year
- 10 VSO relationships × 5 referrals each = 50 referrals/year

### **Conversion Rates**

**VSO referrals convert at higher rates than other sources:**

**Typical conversion: 60-80%**

**Why conversion is high:**
- VSO has pre-qualified veteran (knows veteran needs care, has or will have funding)
- VSO's recommendation carries weight (veteran trusts VSO)
- Veteran expects your call (not cold lead)
- VSO often facilitates warm handoff (three-way call, introduction)

**Reasons for non-conversion:**
- Veteran decides to use family caregiver instead
- Veteran chooses different agency (maybe friend recommended someone)
- Veteran's funding falls through (Aid & Attendance claim denied, takes longer than expected)
- Veteran's health changes (enters nursing home, passes away)

**Compare to other sources:**
- Hospital discharge referrals: 40-60% conversion (competition, veteran may not be ready)
- ASAP referrals: 50-70% conversion (veteran shopping around)
- VSO referrals: 60-80% conversion (warm, qualified leads)

## Tips to Get More Referrals from This Source

### **1. Become an Aid & Attendance Benefit Expert**

**Why this is crucial:**
Aid & Attendance is the #1 benefit VSOs help elderly veterans access for home care. If you don't understand this benefit, VSOs won't refer to you.

**Aid & Attendance basics (learn this thoroughly):**

**What it is:**
- VA benefit providing tax-free cash payment to veterans age 65+ who need assistance with ADLs
- Veteran uses cash to pay for home care, assisted living, nursing home (veteran's choice)

**Payment amounts (2024):**
- Veteran with no dependents: $2,431/month
- Veteran with spouse: $2,846/month
- Surviving spouse: $1,384/month

**Eligibility:**
- Veteran age 65+ (or permanently disabled)
- Served at least 90 days active duty with at least 1 day during wartime
- Need help with 2+ ADLs (bathing, dressing, toileting, eating, transferring, continence)
- Income below limit (but limit is high after deducting medical/care expenses)

**How it works with home care:**
- Veteran applies for Aid & Attendance (VSO helps with application)
- Claim takes 3-12 months to process (frustrating delay)
- Once approved, VA pays veteran monthly
- Veteran pays your agency from Aid & Attendance payment
- **Your agency bills veteran directly (not VA)**

**Be prepared to:**
- Explain Aid & Attendance to veterans VSO refers
- Work with veterans during waiting period (private pay until Aid & Attendance approved, or start care when approved)
- Understand Aid & Attendance doesn't cover 24/7 care (veteran may need to supplement with private funds)

**Resource:**
Read related article [VA Aid & Attendance Benefit](/massachusetts/6-veteran-and-military/va-aid-and-attendance-benefit) for comprehensive understanding

---

### **2. Develop Veteran-Specific Marketing Materials**

**VSOs won't refer to generic home care agency:**
They'll refer to agency that demonstrates understanding and commitment to veteran population.

**Create veteran-focused materials:**

**Veteran services brochure:**
- "Serving Those Who Served" or similar veteran-focused headline
- Emphasize veteran caregivers, military culture training, understanding of service-connected disabilities
- List veteran-specific services (PTSD support, military culture-sensitive care, transition support for homeless veterans)
- Include photos of caregivers with veterans (if you have veteran clients who consent)

**Aid & Attendance information sheet:**
- Simple one-page explanation of Aid & Attendance benefit
- How veteran qualifies, benefit amounts, how to use for home care
- "We accept Aid & Attendance benefit payment" clearly stated
- VSO contact information (shows you're collaborative partner)

**Service-connected disability information:**
- Common service-connected disabilities (Agent Orange, PTSD, combat injuries, TBI)
- How your agency accommodates these conditions
- Specialized training your staff has received

**Veteran testimonials:**
- If you have veteran clients willing to provide testimonials (with proper consent/privacy protections)
- Powerful proof of your veteran expertise

---

### **3. Hire and Promote Veteran Caregivers**

**VSOs strongly prefer agencies with veteran caregivers:**

**Why veteran caregivers matter to VSOs:**
- VSOs are veterans themselves - they trust agencies that employ veterans
- Veterans receiving care trust fellow veterans faster
- Veteran caregivers understand military culture, rank structure, combat trauma
- Hiring veterans demonstrates genuine commitment to veteran community (not just marketing)

**Recruiting veteran caregivers:**
- Massachusetts veteran job fairs (held regularly in Boston, Worcester, Springfield)
- American Legion, VFW job boards
- Hire Heroes USA (veteran employment program)
- VA Bedford Voluntary Service (knows veterans seeking employment)
- Soldier On (homeless veterans program graduates looking for jobs)

**Promote veteran caregivers to VSOs:**
- "We actively recruit veteran caregivers - currently 30% of our staff are veterans"
- Include veteran caregiver bios in materials you leave with VSO
- Veteran caregivers can attend VSO events with you (powerful representation)

**If you don't have veteran caregivers yet:**
Be honest: "We're actively recruiting veteran caregivers and would love your help spreading the word to veterans seeking employment."

---

### **4. Educate VSOs About VA Community Care**

**Many VSOs don't fully understand VA Community Care Network:**

**Common VSO misconception:**
"Veterans can only get home care through VA facilities or by paying privately with Aid & Attendance."

**Reality:**
Veterans can ALSO receive VA-funded home care through community agencies that contract with VA Community Care Network (see related article).

**Your opportunity to educate:**
- If you're VA Community Care Network provider (or working on becoming one), explain this to VSO
- "We contract with VA to provide home care services. Veterans enrolled in VA healthcare can receive care from our agency funded by VA."
- VSO can refer veterans who are waiting for VA home care authorization to your agency as VA contract provider
- VSO can advocate to VA on veteran's behalf to authorize community home care

**Why this matters:**
Many veterans wait months for VA home care because VA direct services are backlogged. If VSO knows you're VA contractor, VSO can advocate for VA to authorize care through your agency (faster access for veteran).

**Educational approach:**
- Bring simple one-page overview of VA Community Care to VSO meeting
- "I wanted you to know we're a VA Community Care provider. If you have veterans waiting for VA home care, we may be able to help get them services faster."
- Offer to explain VA Community Care Network at VSO's next staff meeting

---

### **5. Partner with VSOs on Outreach Events**

**VSOs hold/attend events throughout the year:**

**Annual events:**
- **Stand Down:** 1-2 day events providing services to homeless/at-risk veterans (VSOs heavily involved)
- **Veterans Day events:** Ceremonies, resource fairs, celebrations
- **Memorial Day events:** Parades, remembrances
- **Veteran benefits seminars:** VSOs present about benefits at senior centers, community centers, libraries

**Your partnership offer:**
"We'd love to support your outreach efforts. We can staff an information table at events, co-present about home care and Aid & Attendance, or sponsor event (provide refreshments, materials)."

**At events:**
- Staff information table with brochures, business cards, veteran resources
- Answer questions about home care, Aid & Attendance, VA services
- Don't hard-sell (veterans are skeptical of sales pitches)
- Network with VSO, veterans, community partners
- Follow up with veterans who expressed interest

**Event sponsorship:**
- Offer to sponsor VSO events (cover costs of food, venue, printing)
- "In honor of our veteran clients, we'd like to sponsor your Veterans Day breakfast"
- Sponsorship gets your agency name/logo on materials, demonstrates commitment
- Typically modest cost ($200-1,000) with significant goodwill return

---

### **6. Provide Excellent Care and Report Back to VSO**

**Nothing gets you more referrals than proven quality:**

**After each VSO referral:**

**1. Start care promptly:**
VSO has told veteran about your agency. Veteran expects quick response. Call within 24 hours.

**2. Provide exceptional care:**
This is veteran VSO knows personally. If care is poor, VSO feels personally responsible. Make sure care is excellent.

**3. Close loop with VSO:**
- After veteran starts care: "Thank you for referring Mr. Smith. He started care on Monday and we've matched him with one of our veteran caregivers. He's doing well."
- If veteran doesn't proceed with care: "Thank you for referring Ms. Jones. We spoke, but she decided to use family caregiving for now. We let her know we're here if her needs change."
- **Respects HIPAA** (don't share medical details without veteran consent) but closes referral loop

**4. Report significant concerns to VSO:**
- If veteran is struggling (can't afford care, benefits delayed, unsafe living conditions), alert VSO
- VSO may be able to help (expedite benefits, connect to additional resources)
- "Just wanted to let you know Mr. Williams is doing well with care, but he mentioned his Aid & Attendance claim has been pending for 10 months. Is there anything you can do to help expedite?"

**Building reputation:**
Word spreads among VSOs. If VSO in Springfield refers veteran to your agency and you provide great care, that VSO will tell other VSOs at regional meetings. Strong performance with one VSO leads to referrals from other VSOs.

---

### **7. Understand State Veteran Benefits That Fund Home Care**

**VSOs help veterans access state benefits in addition to federal VA benefits:**

**Massachusetts veteran benefits that fund home care:**

**Welcome Home Bonus:**
- One-time $1,000 payment to qualifying veterans who served in combat zones
- Some veterans use for one-time home modification, equipment

**Veteran Annuity:**
- $2,000/year ($167/month) for qualifying veterans
- Helps supplement home care costs

**Veteran Cemetery benefits:**
- Not directly related to home care, but VSO helps with end-of-life planning

**Property tax exemptions:**
- Reduces veteran's housing costs, freeing up funds for home care

**Why you should know state benefits:**
- Shows VSO you understand full landscape of veteran benefits (not just federal)
- Demonstrates commitment to helping veterans maximize resources
- Positions you as knowledgeable partner

**How to learn state benefits:**
- Massachusetts Department of Veterans' Services website: www.mass.gov/veterans
- Ask VSO during your meeting: "What state benefits do you most frequently help veterans access?"

### **8. Network with VSOs Regionally**

**VSOs meet regularly:**
- County VSOs meet quarterly at Massachusetts Veterans Service Officers Association (MVSOA) meetings
- City VSOs network at regional events
- Stand Downs bring VSOs together

**Your opportunity:**
If you serve multiple counties/cities, build relationships with multiple VSOs, attend events where VSOs gather.

**Regional approach:**
- **MetroWest agency:** Connect with Middlesex County VSO, Worcester County VSO, city VSOs in Framingham, Marlborough, Natick, etc.
- **Western MA agency:** Connect with Hampden County, Hampshire County, Franklin County, Berkshire County VSOs, city VSOs in Springfield, Northampton, Pittsfield
- **South Shore agency:** Connect with Plymouth County VSO, Norfolk County VSO, Bristol County VSO

**Stand Downs:**
Major Stand Down events in Massachusetts:
- **Boston Stand Down:** Annual, serves Boston-area homeless/at-risk veterans
- **Springfield Stand Down:** Annual, serves Western MA veterans
- **Worcester Stand Down:** Annual, serves Central MA veterans

Attend Stand Downs, meet multiple VSOs at once, support homeless veterans (who often need home care for housing stabilization).

## Cost and ROI Analysis

### **Investment Required**

**Initial setup costs:**
- **Printed materials:** $200-500 (brochures, business cards, leave-behinds for VSO offices)
- **Staff time:** 20-40 hours in Year 1 (researching VSOs, scheduling meetings, attending events, follow-up)
- **Event sponsorships (optional):** $200-1,000 per event
- **Transportation:** Mileage/time visiting VSO offices across service area

**Total first-year investment:** $500-2,000 (primarily staff time and materials)

**Ongoing costs:**
- **Quarterly check-ins:** 1-2 hours per quarter per VSO relationship (maintaining relationships)
- **Materials restocking:** $100-200/year (replenishing materials at VSO offices)
- **Event participation:** 2-4 events per year, 4-8 hours each

**Total ongoing investment:** $200-500/year plus staff time

---

### **Expected Return**

**Referral volume (per VSO relationship):**
- **Year 1:** 1-3 referrals (building relationship)
- **Year 2+:** 3-10 referrals per year (established relationship)

**If you build 5 VSO relationships:**
- **Year 1:** 5-15 referrals total
- **Year 2+:** 15-50 referrals per year total

**Average case value:**
- **Payer:** Primarily Aid & Attendance (private pay), some VA contracts, some MassHealth
- **Hours:** 10-20 hours per week average
- **Duration:** 6-24 months (longer than hospital discharge cases - elderly veterans, chronic needs)
- **Hourly rate:** $30-45/hour for private pay Aid & Attendance (higher than VA contract rates)
- **Case value:** $7,000-20,000+ over case lifetime (high value due to long duration and private pay rates)

**Annual revenue potential:**
- **5 VSO relationships, Year 1:** 5-15 referrals × $10,000 average = $50,000-150,000
- **5 VSO relationships, Year 2+:** 15-50 referrals × $10,000 average = $150,000-500,000

**High-value referral source:**
VSO referrals tend to be high-value because:
- Long duration (elderly veterans with chronic needs)
- Private pay rates (Aid & Attendance)
- High conversion (60-80%)
- Low acquisition cost (relationship-based, not paid marketing)

VSO relationships are long-term. Once you're VSO's "go-to" home care agency, you receive referrals for years. Very high lifetime value of VSO relationship.

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "I contacted VSO but haven't heard back"**

**Why it happens:**
VSOs are extremely busy. They serve hundreds to thousands of veterans, often with small staff. Your initial email or voicemail may get lost in shuffle.

**Solutions:**
- **Be persistent (politely):** Follow up weekly for 3-4 weeks
- **Try different contact methods:** If email didn't work, call. If voicemail didn't work, stop by office in person.
- **Best approach:** In-person visit (harder to ignore someone standing in front of you, and shows your commitment)
- **Timing:** Call early in week (Monday/Tuesday) when VSOs less busy than end of week
- **Don't give up:** Persistence shows you're serious about serving veterans

---

### **Challenge 2: "VSO already has relationships with other home care agencies"**

**Why it happens:**
Some VSOs already refer to established agencies.

**This is NOT a problem:**
Most VSOs work with multiple agencies (different specialties, geographic areas, capacity). There's room for you too.

**Solutions:**
- **Ask what gaps exist:** "I understand you work with other agencies. Are there services or areas where you need additional options?" (maybe VSO's current agencies don't cover certain towns, don't accept Aid & Attendance, don't have veteran caregivers)
- **Differentiate:** Emphasize what makes you unique (veteran caregivers, specialized dementia care, Cape Cod coverage, PTSD training)
- **Be additive, not competitive:** "We'd love to be an additional resource for you. We understand you have existing relationships, and we respect that. We'd like to be option for situations where those agencies can't help."

**Reality:**
VSOs want MORE good options, not fewer. Veteran needs vary - having multiple agency relationships allows VSO to match veteran to right agency.

---

### **Challenge 3: "VSO referred veteran but veteran didn't follow through"**

**Why it happens:**
- Veteran not ready to accept care (common - veterans value independence)
- Veteran's financial situation changed (couldn't afford care)
- Veteran chose different agency (maybe friend recommended someone)
- Veteran's health changed (hospitalized, entered nursing home, passed away)

**Solutions:**
- **Don't take personally:** Not every referral converts (normal in home care)
- **Follow up with VSO:** "Thank you for referring Mr. Johnson. We reached out but he wasn't ready to start care at this time. We let him know we're here when he's ready. We appreciate the referral."
- **Stay in touch with veteran:** Some veterans need time to accept care. Follow up in 1-2 months: "Just checking in. Are you ready for home care support now?"

**Don't let one non-conversion discourage you:**
VSOs will refer again if you handled situation professionally (responded promptly, respected veteran's decision, reported back to VSO).

---

### **Challenge 4: "Veteran was referred but Aid & Attendance claim is taking forever - veteran can't afford to pay out of pocket while waiting"**

**Why it happens:**
Aid & Attendance claims take 3-12 months to process (frustrating for everyone). Veteran may need care now but can't afford private pay rates while waiting.

**Solutions:**

**Payment arrangements:**
- Offer to start care at reduced rate while veteran waits for Aid & Attendance (e.g., start at 5 hours/week instead of 15, increase when benefit approved)
- Payment plan (veteran pays what they can now, balance when Aid & Attendance approved)
- Work with VSO to find interim funding (state veteran benefits, veteran charities, community resources)

**Communication with VSO:**
- "Mr. Smith needs care now but Aid & Attendance is still pending. Are there any interim funding options you're aware of?"
- VSO may know resources (local veteran charities, emergency assistance programs)

**Be patient:**
Many Aid & Attendance cases start slow but become long-term, high-value clients once benefit approved. Worth the wait.

**Alternative:**
If veteran truly can't afford any care while waiting, offer to revisit when Aid & Attendance approved. Stay in touch monthly. Start care when benefit comes through.

---

### **Challenge 5: "I've built relationship with VSO but referrals are slow"**

**Why it happens:**
- VSO is conservative about referrals (wants to be sure you're good agency before referring regularly)
- VSO's veteran population may not have high home care need at the moment (cyclical)
- VSO simply hasn't had appropriate cases yet

**Solutions:**

**Stay visible:**
- Quarterly check-ins (call or visit every 3 months)
- Attend VSO events (stays top of mind)
- Share resources (send VSO helpful veteran information, resources - not just "do you have any referrals for me")

**Ask for feedback:**
- "We appreciate the referrals you've sent. Is there anything we could do differently to be more helpful to your veterans?"
- VSO may give you specific suggestions

**Expand VSO relationships:**
- If one VSO slow to refer, build relationships with additional VSOs
- 5 VSOs × 3 referrals each > 1 VSO × 15 referrals (diversification)

**Be patient:**
Some VSO relationships take 12-18 months to really develop. Once trust is established, referrals become more frequent.
