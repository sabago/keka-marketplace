## How Visiting Angels Franchise Works

### **Franchise Structure:**

**Corporate (Visiting Angels LLC):**
- Owns Visiting Angels brand and trademarks
- Develops operational systems and training programs
- Conducts national marketing campaigns
- Provides ongoing support to franchisees
- Enforces quality standards across network

**Franchisee (You):**
- Owns and operates local Visiting Angels franchise
- Hires staff (care coordinators, caregivers, administrative)
- Serves clients in protected territory
- Follows Visiting Angels systems and standards
- Pays royalty and marketing fees to corporate
- Builds local business within franchise framework

**Protected territory:**
- Each franchise has exclusive geographic territory (e.g., "Greater Boston South," "Worcester County")
- Corporate doesn't award competing franchise in your territory
- Territory size based on population density and revenue potential

### **Services Provided to Families:**

Visiting Angels franchises offer standard home care services:

**Personal care:**
- Bathing, dressing, grooming, toileting
- Mobility assistance, transfers
- Medication reminders

**Companionship:**
- Socialization, conversation, activities
- Meal preparation, light housekeeping
- Transportation, errands, appointments

**Specialized care:**
- Dementia and Alzheimer's care (specialized training)
- Respite care for family caregivers
- Post-hospital recovery assistance
- End-of-life care coordination

**Care hours:**
- Hourly shifts (2-4 hour minimum typical)
- Extended shifts (8-12 hours)
- 24/7 live-in care
- Flexible scheduling

**Pricing:**
- Set by franchisee (within market range)
- Typical Massachusetts rates: $28-$38/hour (varies by market and service type)
- Premium pricing vs. independent caregivers, competitive with other agencies

### **Franchise Journey (Becoming Visiting Angels Franchisee):**

**Step 1: Initial inquiry**
- Visit VisitingAngels.com/franchise
- Complete inquiry form (background, financial qualifications, goals)
- Franchise development representative contacts you

**Step 2: Discovery process**
- **Initial call:** Overview of franchise opportunity, answer questions
- **Franchise disclosure document (FDD):** Legal document detailing franchise terms, fees, obligations, financial performance
- **Review financials:** Understand investment required, expected revenue, profitability timeline
- **Validation calls:** Speak with current franchisees (ask about experience, challenges, support, ROI)

**Step 3: Qualification**
- **Financial requirements:** $100k-$150k liquid capital + good credit
- **Background check:** Criminal background, professional references
- **Assessment:** Corporate evaluates if you're good fit (business acumen, caregiving passion, commitment)

**Step 4: Franchise agreement**
- **Legal review:** Attorney reviews franchise agreement (ALWAYS hire franchise lawyer)
- **Negotiate terms:** Some terms negotiable (most not - standard franchise agreement)
- **Sign agreement:** Commit to 10-year franchise term (typical)
- **Pay franchise fee:** $50k-$75k upfront

**Step 5: Training**
- **Corporate training:** 2 weeks at Visiting Angels headquarters (operations, marketing, compliance, technology)
- **Territory launch planning:** Develop business plan for your market
- **Office setup:** Lease office space, hire initial staff, set up technology, obtain licenses

**Step 6: Launch**
- **Pre-launch marketing:** Build awareness before opening (website, local advertising, referral outreach)
- **Grand opening:** Official launch event (corporate support provided)
- **First clients:** Start serving families, hire caregivers, build operations

**Step 7: Ongoing operations**
- **Monthly royalty payments:** 4-5% of gross revenue to corporate
- **Monthly marketing fee:** 2-3% of gross revenue to national marketing fund
- **Ongoing support:** Regional franchise consultant, annual conferences, peer network
- **Quality standards:** Comply with Visiting Angels standards (corporate audits)

## Expected Revenue and Profitability

### **Revenue Trajectory (Typical Franchisee):**

**Year 1:** $250k-$500k
- Building client base from zero
- 10-20 clients by end of year
- Break-even or small loss typical

**Year 2:** $500k-$800k
- 20-35 clients
- Positive cash flow
- Beginning profitability

**Year 3:** $800k-$1.2M
- 35-50 clients
- Strong profitability
- Operations stabilizing

**Year 4-5:** $1M-$2M+
- 50-80+ clients
- Established business
- Owner can step back from day-to-day operations

**Top-performing franchises:** $2M-$5M+ annual revenue
- 80-150+ clients
- Multiple staff, sophisticated operations
- Owner focused on business development vs. operations

**Underperforming franchises:** <$500k after 3+ years
- Insufficient marketing, poor operations, or weak market
- May struggle to cover overhead + franchise fees

### **Profitability:**

**Gross margin:** 30-40%
- Industry standard for home care agencies
- You charge families $30-$35/hour, pay caregivers $15-$18/hour, gross margin covers overhead

**Operating expenses:**
- Rent, staff salaries, insurance, marketing, technology: 20-25% of revenue
- Franchise fees (royalty + marketing): 7-8% of revenue
- **Total overhead:** 27-33% of revenue

**Net profit margin:** 5-15%
- Varies widely based on efficiency, market, and scale
- Example: $1M revenue × 10% net margin = $100k owner income

**Owner compensation:**
- **Small franchise (<$500k revenue):** $50k-$80k/year (mostly working IN the business)
- **Medium franchise ($1M revenue):** $80k-$150k/year (combination of salary + profit)
- **Large franchise ($2M+ revenue):** $150k-$300k+/year (working ON the business, strong team)

**Break-even timeline:**
- Typically 12-18 months to positive cash flow
- 2-3 years to recover initial investment

**Reality check:** Home care franchises are NOT get-rich-quick. It's 3-5 year commitment to build sustainable business. Successful franchisees treat it as long-term investment.

## Visiting Angels vs. Independent Agency: Decision Framework

**Should you franchise with Visiting Angels OR operate independently?**

### **Franchise Advantages (Visiting Angels):**

**1. Brand recognition:**
- Families recognize "Visiting Angels" name (25+ years, national advertising)
- Trust established brand vs. unknown startup
- Accelerates client acquisition (families Google "Visiting Angels [Your Town]")

**Value:** High for startups, moderate for existing agencies (if your local brand already strong)

**2. Turnkey systems:**
- Operations manual (hiring, training, care delivery, quality assurance)
- Technology platform (client management, scheduling, EVV, family portal)
- Compliance frameworks (Massachusetts regulations, policies, procedures)

**Value:** High for entrepreneurs with no home care experience, moderate for existing agencies (may already have systems)

**3. Training and support:**
- 2-week corporate training (business operations, marketing, compliance)
- Ongoing regional support (franchise consultant assigned to your territory)
- Caregiver training curriculum (onboarding, dementia care, safety)

**Value:** High for first-time business owners, moderate for experienced operators

**4. Marketing support:**
- National advertising campaigns (TV, digital, content marketing)
- Local marketing templates (direct mail, print ads, social media)
- SEO and website (Visiting Angels corporate maintains national SEO, you get territory-specific site)

**Value:** Moderate to high (national brand helps, but you still must market locally)

**5. Purchasing power:**
- Group discounts on insurance, background checks, supplies
- Negotiated vendor relationships (technology, payroll, etc.)

**Value:** Moderate (savings may offset 10-20% of franchise fees)

**6. Peer network:**
- 600+ franchisees sharing best practices
- Annual conferences, regional meetings
- Private forums for franchisee collaboration

**Value:** Moderate to high (learning from experienced franchisees accelerates your growth)

**7. Exit value:**
- Franchises typically sell for higher multiples than independent agencies (brand value)
- Corporate helps facilitate franchise resale

**Value:** High if you plan to sell business eventually

### **Independent Agency Advantages:**

**1. No ongoing fees:**
- Visiting Angels: 7-8% of revenue (royalty + marketing)
- Independent: 0% to franchise, invest in your own marketing

**Example:** $1M revenue × 7% = $70,000/year to franchise. Over 10 years = $700k. Could that $700k generate better ROI if invested in your own brand and marketing?

**2. Full control:**
- Brand, pricing, services, operations, culture - all your decisions
- No corporate rules or restrictions
- Pivot quickly when market changes

**3. Flexibility:**
- Innovate without corporate approval
- Specialize in niche markets (cultural, clinical, geographic)
- Differentiate in ways franchise standards might not allow

**4. Local brand:**
- Build YOUR brand equity (not Visiting Angels corporate brand)
- Deeper community relationships (not part of national chain)
- Exit value: Keep 100% of sale proceeds (vs. franchise transfer restrictions)

**5. Lower initial investment:**
- Independent startup: $30k-$60k (vs. $120k-$150k for franchise)
- More accessible for bootstrapping entrepreneurs

### **Decision Matrix:**

**Choose Visiting Angels franchise IF:**
- First-time home care entrepreneur (need systems, training, support)
- Value brand recognition (accelerate client acquisition)
- Want structured framework (operations manual, compliance, training)
- Limited home care experience (franchise provides expertise)
- Plan to scale quickly (franchise resources accelerate growth)
- Exit strategy: Sell business in 7-10 years (franchise has resale value)

**Choose independent agency IF:**
- Experienced home care operator (already have systems and expertise)
- Strong local brand or niche differentiation (don't need national brand)
- Value independence and control (don't want corporate oversight)
- Budget-constrained (lower initial investment)
- Keep ongoing fees for your own marketing (7-8% of revenue stays with you)
- Build business for long-term ownership (not planning to sell)

**Hybrid option: License technology independently**
- Build independent brand BUT license care management software (ClearCare, WellSky) for $500-$1,500/month (vs. $70k/year Visiting Angels fees)
- Get technology support without franchise obligations

**Franchise system is proven:**
- Your first 2-3 years, FOLLOW the Visiting Angels playbook closely
- Don't improvise or skip steps (system works if you work it)
- After you're profitable and stable, adapt/innovate where needed

**Common mistake:** New franchisees think they know better than system, don't follow training, struggle as result.

### **5. Hire great care coordinators:**

**Your business depends on care coordinator quality:**
- They're client-facing, caregiver-facing, revenue-generating
- Invest in hiring experienced coordinators (worth premium salary)
- Train them thoroughly using Visiting Angels curriculum

### **6. Differentiate locally (within franchise framework):**

**Visiting Angels provides brand - you provide local differentiation:**
- Specialize in dementia care, specific cultural community (Portuguese, Brazilian, Chinese)

**Caregiver turnover is #1 operational challenge:**

- Provide meaningful benefits (health insurance if feasible, PTO, bonuses)
- Build culture of appreciation (recognition, team events, career development)
- Consistent hours (caregivers need stable income)

**High retention = better client satisfaction + lower recruitment costs.**

### **9. Ask corporate for support proactively:**

**You pay franchise fees - use the support:**
- Struggling with marketing? Call regional consultant.
- Compliance questions? Call corporate compliance team.
- Technology issues? Call IT support.
- Financial challenges? Request business review with corporate.

**Corporate wants you to succeed (they earn more when you earn more) - leverage their expertise.**

### **10. Plan for 3-year commitment:**

**Franchise takes time to build:**
- Year 1: Break-even or small loss (building foundation)
- Year 2: Positive cash flow, growing (establishing presence)
- Year 3: Strong profitability (reaping rewards)

**Don't judge success in first 12 months - it's 3-year investment.**

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "Franchise fees are expensive - 7-8% of revenue"**

**Reality:** $1M revenue × 7% = $70k/year. Significant expense.

**Solution:**
- **Evaluate ROI:** Does Visiting Angels brand + systems + support generate >$70k value? (More clients due to brand recognition? Higher prices? Operational efficiency?)
- **Compare to alternative:** If independent, would you spend $70k/year on marketing, technology, consulting to achieve same results?
- **Economies of scale:** As you grow, percentage stays same but value increases ($2M revenue × 7% = $140k but you're much more profitable)

**Verdict:** Fees are high. Franchise makes sense if brand and systems deliver sufficient ROI. Track rigorously.

### **Challenge 2: "I can build independent agency for less money"**

**True:** Independent startup $30k-$60k vs. franchise $120k-$150k.

**But consider:**
- **Time to profitability:** Franchise brand recognition accelerates client acquisition (break-even in 12-18 months vs. 24-36 months independent)
- **Mistakes avoided:** Franchise system helps avoid costly errors (compliance, operations, marketing)
- **Total investment over 3 years:** Independent may require more marketing spend to achieve same results as franchise brand

**Solution:** Build financial model for both scenarios (franchise vs. independent). Calculate break-even, 3-year profitability. Make data-driven decision.

### **Challenge 3: "Corporate restricts my independence"**

**Reality:** Franchise agreements impose rules (pricing ranges, services offered, branding, operations).

**Solution:**
- **Accept trade-off:** You get brand and systems, you lose some autonomy. That's franchise model.
- **Review FDD carefully:** Understand all restrictions before signing.
- **Negotiate (limited):** Some terms negotiable, most are not.
- **If you value independence highly:** Don't franchise (frustration guaranteed).

### **Challenge 4: "Competing Visiting Angels franchise opened nearby"**

**Should NOT happen:** Franchise agreement grants exclusive territory.

**If corporate violated territory protection:**
- Review franchise agreement (territory boundaries)
- Contact corporate immediately (contract violation)
- Consult franchise attorney if corporate won't resolve

**If nearby franchise is in different territory but marketing in your area:**
- Allowed (franchisees can advertise outside territory, just can't serve clients outside territory)
- Compete aggressively (local relationships, SEO for your specific towns)

### **Challenge 5: "Corporate marketing doesn't drive leads in my market"**

**Reality:** National TV ads and brand awareness don't automatically generate local clients.

**Solution:**
- **YOU must market locally:** Visiting Angels brand opens doors, but you must build local relationships, optimize local SEO, network with referral sources
- **Don't rely solely on corporate marketing:** It's brand-building, not lead generation
- **Invest 5-10% of revenue in local marketing** (in addition to 2-3% corporate marketing fee)

### **Challenge 6: "Not hitting revenue projections from FDD"**

**Franchise Disclosure Document shows financial performance of other franchises - yours may underperform.**

**Possible reasons:**
1. **Undercapitalized:** Didn't budget enough working capital (running out of money before profitability)
2. **Poor execution:** Not following system, weak marketing, operational issues
3. **Market challenges:** High competition, economic downturn, demographic changes
4. **Unrealistic expectations:** FDD shows average across all franchises (your results may vary)

**Solution:**
- **Request business review with corporate:** Identify problems, develop action plan
- **Increase marketing spend:** Revenue driven by client acquisition
- **Improve operations:** Caregiver retention, client satisfaction, referral generation
- **Consider territory expansion:** If market too small, acquire adjacent territory
- **Last resort:** Sell franchise (recoup some investment) or close (vs. ongoing losses)
