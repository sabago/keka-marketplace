## Overview

Senior Move Managers (SMMs) are **specialized professionals** who help older adults downsize, relocate, and transition to new living arrangements - making them **valuable but underutilized referral partners** for home care agencies. Members of the National Association of Senior & Specialty Move Managers (NASMM), these professionals are the "walking rolodexes" of senior services, observing care needs firsthand during stressful transitions.

**National Association of Senior & Specialty Move Managers (NASMM):**
- **Website:** nasmm.org
- **Find a Move Manager:** Online directory (search by zip code)
- **Membership:** ~1,000 members internationally, 20+ in Massachusetts
- **Certification:** SMM-C (Senior Move Manager-Certified)

**For home care agencies:**
- **Natural transition point:** Seniors moving = high stress, often triggered by health decline
- **Care needs surface:** SMMs observe ADL struggles, safety concerns, cognitive issues during moves
- **Trusted referral partners:** SMMs maintain networks of vetted service providers
- **Bi-directional referrals:** You refer clients TO SMMs (reciprocal relationship)
- **100% FREE partnership** (no cost to agencies)
- **High conversion:** Clients already committed to change, open to additional support

## What Senior Move Managers Do

**Core services:**

### **1. Floor Planning & Space Assessment**
- Measure new living space (assisted living apartment, smaller home, condo)
- **Identify:** What furniture/belongings fit in new space
- **Plan:** Room layouts, accessibility considerations

### **2. Sorting, Organizing, Downsizing**
- Help seniors decide: Keep, donate, gift to family, sell, discard
- **Emotional support:** Decades of possessions, memories, difficult decisions
- **Systems:** Create organizational systems for remaining items

### **3. Estate Dispersal & Donation Coordination**
- **Family distribution:** Coordinate which children/grandchildren receive heirlooms
- **Donations:** Arrange pickups from charities (Goodwill, Salvation Army, Habitat)
- **Estate sales:** Coordinate sales of valuable items
- **Disposal:** Arrange junk removal for remaining items

### **4. Packing & Unpacking Services**
- Professional packing (specialized for fragile items, antiques)
- **Unpacking at new location:** Set up kitchen, bathroom, bedroom (ready to live)
- **Hang pictures, arrange furniture:** Home feels familiar immediately

### **5. Move Coordination**
- Hire and supervise movers
- Coordinate timing (close old home, open new home, moving day)
- **On-site supervision:** Present on moving day to manage process

### **6. Connecting Families with Service Providers**
- **"Walking rolodex":** SMMs maintain extensive referral networks
- Home care agencies, real estate agents, estate sale companies, cleaning services, handymen, elder law attorneys, financial planners

## Why Senior Move Managers Refer to Home Care

**The move as a "care need reveal" moment:**

**Scenario 1: Downsizing due to health decline**
- Elderly couple (80s) selling family home (lived there 50 years), moving to smaller condo
- **SMM hired:** Help sort 50 years of belongings, coordinate move
- **During process, SMM observes:**
  - Husband struggling with stairs (uses furniture to steady himself)
  - Wife forgets conversations (asks same questions repeatedly)
  - Both overwhelmed by daily tasks (dishes piling up, laundry undone)
- **SMM to adult children:** "Your parents are struggling more than you realize. Have you considered home care? Let me give you names of agencies..."

**Scenario 2: Moving to assisted living (but needs bridge care)**
- Senior (75) moving to assisted living in 3 months (waiting list)
- **SMM hired:** Prepare home for sale, coordinate move
- **During sorting, SMM notices:**
  - Client living alone, no family nearby
  - Difficulty with ADLs (bathing, dressing, medication management)
  - Falls risk (clutter, poor lighting, no grab bars)
- **SMM to client:** "You have 3 months before assisted living opens. Would home care help bridge this gap? Here are agencies..."

**Scenario 3: Moving after spouse's death**
- Widow (82) moving to be near daughter (different state)
- **SMM hired:** Downsize lifetime of possessions, coordinate cross-country move
- **During process, SMM observes:**
  - Client grief-stricken, depressed, isolated
  - Not eating properly, losing weight
  - Daughter working full-time, can't provide all support needed
- **SMM to daughter:** "Your mother needs more support than you can provide while working. Home care could help during this transition..."

**Scenario 4: Adult children realize parent's decline**
- Adult children visit parent's home for first time in months (came to help with move)
- **SMM working in home:** Children see clutter, expired food, poor hygiene
- **Family meeting:** Children shocked by parent's condition
- **SMM:** "I see this often - have you considered home care? Your father needs daily support. Here are agencies I trust..."

## How to Build Senior Move Manager Partnerships

### **Step 1: Find Local SMMs**

**Research:**
1. **NASMM directory:** nasmm.org → Find a Move Manager → Enter your zip code
2. **Google:** "Senior Move Manager [your town/region]"
3. **Ask COAs/ASAPs:** "Which move managers serve this area?"
4. **Real estate agents:** Often refer to SMMs (ask for introductions)

**Target:** 5-10 SMMs in your service area

### **Step 2: Reach Out to SMMs**

**Outreach approach:**

**Option A: Email introduction**
```
Subject: Partnership Opportunity - Supporting Your Senior Clients

Dear [SMM Name],

I'm [Your Name], owner of [Agency Name], a home care agency serving [region]. I know many of your clients are navigating challenging transitions and may need ongoing support beyond the move itself.

I'd welcome the opportunity to introduce our agency as a resource for your clients. We help seniors and families with:
- Daily care during stressful moves/transitions
- Support for aging homeowners who are overwhelmed
- Bridge care while waiting for assisted living openings
- Long-term support to age in place successfully

Many senior move managers find value in having trusted home care partners they can recommend.

May I schedule a brief 15-minute call or coffee to discuss how we might support your clients?

Best regards,
[Contact info]
```

**Option B: Phone call**
- Call SMM office (afternoons best, avoid Monday mornings)
- Brief introduction: "I'm a local home care agency owner - I work with families going through similar transitions as your clients. I'd love to introduce our agency as a resource."
- Request: 15-minute coffee meeting

**Option C: Networking events**
- NASMM chapter meetings, COA events, real estate networking
- Introduce yourself, exchange cards, follow up

### **Step 3: Meet with Senior Move Manager**

**Meeting structure (15-20 minutes over coffee):**

**A. Learn about their practice:**
- "What percentage of your clients move due to health decline?"
- "What challenges do your senior clients face during moves?"
- **LISTEN:** Understand their pain points

**B. Position as solution:**
- "We help families during these transitions - managing daily tasks, providing support during stressful moves, ensuring safety"
- "When your clients are overwhelmed, we can step in with temporary or ongoing support"

**C. Propose mutual referrals:**
- "When your clients need care, I'd be honored if you'd recommend us"
- "When OUR clients need to downsize, move, or prepare homes for sale, we'll refer them to you"
- **Win-win partnership**

**D. Provide resources:**
- Leave business cards, brochures
- "Keep these handy - when clients mention being overwhelmed or needing help, think of us"

### **Step 4: Demonstrate Value**

**How to stand out:**

**A. Rapid response:**
- SMM calls: "I have a client who needs help ASAP - move happening next week, client overwhelmed"
- **You respond:** Same-day assessment, start care within 48 hours
- **SMMs remember** agencies that deliver quickly

**B. Move support services:**
- "We can help clients during moves: Supervise home during showings, be present for contractor visits, manage house during packing"
- Makes SMM's job easier

**C. Cross-refer:**
- **When YOUR clients need to move/downsize,** refer to partner SMMs
- "I have a client who needs to downsize - may I connect them with you?"
- **SMMs remember who sends them business**

**D. Co-marketing:**
- "Would you like to co-host a senior downsizing seminar?" (SMM presents moving tips, you present care/resources)
- 50-100 seniors attend, both benefit

**E. Case collaboration:**
- **Complex cases:** Client needs BOTH move management AND home care
- Work together on care plan
- **SMMs appreciate** agencies that collaborate (not compete)

### **Step 5: Stay Connected**

**Ongoing:**
- **Quarterly check-ins:** Email, coffee, "Any clients we can help?"
- **Thank referrals:** When SMM refers, send thank-you note (+ update on client: "They're doing great!")
- **Holiday cards:** Stay top-of-mind
- **Share success stories:** "The Smith family you referred successfully transitioned - they're thriving!"

## Expected Referral Volume from Senior Move Managers

**Volume depends on:**
1. **SMM's client volume:** Active SMM (50+ moves/year) vs. part-time (10 moves/year)
2. **Relationship depth:** Know SMM personally? Or just business card?
3. **Cross-referrals:** Do you refer clients TO SMM? (Reciprocity critical)
4. **Response time:** Same-day assessments when SMM calls?
5. **Quality outcomes:** Does SMM hear positive feedback from shared clients?

**Expected referrals per SMM:**
- **Part-time SMM** (10-20 moves/year): 2-5 home care referrals/year
- **Full-time SMM** (40-60 moves/year): 10-20 home care referrals/year
- **High-volume SMM** (80-100+ moves/year): 20-40 home care referrals/year

**Total volume (5-10 SMM partnerships):**
- **Building relationships:** 5-15 referrals/month
- **Established partnerships:** 15-35 referrals/month

**Conversion rate:** 70-80% (high - clients already in transition, needs evident)

## When to Refer Clients TO Senior Move Managers

**Mutual referrals critical for partnership:**

**Situations where SMMs help YOUR clients:**

**1. Downsizing/moving:**
- Client overwhelmed by possessions (50+ years in home)
- Moving to smaller space (condo, assisted living, adult child's home)
- **SMM benefit:** Professional organizing, emotional support, logistics coordination

**2. Estate clearing:**
- Client passed away, family needs to clear/sell home
- **SMM benefit:** Compassionate estate dispersal, donation coordination, sale preparation

**3. Home preparation for sale:**
- Client moving to assisted living, house needs to be emptied and staged
- **SMM benefit:** Coordinate clearing, cleaning, minor repairs

**4. Relocating to be near family:**
- Client moving to different state to be near children
- **SMM benefit:** Cross-country move coordination, new home setup

**5. Aging in place modifications:**
- Client staying home but needs decluttering for safety (fall prevention)
- **SMM benefit:** Organize home for accessibility, remove hazards

**How to refer:**
- **Identify need:** "It sounds like you're overwhelmed by the move - have you considered a senior move manager?"
- **Provide SMM names:** Give client 2-3 SMM names (your partners)
- **Warm handoff:** "May I tell [SMM Name] to expect your call?" OR "May I have them contact you?"
- **Follow up:** Check with client later ("Did [SMM Name] help you?")

## Best Practices for SMM Partnerships

**1. Build personal relationships:**
- **Don't just exchange business cards** - SMMs refer to partners they KNOW
- Meet for coffee quarterly, visit each other's offices
- **Personal connection = more referrals**

**2. Refer TO SMMs proactively:**
- Don't wait for family to ask about moving help
- **When client mentions downsizing,** recommend SMM: "Let me connect you with a senior move manager - they specialize in this"
- **SMMs remember who sends them business**

**3. Understand SMM process & timeline:**
- **SMM projects:** 2-8 weeks typical (sorting, packing, moving)
- **Your care:** May start DURING move (bridge care) or AFTER move (ongoing support)
- **Coordinate timing:** Know when SMM will finish, when client needs ongoing care to begin

**4. Respect SMM's client relationships:**
- **SMM has established trust** with client/family
- Don't overstep or contradict SMM recommendations
- **Collaborative approach:** "We're both here to support this family"

**5. Offer move-specific services:**
- **Differentiate:** "We provide care during moves - supervising home, being present for contractors, managing medications during chaos"
- SMMs appreciate agencies that understand move-specific needs (not just general home care)

**6. Target SMM-C certified members:**
- **SMM-C = Senior Move Manager-Certified** (NASMM credential)
- Higher professionalism, more established practices
- **More likely to have referral networks** and reciprocate

**7. Understand SMM motivations:**
- **SMMs want clients to succeed** in new living arrangements
- Concerned about clients struggling post-move
- **Value partners who ensure successful transitions** (home care = safety net)

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "SMMs won't meet with me"**
**Solution:** (1) Offer value upfront ("I'd like to be a resource for your clients" vs. "I want referrals"), (2) Get warm introductions (ask real estate agents, COAs who know SMMs), (3) Attend NASMM chapter meetings (if exist in your area), (4) Refer TO SMMs first (demonstrate reciprocity before asking for referrals), (5) Be patient (SMMs are busy during move season - try off-season for initial meetings).

**Challenge 2: "SMM referred family, family didn't hire us"**
**Solution:** SMMs typically recommend 2-3 agencies (not exclusive). Improve: (1) Respond faster than competitors, (2) Emphasize transition support ("We specialize in supporting families during moves"), (3) Flexible packages (short-term move support options), (4) Ask SMM for feedback: "Why did family choose differently?" (learn and adapt).

**Challenge 3: "Don't know when to refer clients TO SMMs"**
**Solution:** Refer when: (1) Client mentions downsizing/moving, (2) Home overwhelmingly cluttered (safety hazard), (3) Moving to assisted living (need to clear/sell house), (4) After client passes (family needs estate clearing), (5) Family caregiver overwhelmed by possessions + care needs. **Proactive referrals** to SMMs = they reciprocate.

**Challenge 4: "SMM referrals are short-term only"**
**Solution:** MIXED - some are short-term (2-8 week move support), some become long-term (after move, client realizes ongoing care needed). That's OKAY: (1) Short-term = quick revenue (vs. waiting months for long-term client), (2) Some DO become long-term (20-30% of move clients transition to ongoing care), (3) Diversify (mix SMM referrals with long-term sources like ASAPs, COAs). Don't rely solely on SMMs.

**Challenge 5: "SMMs forget about us between referrals"**
**Solution:** (1) Quarterly check-ins (stay visible), (2) Refer TO SMMs consistently (top-of-mind), (3) Thank referrals enthusiastically (SMMs want to know recommendations worked out), (4) Share success stories (with consent): "The family you referred successfully transitioned - thank you!", (5) Co-market (joint seminars = ongoing visibility).
