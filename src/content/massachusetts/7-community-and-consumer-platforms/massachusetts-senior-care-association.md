## Overview

The Massachusetts Senior Care Association (MSCA) is the **premier trade association** representing 400+ nursing/rehab facilities, assisted living residences, residential care facilities, and continuing care retirement communities (CCRCs) across Massachusetts. For home care agencies seeking to build relationships with skilled nursing facilities and assisted living communities, MSCA conferences and membership provide unparalleled **networking access** to decision-makers controlling discharge planning and resident referrals.

**Massachusetts Senior Care Association (MSCA):**
- **Website:** maseniorcare.org
- **Membership:** 400+ long-term care providers statewide
- **Focus:** Legislative advocacy, regulatory guidance, continuing education, data analysis
- **Annual events:** Spring Conference & Trade Show, Leadership Conference
- **Founded:** Trade association for Massachusetts long-term care industry

**For home care agencies:**
- **Access to 400+ facilities:** SNFs, assisted living, CCRCs in one association
- **Discharge planner networking:** Meet social workers, case managers controlling referrals
- **Conference trade show:** 50+ sponsors/exhibitors, face-to-face visibility
- **Educational sessions:** Learn MA regulatory environment, discharge planning trends
- **Associate membership available:** Non-facility members can join as allied vendors/service providers
- **Investment required:** Membership fees + conference costs ($500-$3,000 annually)

## How MSCA Generates Home Care Referrals

**Referral flow from MSCA member facilities:**

### **1. Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF) Discharges to Home**

**Scenario 1: Post-rehab discharge**
- Patient completes rehab at SNF (hip replacement, stroke recovery, surgery)
- **Discharge planner assessment:** Patient ready for home but needs ongoing support
- **Discharge planner (met at MSCA conference):** "I remember that home care agency from the trade show - let me call them"
- **Refers patient to home care agency**

**Expected volume per SNF:** 5-15 home care referrals/month (varies by facility size)

### **2. Assisted Living Resident Transitions**

**Scenario 2: Aging in place support**
- Assisted living resident declining (needs more care than AL staff can provide)
- **Two options:** Move to nursing home OR add private home care
- **AL director (connected at MSCA):** "Let's try home care supplement - I know agencies from MSCA conferences"
- **Refers family to home care agency** (agency provides 4-8 hours/day, resident stays in AL)

**Expected volume per AL:** 2-8 home care referrals/month

### **3. CCRC Transitions**

**Scenario 3: Independent living → home care bridge**
- CCRC independent living resident declining
- **Next step typically:** Move to assisted living OR nursing wing
- **Alternative:** Add home care to independent living apartment (delay move)
- **CCRC administrator:** Refers to home care agency for assessment

**Expected volume per CCRC:** 3-10 home care referrals/month

### **4. Waiting List Management**

**Scenario 4: Waiting for facility opening**
- Family seeking assisted living/nursing home placement
- **Facility has waiting list:** 3-6 months until bed available
- **Admission coordinator:** "Use home care as bridge while you wait - here are agencies I know"
- **Refers to home care agency**

**Expected volume:** Variable based on facility occupancy

## How to Join MSCA as Associate Member

### **Step 1: Determine If Membership Makes Sense**

**Investment required:**
- **Associate membership dues:** $500-$1,500/year (varies by agency size/revenue)
- **Spring Conference registration:** $150-$300/person
- **Trade show booth:** $500-$2,000 (if exhibiting)
- **Total annual investment:** $1,000-$4,000

**When membership makes sense:**
- You serve 50+ communities where MSCA members are located
- You want to target SNFs/ALs systematically (not ad hoc)
- You can commit to attending conferences (networking required, not passive)
- You're building SNF/AL partnerships as core strategy
- You have budget for professional development/networking

**When membership may NOT make sense:**
- You serve limited geographic area (only 5-10 communities)
- Budget constraints (can build SNF/AL relationships individually without MSCA)
- You can't attend conferences (membership value mostly in networking)
- Your agency focuses on private pay home-based clients (not facility partnerships)

### **Step 2: Contact MSCA**

**Apply for associate membership:**
- **Website:** maseniorcare.org
- **Look for:** "Membership" or "Join MSCA" section
- **Contact:** MSCA office (info typically on website)
- **Ask:** "How can home care agencies join as associate members?"

**Application process:**
- Complete membership application
- Provide: Agency info, services, coverage area, references
- **Pay dues:** Typically annual (invoiced yearly)
- **Approval:** MSCA board reviews, approves associate members

### **Step 3: Register for Conferences**

**Spring Conference:**
- **Register early:** Conference fills up (400+ attendees)
- **Decide:** Attend only OR exhibit at trade show
  - **Attend only:** Lower cost ($150-$300), less visibility
  - **Exhibit:** Higher cost ($1,000-$3,000), high visibility + lead generation

**Leadership Conference:**
- Smaller, more intimate (fewer attendees than Spring Conference)
- **Value:** Deeper conversations with administrators

### **Step 4: Prepare for Conference**

**If exhibiting at trade show:**
- **Design booth:** Professional display (banners, brochures, giveaways)
- **Staff booth:** 2-3 staff members (owner, care coordinator, marketer)
- **Bring materials:** 500+ brochures, 1,000+ business cards, lead forms
- **Tech:** Tablet for lead capture, email list signup

**If attending without exhibiting:**
- **Business cards:** 500+ (hand out generously)
- **Elevator pitch:** 30-second agency introduction
- **Questions prepared:** "What challenges do you face with home care referrals?"

**For both:**
- **Research attendees:** Review member directory, identify facilities you want to target
- **Set goals:** "Meet 20 discharge planners," "Collect 50 business cards"

## How to Maximize MSCA Conference ROI

### **Before Conference:**

**1. Research member facilities:**
- **Download directory:** If available to associate members
- **Identify targets:** Which facilities in your service area?
- **Prioritize:** Top 20-30 facilities you want to connect with

**2. Reach out pre-conference:**
- **Email discharge planners:** "I'll be at MSCA Spring Conference - would you have 5 minutes to chat at the conference?"
- **Schedule coffee meetings:** Some administrators arrive day before (opportunity for one-on-one)

**3. Prepare materials:**
- **Brochures:** Clear, professional, emphasize SNF/AL partnerships
- **One-pagers:** "Seamless SNF-to-Home Transitions" (how you support discharge planning)
- **Business cards:** Lots of them

### **During Conference:**

**1. Attend educational sessions strategically:**
- **DPH discharge planning sessions:** Understand regulatory requirements (positions you as informed partner)
- **Medicare/payment sessions:** Learn facility financial pressures (helps you understand their challenges)
- **Staffing sessions:** Facilities struggling with staffing = more open to home care partnerships

**2. Work the trade show floor:**
- **Visit every booth:** Not just exhibiting, but observing who facilities talk to
- **Introduce yourself:** "Hi, I'm [Name] from [Agency] - we help SNFs with seamless home discharges"
- **Collect cards:** LOTS of cards (follow up is where deals happen)

**3. Meals and breaks are PRIME NETWORKING:**
- **Don't sit alone:** Sit with facility administrators, discharge planners
- **Ask questions:** "What challenges do you face with discharge planning?"
- **Listen more than talk:** Understand their pain points

**4. Target specific facilities:**
- **Seek out priority targets:** Find administrators/discharge planners from your target facilities
- **Schedule follow-up:** "May I visit your facility next month to introduce our agency?"

### **After Conference:**

**1. Follow up within 48 hours:**
- **Email everyone you met:** "Great meeting you at MSCA Conference - I'd love to continue the conversation"
- **Personalize:** Reference specific discussion point from conference

**2. Schedule facility visits:**
- **Goal:** Convert conference connection → facility partnership
- **Within 2-4 weeks:** Visit 10-20 facilities met at conference

**3. Track leads:**
- **CRM/spreadsheet:** Name, facility, contact info, conversation notes, follow-up status
- **Measure ROI:** How many connections → facility visits → actual referrals?

1. **Conference attendance:** Attend vs. exhibit vs. skip
2. **Follow-up discipline:** How many connections convert to facility partnerships?

**Year 2-3:**
- **Facility partnerships mature:** 10-25 active SNF/AL partnerships

- **ROI:** Positive (2-4x return on MSCA investment)

**Conversion funnel:**
- 60 conference connections
- → 20 facility visits scheduled
- → 10 partnerships established
- → 15 referrals/month average

## Best Practices for MSCA Membership

**1. Attend conferences consistently:**
- **Not one-and-done:** Attend Spring Conference annually (facility administrators recognize you year-over-year)
- **Build familiarity:** "I remember you from last year's conference..."

**2. Exhibit if budget allows:**
- **Higher visibility:** Facilities come to you (vs. chasing them down)
- **Lead capture:** Collect contact info systematically
- **Professional image:** Booth conveys legitimacy, seriousness

**3. Follow up religiously:**
- **Within 48 hours:** Email everyone met at conference
- **Schedule visits:** Within 2-4 weeks (while you're still fresh in their minds)
- **CRM critical:** Track all connections, follow-up status

**4. Position as SNF/AL specialist:**
- **Marketing materials:** "Seamless Transitions from SNF to Home," "Supporting Assisted Living Residents Aging in Place"
- **Differentiate:** Some agencies focus on private pay home-based (you focus on facility partnerships)

**5. Understand facility pressures:**
- **Regulatory:** MA DPH discharge planning requirements
- **Financial:** Medicare reimbursement, MassHealth rates, occupancy challenges
- **Staffing:** Nursing shortages, caregiver turnover
- **Position home care as solution:** "We ease discharge pressure, support aging in place residents, collaborate with your teams"

**6. Build relationships beyond conference:**
- **Quarterly visits** to partner facilities
- **Lunch-and-learns:** Present at SNF/AL staff meetings
- **Share outcomes:** Report back on successful transitions

**7. Know your competition:**
- **Other home care agencies attend MSCA conferences:** Differentiate clearly
- **National chains present:** Emphasize local presence, personalized service

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "MSCA membership expensive for small agency"**
**Solution:** (1) Start with Spring Conference attendance only (no booth = lower cost), (2) Partner with another agency to share booth (split costs), (3) Attend Leadership Conference instead (smaller, cheaper), (4) Calculate ROI (if 2-3 SNF partnerships cover investment, worth it), (5) Consider: can you build SNF partnerships individually without MSCA? (May be more cost-effective for small agencies)

**Challenge 2: "Attended conference, not getting referrals"**
**Solution:** (1) Did you FOLLOW UP within 48 hours? (Conference connections die without follow-up), (2) Did you VISIT facilities? (Meeting at conference ≠ partnership - must visit facility, meet full team), (3) Are you communicating consistently? (Quarterly visits, not just one meeting), (4) Did you target RIGHT people? (Discharge planners, social services directors = referral gatekeepers, not just administrators), (5) Be patient (takes 6-12 months to see consistent referrals from new SNF partnerships)

**Challenge 3: "Too much competition at conference"**
**Solution:** (1) Differentiate clearly (specialize in post-surgical, dementia, complex cases), (2) Target specific niches (SNFs with orthopedic programs, ALs with memory care), (3) Build deeper relationships (not just business cards - meaningful conversations), (4) Follow up better than competitors (most don't follow up - you can stand out), (5) Emphasize local presence (vs. national chains)

**Challenge 4: "Don't know what to say at conference"**
**Solution:** (1) Prepare elevator pitch (30-second agency introduction), (2) Ask questions, don't just talk ("What challenges do you face with home discharges?"), (3) Listen for pain points (staffing, readmissions, difficult families), (4) Position as solution to their problems (not generic sales pitch), (5) Practice with colleagues before conference

**Challenge 5: "Member facilities already have preferred home care agencies"**
**Solution:** TRUE - many facilities have established relationships. (1) Position as ADDITIONAL resource (not replacement), (2) Differentiate ("We specialize in complex cases," "We accept all insurance"), (3) Build relationship over time (preferred status changes as relationships develop), (4) Target NEW facilities (startups, new management = open to new partnerships), (5) Some facilities WANT multiple agencies (don't assume exclusivity)
