## Overview

MassOptions is Massachusetts' statewide **Aging and Disability Resource Center** (ADRC), providing FREE information, referrals, and access to services for older adults and people with disabilities. Operating 24/7 with multilingual support in over 100 languages, MassOptions serves as the **front door** to Massachusetts' aging services network.

**Call:** **1-800-243-4636** (toll-free, 24/7)
**Website:** https://www.massoptions.org

For home care agencies, MassOptions is the **primary gateway** through which thousands of Massachusetts families discover and access home care services. When seniors or families call MassOptions, they are connected to their local Aging Services Access Point (ASAP), which then provides direct referrals to home care agencies.

**Key value:** MassOptions generates thousands of calls monthly from families actively seeking home care solutions—positioning your agency with local ASAPs ensures you receive these referrals.

## Who Sends Referrals Through This Source

MassOptions itself is an **information and referral hub** operated by the Executive Office of Aging & Independence (AGE). The referral flow works like this:

1. **Family or senior calls MassOptions** (1-800-243-4636) seeking help with home care, caregiving, or aging services
2. **MassOptions I&R specialist** assesses needs and determines appropriate resources
3. **Specialist connects caller to local ASAP** based on ZIP code/city
4. **ASAP care manager** conducts comprehensive assessment
5. **ASAP refers to home care agencies** in their network

**Who calls MassOptions:**
- Family caregivers seeking support for aging parents
- Seniors themselves needing assistance at home
- Hospital discharge planners or social workers seeking resources for patients
- Primary care physicians referring patients needing home support
- Community organizations (senior centers, churches, housing authorities)

**Call volume:** Thousands of calls monthly across Massachusetts, representing significant referral potential for agencies connected to the ASAP network.

## Who Can Receive Referrals

Indirectly through ASAPs, MassOptions connects families with:
- **Private-pay home care agencies**
- **Medicare-certified home health agencies**
- **Frail Elder Waiver homemaker providers**
- **Adult day health programs**
- **Meals on Wheels and nutrition services**
- **Transportation services**
- **Senior housing and assisted living**

**For home care agencies:** To receive referrals generated by MassOptions, you must be connected with local ASAPs (see Related Resources below).

## How It Works (From an Agency's Perspective)

**Direct relationship with MassOptions:**
MassOptions does NOT maintain a provider directory or make direct referrals to home care agencies. Instead, it routes calls to ASAPs, which then make provider referrals.

**To benefit from MassOptions volume:**

1. **Build relationships with local ASAPs** (see Massachusetts ASAPs Overview)
2. **Get listed as preferred provider** with ASAPs in your service area
3. **ASAPs will refer MassOptions callers** to your agency

**Typical flow:**
- Family calls MassOptions: "My mom needs help at home"
- MassOptions assesses: "It sounds like she could benefit from home care services and possibly Frail Elder Waiver. Let me connect you with [Local ASAP Name]"
- Family is transferred or given direct number to ASAP
- ASAP care manager assesses mom's needs and financial situation
- ASAP refers family to your agency (if you're in their network)
- Family calls you to schedule assessment

**Timeline:** From initial MassOptions call to agency contact can be same-day to 1-2 weeks depending on ASAP capacity and urgency.

## How to Get Listed / Enrolled

**You cannot enroll directly with MassOptions.** Instead:

**Step 1: Identify Your Local ASAPs**
- Determine which ASAPs cover your service area (see Massachusetts ASAPs Overview)
- Most agencies should connect with 3-5 AS APs covering their territory

**Step 2: Build ASAP Relationships**
- Contact each ASAP's care management or provider relations team
- Schedule introductory meetings
- Complete any provider enrollment forms
- Provide agency materials and insurance certificates

**Step 3: Maintain ASAP Presence**
- Visit quarterly
- Respond quickly to referrals
- Provide excellent service and feedback
- Participate in ASAP provider meetings and events

**Step 4: Benefit from MassOptions Volume**
- Once connected with ASAPs, you'll automatically receive referrals from MassOptions-generated calls

## Cost and Lead Quality

**Cost:**
- **FREE** - MassOptions is a free state service; no fees for agencies

**Lead Quality:**
- **Very high** - callers have identified need and are actively seeking solutions
- **Pre-qualified** - ASAPs assess needs before referring to agencies
- **Immediate need** - families calling MassOptions typically need services within days or weeks
- **Mix of payers** - MassHealth/Frail Elder Waiver, private-pay, Medicare Advantage
- **High conversion** - 60-80% conversion rate for referred families (they've already taken initiative to call)

**Volume:**
- Varies by ASAP relationship strength
- Well-connected agencies may receive 10-30+ MassOptions-sourced referrals monthly

## Pros and Cons for Massachusetts Home Care Agencies

**Pros:**
- **Massive awareness campaign** - MassOptions is heavily advertised across MA (TV, radio, online, community)
- **24/7 availability** - families can call anytime, generating consistent referral flow
- **Multilingual support** - connects diverse populations (100+ languages)
- **Pre-qualified leads** - ASAP assessment filters for appropriate referrals
- **FREE service** - no platform fees or commissions
- **Statewide reach** - connects families across all regions of MA
- **Trusted source** - state-backed service provides credibility

**Cons:**
- **Indirect relationship** - agencies don't interact directly with MassOptions
- **Dependent on ASAP relationships** - must maintain strong ASAP connections to benefit
- **Variable timeline** - referral may take days/weeks as call flows through ASAP assessment
- **No direct control** - can't influence MassOptions call handling or ASAP referral decisions

## How This Fits Mastering Home Care's Data Flow

MassOptions referrals arrive **indirectly via ASAPs** through phone, fax, or email:

**Ingest Path:**
1. Family calls MassOptions → routed to ASAP → ASAP assesses and refers to your agency
2. ASAP contacts agency via phone, fax, or email (see Massachusetts ASAPs Overview for details)
3. Mastering Home Care ingests referral through standard ASAP channels

**Automation:**
- **Source tracking:** Tag referrals as "MassOptions → [ASAP Name]" to track this funnel
- **Volume analysis:** Measure how many referrals come via MassOptions pathway vs. other ASAP sources
- **ROI by ASAP:** Identify which ASAPs generate most MassOptions volume

**Note:** Since MassOptions is an intermediary, integration strategy focuses on optimizing ASAP referral ingestion (see Massachusetts ASAPs Overview).

## Best Practices to Maximize Referrals from This Source

**1. Strengthen ASAP Relationships:**
- MassOptions volume flows through ASAPs—invest in these relationships
- Be the ASAP's "go-to" agency for quick, reliable placements

**2. Educate ASAPs on Your Capabilities:**
- Highlight services that match common MassOptions caller needs (companion care, personal care, dementia support)
- Emphasize responsiveness and cultural competency

**3. Track MassOptions-Sourced Referrals:**
- Ask families during intake: "How did you hear about us?"
- If they mention MassOptions, document this to understand volume
- Use Mastering Home Care to analyze MassOptions → ASAP → Agency funnel

**4. Participate in MassOptions Awareness:**
- Mention MassOptions in your marketing materials: "Not sure where to start? Call MassOptions at 1-800-243-4636"
- This builds credibility and directs families to system that may refer back to you

**5. Respond Quickly to ASAP Referrals:**
- MassOptions callers are often in urgent need—fast response captures these clients
- Aim for same-day contact when ASAP refers a MassOptions caller
