## Overview

A Place for Mom is the **largest senior living referral service in North America**, helping 3+ million families annually find assisted living, memory care, independent living, and home care. With 20+ years in the senior care industry and 200+ Local Senior Living Advisors nationwide, A Place for Mom represents a **significant free referral opportunity** for home care agencies willing to partner as community resource providers.

**What A Place for Mom provides:**
- **FREE advisory service** for families (no cost to families)
- **Senior Living Advisors:** Trained professionals helping families explore care options
- **Comprehensive database:** 50,000+ senior living communities + home care agencies
- **Educational resources:** Articles, guides, checklists for family caregivers

**Business model:**
- **Communities pay:** Assisted living/memory care communities pay A Place for Mom when resident moves in ($3,000-$6,000 per placement)
- **Home care agencies DON'T pay:** Agencies are resource partners (not revenue source)
- **Mutual benefit:** Advisors refer families TO agencies, agencies refer families TO advisors (reciprocal relationship)

## How A Place for Mom Works

### **Family Journey:**

**Step 1: Family contacts A Place for Mom**
- **Google search:** "Assisted living near me" → A Place for Mom appears (strong SEO + ads)
- **Call:** 1-800-355-8221 (toll-free) OR online form

**Step 2: Senior Living Advisor assessment**
- **Phone consultation (30-45 minutes):**
  - What are parent's care needs? (ADLs, dementia, medical conditions)
  - What's the budget? (Income, assets, long-term care insurance?)
  - What's the location preference? (Stay near family? Specific towns?)
  - What's the timeline? (Need care now vs. planning ahead)
  - **Preference:** Facility OR home care?

**Step 3: Advisor recommends options**
- **If family wants facility:** Advisor recommends 3-5 assisted living communities (arranged tours)
- **If family wants home care:** Advisor recommends 2-3 home care agencies (provides contact info)
- **If family unsure:** Advisor suggests trying home care FIRST, then facility if needed (THIS is where your agency comes in)

**Step 4: Family contacts agencies**
- Warm introduction: "A Place for Mom advisor gave me your name..."
- Family evaluates agencies, chooses one

### **Why A Place for Mom Refers to Home Care:**

**Scenario A: "Try home care first"**
- Family called about assisted living but hesitant
- **Advisor:** "Before you decide on facility, try home care for 2-3 months - less disruptive, may work"
- Advisor refers to home care agencies

**Scenario B: "Waiting list for facility"**
- Family chose assisted living community but 3-6 month waiting list
- **Advisor:** "Use home care as bridge while waiting"
- Advisor refers to agencies

**Scenario C: "Home care more affordable"**
- Family researches assisted living costs ($5,000-$8,000/month)
- **Decides:** "Let's try home care instead - cheaper" (20 hours/week @ $30/hour = $2,400/month vs. $6,000/month assisted living)
- Advisor refers to agencies

## How to Become A Place for Mom Resource Partner

### **Step 1: Understand Partnership Model**

**A Place for Mom does NOT sell leads to home care agencies:**
- **NOT pay-per-lead** (unlike Caring.com)
- **NOT subscription model**
- **100% FREE resource partnership**

**Why free?**
- A Place for Mom makes money from assisted living communities (not home care agencies)
- Home care agencies are RESOURCE to advisors (when families want home care vs. facility)
- **Mutual referral relationship:** Advisors refer TO you, you refer TO advisors

**Implication:**
- Referral volume moderate (5-20/month typical), not high-volume lead gen
- **High-quality referrals** (professionally screened by advisors)
- Requires reciprocal referrals (you refer families TO A Place for Mom when facility needed)

### **Step 2: Contact A Place for Mom Community Relations**

**Outreach:**
- **Phone:** 1-800-355-8221
- **Ask for:** "Community Relations" or "Agency Partnerships"
- **Introduce agency:** "We're a Massachusetts home care agency, we'd like to be a resource for your advisors when families need home care"

**Be prepared to discuss:**
- **Service area:** Which MA cities/towns do you serve?
- **Services:** Personal care, companionship, dementia care, 24/7 live-in?
- **Languages:** Spanish, Portuguese, Chinese, etc.?
- **Specializations:** Any unique capabilities (post-surgical, Alzheimer's, etc.)?
- **References:** Happy clients, quality metrics?

### **Step 3: Complete Agency Profile**

**A Place for Mom creates agency profile (online directory):**
- Agency name, contact info, website
- Service area (towns/cities served)
- Services offered
- Languages available
- Specializations
- Photos (office, caregivers, services)
- Client testimonials/reviews

**Profile benefits:**
- **Families can FIND you** if they search A Place for Mom website
- **Advisors see your profile** when recommending agencies
- **SEO benefit:** Link from A Place for Mom to your website (improves Google rankings)

### **Step 4: Build Relationship with Local Advisors**

**A Place for Mom has Local Senior Living Advisors in every MA region:**
- Greater Boston advisors
- Worcester area advisors
- North Shore advisors
- South Shore advisors
- Western MA advisors
- Cape Cod advisors

**Strategy:**
- **Meet your local advisor(s):** Request phone or coffee meeting
- **Introduce agency:** Share capabilities, service area, specializations
- **Discuss mutual referrals:** "When we have clients who need facilities, we'll refer to you. When you have families who want home care, please refer to us."
- **Stay in touch:** Quarterly check-ins (email, phone, coffee)

**Advisors refer to agencies they KNOW and TRUST:**
- One meeting ≠ automatic referrals
- **Build relationship over time** (6-12 months to see consistent referrals)

### **Step 5: Refer TO A Place for Mom**

**Critical for partnership:**
- **When YOUR home care clients need facilities,** refer to A Place for Mom
- Call advisor: "I have a client family - Mom's care needs have exceeded home care capabilities - can you help them find assisted living?"
- **Advisors remember agencies that refer TO them**

**Reciprocity = more referrals:**
- After you refer 3-5 families TO A Place for Mom, advisors start referring back to you
- **Mutual benefit** (strongest partnerships)

**Volume depends on:**
1. **Relationship with local advisors:** Know them personally? Or just in database?
2. **Reciprocal referrals:** Do you refer families TO A Place for Mom? (They remember)
3. **Service area:** Broad coverage (50+ towns) or narrow (5 towns)?
4. **Specializations:** Dementia care? 24/7 live-in? (Advisors need specialists for complex cases)

**Reality check:**
- A Place for Mom = **SUPPLEMENT** to other referral sources (not primary source)
- **Best agencies use A Place for Mom AS PART OF** comprehensive referral strategy (not sole source)

## When to Refer Clients TO A Place for Mom

**Situations where A Place for Mom helps:**

**1. Home care no longer sufficient:**
- Client needs 24/7 care but family can't afford ($15,000-$20,000/month for live-in care)
- **Assisted living cheaper** ($5,000-$8,000/month for 24/7 facility care)

**2. Safety concerns:**
- Wandering dementia (home no longer safe even with caregivers)
- Medical needs exceed home care capabilities
- Family caregiver burnout (can't continue)

**3. Socialization needs:**
- Elder isolated at home (even with caregiver visits)
- **Assisted living provides:** Activities, meals, social interaction

**4. Family requests guidance:**
- Family asks: "How do we find assisted living?"
- **You:** "I work with A Place for Mom - they're experts in finding the right community. Let me connect you."

**How to refer:**
- Call A Place for Mom: 1-800-355-8221
- Ask for local advisor serving your client's area
- **Warm handoff:** "I have a client family - can I give them your contact info?" OR "Can I email you client details for you to reach out?"
- Follow up: Check with family later ("Did A Place for Mom advisor help you?")

## Best Practices for A Place for Mom Partnership

**1. Build personal relationships with local advisors:**
- **Don't just be in database** - advisors refer to agencies they KNOW
- Meet for coffee, visit each other's offices, quarterly check-ins
- **Personal connection = more referrals**

**2. Refer TO A Place for Mom proactively:**
- Don't wait for family to ask about facilities
- **When home care isn't working,** recommend A Place for Mom: "Let me connect you with senior living advisor - they'll help explore options"
- **Advisors remember who sends them business**

**3. Provide feedback on referrals:**
- When advisor refers family to you, **update advisor:** "Thank you for referring the Smith family - they started care, everything going well"
- **OR:** "The Smith family chose another agency - here's why..." (advisors appreciate honest feedback)
- Communication strengthens partnership

**4. Position as home care alternative (not competitor):**
- A Place for Mom focuses on facilities (their revenue source)
- You focus on home care (your revenue source)
- **Complementary, not competitive:** "We serve families who want to stay home; you serve families who need facilities - together we help all families find the right solution"

**5. Specialize to stand out:**
- A Place for Mom database has 100+ Massachusetts agencies
- **Differentiate:** "We specialize in dementia care" or "We serve Brazilian community (Portuguese speakers)" or "We excel at post-surgical care"
- Advisors remember specialists (vs. generalists)

**6. Attend A Place for Mom events:**
- A Place for Mom hosts professional networking events (senior care community)
- **Attend:** Build visibility, meet advisors, network with assisted living communities
- Shows commitment to partnership

**7. Be patient:**
- Relationships take time (6-12 months to see consistent referrals)
- **Don't expect 30 referrals/month immediately**
- Long-term investment

## A Place for Mom vs. Other Lead Sources

**A Place for Mom vs. Caring.com:**
- **Caring.com:** Pay-per-lead ($50-$200/lead), high volume, transactional
- **A Place for Mom:** FREE partnership, moderate volume, relationship-based
- **A Place for Mom advantage:** FREE (no lead costs)
- **Caring.com advantage:** Higher volume (if you pay)

**A Place for Mom vs. COAs/ASAPs:**
- **COAs/ASAPs:** Community-based, FREE, high trust, high conversion (70-80%)
- **A Place for Mom:** National platform, FREE, moderate trust, moderate conversion (60-75%)
- **COAs/ASAPs advantage:** Warmer local referrals, higher conversion
- **A Place for Mom advantage:** Broader reach (national marketing), tech platform

**A Place for Mom vs. Google Business Profile:**
- **Google:** FREE, high volume (if optimized), self-service (families find you)
- **A Place for Mom:** FREE, moderate volume, advisor-assisted (warmer referrals)
- **Google advantage:** Higher volume potential
- **A Place for Mom advantage:** Pre-screened leads (advisor filters unqualified families)

**Strategic mix:**
- **Best agencies use ALL free sources:** A Place for Mom + Google + COAs + ASAPs + hospitals
- **A Place for Mom = piece of puzzle** (not sole source)

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "Not getting referrals from A Place for Mom"**
**Solution:** (1) Have you MET local advisors personally? (Database listing ≠ referrals), (2) Have you referred families TO A Place for Mom? (Reciprocity critical), (3) Follow up with Community Relations (ask: "How can we strengthen partnership?"), (4) Be patient (takes 6-12 months to build).

**Challenge 2: "Advisors refer to competitors instead"**
**Solution:** (1) Build deeper relationship (meet advisor quarterly, not just once), (2) Refer MORE families to advisor (top of mind), (3) Differentiate (specialize in dementia/languages/24-7), (4) Ask advisor directly: "What would make our agency a better fit for your referrals?"

**Challenge 3: "Families referred by A Place for Mom choose facility anyway"**
**Solution:** NORMAL. Many families exploring home care ultimately choose facility (that's okay - advisor's job is to present ALL options). Your goal: Be the home care option advisors present. Some families WILL choose home care. Conversion rate 60-75% is good.

**Challenge 4: "Don't know when to refer clients TO A Place for Mom"**
**Solution:** Refer when: (1) Client needs 24/7 care (facility cheaper than 24/7 home care), (2) Dementia progressing (wandering, aggression), (3) Medical needs exceed home care, (4) Family asks about facilities. Don't wait too long - proactive referrals build advisor relationships.

**Challenge 5: "A Place for Mom advisors don't respond"**
**Solution:** (1) Call main number (1-800-355-8221), ask to be transferred to advisor, (2) Email advisor (if you have contact), (3) Persistent but patient (advisors busy helping families), (4) Provide complete client info upfront (care needs, budget, location - makes advisor's job easier).
