## Overview

Pharmacies - both **community/retail pharmacies** (CVS, Walgreens, independent pharmacies) and **long-term care pharmacies** (PharMerica, Omnicare) - are **underutilized but valuable referral partners** for home care agencies. Pharmacists interact with aging patients FREQUENTLY (monthly medication refills), observe medication management challenges, and are well-positioned to identify seniors needing home care support.

**Why pharmacies refer:**
- **Monthly patient interactions:** Seniors visit/call pharmacies 1-3 times/month (medication refills, consultations)
- **Medication management concerns:** Pharmacists observe medication non-adherence, confusion, missed refills
- **Caregiver conversations:** Family members picking up medications mention struggles: "Mom forgets her pills"
- **Trusted advisor role:** Patients ask pharmacists for advice (not just medications) - "Do you know anyone who can help my mother at home?"
- **Care coordination:** Pharmacists want patients to take medications correctly = better health outcomes

**Referral scenarios:**
- "This patient is confused about medications - needs daily reminders"
- "Family member mentioned parent living alone, struggling to manage multiple prescriptions"
- "Patient keeps missing refills - may need home care support"

## Types of Pharmacies & Referral Opportunities

### **1. Community/Retail Pharmacies (Moderate Volume, Broad Reach)**

**What they are:**
- **Retail pharmacies:** CVS, Walgreens, Rite Aid
- **Independent community pharmacies:** Locally-owned pharmacies serving neighborhoods
- **Grocery/big box pharmacies:** Stop & Shop, Target, Walmart pharmacies

**Patient population:**
- **Seniors living at home:** Picking up medications independently or with family help
- **High-frequency contact:** Monthly visits/calls for refills

**Referral dynamics:**
- **Pharmacist observes:** Patient declining (confusion, multiple missed refills, medication errors)
- **Family member mentions:** "My mother is struggling at home - do you know anyone who can help?"
- **Pharmacist to family:** "Have you considered home care? Here are agencies that can help with medication management..."

**Expected referrals per pharmacy:**
- **Retail chain (CVS, Walgreens):** 1-3 referrals/month (less personalized)
- **Independent community pharmacy:** 3-10 referrals/month (more personal relationships, pharmacist knows patients)

### **2. Long-Term Care (LTC) Pharmacies (High Volume, Institutional)**

**What they are:**
- **Specialized pharmacies:** PharMerica, Omnicare, BrightSpring (serve assisted living, nursing homes, group homes)
- **Services:** Medication packaging (bubble packs, blister packs), delivery to facilities, medication reviews

**Patient population:**
- **Assisted living residents:** Seniors living in assisted living communities
- **Group home residents:** Adults with disabilities
- **Home care clients:** Some LTC pharmacies serve home-based clients (medication delivery to homes)

**Referral dynamics:**
- **Assisted living discharge:** Resident moving home from assisted living → needs home care support
- **Medication complexity:** Patient at home needs medication packaging + daily support → LTC pharmacy partners with home care agency
- **Medication reviews:** LTC pharmacist conducts medication review, identifies patient needs home care

**Expected referrals per LTC pharmacy:**
- **LTC pharmacy partnership:** 5-15 referrals/month (if formalized partnership)

### **3. Hospital/Integrated Health System Pharmacies (Indirect Referrals)**

**What they are:**
- **Hospital pharmacies:** Mass General Brigham, Beth Israel Lahey, Tufts pharmacies
- **Integrated health system outpatient pharmacies:** On-site pharmacies at clinics, medical centers

**Patient population:**
- **Hospital discharge patients:** Recently hospitalized
- **Chronic disease patients:** Managing complex medication regimens

**Referral dynamics:**
- **Discharge pharmacist:** Counsels patient on new medications after hospital discharge
- **Identifies barriers:** "Do you have someone at home to help with medications?"
- **Refers to home care:** Connects patient/family with agencies

**Expected referrals:**
- **Indirect (via hospital discharge planning):** 2-8 referrals/month

## Why Pharmacists Refer to Home Care

**The medication management moment:**

**Scenario 1: Pharmacist observes confusion**
- Elderly patient (80) comes to pharmacy for refill
- **Pharmacist asks:** "How are your medications working?"
- **Patient confused:** "I don't know which pill to take when..."
- **Pharmacist to family member (picking up medications):** "Your mother seems confused about her medications. Have you considered home care? Caregivers can help with medication reminders."
- **Family:** "I didn't know that was an option - who do I call?"
- **Pharmacist:** Provides home care agency names

**Scenario 2: Multiple missed refills**
- Pharmacist reviews patient profile: 5 medications, hasn't refilled in 6+ weeks
- **Pharmacist calls patient:** "You haven't picked up your heart medication - is everything okay?"
- **Patient:** "I keep forgetting to come to the pharmacy..."
- **Pharmacist:** "Would it help to have someone at home reminding you? I can give you names of home care agencies..."

**Scenario 3: Family member mentions struggles**
- Adult daughter picking up mother's medications
- **Daughter to pharmacist:** "I'm worried - Mom forgets to take her pills, I can't be there every day"
- **Pharmacist:** "Have you considered professional caregivers? They can provide daily medication reminders. Here are agencies..."

**Scenario 4: New complex medication regimen**
- Patient discharged from hospital with 8 new medications (complex schedule)
- **Pharmacist counsels:** "This is a lot to manage - do you live alone?"
- **Patient:** "Yes, and I'm overwhelmed..."
- **Pharmacist:** "Let me give you names of home care agencies - they help people manage medications at home"

## How to Build Pharmacy Partnerships

### **Step 1: Identify Target Pharmacies**

**Research:**
1. **Community pharmacies in your service area:**
   - Google Maps: "Pharmacies near me"
   - Focus on: Independent community pharmacies (personalized service, more referral-oriented than chains)
   - CVS, Walgreens, Stop & Shop pharmacies (high volume, less personalized but worth approaching)

2. **Long-term care pharmacies:**
   - PharMerica, Omnicare, BrightSpring (largest LTC pharmacies)
   - Google: "Long-term care pharmacy Massachusetts"

3. **Hospital/health system pharmacies:**
   - Mass General Brigham retail pharmacies
   - Beth Israel Lahey outpatient pharmacies

**Target:** 10-15 pharmacies to start (mix of community + LTC)

### **Step 2: Reach Out to Pharmacies**

**Outreach approach:**

**Option A: In-person visit (BEST for community pharmacies)**
- **Visit pharmacy** (Tuesday-Thursday afternoons best - avoid Monday rush, lunchtime, weekends)
- **Ask for pharmacist:** "Hi, is the pharmacist available for a brief conversation?"
- **Brief introduction:** "I'm [Name], owner of [Agency], a local home care agency. We help seniors manage medications at home. Many of your customers likely need medication reminders, daily support. I'd love to leave you information as a resource."
- **Leave materials:** Brochures, business cards (50+)
- **Request:** "When your customers mention needing help at home, please think of us"

**Option B: Phone call**
- Call pharmacy (afternoons best)
- Ask for pharmacist-in-charge
- Brief introduction: "We're a home care agency - many of your senior customers need medication support at home. May I stop by to introduce our services?"
- Request in-person meeting

**Option C: Email (for LTC pharmacies)**
- Long-term care pharmacies have business development teams
- Email: "We're interested in partnering with your pharmacy - our home care clients need medication packaging, delivery, reviews"

### **Step 3: Meet with Pharmacy Staff**

**Meeting structure (10-15 minutes at pharmacy):**

**A. Understand their patient needs:**
- "What challenges do your senior customers face with medications?"
- **LISTEN:** Let pharmacist describe common issues (confusion, non-adherence, family concerns)

**B. Position as solution:**
- "We help with exactly those issues - our caregivers provide medication reminders, organize pill boxes, communicate with pharmacists and physicians"
- "When your customers are struggling, we can support them at home"

**C. Mutual benefit:**
- "When our clients need medication packaging, delivery, reviews - we'll refer to you"
- "When your customers need home care - we'd be honored if you'd refer to us"
- **Win-win partnership**

**D. Leave resources:**
- **Business cards:** 50-100 (pharmacist + pharmacy techs need cards)
- **Brochures:** 25-50
- **Medication management flyer:** "How Home Care Helps with Medications" (one-pager for pharmacist to give customers)

### **Step 4: Demonstrate Value**

**How to stand out:**

**A. Medication communication:**
- **When YOUR clients use that pharmacy,** tell pharmacist: "Mrs. Smith is our home care client - we're helping with her medications. Please call us if you notice any concerns."
- **Proactive communication:** If caregiver observes medication issues, call pharmacist: "Mrs. Smith's blood pressure medication seems ineffective - can you review?"
- **Pharmacists LOVE agencies that communicate** (improves medication management)

**B. Cross-refer:**
- **When YOUR clients need pharmacy services,** refer to partner pharmacy
- "We work with [Pharmacy Name] - they deliver medications, offer free consultations..."
- **Pharmacists remember who sends them business**

**C. Educational collaboration:**
- Offer to co-present at senior center: "Medication Safety at Home" (pharmacist presents medication tips, you present how caregivers help)
- **Builds visibility, positions as partners**

**D. Medication management expertise:**
- **Train YOUR caregivers:** Medication reminders, pill box organization, observing side effects, communicating with pharmacists
- **Pharmacists confident referring to agencies** with trained caregivers

### **Step 5: Stay Connected**

**Ongoing:**
- **Quarterly pharmacy visits:** Stop by, refresh materials, chat with pharmacist
- **Share success stories:** "The Smith family you referred is doing great - medication adherence improved!"
- **Holiday cards:** Thanksgiving/winter holidays to pharmacy staff
- **Thank referrals:** When pharmacy refers, send thank-you note

## LTC Pharmacy Partnership Model (Advanced)

**For agencies serving assisted living/group home residents:**

**Formalized partnership structure:**

**1. Service agreement:**
- Home care agency + LTC pharmacy **formal partnership**: Agency provides care, pharmacy provides medications
- **Benefit for LTC pharmacy:** Consistent medication orders from home care agency's clients
- **Benefit for agency:** Reliable medication delivery, packaging, review for clients

**2. Assisted living discharge referrals:**
- **LTC pharmacy serves assisted living communities:** When resident moves HOME from assisted living, LTC pharmacy refers to home care partner
- **Scenario:** Resident moving home, needs medication management + daily care → LTC pharmacy: "Here's our partner home care agency..."

**3. Medication review collaboration:**
- **LTC pharmacist conducts medication review** for agency client (identifies issues, simplifies regimen)
- **Agency caregiver implements recommendations** (medication reminders, administration)

**4. Medication packaging:**
- **LTC pharmacy provides bubble packs/blister packs** for agency clients (pre-sorted medications by date/time)
- **Caregiver administers** from clearly labeled packages
- **Reduces medication errors** (improved outcomes)

## Expected Referral Volume from Pharmacy Partnerships

**Volume depends on:**
1. **Pharmacy type:** Independent community pharmacy (high) vs. chain retail (low)
2. **Relationship depth:** Know pharmacist personally? Or just business cards at counter?
3. **Senior clientele percentage:** Pharmacy serving retirement community (high) vs. general population (moderate)
4. **Cross-referrals:** Do you refer clients TO pharmacy? (Reciprocity matters)
5. **Communication:** Do you update pharmacist on client medication status?

**Expected referrals per pharmacy:**
- **Independent community pharmacy (strong relationship):** 3-10 referrals/month
- **Chain retail pharmacy (passive relationship):** 1-3 referrals/month
- **LTC pharmacy (formalized partnership):** 5-15 referrals/month

**Total volume (10 pharmacy partnerships):**
- **Building relationships:** 10-30 referrals/month
- **Established partnerships:** 30-80 referrals/month

**Conversion rate:** 60-70% (warm pharmacy referrals, but some families decline care)

## Best Practices for Pharmacy Partnerships

**1. Target independent community pharmacies:**
- **Independent pharmacies >> chain retail:** Personalized service, pharmacist knows customers by name, more willing to refer
- **Chain pharmacies:** Worth approaching but lower referral volume (pharmacist less personal relationships)

**2. Medication communication is KEY:**
- **Pharmacists value agencies that communicate:** When YOUR clients use that pharmacy, let pharmacist know
- **Update pharmacist on medication concerns:** "Mrs. Smith's blood pressure still high - can you review?"
- **Pharmacists refer to agencies they TRUST** (communication builds trust)

**3. Cross-refer to partner pharmacies:**
- **When YOUR clients need pharmacy services,** refer to partner pharmacy
- "We work with [Pharmacy Name] - they deliver medications, offer free consultations"
- **Reciprocity critical** (pharmacists remember who sends business)

**4. Train caregivers on medication management:**
- **Caregivers must be competent:** Medication reminders, pill box organization, recognizing side effects
- **Pharmacists confident referring** to agencies with trained caregivers
- **Medication errors = no more referrals** (quality critical)

**5. Focus on medication management positioning:**
- **Marketing to pharmacies:** "We specialize in medication management support at home"
- **NOT general home care pitch:** Pharmacies refer for MEDICATION-SPECIFIC needs
- **Emphasize:** Daily medication reminders, pill box organization, communication with pharmacists/physicians

**6. Visit pharmacies regularly:**
- **Quarterly visits:** Stop by, chat with pharmacist, refresh materials
- **Don't just leave materials and disappear:** Relationships take ongoing cultivation

**7. LTC pharmacy partnerships for scale:**
- **Formalized LTC pharmacy partnerships:** Higher volume, more structured referrals
- **Target:** PharMerica, Omnicare, BrightSpring (largest LTC pharmacies in MA)
- **Propose partnership:** "We serve home-based clients needing medication packaging, delivery - can we partner?"

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "Pharmacists too busy to meet"**
**Solution:** (1) Visit during slow times (Tuesday-Thursday afternoons, avoid Monday/lunchtime/weekends), (2) Keep introduction BRIEF (5-10 minutes max), (3) In-person better than phone (show face, leave materials), (4) Offer value upfront ("We'll help your customers with medications" vs. "I want referrals").

**Challenge 2: "Pharmacy refers to competitors"**
**Solution:** Pharmacies typically give 2-3 agency names (not exclusive). Differentiate: (1) Visit pharmacy MORE often than competitors (top-of-mind), (2) Communicate about shared clients (pharmacists remember agencies that coordinate), (3) Cross-refer clients TO pharmacy (reciprocity), (4) Train caregivers well (quality = more referrals).

**Challenge 3: "Not getting referrals despite relationship"**
**Solution:** (1) Are you COMMUNICATING about shared clients? (Pharmacists refer to agencies they TRUST = communication builds trust), (2) Do you CROSS-REFER to pharmacy? (Reciprocity critical), (3) Visit more frequently (quarterly minimum), (4) Ask pharmacist directly: "When customers mention needing help at home, please think of us - here are more business cards."

**Challenge 4: "Don't know which pharmacies to target"**
**Solution:** (1) Target pharmacies YOUR CLIENTS already use (ask clients: "Which pharmacy do you use?"), (2) Target pharmacies near assisted living communities (high senior clientele), (3) Google "independent pharmacy [your town]" (independent > chain), (4) Visit pharmacies in senior-dense neighborhoods.

**Challenge 5: "Medication management concerns - what can caregivers legally do?"**
**Solution:** (1) Caregivers can REMIND (not administer) medications for cognitively intact clients, (2) Caregivers can SET UP pill boxes (but client takes own medications), (3) For clients needing ADMINISTRATION (caregiver physically gives medications), agency may need skilled nursing license OR medication administration program (check MA DPH regulations), (4) Consult attorney on medication scope of practice.
