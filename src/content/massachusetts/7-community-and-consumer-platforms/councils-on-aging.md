## Overview

Every city and town in Massachusetts has a **Council on Aging (COA)**—350+ municipal agencies providing senior services, programming, and resource connections. COAs operate senior centers, offer educational programs, coordinate transportation, provide wellness activities, and connect seniors with supportive services including home care.

**Key insight:** COAs are the **most trusted local resource** for seniors and families. A strong relationship with your local COAs generates consistent, high-quality referrals at ZERO cost.

**Find COAs:** mass.gov/info-details/find-your-local-council-on-aging
**MA COA Association:** mcoaonline.org

## Who Sends Referrals

- **COA directors and staff** who know local seniors' needs
- **Senior center program coordinators** who interact daily with active seniors
- **Outreach workers** who visit homebound seniors
- **Nutrition program staff** (meals on wheels coordinators)
- **SHINE counselors** (health insurance assistance)

## Who Can Receive Referrals

All aging services providers:
- Home care agencies
- Home health agencies
- Adult day programs
- Transportation services
- Home modification contractors
- Senior housing

## How It Works

1. **Identify COAs in your service area** (typically 5-15 towns/cities)
2. **Visit each COA** and introduce your agency to director/staff
3. **Provide marketing materials:** Business cards, brochures, service menus
4. **Offer to present** at senior center programs (lunch & learns, health fairs)
5. **Stay engaged:** Visit quarterly, sponsor events, volunteer

**Referral flow:**
- Senior/family visits COA seeking help with aging at home
- COA staff provides resource list including your agency
- Family contacts you directly

**Volume:** Varies by COA size; larger COAs (Boston, Worcester, Cambridge) can refer 5-15 families/month; smaller towns 1-3/month

## How to Get Listed / Enrolled

**Step 1:** Find local COAs
- Visit mass.gov site or search "[City Name] Council on Aging"

**Step 2:** Schedule meetings
- Call COA: "I'd like to introduce our home care agency as a community resource"
- Ask for 15-minute meeting with director or outreach coordinator

**Step 3:** Prepare materials
- One-page agency overview
- Business cards (lots of them!)
- Brochure or service menu
- Pricing sheet (if comfortable sharing)

**Step 4:** Present value proposition
- "We provide companion care and personal care to help seniors age in place"
- "We serve [list towns/cities]"
- "We offer flexible scheduling, multilingual caregivers, affordable rates"
- "How can we support YOUR seniors?"

**Step 5:** Leave materials
- Ask to be included in resource binder/database
- Provide stack of business cards for distribution

**Step 6:** Follow up
- Visit quarterly with donuts/coffee
- Offer to present at senior programs
- Sponsor COA events or newsletters

## Cost and Lead Quality

**Cost:** **100% FREE**—no fees, only time investment

**Lead Quality:** **Very high**
- **Trusted referrals:** Families trust COA recommendations
- **Local connections:** COAs know community well
- **Active seniors:** COA users tend to be engaged, social, health-conscious
- **Family caregivers:** Many COA visitors are adult children seeking help for parents
- **Conversion:** 50-70% because of trust factor

## Best Practices

1. **Build personal relationships:** Learn staff names, remember details
2. **Visit in person:** Face time beats email/phone
3. **Offer value beyond referrals:** Free educational workshops, health screenings, caregiver support groups
4. **Sponsor events:** COA fundraisers, senior picnics, holiday parties
5. **Volunteer:** Help with COA programs to build goodwill
6. **Stay top-of-mind:** Quarterly visits, holiday cards, occasional treats for staff
7. **Track referrals by COA:** Use Mastering Home Care to measure which COAs generate most business
8. **Participate in COA Health Fairs:** Set up booth, offer free BP checks or care consultations
9. **Provide transportation:** Offer to transport seniors to/from senior center (builds relationships)
10. **Celebrate success stories:** Share (with permission) how your services helped local seniors

## How This Fits Mastering Home Care

**Ingest:** Phone calls from families who received your info from COA

**Automation:**
- **Lead source tracking:** Tag as "COA - [Town Name]"
- **COA relationship management:** Track visits, events, referral volume by COA
- **Thank-you automation:** Send automated thank-you emails to COAs when referrals convert
- **ROI by COA:** Measure which COAs generate most referrals → prioritize relationship-building
