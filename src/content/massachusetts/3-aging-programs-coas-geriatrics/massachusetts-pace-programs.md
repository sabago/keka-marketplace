## Overview

PACE (Program of All-Inclusive Care for the Elderly) is an all-inclusive care model serving **frail elders** who are nursing-home eligible but prefer to remain at home. Massachusetts has **8 PACE organizations** serving 271 of 351 cities/towns, making PACE an important home care partnership opportunity.

**What PACE Provides:**
- Medical care (primary care, specialty care, hospital, ER)
- Adult day health center attendance
- Home care services (nursing, personal care, homemaker)
- Medications, medical equipment, transportation
- Social services, care coordination

**All services covered by PACE** (no copays for participants).

---

## Why PACE Matters for Home Care Agencies

**Partnership model:**
- PACE organizations **contract with community home care agencies** to provide home services
- PACE participants need home care (mornings/evenings/weekends when not at PACE center)
- **Stable, long-term clients:** PACE participants stay enrolled for years
- **Guaranteed payment:** PACE organization pays agencies (not fee-for-service billing)

**Key benefits:**
- Long-term client relationships (average PACE enrollment 3-5 years)
- Predictable monthly revenue (PACE contracts = recurring business)
- Integrated care team (work alongside PACE nurses, social workers, therapists)
- Quality focus (PACE tracks outcomes, values agencies with good performance)

## How PACE Partnership Works

### **Step 1: PACE Assesses Participant Needs**

- PACE interdisciplinary team (physician, nurse, social worker, PT/OT) assesses participant
- Team determines home care needs: ADL assistance, medication reminders, meal prep, etc.
- Team develops care plan specifying hours/days of home care

### **Step 2: PACE Contracts with Home Care Agency**

- PACE reaches out to contracted agencies: "We have participant needing 15 hours/week personal care"
- Agency accepts assignment, completes assessment
- Agency provides services per PACE care plan

### **Step 3: Agency Provides Services**

- Caregiver visits participant at home (mornings, evenings, weekends)
- Caregiver provides personal care, homemaker services per care plan
- Caregiver reports to PACE team (daily/weekly communication)

### **Step 4: PACE Pays Agency**

- Agency invoices PACE organization monthly
- PACE pays agency per contract rates (not fee-for-service)
- Payment typically 30-45 days

## How to Become PACE Provider

### **Step 1: Identify PACE Organization in Your Service Area**

**Match service area:**
- Serve Boston/Everett → Elder Service Plan
- Serve Worcester → Fallon PACE
- Serve MetroWest (Framingham/Milford) → Tri-Valley PACE
- Serve Springfield → Weldon Center PACE
- Serve Lowell → CareWell PACE
- Serve Brighton/Newton → Elder PACE
- Serve Brockton → PrimeLife PACE
- Serve Salem/Lynn → Senior Life PACE

### **Step 2: Contact PACE Provider Relations**

**Process:**
1. Call PACE main number (listed above)
2. Ask for "provider relations" or "community partnerships"
3. Express interest in providing home care services for PACE participants
4. Request provider application packet

### **Step 3: Complete Credentialing**

**Typical requirements:**
- Home care agency license (if required by MA)
- Liability insurance ($1-2M)
- Workers' compensation insurance
- Background check policies for all caregivers
- Caregiver training protocols
- Emergency/on-call procedures
- References from other clients/partners
- Site visit by PACE team (optional)

**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks typical

### **Step 4: Sign Provider Agreement**

**Contract terms:**
- Payment rates (negotiated, typically MassHealth-level)
- Service requirements (response time, documentation, communication)
- Quality standards (satisfaction surveys, care plan adherence)
- Term (1-2 years, renewable)

### **Step 5: Receive Participant Referrals**

**Ongoing:**
- PACE care coordinator calls when participant needs home care
- You accept assignment, complete assessment, start care
- Provide ongoing services per care plan
- Communicate regularly with PACE team

## Cost and ROI

**Cost:** 100% FREE to apply/credential

**ROI:** High
- **Lead quality:** Excellent (PACE team has professionally assessed needs)
- **Conversion rate:** 90%+ (PACE assigns you participant, not competitive bidding)
- **Client duration:** Very long-term (PACE participants stay 3-5+ years average)
- **Payment reliability:** Excellent (PACE organizations well-funded by CMS + MassHealth)
- **Volume:** Moderate (each PACE has 100-800 participants, not all need home care)

**Expected referral volume:**
- Small PACE (100-150 participants): 5-15 home care assignments/year
- Medium PACE (200-400 participants): 15-30 assignments/year
- Large PACE (500-800 participants): 30-60 assignments/year

## Best Practices

**1. Partner with PACE closest to your service area first:**
- Don't try to partner with all 8 PACEs initially
- Focus on 1-2 PACEs where you have strong geographic coverage

**2. Emphasize communication:**
- PACE teams value agencies that communicate proactively
- Report changes in participant status immediately
- Attend care team meetings (if invited)

**3. Be flexible:**
- PACE participants are frail, care needs change frequently
- Be prepared to adjust hours, services on short notice

**4. Track quality metrics:**
- PACE monitors hospitalization rates, satisfaction, outcomes
- Agencies with poor performance may lose contracts

**5. Build relationships with PACE care managers:**
- Care managers decide which agency gets which participant
- Visit PACE center, meet team, introduce your agency
