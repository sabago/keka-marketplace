## Overview

The Executive Office of Aging & Independence (EOEA/AGE, formerly Executive Office of Elder Affairs) is the **state agency overseeing all aging services in Massachusetts**. EOEA contracts with and funds ASAPs, manages home care programs, administers Frail Elder Waiver, supports Councils on Aging, and sets policy for aging services across the state.

For home care agencies, EOEA represents the **structural framework** within which most state-funded aging services operate. Understanding EOEA programs and priorities helps agencies navigate the MA aging services ecosystem and identify partnership opportunities.

**Key EOEA Programs:**
- Aging Services Access Points (ASAPs) - 24 regional agencies
- Home Care Program - state-funded supportive services
- Frail Elder Waiver - MassHealth HCBS program
- Councils on Aging Support - 350+ municipal agencies
- Protective Services - elder abuse prevention
- Health Insurance Counseling (SHINE)
- Elder Mental Health Outreach Teams
- Money Management Program

## Who Sends Referrals

EOEA doesn't directly refer clients to home care agencies. Instead, it oversees entities that do:
- ASAPs (contracted by EOEA) make direct referrals
- Councils on Aging (supported by EOEA) connect seniors to services
- Protective Services may coordinate home care for at-risk seniors

**To receive EOEA-funded referrals:** Build relationships with ASAPs and COAs.

## Who Can Receive Referrals

Through EOEA-funded programs:
- Homemaker service providers (contracted with ASAPs)
- Private-pay home care agencies (via ASAP referrals)
- Adult day health programs
- Home-delivered meals providers
- Transportation services

## How It Works

**Indirect pathway:**
1. EOEA funds ASAPs and Councils on Aging
2. ASAPs and COAs connect seniors with home care agencies
3. Agencies partner with ASAPs/COAs to receive referrals

**Direct opportunities:** Occasionally EOEA issues RFPs for pilot programs or statewide initiatives—agencies can bid on these contracts.

## How to Get Listed / Enrolled

**Strategy 1: Partner with ASAPs**
- Build relationships with 24-27 ASAPs contracted by EOEA
- Become preferred provider for Frail Elder Waiver and Home Care Program services

**Strategy 2: Partner with Councils on Aging**
- Connect with 350+ municipal COAs supported by EOEA
- Get listed in COA resource directories

**Strategy 3: Monitor EOEA RFPs**
- Visit mass.gov/eoea regularly for grant opportunities and RFPs
- Submit bids for statewide programs or pilot initiatives

**Strategy 4: Participate in EOEA Initiatives**
- Attend EOEA conferences and stakeholder meetings
- Join workgroups on aging services policy
- Provide input on strategic planning

## Cost and Lead Quality

**Cost:** FREE partnerships with ASAPs/COAs; competitive bidding for contracts

**Lead Quality:** High—EOEA-funded programs serve vulnerable, high-need seniors

## Pros and Cons

**Pros:**
- EOEA oversees $1B+ aging services budget—significant opportunity
- Statewide reach through ASAP and COA networks
- Mission-aligned programs serving vulnerable populations
- Professional credibility through state partnerships

**Cons:**
- Indirect relationship (must work through ASAPs/COAs)
- Bureaucratic processes for contracts
- Lower reimbursement rates for state-funded services

## How This Fits Mastering Home Care

EOEA-sourced referrals flow through ASAPs/COAs using standard channels:
- **Phone/fax/email from ASAPs** → see Massachusetts ASAPs Overview for integration
- **Walk-in referrals from COAs** → see Councils on Aging for details

**Tracking:** Tag referrals with "EOEA Program" funding source to analyze this pathway's ROI.

## Best Practices

1. **Understand EOEA priorities:** Focus on dementia care, caregiver support, fall prevention, elder abuse prevention
2. **Engage with policy:** Participate in EOEA stakeholder meetings to shape aging services policy
3. **Monitor funding opportunities:** Check mass.gov/eoea for RFPs and grants
4. **Build ASAP/COA relationships:** These are EOEA's implementation arms
5. **Align with state initiatives:** Position agency as partner in EOEA strategic goals (aging-in-place, caregiver support)
