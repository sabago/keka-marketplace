## Key Programs for Home Care Referrals

Springwell operates **multiple programs** that generate home care referrals for enrolled agencies. Understanding each program helps agencies position services appropriately.

### **1. MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver (Primary Referral Source)**

**What it is:**
- MassHealth-funded home care for seniors who meet **nursing home level of care** criteria but prefer to remain at home
- Covers personal care, homemaking, case management, adult day health, respite care

**Eligibility:**
- Age 60+
- MassHealth eligible (income limits: $11,495 single, $15,544 couple annually for MassHealth Standard)
- Meet nursing facility level of care (require assistance with 2+ ADLs)
- Choose to remain at home rather than enter nursing facility

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Highest-volume referral source** from Springwell
- **Long-term care relationships:** Clients typically need ongoing services for months/years
- **Stable funding:** MassHealth pays agencies directly (no collection issues)

**Expected volume:** 20-40 referrals/month from Frail Elder Waiver program

### **2. State-Funded Home Care Program**

**What it is:**
- State-funded personal care, homemaking, and case management for seniors not MassHealth-eligible
- "Middle-income" seniors who don't qualify for MassHealth but can't afford full private pay

**Eligibility:**
- Age 60+
- Income above MassHealth limits but below ~$75,000 (varies)
- Need assistance with ADLs or IADLs
- Assessed by Springwell case manager

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Second-largest referral source** from Springwell
- Clients transitioning from state-funded to private pay as needs increase
- Bridge between MassHealth and full private pay

**Expected volume:** 10-20 referrals/month

### **3. Information & Referral (I&R) - Highest Volume**

**What it is:**
- **Front door to Springwell:** Families call 617-926-4100 or 508-573-7200 asking for resources
- **80-150 inquiries/day:** "My mother needs help at home - what do I do?"
- **I&R specialists:** Trained staff assess needs, provide resource referrals

**Home care referral process:**
- **Family calls:** "I need home care for my father in Newton"
- **I&R specialist asks:** Care needs, budget (private pay vs. MassHealth), language needs
- **I&R provides:** 2-3 home care agency names serving Newton, matching family's needs
- **Warm referral:** "Call these agencies for free assessments"

**Volume:** 20-50 home care referrals/day (600-1,500/month from I&R alone)

**How to get I&R referrals:**
- **Be on Springwell's provider list:** Complete PCMS enrollment + RFP application
- **Stay updated:** Quarterly visits to Waltham/Marlborough offices
- **Respond rapidly:** When families call saying "Springwell gave me your name," respond same-day

### **4. Care Management - High Quality Referrals**

**What it is:**
- **Care managers (RNs, social workers):** Coordinate complex care for high-risk seniors
- **Who receives care management:** Seniors with multiple chronic conditions, recent hospitalizations, at risk of nursing home placement

**Home care referral process:**
- **Care manager assesses:** "This senior needs daily home care support"
- **Care manager to family:** "I recommend home care - here are agencies I work with..."
- **Direct coordination:** Care manager may call agency directly to arrange assessment

**Volume:** 15-30 home care referrals/month from care management

**Referral quality:** VERY HIGH (care manager-referred = complex cases, higher hours, longer duration)

### **5. Community Choices Program**

**What it is:**
- MassHealth waiver program for seniors transitioning from nursing facilities back to community
- Also serves at-risk seniors to prevent institutionalization

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Pre-discharge planning:** Springwell coordinates with nursing facilities, refers to home care agencies for post-discharge support
- **High acuity:** Clients often have complex medical needs, require experienced caregivers

**Expected volume:** 5-12 referrals/month

### **6. VD-HCBS Program (Veterans-Directed Home and Community Based Services)**

**What it is:**
- VA-funded program for veterans who want to self-direct their care
- Springwell serves as VD-HCBS program administrator for MetroWest region

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Veteran population:** Many Springwell communities have significant veteran populations (Newton, Framingham, Needham)
- **VA-funded:** Veterans receive budget to purchase home care services
- **Referral opportunity:** Springwell refers veterans to contracted agencies

**Expected volume:** 3-8 referrals/month

### **7. SHINE (Serving Health Insurance Needs of Everyone)**

**What it is:**
- Health insurance counseling: Helping seniors understand Medicare, MassHealth, Medigap, Medicare Advantage
- Common question: "Does Medicare cover home care?" (Answer: Only skilled Medicare home health, NOT personal care)

**Home care referral process:**
- **SHINE counselor educates:** "Medicare doesn't cover personal care, but you can pay privately or use long-term care insurance"
- **SHINE counselor refers:** "Contact these home care agencies for pricing..."

**Volume:** 8-15 home care referrals/month from SHINE

## Tips to Get More Referrals from Springwell

**Strategies to maximize referral volume and build strong Springwell partnership:**

### **1. Serve All 22 Springwell Towns (or as many as possible)**

**Geographic coverage = referral volume:**
- Agencies serving 18-22 towns get FAR more referrals than agencies serving only 5-8 towns
- **Strategy:** If you can't serve all 22 initially, prioritize high-volume clusters:
  - **Inner suburbs:** Brookline, Newton, Needham, Wellesley, Weston (40% of referrals)
  - **MetroWest core:** Framingham, Natick, Wayland (30% of referrals)
  - **MetroWest ring:** Ashland, Franklin, Walpole, Hopkinton (30% of referrals)

### **2. Respond to Referrals Within 2-4 Hours**

**Speed wins referrals:**
- Springwell care managers call **3-5 agencies per referral**
- **First agency to respond and accept client wins** ~70% of the time
- Provide care managers with **direct cell phone** for intake coordinator (not main office number)

### **3. Build Relationships with Springwell Care Managers**

**Personalize outreach:**
- **Visit Springwell offices:** Waltham (144 Watertown Street) and Marlborough (63 Union Street) - quarterly visits recommended
- **Meet care management team:** Request introduction after contract award
- **Provide case updates:** After starting care, call care manager to report: "Mrs. Lee is doing great - thank you for the referral"
- **Ask for feedback:** "What can we do better to support your clients?"

### **4. Premium Service Positioning**

**Springwell service area = affluent:**
- Newton, Brookline, Wellesley, Weston among Massachusetts' wealthiest communities
- **Expectations high:** White-glove service, excellent communication, professional caregivers
- **Investment:** Train caregivers on affluent client expectations (discretion, communication, reliability)
- **Quality over volume:** One excellent outcome = 5+ referrals from word-of-mouth

### **5. Accept Both MassHealth and Private Pay**

**Diversified payer mix = more referrals:**
- ~30-35% of Springwell referrals are MassHealth-funded (Frail Elder Waiver, state home care)
- ~65-70% are private pay or long-term care insurance
- **Agencies that accept MassHealth receive ALL referral types** (not just private pay)

### **6. Long-Term Care Insurance Expertise**

**Springwell service area has HIGHEST long-term care insurance penetration in Massachusetts:**
- 30-40% of affluent families have policies (vs. 15-20% statewide)
- **Train staff:** How to bill long-term care insurance, work with insurance coordinators
- **Communicate to families:** "We work with all major long-term care insurance companies"
- **Become expert:** Understand different policy types, benefit triggers, daily/monthly maximums

### **7. Multilingual Capabilities**

**While Springwell service area is primarily English-speaking, multilingual capabilities are valuable:**
- **Spanish:** Essential for Framingham, Walpole, Ashland (10-15% of referrals)
- **Portuguese:** Important for Framingham, Bellingham (5-8% of referrals)
- **Chinese (Mandarin/Cantonese):** Valuable for Newton, Brookline (5-8% of referrals)
- **Russian:** Helpful for Brookline, Newton (3-5% of referrals)

### **8. Communicate Proactively with Care Managers**

**Build trust through communication:**
- **Report concerns immediately:** If client's condition changes, family issues arise, caregiver challenges occur - call care manager right away
- **Ask for support:** Care managers have resources (adult day health, meal programs, assistive equipment) that can help your clients
- **Share successes:** When client thriving, tell care manager (reinforces quality of care)

### **9. Attend Springwell Events**

**Visibility builds referrals:**
- **Provider meetings:** Springwell hosts quarterly provider meetings (training, updates)
- **Health fairs:** Springwell sponsors community health fairs, senior expos
- **Caregiver conferences:** Springwell hosts caregiver support events
- **Attend/sponsor:** Builds visibility, demonstrates commitment

### **10. Specialize in High-Need Populations**

**Differentiate your agency:**
- **Post-stroke care** (Springwell sees many stroke survivors needing home care)
- **Dementia/Alzheimer's care** (growing need as population ages)
- **Complex medical needs** (diabetes management, COPD, CHF)
- **Live-in care** (high demand in affluent suburbs for 24/7 support)
