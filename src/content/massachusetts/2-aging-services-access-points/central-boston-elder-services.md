## Overview

Central Boston Elder Services (also known as One Central Boston) is one of **three Aging Services Access Points (ASAPs) serving Boston**, covering eight diverse inner-city neighborhoods including Allston, Brighton, Fenway, Mission Hill, North Dorchester, North Jamaica Plain, South End, and Roxbury. Founded to serve Boston's most densely populated urban core, Central Boston Elder Services provides critical home care referrals to agencies serving one of Massachusetts' most culturally and economically diverse areas.

**Central Boston Elder Services Quick Facts:**
- **Service Area:** 8 Boston neighborhoods (Allston, Brighton, Fenway, Mission Hill, North Dorchester, North JP, South End, Roxbury)
- **Population Served:** 40,000+ seniors annually
- **Main Office:** 2315 Washington Street, Roxbury, MA 02119
- **Phone:** 617-277-7416
- **Websites:** centralboston.org and onecentralboston.org (both active)
- **Founded:** 1970s as part of Boston's ASAP network

**For home care agencies:**
- **Expected referral volume:** 35-65 referrals/month
- **Part of Boston Consortium:** Coordinate with Ethos and Boston Senior Home Care (can apply to all 3)
- **Cultural diversity:** Spanish, Chinese, Vietnamese, Somali, Arabic essential
- **MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver:** Primary referral source
- **VD-HCBS Program:** Veterans-Directed Home and Community Based Services participant
- **Investment:** Free to enroll as home care provider (MassHealth enrollment required)

**Why Central Boston matters:** Serving Boston's densest urban core with extraordinary linguistic and cultural diversity (50+ languages spoken), Central Boston Elder Services represents a gateway to neighborhoods with HIGH home care need, significant student/young professional populations caring for aging parents, and strong institutional partnerships (Boston Medical Center, Brigham & Women's, Harvard Medical School area).

## Part of Boston Consortium

**Central Boston Elder Services is one of three ASAPs serving Boston:**

### **The Three Boston ASAPs:**

**1. Central Boston Elder Services**
- **Neighborhoods:** Allston, Brighton, Fenway, Mission Hill, North Dorchester, North Jamaica Plain, South End, Roxbury

**2. Ethos**
- **Neighborhoods:** Jamaica Plain, Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park, Mattapan
- **Plus:** Elder Protective Services (all Boston)

**3. Boston Senior Home Care (BSHC)**
- **Neighborhoods:** East Boston, Charlestown, North End, West End, Beacon Hill, Downtown, Chinatown, South Cove, South Boston, sections of Dorchester/Mattapan

### **Why This Matters for Home Care Agencies:**

**Apply to all three Boston ASAPs simultaneously:**
- Each ASAP operates independently but coordinates services
- Agencies serving multiple Boston neighborhoods should partner with ALL THREE
- **Strategy:** Apply to Central Boston (if serving Allston/Brighton/Roxbury/South End), Ethos (if serving JP/Roslindale/West Roxbury), and BSHC (if serving East Boston/Charlestown/South Boston)
- **Result:** 3x referral volume by covering entire Boston

**Coordinated programs:**
- All three Boston ASAPs participate in VD-HCBS (Veterans-Directed HCBS)
- Shared training, best practices
- Sometimes coordinate on complex cases spanning multiple neighborhoods

## Key Programs for Home Care Referrals

Central Boston Elder Services operates **multiple programs** that generate home care referrals for enrolled agencies.

### **1. MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver (Primary Referral Source)**

**What it is:**
- MassHealth-funded home care for seniors who meet **nursing home level of care** criteria but prefer to remain at home
- Covers personal care, homemaking, case management, adult day health, respite care

**Eligibility:**
- Age 60+
- MassHealth eligible (income limits: $11,495 single, $15,544 couple annually)
- Meet nursing facility level of care (require assistance with 2+ ADLs)
- Choose to remain at home rather than enter nursing facility

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Highest-volume referral source** from Central Boston
- **Long-term care relationships:** Clients typically need ongoing services for months/years
- **Stable funding:** MassHealth pays agencies directly
- **High MassHealth population:** 35-45% of Central Boston service area is MassHealth-eligible

**Expected volume:** 25-40 referrals/month from Frail Elder Waiver program

### **2. State-Funded Home Care Program**

**What it is:**
- State-funded personal care, homemaking, case management for seniors not MassHealth-eligible
- "Middle-income" seniors who don't qualify for MassHealth but can't afford full private pay

**Expected volume:** 10-18 referrals/month

### **3. VD-HCBS Program (Veterans-Directed Home and Community Based Services)**

**What it is:**
- VA-funded program for veterans who want to self-direct their care
- Central Boston participates in VD-HCBS program for Boston region (coordinates with Ethos and BSHC)

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Veteran population:** Roxbury, Brighton, Allston have veteran communities
- **VA-funded:** Veterans receive budget to purchase home care services

**Expected volume:** 5-12 referrals/month

### **4. Information & Referral (I&R) - Highest Volume**

**What it is:**
- **Front door to Central Boston:** Families call 617-277-7416 asking for resources
- **60-120 inquiries/day:** "My mother needs help at home - what do I do?"
- **I&R specialists:** Multilingual staff (Spanish, Chinese, Russian) assess needs, provide referrals

**Volume:** 15-35 home care referrals/day (450-1,050/month from I&R alone)

### **5. Care Management - High Quality Referrals**

**What it is:**
- **Care managers (RNs, social workers):** Coordinate complex care for high-risk seniors

**Volume:** 12-25 home care referrals/month

**Referral quality:** VERY HIGH (complex cases, higher hours, longer duration)

### **6. Hospital Discharge Planning Partnerships**

**What it is:**
- Central Boston Elder Services partners with **Boston Medical Center (BMC)** and **Brigham & Women's Hospital** for discharge planning
- Many Central Boston neighborhoods are primary service areas for these hospitals

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Pre-discharge referrals:** Care managers coordinate with hospital social workers
- **Post-acute care:** Clients transitioning from hospital to home need immediate home care start
- **High acuity:** Hospital discharge clients often have complex medical needs

**Expected volume:** 8-15 referrals/month from hospital partnerships

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Partner with Central Boston

**Becoming a Central Boston Elder Services home care provider:**

### **Step 1: Verify MassHealth Provider Enrollment**

**Requirement:** Central Boston requires home care agencies to be **enrolled MassHealth providers** before accepting referrals.

**If not enrolled:**
1. Visit mass.gov/masshealth-provider-enrollment
2. Complete Provider Enrollment Application (60-90 days)
3. Obtain MassHealth provider number

### **Step 2: Contact Central Boston Provider Relations**

**Call main office:**
- **Address:** 2315 Washington Street, Roxbury, MA 02119
- **Phone:** 617-277-7416
- **Websites:** centralboston.org OR onecentralboston.org
- **Ask for:** Provider Relations Department or Care Coordination Manager

**What to say:**
> "Hi, I'm [Your Name] from [Agency Name]. We're a licensed home care agency serving [your coverage area]. We're MassHealth enrolled and interested in becoming a Central Boston Elder Services provider. Who should I speak with about enrollment?"

### **Step 3: Complete Provider Application**

**Required documentation:**
- Proof of MassHealth provider enrollment
- Massachusetts home care agency license (DPH)
- Liability insurance certificate ($1-2M recommended)
- Workers' compensation insurance
- Service area coverage (which of 8 neighborhoods?)
- **Languages spoken by caregivers** (CRITICAL - Spanish, Chinese, Russian, Vietnamese, Somali highly desired)
- Services offered
- References (if available)

**Timeline:** 4-6 weeks for review and approval

### **Step 4: Meet Care Management Team**

**Relationship building:**
- **Visit office:** 2315 Washington Street, Roxbury
- **Meet care managers:** Introduce agency, emphasize multilingual capabilities
- **Highlight cultural competency:** African, Latino, Asian, Russian communities

## Tips to Get More Referrals from Central Boston

### **1. PRIORITIZE Multilingual Staffing**

**Non-negotiable for Central Boston:**
- **Spanish-speaking caregivers:** ESSENTIAL (25-30% of referrals)
- **Chinese-speaking caregivers (Mandarin/Cantonese):** CRITICAL (10-15% of referrals)
- **Russian-speaking caregivers:** Important (7-10% of referrals)
- **Vietnamese-speaking caregivers:** Valuable (3-5% of referrals)
- **Somali-speaking caregivers:** Helpful (2-3% of referrals)

### **2. Build Cultural Competency**

**Beyond language:**
- **Asian cultures:** Understand Chinese, Vietnamese, Korean family structures, dietary needs, religious practices
- **Latino cultures:** Dominican ≠ Puerto Rican ≠ Salvadoran - recognize differences
- **African immigrant cultures:** Somali (Muslim), Ethiopian/Eritrean (Orthodox Christian), understand immigration trauma
- **Russian immigrant culture:** Language, Orthodox Christian traditions, immigrant experience

### **3. Accept Complex Urban Cases**

**Central Boston needs agencies for challenging situations:**
- **Dense urban environments:** Walk-up apartments, limited parking, public transportation access
- **Mental health/substance abuse:** Family dynamics can be challenging
- **Low-income housing:** Projects, subsidized housing
- **Agencies that accept complex cases = 2x more referrals**

### **4. Respond Within 2-4 Hours**

**Speed critical in dense urban market:**
- Care managers call 3-5 agencies per referral
- First to respond wins ~70% of time

### **5. Partner with All Three Boston ASAPs**

**Maximum Boston coverage:**
- Central Boston (Allston, Brighton, Roxbury, South End, etc.)
- Ethos (JP, Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park, Mattapan)
- Boston Senior Home Care (East Boston, Charlestown, North End, South Boston)
- **Result:** 100-180 referrals/month from Boston

### **6. Build Relationships with Hospital Partners**

**Central Boston coordinates with:**
- Boston Medical Center (BMC)
- Brigham & Women's Hospital
- **Strategy:** Also partner directly with these hospitals for discharge referrals

### **7. Serve Broad Geographic Area**

**Agencies serving 6-8 neighborhoods get most referrals:**
- Don't limit to 1-2 neighborhoods
- Urban neighborhoods are geographically compact - can cover multiple efficiently

### **8. Accept MassHealth Rates**

**35-45% of Central Boston referrals are MassHealth-funded:**
- Volume + payment reliability compensates for lower rates
- Mix with private pay clients from gentrifying South End, Fenway

### **9. LGBTQ+ Competency**

**South End and Fenway have significant LGBTQ+ senior populations:**
- Train staff on LGBTQ+ cultural competency
- Market LGBTQ+-friendly services
- Partner with Fenway Health, other LGBTQ+ organizations

### **10. Communicate Proactively**

**Build trust with care managers:**
- Report concerns immediately
- Share successes
- Ask for support from care managers
