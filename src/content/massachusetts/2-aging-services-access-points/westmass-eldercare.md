## Overview

WestMass ElderCare (now operating as **Access Care Partners**) is the **Aging Services Access Point (ASAP) for Springfield/Holyoke metro area**, serving 70,000+ seniors (age 60+) across 20+ communities in Hampden County. As the gateway to MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver, care management, and community resources for Western Massachusetts' largest urban area, WestMass ElderCare is an **ESSENTIAL partnership** for home care agencies serving the Springfield region.

**WestMass ElderCare / Access Care Partners:**
- **Location:** Holyoke, MA (serving Hampden County communities)
- **Phone:** (413) 538-9020
- **Service area:** Springfield, Holyoke, Chicopee, Westfield, plus 20+ surrounding communities
- **Population served:** 70,000+ residents age 60+
- **ASAP designation:** Massachusetts Executive Office of Elder Affairs contracted ASAP

**For home care agencies:**
- **Gateway City market:** Springfield (4th largest MA city, 155,000 population) = high volume potential
- **Diverse populations:** Hispanic (45% Springfield), Polish, Vietnamese, African - multilingual capability ESSENTIAL
- **MassHealth dominant:** Higher MassHealth percentage than Boston/MetroWest (Springfield lower-income)
- **MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver:** WestMass conducts assessments, authorizes waiver services
- **Care management:** WestMass employs care managers who directly refer families to home care agencies
- **Information & Referral:** 80+ daily inquiries from families seeking home care
- **100% FREE partnership** (no cost to agencies)

## Why WestMass ElderCare Matters

**The Springfield ASAP referral moment:**

**Scenario 1: Family calls WestMass (daily occurrence)**
- Adult daughter (Puerto Rican family, Springfield) calls WestMass: "Mi madre necesita ayuda en casa" ("My mother needs help at home")
- **WestMass bilingual intake specialist assesses:** Mom's needs (ADL assistance, Spanish-speaking caregiver required)
- **Intake specialist to daughter:** "Aquí hay agencias de cuidado en el hogar que tienen cuidadores que hablan español..." ("Here are home care agencies with Spanish-speaking caregivers...")
- **Warm referral:** WestMass provides 2-3 agency names, family calls agencies

**Scenario 2: MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver assessment**
- WestMass care manager visits 80-year-old Polish woman in Chicopee (MassHealth assessment)
- **Assessment determines:** Eligible for MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver (nursing home level of care, but can be served at home)
- **Care manager authorizes:** 25 hours/week home care (MassHealth-funded)
- **Care manager refers:** Contacts MassHealth-contracted agencies with Polish-speaking caregivers

**Scenario 3: Hospital discharge coordination**
- 75-year-old man hospitalized at Baystate Medical Center (Springfield resident)
- **Hospital discharge planner contacts WestMass:** "Patient being discharged, needs home care"
- **WestMass care manager coordinates:** Assesses patient's needs, arranges home care
- **Refers to agency:** Provides family with 2-3 agency names

**Scenario 4: COA refers to WestMass**
- Springfield Council on Aging social worker identifies senior needing care
- **COA to family:** "Contact WestMass ElderCare - they can help arrange services"
- **Family calls WestMass:** Assesses, refers to home care agencies

## WestMass ElderCare Programs That Generate Home Care Referrals

### **1. Information & Referral (I&R) - HIGHEST VOLUME**

**What it is:**
- **Front door to WestMass:** Families call (413) 538-9020 asking for resources
- **80-100+ inquiries/day:** "My mother needs help at home - what do I do?"
- **Bilingual I&R specialists:** Spanish/English staff (critical for Springfield)

**Home care referral process:**
- **Family calls:** "I need home care for my father in Springfield"
- **I&R specialist asks:** Care needs, budget (MassHealth vs. private pay), language needs (Spanish? Polish? Vietnamese?)
- **I&R provides:** 2-3 home care agency names matching needs
- **Warm referral:** "Call these agencies for free assessments"

**Volume:** 12-30 home care referrals/day (360-900/month from I&R alone)

**How to get I&R referrals:**
- **Be on WestMass resource list:** Visit WestMass, introduce agency, leave materials (Spanish-language materials CRITICAL)
- **Emphasize languages:** "We have Spanish-speaking caregivers," "We serve Polish community," "Vietnamese caregivers available"
- **Stay updated:** Quarterly visits (refresh materials)

### **2. Care Management - HIGH VOLUME**

**What it is:**
- **Care managers (RNs, social workers):** Coordinate complex care for high-risk seniors
- **Who receives care management:** Seniors with multiple chronic conditions, recent hospitalizations, at nursing home risk

**Home care referral process:**
- **Care manager assesses:** "This senior needs daily home care support"
- **Care manager to family:** "I recommend home care - here are agencies..."
- **Direct coordination:** Care manager may call agency directly

**Volume:** 15-35 home care referrals/month from care management

**Referral quality:** HIGH (complex cases, higher hours, longer duration)

### **3. MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver - MASSHEALTH REFERRALS**

**What it is:**
- WestMass conducts **nursing home level of care assessments** for MassHealth
- **Determines eligibility:** Does senior meet nursing home criteria but can be safely served at home?
- **Authorizes waiver services:** Home care, adult day health, personal care homemaker

**Home care referral process:**
- **WestMass care manager assesses:** Senior eligible for waiver
- **Care manager authorizes:** 20-30 hours/week home care (MassHealth-funded)
- **Care manager refers:** Contacts MassHealth-contracted home care agencies
- **Agency provides services:** Billed to MassHealth at contracted rates

**Volume:** 10-25 Frail Elder Waiver home care referrals/month

**Requirements:** Agency must be MassHealth-contracted (enrolled as MassHealth provider)

### **4. SHINE Program - INDIRECT REFERRALS**

**What it is:**
- **Health insurance counseling:** Helping seniors understand Medicare, MassHealth
- **Common question:** "Does Medicare cover home care?"

**Home care referral process:**
- **SHINE counselor educates:** "Medicare doesn't cover personal care, but you may qualify for MassHealth"
- **SHINE counselor refers:** "Contact WestMass care management for MassHealth assessment" OR "Here are private pay home care agencies..."

**Volume:** 5-12 home care referrals/month from SHINE

### **5. Family Caregiver Support Program**

**What it is:**
- **Respite grants, caregiver training, support groups** for family caregivers
- **Common scenario:** Caregiver burned out, needs professional help

**Home care referral process:**
- **WestMass caregiver specialist:** "Have you considered hiring professional caregivers?"
- **Refers to agencies:** Provides agency names

**Volume:** 5-10 home care referrals/month

## How to Build WestMass ElderCare Partnership

### **Step 1: Visit WestMass (Holyoke Office)**

**Initial contact:**
1. **Call:** (413) 538-9020
2. **Ask for:** Director of Community Services OR Care Management Director
3. **Request:** 15-minute meeting to introduce your agency

**In-person visit:**
- **Address:** Holyoke, MA (contact for exact office location)
- **What to bring:** Brochures (100+ copies - SPANISH versions CRITICAL), business cards, services overview, languages list
- **Timing:** Tuesday-Thursday mornings best

**Meeting agenda:**
- **Introduce agency:** Services, coverage area (which Springfield-area towns?), specializations
- **EMPHASIZE LANGUAGES:** "We have Spanish-speaking caregivers," "Polish caregivers," "Vietnamese caregivers"
- **Request:** Permission to leave materials, be added to I&R resource list

### **Step 2: Build Relationship with Key Staff**

**Information & Referral Specialists:**
- **These staff make MOST referrals** (80+ inquiries/day → 12-30 home care referrals/day)
- **Meet I&R team:** Introduce yourself, emphasize languages
- **Provide Spanish materials:** Brochures, flyers, business cards in Spanish

**Care Managers:**
- **High-volume referrals:** Complex MassHealth cases
- **Meet care management team:** Emphasize MassHealth experience, multilingual capabilities
- **Communicate outcomes:** Update care managers on client progress

**Bilingual staff:**
- **WestMass employs bilingual Spanish/English staff:** Build relationships with Spanish-speaking staff (they make most referrals to Hispanic families)

### **Step 3: Provide Value to WestMass**

**How to stand out:**

**A. Spanish-language presentations:**
- Offer to present at WestMass events: "Opciones de Cuidado en el Hogar" (Home Care Options in Spanish)
- **Benefits:** Visibility with Spanish-speaking seniors, positions as culturally competent

**B. Sponsor WestMass programs:**
- WestMass hosts senior expos, health fairs, caregiver conferences
- **Sponsorship:** $150-$500 (logo, table, recognition)
- Builds goodwill

**C. Multilingual resource materials:**
- Provide helpful guides in Spanish, Polish, Vietnamese: "Preguntas Para Hacer a las Agencias de Cuidado" ("Questions to Ask Home Care Agencies")
- **WestMass distributes:** Keeps your agency top-of-mind with diverse populations

### **Step 4: Stay Connected**

**Ongoing:**
- **Quarterly visits:** Stop by WestMass, refresh materials, check in with I&R/care management teams
- **Attend WestMass events:** Senior expos, conferences
- **Holiday cards:** Thank staff
- **Thank referrals:** When WestMass refers, send thank-you note

## Best Practices

**1. Spanish-speaking capability MANDATORY:**
- **45% of Springfield is Hispanic:** Without Spanish-speaking caregivers, WestMass volume SEVERELY LIMITED
- **Recruit Spanish speakers:** Puerto Rican community, Dominican, Colombian caregivers
- **All materials in Spanish:** Brochures, business cards, website

**2. Polish-speaking competitive advantage:**
- **Chicopee = largest Polish population in MA:** Few agencies have Polish speakers
- **Competitive advantage:** Polish caregivers = unique capability (WestMass will prioritize your agency for Polish families)

**3. MassHealth contracting ESSENTIAL:**
- **50% of WestMass referrals are MassHealth:** Frail Elder Waiver, Standard MassHealth
- **Must be MassHealth-contracted:** Without MassHealth, miss half the volume

**4. Serve Springfield + Holyoke + Chicopee (70% of volume):**
- **Core Springfield-area cities = most volume:** Don't limit to just Springfield OR just suburbs
- **Target:** Serve at minimum Springfield, Holyoke, Chicopee, Westfield (covers 70%+ of volume)

**5. Build I&R relationships:**
- **I&R specialists = referral gatekeepers:** Most families call I&R first
- **Meet I&R team:** Introduce yourself, emphasize languages, provide Spanish materials
- **Stay visible:** Quarterly check-ins

**6. Understand urban Springfield challenges:**
- **Safety:** Some Springfield neighborhoods require caregiver safety protocols
- **Multi-family homes:** Many Springfield seniors live in triple-deckers (accessibility considerations)
- **Transportation:** Caregivers need reliable transportation (parking challenges in urban areas)

**7. Cultural competency training:**
- **Puerto Rican culture:** Understand cultural values (family-oriented, respect for elders, religious traditions)
- **Polish traditions:** Catholic faith, dietary preferences, cultural holidays
- **Train caregivers:** Cultural sensitivity (not just language translation)

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "Don't have Spanish-speaking caregivers"**
**Solution:** CRITICAL to hire Spanish speakers for Springfield market. Recruit: (1) Puerto Rican community organizations, (2) Spanish churches, (3) Holyoke Community College ESL programs, (4) Springfield Technical Community College, (5) Community health centers. Without Spanish, WestMass volume VERY LIMITED.

**Challenge 2: "MassHealth rates too low"**
**Solution:** (1) Springfield = MassHealth-dominant market (50%+ MassHealth) - if you don't accept MassHealth, Springfield NOT viable market, (2) Operate efficiently (optimize scheduling, reduce overhead), (3) Mix MassHealth + private pay (50% MassHealth + 50% private pay), (4) Consider declining Springfield market if MassHealth unprofitable (focus on MetroWest/affluent suburbs instead).

**Challenge 3: "WestMass staff don't remember us"**
**Solution:** (1) Visit MORE frequently (quarterly), (2) Meet I&R team personally, (3) Provide Spanish materials (stand out), (4) Attend WestMass events, (5) Communicate outcomes (WestMass wants to know referrals worked out).

**Challenge 4: "Springfield neighborhoods have safety concerns"**
**Solution:** Some Springfield neighborhoods (North End, Six Corners) require caregiver safety protocols. Implement: (1) Safety training, (2) Buddy systems for high-risk areas, (3) Daytime-only visits if needed, (4) Hire caregivers FROM Springfield (local knowledge), (5) Cell phones + emergency contacts.

**Challenge 5: "WestMass refers to multiple agencies"**
**Solution:** TRUE - WestMass provides 2-3 agency names (not exclusive). Differentiate: (1) Languages (Spanish, Polish = competitive advantage), (2) Respond faster, (3) MassHealth expertise, (4) Build personal relationships with I&R specialists (top-of-mind), (5) Cultural competency (Puerto Rican families value culturally-matched care).
