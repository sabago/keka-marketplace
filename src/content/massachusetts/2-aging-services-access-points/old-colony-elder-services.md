## Overview

Old Colony Elder Services, Inc. (OCES) is the **largest nonprofit provider of in-home and community-based services** for older adults and people living with disabilities in southeastern Massachusetts. Serving 23 communities across Greater Plymouth County, OCES provides a critical referral pipeline for home care agencies covering this geographically diverse region—from the urban core of Brockton (Massachusetts' 6th largest city) to affluent coastal towns like Duxbury and Marshfield, to suburban communities throughout the South Shore.

**OCES Quick Facts:**
- **Service Area:** 23 communities in Greater Plymouth County / South Shore
- **Status:** "Largest non-profit provider" of home/community services in Southeastern MA (per OCES)
- **Service Philosophy:** "All people, all ages, all incomes" (broad inclusive mission)
- **Founded:** Decades of operation (established legacy organization)
- **Main Office:** 144 Main Street, Brockton, MA 02301
- **Phone:** 508-584-1561
- **Website:** ocesma.org

**For home care agencies:**
- **Expected referral volume:** 40-70 referrals/month (large 23-town service area creates substantial volume)
- **Geographic diversity:** Urban (Brockton), suburban (Easton, Rockland), coastal (Duxbury, Marshfield, Plymouth)
- **Payer mix:** Blend of MassHealth (Brockton, working-class towns) + private pay (affluent coastal/suburban)
- **Growing Behavioral Health focus:** Recent expansion indicates increasing referrals for seniors with mental health needs
- **Investment:** Free to enroll as home care provider (MassHealth enrollment required)

**Why OCES matters:** As southeastern Massachusetts' largest home care provider, OCES serves a **geographically diverse 23-town region** with varied demographics—urban gateway city (Brockton), affluent coastal retirement communities (Plymouth, Duxbury, Marshfield), and middle-class suburban towns. This diversity creates **referral opportunities across the payer spectrum** (MassHealth to high-end private pay) within a single ASAP partnership.

## Key Programs for Home Care Referrals

OCES operates **comprehensive programs** generating substantial home care referrals across the 23-town service area.

### **1. Services at Home (Core Home Care Program)**

**What it is:**
- Personal care, homemaking, case management for seniors and adults with disabilities
- Includes MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver and state-funded home care
- OCES describes itself as "largest provider" of in-home services in southeastern MA

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Primary referral source** from OCES (most referrals flow through this program)
- Serves both elderly and younger adults with disabilities ("all ages" mission)
- High volume due to 23-town coverage and established reputation

**Expected volume:** 25-45 referrals/month (largest OCES referral source)

### **2. MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver**

**What it is:**
- MassHealth-funded home care for seniors meeting nursing home level of care
- Covers personal care, homemaking, case management, adult day health, respite

**Eligibility:**
- Age 60+
- MassHealth eligible (low income: $11,495 single, $15,544 couple annually)
- Meet nursing facility level of care (2+ ADL assistance needs)
- Choose home over nursing facility

**OCES context:**
- **Brockton high eligibility** (lower incomes = higher MassHealth enrollment)
- Working-class towns (Bridgewater, Whitman, Abington) also significant Frail Elder Waiver volume
- "All incomes" mission means OCES actively serves MassHealth population

**Expected volume:** Integrated into "Services at Home" above (20-35 referrals/month specifically Frail Elder Waiver-funded)

### **3. Adult Family Care Program**

**What it is:**
- Compensation and support for informal caregivers (family, friends) providing care in their homes
- OCES pays caregiver directly, provides training, monitoring, respite assistance

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Respite opportunities:** When informal caregiver needs temporary break, OCES refers to agencies
- **Transition referrals:** When informal arrangement becomes unsustainable (caregiver burnout, client needs increase), OCES refers to formal home care

**Expected volume:** 2-5 referrals/month

### **4. Behavioral Health Services (Growing Program)**

**What it is:**
- Case management, counseling, crisis support for seniors 60+ with mental health needs
- **Recent focus area:** OCES's #GivingTuesday 2025 campaign targeted $20,000 for Behavioral Health Programs
- Indicates program expansion and organizational priority

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Growing referral stream:** Behavioral health expansion = more referrals for seniors with depression, anxiety, dementia-related behaviors
- **Complex cases:** Clients may need caregivers with behavioral health training, de-escalation skills
- **Underserved population:** Many seniors with mental health needs don't receive adequate support—OCES addressing this gap

**Expected volume:** 3-6 referrals/month (growing as program expands)

### **5. Nutrition Programs (Gateway to Home Care)**

**What it is:**
- Home-delivered meals, community dining, nutrition counseling

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Meal delivery observation network:** Volunteers observe declining mobility, confusion, safety concerns
- Seniors trust meal delivery volunteers → more likely to accept home care referrals
- Gateway service (families call about meals, discover full home care services)

**Expected volume:** 4-8 referrals/month triggered by meal program observations

### **6. Caregiver Support Services**

**What it is:**
- Family caregiver guidance, counseling, training, respite assistance
- National Family Caregiver Support Program (federally funded)

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Caregiver burnout identification:** OCES counselors work with exhausted family caregivers, recommend professional support
- **Respite care:** Families need temporary breaks, OCES refers for short-term respite services (often transitions to ongoing care)

**Expected volume:** 4-7 referrals/month

### **7. Community Housing Options**

**What it is:**
- Housing support and alternatives assessment
- Helps seniors explore assisted living, supportive housing, or aging-in-place options

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Aging-in-place assessment:** When seniors choose to remain at home (vs. move to assisted living), home care is essential support
- **Bridge care:** While waiting for assisted living opening, families use home care
- **Options counseling:** OCES explains home care as alternative to institutional placement

**Expected volume:** 3-5 referrals/month

### **8. Elder Abuse Services/Education**

**What it is:**
- Elder abuse prevention, investigation, intervention programs

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Crisis situations:** Abuse/neglect cases require immediate home care intervention
- **Protective services:** OCES coordinates protective placements, refers to agencies for oversight
- **Higher acuity:** These clients often need experienced caregivers, frequent check-ins

**Expected volume:** 2-4 referrals/month

### **9. Life Planning at Any Age (Options Counseling)**

**What it is:**
- Comprehensive options counseling for care decisions
- Helps families choose between home care, assisted living, nursing homes

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Pre-decision consultation:** Families contact OCES BEFORE deciding on care approach
- OCES educates families on home care as option, refers to agencies for assessment
- **Informed families:** Families choosing home care after counseling are committed, realistic

**Expected volume:** 3-6 referrals/month

## How to Partner with OCES

**Enrolling as an OCES home care provider** requires MassHealth enrollment plus OCES-specific credentialing. Geographic coverage is key—OCES's 23-town service area requires agencies to clearly define which towns they serve.

### **Step 1: Verify MassHealth Provider Enrollment**

**Requirement:** OCES requires home care agencies to be **enrolled MassHealth providers**.

**If not enrolled:**
1. Visit mass.gov/masshealth-provider-enrollment
2. Complete Provider Enrollment Application (60-90 days processing)
3. Obtain MassHealth provider number

**If already enrolled:** Proceed to Step 2.

### **Step 2: Assess Your Geographic Coverage**

**Critical consideration:** Can you serve the OCES area you're targeting?

**Geographic scenarios:**

**Scenario A: Full 23-town coverage**
- Ideal but challenging (35-mile north-south span)
- Requires large caregiver network
- **Referral volume:** 50-70 referrals/month

**Scenario B: Brockton + surrounding towns (8-10 towns)**
- Most common (Brockton, Bridgewater, Easton, Abington, Whitman, Rockland, Hanover)
- Captures 60-70% of OCES referrals
- **Referral volume:** 35-50 referrals/month

**Scenario C: Coastal towns (Plymouth, Duxbury, Marshfield, Kingston)**
- Smaller referral volume but higher private pay rates
- Less competition (fewer agencies willing to serve coastal areas exclusively)
- **Referral volume:** 12-20 referrals/month

**Scenario D: Suburban cluster (Easton, Hanover, Rockland, Norwell)**
- Mid-sized volume, middle-income payer mix
- **Referral volume:** 10-18 referrals/month

**Recommendation:** At minimum, serve Brockton + 4-5 surrounding towns to capture meaningful volume.

### **Step 3: Contact OCES Provider Relations**

**Call or email:**
- **Address:** 144 Main Street, Brockton, MA 02301
- **Phone:** 508-584-1561
- **TTY:** 508-587-0280
- **Hours:** Monday-Friday, 9:00am-5:00pm
- **Website:** ocesma.org
- **Ask for:** Provider Relations, Care Coordination Manager, or Director of Home Care Services

**What to say:**
> "Hi, I'm [Your Name] from [Agency Name]. We're a licensed home care agency serving [your coverage area within OCES region]. We're MassHealth enrolled and interested in becoming an OCES provider to receive home care referrals. Can you guide me through your enrollment process?"

### **Step 4: Complete OCES Provider Application**

**Required documentation:**
- Proof of MassHealth provider enrollment (provider number)
- Home care agency license (MA Department of Public Health)
- Liability insurance certificate
- **Service area coverage** (CRITICAL: which of 23 towns you serve—be specific)
- Languages spoken by caregivers (Portuguese helpful for Brockton)
- Services offered (personal care, homemaking, respite, 24/7 care, behavioral health support)
- References from other ASAPs or healthcare partners

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks for OCES review and approval

### **Step 5: Meet OCES Team**

**Build relationships:**
- **Visit OCES office:** 144 Main Street, Brockton (meet case managers in person)
- **Introduce your agency:** Bring brochures, business cards, emphasize geographic coverage
- **Highlight relevant experience:**
  - If you serve urban areas: Emphasize Brockton expertise
  - If you serve coastal towns: Highlight affluent client experience
  - If you offer behavioral health support: Emphasize this (aligns with OCES priority)

## Referral Volume & Expectations

**Expected monthly referrals from OCES:** 40-70 referrals/month (varies significantly based on geographic coverage)

### **Factors Affecting Your Referral Volume:**

**1. Geographic coverage (PRIMARY FACTOR):**

- **Full 23-town coverage:** 50-70 referrals/month
- **Brockton + 8-10 surrounding towns:** 35-50 referrals/month
- **Brockton only:** 25-35 referrals/month
- **Coastal cluster (Plymouth, Duxbury, Marshfield, Kingston):** 12-20 referrals/month
- **Suburban cluster (Easton, Hanover, Rockland, Norwell):** 10-18 referrals/month

**Reality:** **Brockton is the volume driver** (50-60% of OCES services). Agencies not serving Brockton miss majority of referrals.

**2. Payer mix flexibility:**

- **Accept MassHealth:** Essential for Brockton, working-class towns (60-70% of referrals)
- **Offer private pay:** Captures affluent coastal/suburban referrals (30-40% of referrals)
- **"All incomes" alignment:** OCES's mission is inclusive—agencies that embrace full payer spectrum receive more referrals

**3. Service capacity:**

- **High-capacity agencies** (30+ caregivers): 50-70 referrals/month
- **Medium agencies** (15-25 caregivers): 30-45 referrals/month
- **Small agencies** (8-12 caregivers): 18-28 referrals/month

**4. Behavioral health capability (Emerging differentiator):**

- OCES is expanding Behavioral Health Services
- **Agencies with behavioral health-trained caregivers** (dementia care, depression support, de-escalation skills) receive priority for these referrals
- Growing segment as OCES focuses on underserved mental health population

**5. Responsiveness:**

- **< 2 hours response:** Case managers call you first (50-70 referrals/month)
- **< 24 hours response:** Average volume (30-45 referrals/month)
- **> 48 hours response:** Case managers move to other agencies (15-25 referrals/month)

**6. Competition:**

- OCES works with 40-60+ home care agencies across 23-town area
- **Coastal towns:** Lower competition (fewer agencies serve exclusively coastal)
- **Brockton:** Higher competition (many agencies serve urban area)
- **Strategy:** Differentiate through service area breadth, responsiveness, behavioral health expertise

### **Conversion Rate:**

**From referral to active client:** 70-80%

**Why some referrals don't convert:**
- Family chooses different agency (15-20%)
- Agency can't meet geographic/scheduling needs (5-10%)
- Client/family declines all services after assessment (5-10%)

### **Timeline to First Referrals:**

- **Week 1-2:** Provider application submitted, under review
- **Week 3-4:** Application approved, added to provider list
- **Week 5-6:** First referrals received (2-5 referrals in first month)
- **Month 2-3:** Referral volume increases as case managers learn your agency (10-20 referrals/month)
- **Month 4-6:** Steady-state volume achieved (30-50+ referrals/month based on coverage)

## Best Practices for Success with OCES

**Strategies to maximize referral volume from OCES:**

### **1. Serve Brockton to Capture Majority of Referrals**

**Brockton = 50-60% of OCES services:**
- If you only serve suburban/coastal towns, you're missing majority of referrals
- **Recommendation:** Even if Brockton is outside your comfort zone, consider expanding—volume justifies investment
- Hire Brockton-based caregivers (local knowledge, cultural fit)

### **2. Balance MassHealth + Private Pay**

**Accept both payer types:**
- **MassHealth/state-funded (Brockton, working-class towns):** Volume, stable funding
- **Private pay (Duxbury, Marshfield, Easton):** Margins, supplemental services
- OCES's "all incomes" mission means agencies that embrace full spectrum receive more referrals

### **3. Offer Behavioral Health Support (Emerging Differentiator)**

**OCES priority area:**
- Train caregivers on dementia care, depression support, de-escalation techniques
- **Market behavioral health capability** to OCES (aligns with organizational priority)
- Agencies offering this receive priority for behavioral health referrals (growing segment)

### **4. Build Geographic Coverage Strategically**

**Expand incrementally:**
- **Start:** Brockton + 3-4 surrounding towns (70% of referrals accessible)
- **Expand:** Add coastal towns OR additional suburban towns (increase to 85% accessibility)
- **Mature:** Full 23-town coverage (100% accessibility, maximize volume)

### **5. Partner with Brockton Healthcare Providers**

**Synergy with hospital discharge planning:**
- **Good Samaritan Medical Center** (Brockton's primary hospital)
- **Signature Healthcare Brockton Hospital**
- Build relationships with discharge planners → hospital + OCES both referring = highest volume

### **6. Cultural Competency for Brockton's Diversity**

**Prepare for:**
- **Haitian families:** Haitian Creole-speaking caregivers valued, understanding of Haitian culture
- **Hispanic/Latino families:** Spanish-speaking caregivers, Latino family caregiving traditions
- **Cape Verdean families:** Portuguese/Creole speakers, Cape Verdean cultural norms

### **7. Differentiate for Coastal Affluent Market**

**Premium service delivery:**
- Professional presentation (uniforms, branded vehicles)
- Proactive communication (daily/weekly family updates)
- Discretion and privacy (high-profile clients, waterfront estates)
- Premium pricing ($40-$60+/hour)—coastal families expect and pay for quality

### **8. Respond Quickly (Large Geographic Area = Logistics Challenges)**

**OCES needs reliable partners:**
- 23-town service area creates logistics challenges (case managers value agencies that respond quickly despite distance)
- **Provide direct cell phone** for intake coordinator
- **Commit to geographic coverage:** Don't say you serve a town if you're unreliable there (damages relationship)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Do I need to serve all 23 OCES towns to receive referrals?**

**A:** No. OCES refers based on your stated coverage. However, **Brockton is critical** (50-60% of referrals). If you serve only suburban/coastal towns (no Brockton), expect 15-25 referrals/month. Add Brockton, volume jumps to 35-50 referrals/month. **Recommendation:** Serve Brockton + 6-8 surrounding towns for optimal volume (70-80% of total referrals accessible).

**Q2: What's the payer mix for OCES referrals?**

**A:** **Overall: 60-70% MassHealth/state-funded, 30-40% private pay.** Breakdown by area: **Brockton** (75-80% MassHealth), **Suburban towns** (50-50 mix), **Coastal towns** (60-70% private pay). **Strategy:** Serve mix of areas to balance volume (MassHealth) and margins (private pay).

**Q3: How competitive is the OCES provider network?**

**A:** **Moderate to high competition.** OCES works with 40-60+ agencies across 23 towns. **Less competition:** Coastal towns (fewer agencies serve), behavioral health services (new focus area, limited agencies offer). **More competition:** Brockton and inner suburbs (many agencies serve). **Differentiation keys:** Geographic breadth, responsiveness, behavioral health capability.

**Q4: What languages are needed for OCES referrals?**

**A:** **Essential:** English (all areas), Spanish (Brockton). **Highly valued:** Haitian Creole (Brockton), Cape Verdean Creole/Portuguese (Brockton, coastal). **English-only agencies** can succeed in suburban/coastal towns but miss majority of Brockton referrals (which are 50-60% of total OCES volume).

**Q5: What does OCES's Behavioral Health focus mean for home care agencies?**

**A:** **Growing referral opportunity.** OCES is expanding services for seniors 60+ with mental health needs (depression, anxiety, dementia-related behaviors). **Agencies should:** (1) Train caregivers on behavioral health support, (2) Market this capability to OCES, (3) Expect increasing referrals for clients with complex behavioral needs. **Currently:** 3-6 referrals/month. **Projected growth:** Could reach 10-15 referrals/month as program matures.

**Q6: How long does provider enrollment take?**

**A:** **2-4 weeks** from application to first referral, assuming MassHealth enrollment complete. **Tip:** Visit OCES office in Brockton during application process to meet case managers in person (builds rapport, demonstrates commitment, accelerates first referrals).
