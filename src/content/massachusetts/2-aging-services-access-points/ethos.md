## Overview

Ethos is one of **three Aging Services Access Points (ASAPs) serving Boston**, with a unique distinction: while Ethos directly serves Jamaica Plain, Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park, and Mattapan, it also administers **Elder Protective Services for ALL of Boston**. Founded in 1972 as Jamaica Plain Council on Aging, Ethos has 50+ years of experience serving Boston's diverse, multicultural neighborhoods.

**Ethos Quick Facts:**
- **Service Area:** Jamaica Plain, Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park, Mattapan + Elder Protective Services (all Boston)
- **Population Served:** 35,000+ seniors annually across direct service area
- **Founded:** 1972 (one of Massachusetts' oldest ASAPs)
- **Home Care Clients:** 2,500+ individuals receiving in-home support
- **Main Office:** 555 Amory Street, Jamaica Plain, MA 02130
- **Phone:** 617-522-6700
- **Website:** ethocare.org

**For home care agencies:**
- **Expected referral volume:** 30-60 referrals/month
- **Part of Boston Consortium:** Coordinate with Central Boston Elder Services and Boston Senior Home Care (can apply to all 3)
- **Cultural diversity:** Spanish, Haitian Creole, Cape Verdean Creole, Portuguese essential
- **MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver:** Primary referral source
- **VD-HCBS Program:** Veterans-Directed Home and Community Based Services administrator
- **Investment:** Free to enroll as home care provider (MassHealth enrollment required)

**Why Ethos matters:** As one of Boston's three ASAPs and the administrator of citywide Elder Protective Services, Ethos represents a critical gateway to Boston's southwestern neighborhoods - areas with significant Latino, Haitian, Cape Verdean, and African American populations requiring culturally competent home care services.

### **The Three Boston ASAPs:**

**1. Ethos**
- **Neighborhoods:** Jamaica Plain, Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park, Mattapan
- **Plus:** Elder Protective Services (all Boston)

**2. Central Boston Elder Services**
- **Neighborhoods:** Allston, Brighton, Fenway, Mission Hill, North Dorchester, North Jamaica Plain, South End, Roxbury

**3. Boston Senior Home Care (BSHC)**
- **Neighborhoods:** East Boston, Charlestown, North End, West End, Beacon Hill, Downtown, Chinatown, South Cove, South Boston, sections of Dorchester/Mattapan

### **Why This Matters for Home Care Agencies:**

**Apply to all three Boston ASAPs simultaneously:**
- Each ASAP operates independently but coordinates services
- Agencies serving multiple Boston neighborhoods should partner with ALL THREE
- **Strategy:** Apply to Ethos (if serving JP/Roslindale/West Roxbury/Hyde Park/Mattapan), Central Boston (if serving Allston/Brighton/Roxbury), and BSHC (if serving East Boston/Charlestown/South Boston)
- **Result:** 3x referral volume by covering entire Boston

**Coordinated programs:**
- All three Boston ASAPs participate in VD-HCBS (Veterans-Directed HCBS)
- Shared training, best practices
- Sometimes coordinate on complex cases spanning multiple neighborhoods

## Key Programs for Home Care Referrals

Ethos operates **multiple programs** that generate home care referrals for enrolled agencies.

### **1. MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver (Primary Referral Source)**

**What it is:**
- MassHealth-funded home care for seniors who meet **nursing home level of care** criteria but prefer to remain at home
- Covers personal care, homemaking, case management, adult day health, respite care

**Eligibility:**
- Age 60+
- MassHealth eligible (income limits: $11,495 single, $15,544 couple annually)
- Meet nursing facility level of care (require assistance with 2+ ADLs)
- Choose to remain at home rather than enter nursing facility

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Highest-volume referral source** from Ethos
- **Long-term care relationships:** Clients typically need ongoing services for months/years
- **Stable funding:** MassHealth pays agencies directly
- **High MassHealth population:** 30-40% of Ethos service area is MassHealth-eligible (higher than wealthy suburbs)

**Expected volume:** 20-35 referrals/month from Frail Elder Waiver program

### **2. State-Funded Home Care Program**

**What it is:**
- State-funded personal care, homemaking, case management for seniors not MassHealth-eligible
- "Middle-income" seniors who don't qualify for MassHealth but can't afford full private pay

**Eligibility:**
- Age 60+
- Income above MassHealth limits but below ~$75,000
- Need assistance with ADLs or IADLs
- Assessed by Ethos case manager

**Expected volume:** 8-15 referrals/month

### **3. VD-HCBS Program (Veterans-Directed Home and Community Based Services)**

**What it is:**
- VA-funded program for veterans who want to self-direct their care
- Ethos serves as VD-HCBS program administrator for Boston region (coordinates with Central Boston Elder Services and BSHC)

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Veteran population:** Significant veteran populations in West Roxbury, Roslindale, Hyde Park
- **VA-funded:** Veterans receive budget to purchase home care services
- **Referral opportunity:** Ethos refers veterans to contracted agencies

**Expected volume:** 5-10 referrals/month

### **4. Elder Protective Services (All Boston)**

**What it is:**
- Investigation and intervention for elder abuse, neglect, exploitation, abandonment
- Mandated reporting system for professionals (doctors, nurses, social workers must report suspected abuse)
- **Ethos administers EPS for ALL of Boston** (not just 5 neighborhoods)

**Home care referral process:**
- **EPS investigates report:** Social worker visits elder's home, assesses safety
- **Care plan developed:** Often includes home care services for monitoring, safety, ADL assistance
- **EPS refers to agencies:** Contacts home care agencies to provide protective supervision, companionship, personal care

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Unique referral source:** Only Ethos has citywide EPS jurisdiction
- **Complex cases:** Require experienced, sensitive caregivers (trauma-informed care)
- **High supervision needs:** EPS clients often need daily monitoring
- **Longer engagements:** Safety monitoring can last months/years

**Expected volume:** 5-15 referrals/month from EPS

**Special considerations:**
- EPS cases may involve family conflict, substance abuse, mental health issues, hoarding
- Agencies must be comfortable with complex social situations
- Training in trauma-informed care, cultural competency highly valuable

### **5. Information & Referral (I&R)**

**What it is:**
- **Front door to Ethos:** Families call 617-522-6700 asking for resources
- **50-100 inquiries/day:** "My mother needs help at home - what do I do?"
- **I&R specialists:** Bilingual staff (Spanish, Haitian Creole) assess needs, provide referrals

**Home care referral process:**
- **Family calls:** "I need home care for my father in Jamaica Plain"
- **I&R specialist asks:** Care needs, budget, language needs
- **I&R provides:** 2-3 home care agency names serving JP, matching family's needs
- **Warm referral:** "Call these agencies for free assessments"

**Volume:** 10-25 home care referrals/day (300-750/month from I&R alone)

### **6. Care Management**

**What it is:**
- **Care managers (RNs, social workers):** Coordinate complex care for high-risk seniors
- **Who receives care management:** Seniors with multiple chronic conditions, recent hospitalizations, at risk of nursing home placement

**Home care referral process:**
- **Care manager assesses:** "This senior needs daily home care support"
- **Care manager to family:** "I recommend home care - here are agencies I work with..."
- **Direct coordination:** Care manager may call agency directly to arrange assessment

**Volume:** 10-20 home care referrals/month

**Referral quality:** VERY HIGH (complex cases, higher hours, longer duration)

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Partner with Ethos

**Becoming an Ethos home care provider** requires MassHealth provider enrollment plus Ethos-specific credentialing.

### **Step 1: Verify MassHealth Provider Enrollment**

**Requirement:** Ethos requires home care agencies to be **enrolled MassHealth providers** before accepting referrals.

**If not enrolled:**
1. Visit mass.gov/masshealth-provider-enrollment
2. Complete Provider Enrollment Application (60-90 days for approval)
3. Obtain MassHealth provider number

**If already enrolled:** Proceed to Step 2.

### **Step 2: Contact Ethos Provider Relations**

**Call Ethos main office:**
- **Address:** 555 Amory Street, Jamaica Plain, MA 02130
- **Phone:** 617-522-6700
- **Email:** info@ethocare.org
- **Ask for:** Provider Relations Department or Care Coordination Manager

**What to say:**
> "Hi, I'm [Your Name] from [Agency Name]. We're a licensed home care agency serving [your coverage area]. We're MassHealth enrolled and interested in becoming an Ethos provider to receive home care referrals. Who should I speak with about enrollment?"

### **Step 3: Complete Ethos Provider Application**

**Required documentation:**
- Proof of MassHealth provider enrollment (provider number)
- Massachusetts home care agency license (DPH)
- Liability insurance certificate ($1-2M recommended)
- Workers' compensation insurance
- Service area coverage (which neighborhoods: JP, Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park, Mattapan?)
- **Languages spoken by caregivers** (CRITICAL - Spanish, Haitian Creole, Cape Verdean Creole highly desired)
- Services offered (personal care, homemaking, respite, 24/7 care, etc.)
- References from other ASAPs or healthcare partners (if available)

**Timeline:** 3-6 weeks for Ethos to review and approve application

### **Step 4: Attend Provider Orientation (If Required)**

**What to expect:**
- Ethos may require new providers to attend orientation session
- Topics: Referral process, cultural competency, documentation, billing, case manager communication
- Duration: 2-3 hours (typically at JP office or virtual)

### **Step 5: Meet Ethos Care Management Team**

**Relationship building (critical for referral volume):**
- **Request to visit office:** 555 Amory Street, Jamaica Plain
- **Meet care managers in person:** Introduce agency, emphasize multilingual capabilities
- **Highlight cultural competency:** If you have Haitian, Dominican, Cape Verdean caregivers - emphasize this
- **Demonstrate responsiveness:** Provide direct cell phone for immediate response to referrals

## Provider Enrollment Process

**Step-by-step enrollment for new Ethos providers:**

### **Phase 1: Initial Contact (Week 1)**

1. **Call 617-522-6700** and ask for Provider Relations or Care Coordination Manager
2. **Express interest:** "We want to partner with Ethos to serve Jamaica Plain, Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park, Mattapan seniors"
3. **Request application materials:** May be email, online form, or paper packet

### **Phase 2: Application Preparation (Week 1-2)**

4. **Gather required documents:**
   - MassHealth provider number
   - Home care agency license
   - Insurance certificates (liability, workers' comp)
   - Service area map
   - Staff languages list
   - Sample care plans, policies & procedures (if requested)

5. **Complete application forms:**
   - Agency information
   - Services offered
   - Geographic coverage
   - Quality measures (client satisfaction, caregiver retention, etc.)

### **Phase 3: Submission (Week 2)**

6. **Submit complete application:**
   - Email, mail, or hand-deliver to 555 Amory Street
   - Request confirmation of receipt

### **Phase 4: Review & Approval (Week 3-6)**

7. **Ethos reviews application:**
   - Verification of licenses, insurance
   - Reference checks (if provided)
   - Assessment of cultural competency, multilingual capabilities

8. **Ethos may conduct site visit or interview:**
   - Tour your office
   - Meet key staff
   - Review operations, training programs, supervision model

9. **Approval decision:**
   - If approved: Added to Ethos provider list
   - If not approved: Feedback provided, opportunity to reapply

### **Phase 5: Onboarding (Week 6-8)**

10. **Provider orientation:**
    - Attend mandatory orientation (if required)
    - Meet care management team
    - Review referral protocols, documentation requirements

11. **Set up billing/communication systems:**
    - Establish billing processes
    - Provide contact information for referrals (direct phone, fax, email)

12. **Start receiving referrals:**
    - Typically within 1-2 weeks after orientation
    - First referrals often "test cases" - excellent outcomes lead to more referrals

## Expected Referral Volume

**Expected monthly referrals from Ethos:** 30-60 referrals/month

### **Factors Affecting Your Referral Volume:**

**1. Geographic coverage:**
- **All 5 neighborhoods:** 50-60 referrals/month
- **3-4 neighborhoods:** 30-40 referrals/month
- **1-2 neighborhoods:** 15-25 referrals/month

**2. Languages spoken (MOST CRITICAL FACTOR):**
- **Spanish + Haitian Creole + English:** 50-60 referrals/month (full Ethos service area coverage)
- **Spanish + English:** 30-40 referrals/month (can serve JP, Roslindale, parts of Hyde Park)
- **English only:** 15-20 referrals/month (limited to West Roxbury, some JP/Roslindale)
- **Agencies with multilingual staff receive 3-4x more referrals** than English-only agencies in Ethos service area

**3. Cultural competency:**
- **Haitian culturally competent caregivers:** CRITICAL for Mattapan, Hyde Park (15-20% of Ethos referrals)
- **Latino culturally competent caregivers:** ESSENTIAL for JP, Roslindale (25-30% of referrals)
- **Cape Verdean culturally competent:** Valuable for Mattapan, Dorchester areas (5-8% of referrals)
- **Training:** Cultural competency training demonstrates commitment (Ethos values this highly)

**4. Responsiveness:**
- **< 2 hours response time:** 50-60 referrals/month
- **< 24 hours response time:** 30-45 referrals/month
- **> 48 hours response time:** 15-25 referrals/month

**5. Willingness to accept complex cases:**
- **EPS referrals (elder abuse/neglect):** Require experienced, trauma-informed caregivers
- **Hoarding cases:** Common in Ethos service area
- **Mental health/substance abuse:** Family dynamics can be challenging
- **Agencies that accept complex cases receive 2x more referrals** (Ethos desperately needs agencies for challenging situations)

### **Conversion Rate:**

**From referral to active client:** 70-80% conversion

**Why some referrals don't convert:**
- Family chooses different agency (10-15% of referrals)
- Client/family declines services after intake (5-10%)
- Language/cultural mismatch (5-10% - reason multilingual capabilities are critical)

### **Timeline to First Referrals:**

- **Week 1-6:** Provider application submitted, under review
- **Week 6-8:** Application approved, orientation completed
- **Week 8-10:** First referrals received (typically 3-8 referrals in first month)
- **Month 2-4:** Referral volume increases as care managers learn your agency (15-30 referrals/month)
- **Month 5+:** Steady-state volume achieved (30-60 referrals/month based on factors above)

## Tips to Get More Referrals from Ethos

**Strategies to maximize referral volume and build strong Ethos partnership:**

### **1. PRIORITIZE Multilingual Staffing (Non-Negotiable)**

**Cannot be overstated:**
- **Spanish-speaking caregivers:** ESSENTIAL for 25-30% of Ethos referrals
- **Haitian Creole-speaking caregivers:** CRITICAL for 15-20% of referrals
- **Cape Verdean Creole-speaking caregivers:** Valuable for 5-8% of referrals
- **Without multilingual staff, you will receive < 20% of potential Ethos referrals**

**How to build multilingual capacity:**
- **Hire directly:** Recruit Spanish-speaking, Haitian Creole-speaking caregivers from community
- **Partner with immigrant service organizations:** Connect with Haitian Multi-Service Center, Dominican Development Center, etc.
- **Train existing staff:** Language classes, cultural competency training
- **Market multilingual capabilities:** Prominently feature on materials to Ethos

### **2. Accept Complex Cases (EPS, Hoarding, Mental Health)**

**Ethos needs agencies willing to serve challenging situations:**
- **Elder Protective Services cases:** Abuse/neglect victims require trauma-informed care
- **Hoarding cases:** Common in Ethos service area (10-15% of referrals)
- **Mental health/substance abuse:** Family conflict, challenging dynamics
- **Saying "yes" to complex cases = 2x more referrals** (Ethos prioritizes agencies that don't cherry-pick)

**Training investment:**
- Trauma-informed care training
- Hoarding disorder education
- De-escalation techniques for family conflict
- Mental health first aid

### **3. Build Personal Relationships with Care Managers**

**Visit Ethos office regularly:**
- **Address:** 555 Amory Street, Jamaica Plain, MA 02130
- **Frequency:** Quarterly visits minimum
- **Purpose:** Meet care managers, provide updates, refresh materials

**Communication:**
- **Share outcomes:** "The client you referred is doing great - thank you!"
- **Report concerns early:** Don't wait for crisis to call care manager
- **Ask for feedback:** "How can we better support your clients?"

### **4. Serve All 5 Neighborhoods (or Partner Strategically)**

**Full coverage = maximum referrals:**
- Agencies serving all 5 neighborhoods (JP, Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park, Mattapan) receive most referrals
- **If can't serve all 5:** Prioritize based on your strengths:
  - **Spanish-speaking agency:** Focus on JP, Roslindale
  - **Haitian Creole-speaking agency:** Focus on Mattapan, Hyde Park
  - **English-focused agency:** Focus on West Roxbury
- **Geographic clustering:** Serve contiguous neighborhoods to minimize drive time

### **5. Partner with Boston's Other ASAPs**

**Apply to all three Boston ASAPs:**
- **Ethos** (JP, Roslindale, West Roxbury, Hyde Park, Mattapan)
- **Central Boston Elder Services** (Allston, Brighton, Roxbury, South End, Fenway)
- **Boston Senior Home Care** (East Boston, Charlestown, North End, South Boston)
- **Result:** 3x referral volume by covering entire Boston

### **6. Demonstrate Cultural Competency**

**Beyond language - cultural understanding matters:**
- **Haitian culture:** Understand family structures, religious practices (Catholic, Vodou), immigration trauma
- **Latino cultures:** Recognize differences (Dominican ≠ Puerto Rican ≠ Central American)
- **Cape Verdean culture:** Language (Creole Portuguese), Catholic faith, family-centered care
- **LGBTQ+ competency:** Jamaica Plain has significant LGBTQ+ senior population

**How to demonstrate:**
- **Staff diversity:** Hire caregivers from communities you serve
- **Training:** Cultural competency workshops for all staff
- **Materials:** Translate brochures, intake forms into Spanish, Haitian Creole
- **Community engagement:** Partner with community organizations (Haitian Multi-Service Center, Hyde Square Task Force, etc.)

### **7. Respond to Referrals Within 2-4 Hours**

**Speed wins referrals:**
- Ethos care managers call 3-5 agencies per referral
- **First agency to respond and accept client wins** ~70% of the time
- Provide care managers with **direct cell phone** for intake coordinator

### **8. Accept MassHealth Rates Without Complaint**

**Financial reality:**
- 30-40% of Ethos referrals are MassHealth-funded (Frail Elder Waiver, state home care)
- MassHealth rates are lower than private pay (~$25-30/hour vs. $35-45/hour)
- **Agencies that accept MassHealth gracefully receive more referrals** than agencies that complain

**Strategy:**
- Mix MassHealth clients with private pay clients from gentrifying JP, West Roxbury
- Volume + payment reliability compensates for lower rates

### **9. Attend Ethos Events**

**Visibility builds referrals:**
- **Health fairs:** Ethos sponsors community events in JP, Roslindale, Mattapan
- **Caregiver conferences:** Ethos hosts caregiver support events
- **Provider meetings:** Quarterly provider trainings (if offered)
- **Attend/sponsor:** Builds visibility, demonstrates commitment

### **10. Communicate Proactively**

**Build trust through communication:**
- **Report concerns immediately:** Client condition changes, family issues, caregiver challenges
- **Ask for support:** Care managers have resources (adult day health, meals, equipment)
- **Share successes:** Client outcomes, caregiver training updates
