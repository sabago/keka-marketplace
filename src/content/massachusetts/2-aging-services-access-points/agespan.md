## Overview

AgeSpan (formerly Elder Services of the Merrimack Valley and North Shore Elder Services) is one of Massachusetts' **largest Aging Services Access Points (ASAPs)**, serving 40,000+ older adults and adults with disabilities across 28 cities and towns in the Merrimack Valley and North Shore regions. As both a state-designated ASAP and a federally-designated Area Agency on Aging (AAA), AgeSpan provides critical home care referrals to agencies serving the Greater Haverhill, Greater Lawrence, Greater Lowell, and North Shore areas.

**AgeSpan Quick Facts:**
- **Service Area:** 28 communities (Merrimack Valley + North Shore)
- **Seniors Served Annually:** 40,000+ older adults and adults with disabilities
- **Founded:** 1974 (50+ years of operation)
- **Home Care Services:** 5,000+ individuals receiving in-home support
- **Meals Program:** 225,000+ meals delivered annually
- **Status:** Private nonprofit with ASAP + AAA dual designation
- **Accreditation:** NCQA 3-Year accreditation for case management excellence
- **Main Offices:** Lawrence (280 Merrimack Street) and Danvers (300 Rosewood Drive)

**For home care agencies:**
- **Expected referral volume:** 40-80 referrals/month (one of highest volumes statewide)
- **Service area distribution:** 47% Lawrence area, 40% Lowell area, 13% Haverhill area
- **Multilingual requirements:** Spanish, Russian, Khmer (Cambodian) essential
- **MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver:** Primary referral source (nursing home level of care)
- **Partnership model:** Also works with SeniorCare Inc. for Beverly area coverage
- **Investment:** Free to enroll as home care provider (MassHealth enrollment required)

**Why AgeSpan matters:** With 28 communities served and 40,000+ seniors in the system, AgeSpan represents the **largest geographic coverage** of any uncovered ASAP in Massachusetts. Agencies serving the Merrimack Valley, Greater Lowell, or North Shore without an AgeSpan partnership are missing one of the state's highest-volume referral sources.

## Key Programs for Home Care Referrals

AgeSpan operates **multiple programs** that generate home care referrals for enrolled agencies. Understanding each program helps agencies position services appropriately.

### **1. MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver (Primary Referral Source)**

**What it is:**
- MassHealth-funded home care for seniors who meet **nursing home level of care** criteria but prefer to remain at home
- Covers personal care, homemaking, case management, adult day health, respite care

**Eligibility:**
- Age 60+
- MassHealth eligible (low income: $11,495 single, $15,544 couple annually for MassHealth Standard)
- Meet nursing facility level of care (require assistance with 2+ ADLs)
- Choose to remain at home rather than enter nursing facility

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Highest-volume referral source** from AgeSpan
- **Long-term care relationships:** Clients typically need ongoing services for months/years
- **Stable funding:** MassHealth pays agencies directly (no collection issues)

**Expected volume:** 25-50 referrals/month from Frail Elder Waiver program

### **2. State-Funded Home Care Program**

**What it is:**
- State-funded personal care, homemaking, and case management for seniors not MassHealth-eligible
- "Middle-income" seniors who don't qualify for MassHealth but can't afford private pay

**Eligibility:**
- Age 60+
- Income above MassHealth limits but below ~$75,000 (varies)
- Need assistance with ADLs or IADLs
- Assessed by AgeSpan case manager

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Second-largest referral source** from AgeSpan
- Clients transitioning from state-funded to private pay as needs increase
- Smaller case loads than Frail Elder Waiver but steady volume

**Expected volume:** 10-20 referrals/month

### **3. Adult Family Care Program**

**What it is:**
- Compensation and support for **informal caregivers** (family members, friends) who provide care in their homes
- AgeSpan pays caregiver directly, coordinates care, provides respite support

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Respite care opportunities:** When adult family caregiver needs break, AgeSpan refers to agencies for temporary respite
- **Transition opportunities:** When informal arrangement no longer sustainable, AgeSpan refers to agencies for formal home care

**Expected volume:** 2-5 referrals/month (lower volume but high-value relationships)

### **4. Community Choices Program**

**What it is:**
- MassHealth waiver program for seniors transitioning from nursing facilities back to community
- Also serves at-risk seniors to prevent institutionalization

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Pre-discharge planning:** AgeSpan coordinates with nursing facilities, refers to home care agencies for post-discharge support
- **High acuity:** Clients often have complex medical needs, require experienced caregivers

**Expected volume:** 3-8 referrals/month

### **5. Care Coordination & Case Management (NCQA Accredited)**

**What it is:**
- AgeSpan holds **NCQA 3-Year accreditation** for case management excellence
- Case managers conduct in-home assessments, develop care plans, coordinate services

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Pre-qualified leads:** Case managers already assessed client needs before referring to agencies
- **Ongoing coordination:** Case managers stay involved, provide support for complex cases
- **Quality emphasis:** NCQA accreditation means case managers refer to high-quality agencies only

**Expected volume:** Integrated into all program referrals above

### **6. Meals on Wheels (Gateway to Home Care)**

**What it is:**
- 225,000+ home-delivered meals annually
- Meal delivery drivers often first to observe escalating care needs (mobility decline, confusion, safety concerns)

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Observation network:** Drivers report concerns to case managers, triggering home care assessments
- **Trust building:** Seniors receiving meals trust AgeSpan, more likely to accept home care referrals

**Expected volume:** 5-10 referrals/month triggered by meal delivery observations

### **7. Options Counseling**

**What it is:**
- One-on-one counseling to help families explore long-term care options
- Case managers discuss home care, assisted living, nursing homes, adult day health

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Early intervention:** Families contact AgeSpan BEFORE crisis, allowing planned home care start
- **Informed decision-making:** Families choosing home care after counseling are committed, realistic about needs

**Expected volume:** 5-10 referrals/month

### **8. Caregiver Support Programs**

**What it is:**
- Family caregiver assessment, counseling, training, respite assistance
- National Family Caregiver Support Program (federally funded)

**Why agencies should care:**
- **Caregiver burnout:** When family caregivers exhausted, AgeSpan recommends professional home care to prevent breakdown
- **Respite care:** Families need temporary breaks, AgeSpan refers for respite services

**Expected volume:** 3-6 referrals/month

## How to Partner with AgeSpan

**Enrolling as an AgeSpan home care provider** requires MassHealth provider enrollment plus AgeSpan-specific credentialing. Follow these steps:

### **Step 1: Verify MassHealth Provider Enrollment**

**Requirement:** AgeSpan requires home care agencies to be **enrolled MassHealth providers** before accepting referrals.

**If not enrolled:**
1. Visit mass.gov/masshealth-provider-enrollment
2. Complete Provider Enrollment Application (may take 60-90 days for approval)
3. Obtain MassHealth provider number

**If already enrolled:** Proceed to Step 2.

### **Step 2: Contact AgeSpan Provider Relations**

**Call main office:**
- **Lawrence office:** 280 Merrimack Street, Suite 400, Lawrence, MA 01843
- **Phone:** 1-800-892-0890 or 978-683-7747
- **Email:** info@agespan.org
- **Ask for:** Provider Relations Department or Care Coordination Manager

**What to say:**
> "Hi, I'm [Your Name] from [Agency Name]. We're a licensed home care agency serving [your coverage area]. We're MassHealth enrolled and interested in becoming an AgeSpan provider to receive home care referrals. Who should I speak with about enrollment?"

### **Step 3: Complete AgeSpan Provider Application**

**Required documentation:**
- Proof of MassHealth provider enrollment (provider number)
- Home care agency license (MA Department of Public Health)
- Liability insurance certificate
- Service area coverage (which towns you serve)
- Languages spoken by caregivers
- Services offered (personal care, homemaking, respite, 24/7 care, etc.)
- References from other ASAPs or healthcare partners (if available)

**Timeline:** 2-4 weeks for AgeSpan to review and approve application

### **Step 4: Attend Provider Orientation (If Required)**

**What to expect:**
- AgeSpan may require new providers to attend orientation session
- Topics: Referral process, documentation requirements, billing procedures, case manager communication protocols
- Duration: 1-2 hours (typically virtual or at Lawrence/Danvers office)

### **Step 5: Set Up Referral Communication**

**Establish communication protocol:**
- **How referrals are received:** Phone call, fax, email, or online portal (ask AgeSpan for current process)
- **Response time expectation:** How quickly must agencies respond to referrals? (Typically 24-48 hours)
- **Point of contact:** Who at your agency receives referrals? (Provide cell phone, email for immediate response)

### **Step 6: Meet Case Management Team**

**Relationship building (critical for referral volume):**
- **Request to visit offices:** Ask to visit Lawrence and/or Danvers office to meet case managers in person
- **Introduce your agency:** Bring brochures, business cards, introduce key staff
- **Emphasize responsiveness:** "We respond to referrals within 2-4 hours and can start care within 24 hours"
- **Highlight specializations:** If you specialize in dementia care, stroke recovery, Parkinson's, multilingual caregivers—emphasize this

## Referral Volume & Expectations

**Expected monthly referrals from AgeSpan:** 40-80 referrals/month (among highest volumes for any Massachusetts ASAP)

### **Factors Affecting Your Referral Volume:**

**1. Geographic coverage:**
- **Full 28-town coverage:** 60-80 referrals/month (agencies serving entire AgeSpan area)
- **Lawrence area only:** 25-35 referrals/month (47% of AgeSpan services)
- **Lowell area only:** 20-30 referrals/month (40% of AgeSpan services)
- **Haverhill/North Shore only:** 8-15 referrals/month (13% of AgeSpan services)

**2. Languages spoken:**
- **Spanish-speaking caregivers:** ESSENTIAL for Lawrence area (77.4% Hispanic/Latino)
- **Khmer/Cambodian-speaking caregivers:** CRITICAL for Lowell area (large Cambodian community)
- **Russian-speaking caregivers:** Needed for Haverhill, Peabody areas
- **Agencies with multilingual staff receive 2-3x more referrals** than English-only agencies

**3. Service capacity:**
- **High-capacity agencies** (50+ caregivers, can accept 10+ new clients/month): 50-80 referrals/month
- **Medium agencies** (15-30 caregivers): 25-40 referrals/month
- **Small agencies** (5-10 caregivers): 10-20 referrals/month

**4. Responsiveness:**
- **< 2 hours response time:** Case managers call you FIRST (60-80 referrals/month)
- **< 24 hours response time:** Average referral volume (30-50 referrals/month)
- **> 48 hours response time:** Case managers move to other agencies (10-20 referrals/month)

**5. Competition:**
- AgeSpan works with **50-100+ home care providers** across 28-town service area
- High-performing agencies with strong case manager relationships receive disproportionate share
- **Strategy:** Build personal relationships with individual case managers (consistent communication, excellent outcomes)

### **Conversion Rate:**

**From referral to active client:** 70-85% conversion

**Why some referrals don't convert:**
- Family chooses different agency (15-20% of referrals)
- Client/family declines services after intake (5-10%)
- Agency can't meet specific needs (language, schedule, geographic area) (5-10%)

### **Timeline to First Referrals:**

- **Week 1-2:** Provider application submitted, under review
- **Week 3-4:** Application approved, added to provider list
- **Week 5-6:** First referrals received (typically 2-5 referrals in first month)
- **Month 2-3:** Referral volume increases as case managers learn your agency (10-20 referrals/month)
- **Month 4-6:** Steady-state volume achieved (20-40+ referrals/month based on factors above)

## Service Area Demographics

Understanding AgeSpan's service area demographics helps agencies prepare for specific client populations and care needs.

### **Lawrence Area (47% of AgeSpan Services)**

**Population:** ~80,000 (urban gateway city)

**Senior population:** Under 10% of population (but absolute numbers still significant: 7,000-8,000 seniors)

**Demographics:**
- **77.4% Hispanic/Latino** (largest percentage of any Massachusetts city)
- Primary language: **Spanish** (many seniors speak limited/no English)
- Median household income: Lower than state average ($45,000-$55,000)
- Working-class community with high service needs

**Home care implications:**
- **Spanish-speaking caregivers ESSENTIAL** (non-negotiable for Lawrence referrals)
- Cultural competency: Understanding Latino family structures, caregiving roles, Catholic faith traditions
- MassHealth eligibility high (lower incomes = more Frail Elder Waiver clients)
- Multi-generational households common (caregivers must navigate family dynamics)

### **Lowell Area (40% of AgeSpan Services)**

**Population:** ~115,000 (second-largest city in Massachusetts)

**Senior population:** 14,188 residents (12.36% of population)

**Adults 60+:** 15% of population (~17,250 residents)

**Demographics:**
- **Diverse:** Large Cambodian/Southeast Asian community (second-largest in US after Long Beach, CA)
- **Languages:** Khmer (Cambodian), Spanish, Portuguese, English
- Median household income: $66,000 (working-class to middle-class)
- Mix of urban and suburban neighborhoods

**Home care implications:**
- **Khmer-speaking caregivers CRITICAL** for Cambodian community (large percentage of AgeSpan's Southeast Asian clients)
- Cultural competency: Understanding Buddhist traditions, Cambodian family structures, trauma-informed care (many Cambodian elders are Khmer Rouge genocide survivors)
- Diverse referral mix: Cambodian, Hispanic/Latino, Portuguese, White American families
- Community organizations: AgeSpan partners with Cambodian Mutual Assistance Association (CMAA), Lowell Community Health Center

### **Haverhill/North Shore Area (13% of AgeSpan Services)**

**Population:** Haverhill ~67,000 + North Shore towns (Salem ~44,000, Peabody ~54,000, others)

**Senior population:** 13-15% of population

**Demographics:**
- Mix of urban (Haverhill, Salem) and suburban towns (Andover, North Andover, Boxford, Newburyport)
- **Languages:** English (primary), Spanish, Russian, Portuguese
- Economic mix: Working-class urban cores + affluent suburban towns
- Median household income: $60,000-$90,000 (varies widely by town)

**Home care implications:**
- **Geographic spread:** Requires agencies with broad coverage (can't serve North Shore without covering 30+ mile radius)
- Economic diversity: Mix of MassHealth (urban) and private pay (suburban) clients
- Russian-speaking caregivers needed for Haverhill, Peabody Russian communities
- Affluent towns (Andover, Boxford, Newburyport) = private pay opportunities

## Unique Characteristics of AgeSpan

**What makes AgeSpan different from other Massachusetts ASAPs:**

### **1. Largest Service Area Post-Merger**

**AgeSpan created through merger** of Elder Services of the Merrimack Valley and North Shore Elder Services, creating one of Massachusetts' largest ASAPs:
- **28 communities served** (only surpassed by Senior Connection which serves 30)
- **Three major urban centers:** Lawrence, Lowell, Haverhill (most ASAPs serve one or zero urban cores)
- **Massive geographic reach:** ~45-mile north-south corridor

### **2. Triple Accreditation/Recognition**

**National & State Recognition:**
1. **NCQA 3-Year Accreditation** for case management excellence (only a handful of Massachusetts ASAPs hold this prestigious accreditation)
2. **Candid Platinum Seal** for organizational transparency and financial accountability
3. **Dual Designation:** Both ASAP (state) and Area Agency on Aging/AAA (federal) providing broader funding access

### **3. Exceptional Service Volume**

**Statistics demonstrating scale:**
- **40,000+ seniors/adults with disabilities served annually** (among top 3-5 ASAPs statewide)
- **5,000+ receiving in-home services** (massive home care program)
- **225,000+ meals delivered annually** (~18,750/month)
- **Three Pillars:** Independence, Social Wellness, Equity & Community

### **4. Multilingual Expertise Required**

**AgeSpan serves most diverse ASAP population in Massachusetts:**
- **Spanish:** Essential for Lawrence (77.4% Hispanic/Latino)
- **Khmer (Cambodian):** Critical for Lowell (second-largest Cambodian community in US)
- **Russian:** Needed for Haverhill, Peabody communities
- **Portuguese:** Lowell, North Shore communities

**AgeSpan employs bilingual case managers** (Russian, Spanish, Khmer) and offers translation services—agencies should match this multilingual capacity.

### **5. Partnership Model with SeniorCare Inc.**

**Beverly area coverage:** AgeSpan partners with SeniorCare Inc. to extend services to Beverly and additional North Shore communities, creating collaborative regional network rather than competitive silos.

### **6. Gateway Cities Focus**

**Urban expertise:** AgeSpan's service area includes **three gateway cities** (Lawrence, Lowell, Haverhill)—Massachusetts' designation for mid-sized urban centers with diverse, working-class populations. This creates unique service delivery challenges:
- High diversity requiring multilingual services
- Lower incomes = high MassHealth eligibility
- Multi-generational households with complex family dynamics
- Transportation barriers (many seniors without cars)

### **7. One Care Program Participation**

**Integrated care for dual eligibles:** AgeSpan participates in MassHealth's One Care program (integrated Medicare/MassHealth for individuals under 65 with disabilities), creating additional referral pathways for younger adults with disabilities needing home care.

### **8. Recent Rebranding (2023-2024)**

**Modern identity:** Recent rebrand to "AgeSpan" (from Elder Services of the Merrimack Valley) reflects forward-thinking, person-centered approach. Name change signals organizational evolution while maintaining 50+ year legacy.

## Best Practices for Success with AgeSpan

**Strategies to maximize referral volume and build strong AgeSpan partnership:**

### **1. Prioritize Multilingual Staffing**

**Non-negotiable for high referral volume:**
- **Hire Spanish-speaking caregivers** (or partner with Spanish-speaking agencies for Lawrence referrals)
- **Hire Khmer/Cambodian-speaking caregivers** (or partner for Lowell referrals)
- **Train all staff on cultural competency** (Latino, Cambodian, Russian, Portuguese communities)

**If you don't have multilingual staff yet:** Start by serving English-speaking referrals in suburban towns (Andover, Chelmsford, Westford, Peabody) while building multilingual capacity.

### **2. Respond to Referrals Within 2-4 Hours**

**Speed wins referrals:**
- AgeSpan case managers call **3-5 agencies per referral**
- **First agency to respond and accept client wins** ~70% of the time
- Provide case managers with **direct cell phone** for intake coordinator (not main office number that goes to voicemail)

### **3. Build Relationships with Individual Case Managers**

**Personalize outreach:**
- **Visit AgeSpan offices monthly:** Lawrence and Danvers locations
- **Ask case managers:** "What challenges are you facing with home care referrals?" (Listen for pain points your agency can solve)
- **Provide case updates:** After starting care, call case manager to report: "Mrs. Garcia is doing great—thank you for the referral"
- **Invite case managers to your office:** Host "Agency Open House" for case managers to meet caregivers, see operations

### **4. Offer Flexible Geographic Coverage**

**Geographic flexibility increases referrals:**
- If you serve Lawrence area, consider expanding to Lowell (40% of AgeSpan services)
- If you serve Lowell, consider Haverhill/North Shore (many families have relatives across region)
- **Full 28-town coverage = maximum referral volume** (60-80 referrals/month)

### **5. Specialize in High-Need Populations**

**Differentiate your agency:**
- **Post-stroke care** (AgeSpan sees many stroke survivors needing home care)
- **Dementia/Alzheimer's care** (growing need as population ages)
- **Complex medical needs** (diabetes management, COPD, CHF)
- **Cambodian community expertise** (if you have Khmer-speaking staff, emphasize this—high demand, low supply)

### **6. Accept MassHealth Rates Without Complaint**

**Financial reality:**
- Most AgeSpan referrals are **MassHealth-funded** (Frail Elder Waiver, state-funded home care)
- MassHealth rates are lower than private pay (~$25-30/hour vs. $35-45/hour private pay)
- **Agencies that accept MassHealth gracefully receive more referrals** than agencies that complain about rates

### **7. Communicate Proactively with Case Managers**

**Build trust through communication:**
- **Report concerns immediately:** If client's condition changes, family issues arise, caregiver challenges occur—call case manager right away
- **Ask for support:** Case managers have resources (adult day health, meal programs, assistive equipment) that can help your clients—partner, don't work in isolation
- **Share successes:** When client thriving, tell case manager (reinforces quality of care)

### **8. Join AgeSpan Provider Meetings (If Offered)**

**Participate in provider network:**
- Some ASAPs host quarterly provider meetings (training, updates, networking)
- **Attend if offered:** Builds visibility, demonstrates commitment, creates networking opportunities with other providers

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q1: Do I need to serve all 28 AgeSpan communities to receive referrals?**

**A:** No. AgeSpan refers based on **your stated service area**. If you serve only Lawrence and surrounding towns, you'll receive Lawrence-area referrals. If you serve the full 28 communities, you'll receive referrals from across the region. However, **broader coverage = higher referral volume** (agencies serving 20+ towns receive 3-4x more referrals than agencies serving 5 towns).

**Q2: What languages do I need to serve AgeSpan referrals?**

**A:** **Minimum:** English (for Haverhill, North Shore suburban towns). **Highly recommended:** Spanish (Lawrence area, 47% of services), Khmer/Cambodian (Lowell area, 40% of services). **Helpful:** Russian (Haverhill, Peabody), Portuguese (Lowell, North Shore). **Reality:** Agencies with Spanish + Khmer caregivers receive 70-80% of AgeSpan referrals (vast majority of clients speak these languages).

**Q3: How long does provider enrollment take?**

**A:** **2-6 weeks** from application to first referral, assuming MassHealth enrollment already complete. Breakdown: 1-2 weeks for application review, 1-2 weeks for orientation/setup, 1-2 weeks for case managers to learn about your agency and start referring.

**Q4: What's the average client duration for AgeSpan referrals?**

**A:** **MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver clients:** 12-24+ months average (long-term care relationships). **State-funded home care:** 6-18 months average. **Respite/short-term:** 1-3 months. **Overall:** AgeSpan referrals tend to be **long-term relationships** (clients with chronic conditions requiring ongoing support), creating stable revenue for agencies.

**Q5: Do I need special software or technology to receive AgeSpan referrals?**

**A:** No specialized software required. AgeSpan sends referrals via **phone, fax, or email** (varies by case manager preference). However, having **electronic health records (EHR)** or **home care management software** (ClearCare, WellSky, etc.) helps manage documentation, scheduling, and communication with case managers. Ask AgeSpan if they use any provider portals during enrollment.

**Q6: Can I receive referrals from AgeSpan AND other ASAPs simultaneously?**

**A:** **YES.** Most Massachusetts home care agencies work with **multiple ASAPs** simultaneously. If you serve communities covered by AgeSpan AND neighboring ASAPs (e.g., Mystic Valley Elder Services, Minuteman Senior Services, Senior Connection), enroll with all of them. Each ASAP operates independently—one referral source doesn't exclude others.
