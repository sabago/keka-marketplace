## Overview

Elder Services of Greater Boston (operated by Ethos) is the **largest Aging Services Access Point (ASAP) in Massachusetts**, serving 300,000+ seniors across 31 cities and towns in Greater Boston. As the gateway to **MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver** and other state-funded programs, Ethos is the **#1 most important referral partnership** for home care agencies serving Boston and surrounding communities.

**Service Area (31 cities/towns):**
- **Boston neighborhoods:** All (Allston, Brighton, Back Bay, Beacon Hill, Charlestown, Chinatown, Dorchester, East Boston, Fenway, Hyde Park, Jamaica Plain, Mattapan, Mission Hill, North End, Roslindale, Roxbury, South Boston, South End, West End, West Roxbury)
- **Inner suburbs:** Brookline, Chelsea, Revere, Winthrop
- **Metro North:** Everett, Malden, Medford, Melrose
- **Metro West suburbs:** Belmont, Watertown, Waltham, Newton, Needham, Dedham, Wellesley, Natick, Wayland, Weston, Lincoln

**Programs managed:**
- MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver (largest volume in MA)
- Community Choices program
- Enhanced Community Options Program (ECOP)
- Family Caregiver Support Program
- Senior Care Options (SCO) coordination
- Information & referral services

**The numbers:**
- **300,000+ seniors** in service area (largest ASAP population)
- **3,000+ MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver participants** active at any time
- **$50+ million** in state funding managed annually
- **Guaranteed state payment** for Waiver participants (no private pay billing uncertainty)

**For home care agencies:**
- **THE gateway to MassHealth funding:** 60-70% of Ethos waiver participants need home care agencies (NOT just Ethos staff)
- **Volume:** 50-200+ referrals/month available (if enrolled as Ethos provider)

- **Long-term clients:** Waiver participants stay enrolled for years (stable revenue)
- **100% FREE to enroll** as Ethos provider (no fees, just credentialing)

## MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver Through Ethos

**What is the Waiver:**
- Medicaid program helping frail elders stay home (vs. nursing facility)
- **Eligibility:** Age 60+, nursing-home eligible (need help with 2+ ADLs), MassHealth-eligible (income <$1,732/month, assets <$2,000)
- **Services covered:** Personal care, homemaking, home-delivered meals, adult day health, care coordination

**How it works:**
1. **Senior/family contacts Ethos:** "My mother needs help at home, she has MassHealth..."
2. **Ethos assesses eligibility:** Nurse assessment, financial screening, care planning
3. **Ethos enrolls in Waiver:** Senior approved for services
4. **Ethos develops care plan:** "Mrs. Smith needs 15 hours/week personal care + homemaker services"
5. **Ethos refers to home care agency:** "We're authorizing [Agency Name] to provide care"
6. **Agency provides services:** Caregiver visits per care plan
7. **Agency bills MassHealth:** Monthly billing, MassHealth pays agency directly
8. **Ethos monitors quality:** Care coordinator checks in monthly, tracks outcomes

**Your agency's role:**
- Provide personal care, homemaker services per Ethos care plan
- Submit monthly billing to MassHealth (not to Ethos)
- Communicate with Ethos care coordinator (any concerns, changes in client status)
- **MassHealth pays you directly** (Ethos authorizes, MassHealth pays)

**Understanding where referrals come from:**

### **Boston (60%+ of Ethos volume)**

**Highest-need Boston neighborhoods:**
- **Dorchester:** Largest Boston neighborhood, diverse (Haitian, Vietnamese, Cape Verdean, Irish), high elderly population
- **Roxbury, Mattapan:** Lower-income, African American, Caribbean communities
- **East Boston:** Hispanic (primarily Central American), Italian
- **Hyde Park, Roslindale, West Roxbury:** Working-class, mix of longtime residents + new immigrants
- **Chinatown, Allston/Brighton:** Asian, Chinese-speaking, Brazilian/Russian populations

**Language needs (Boston):**
- Spanish, Haitian Creole, Cape Verdean Creole, Vietnamese, Chinese (Cantonese/Mandarin), Portuguese, Russian

### **Inner Suburbs (20% of volume)**

- **Chelsea, Revere, Everett:** Working-class, Hispanic, Southeast Asian populations
- **Malden, Medford:** Diverse, Chinese, Brazilian communities
- **Brookline:** Affluent, diverse (Russian, Chinese, Middle Eastern)

### **Metro West Suburbs (20% of volume)**

- **Watertown, Belmont, Waltham:** Middle-class suburbs, Armenian, Brazilian, Russian populations
- **Newton, Needham, Wellesley:** Affluent suburbs (higher private pay mix, but MassHealth participants exist)
- **Natick, Wayland, Weston:** Suburban, less diverse

**Agency strategy:**
- **Urban focus:** If you specialize in Boston urban neighborhoods (Dorchester, Roxbury, East Boston), massive Ethos volume
- **Suburban focus:** If you serve affluent suburbs (Newton, Wellesley), lower Ethos volume but exists
- **Multilingual essential:** Spanish, Haitian Creole, Portuguese, Vietnamese, Chinese for urban Ethos clients

## How to Become Ethos Provider

### **Step 1: Confirm Agency Eligibility**

**Ethos requires:**
- **Home care agency license** (if required by MA - personal care homemaker agencies may not need license)
- **MassHealth provider enrollment:** MUST be enrolled as MassHealth provider (PCA/homemaker provider type)
- **Liability insurance:** $1-2 million coverage
- **Workers' compensation insurance**
- **Background checks** for all caregivers (CORI, SORI, national criminal checks)
- **Caregiver training protocols**

**If not enrolled with MassHealth yet:**
1. Complete MassHealth Home Health Agency Provider Enrollment Application
2. Allow 60-90 days for MassHealth approval
3. THEN apply to Ethos (Ethos requires MassHealth enrollment first)

### **Step 2: Contact Ethos Provider Relations**

**Ethos Provider Relations Department:**
- **Phone:** 617-522-7777
- **Ask for:** "Provider Relations" or "Agency Contracting"
- **Express interest:** "We're a home care agency serving [service area], we'd like to become an Ethos provider"

**Provider Relations will:**
- Send provider application packet
- Explain credentialing process
- Schedule orientation (if needed)

### **Step 3: Complete Provider Application**

**Application includes:**
- Agency information (license, tax ID, address, contact)
- Insurance certificates (liability, workers' comp)
- MassHealth provider number (MUST have this)
- Service area (which Ethos cities/towns you serve)
- Languages available (caregivers' language capabilities)
- Specializations (dementia care, 24/7 care, cultural specialties)
- References (other agencies, clients, partners)

**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks for Ethos to process application

### **Step 4: Sign Provider Agreement**

**Contract terms:**
- **Payment rates:** MassHealth rates (set by state, not negotiable)
- **Service requirements:** Response time (accept/decline referrals within 48 hours), communication protocols
- **Quality standards:** Care plan adherence, documentation, incident reporting
- **Term:** Ongoing (no set end date, terminable by either party with notice)

### **Step 5: Receive Referrals**

**Ongoing process:**
- **Ethos care coordinator calls/emails:** "I have a waiver participant needing care: Mrs. Jones, lives in Dorchester, needs 12 hours/week personal care + homemaker"
- **You accept or decline** (within 48 hours)
- **If accept:** Complete your own assessment, start care per Ethos care plan
- **Bill MassHealth monthly:** Submit claims to MassHealth (not Ethos)
- **Communicate with Ethos:** Update care coordinator monthly (or when issues arise)

- **Large agency** (serve all 31 towns, multilingual, 24/7 capability): 40-100+ referrals/month

**Conversion rate:** 70-80% (warm referrals from Ethos, family expects care)

## Payment & Billing

**How MassHealth payment works:**

**Rates (2025):**
- **Personal Care:** ~$25-30/hour (MassHealth rate, set by state)
- **Homemaker:** ~$22-27/hour
- **Live-in care:** ~$180-250/day

**Billing process:**
1. Provide care per Ethos authorization
2. **Track hours** (daily service logs)
3. **Submit monthly claim to MassHealth** (via MassHealth billing portal)
4. **MassHealth pays directly** (net 30-45 days)

**Important:**
- **Bill MassHealth, not Ethos** (Ethos authorizes care, MassHealth pays)
- **Cannot bill client** (waiver participants pay $0 out-of-pocket)
- **Cannot exceed authorized hours** (if Ethos authorizes 15 hours/week, cannot bill for 20 hours without additional authorization)

**Payment reliability:**
- **Excellent:** MassHealth is state-funded (reliable payment)
- Payment delays rare (net 30-45 days typical)

## Best Practices for Ethos Partnership

**1. Serve broad geographic area:**
- Ethos covers 31 cities/towns
- **Agencies serving 20-30 towns get more referrals** than agencies serving only 5 towns
- Expand coverage to maximize referrals

**2. Multilingual capability ESSENTIAL:**
- Boston Ethos clients speak 50+ languages
- **Priority languages:** Spanish, Haitian Creole, Portuguese, Vietnamese, Chinese, Cape Verdean Creole
- Hire caregivers from immigrant communities (cultural + language match)

**3. Communicate proactively with care coordinators:**
- Update care coordinator monthly: "Mrs. Smith is doing well, no concerns"
- **Immediate notification:** Falls, hospitalizations, family concerns, caregiver issues
- Ethos values agencies that communicate (good communicators get more referrals)

**4. Accept challenging cases:**
- Dementia with behaviors, hoarding, family conflict, unsafe housing
- **Agencies that say "yes" = valued** (care coordinators remember who helps vs. who declines)
- If you must decline, explain why (capacity, safety concerns) and suggest alternative agency

**5. Respond quickly to referrals:**
- **Accept/decline within 24 hours** (Ethos gives 48 hours, but faster = better)
- Start care within 3-5 days of acceptance
- Care coordinators remember responsive agencies

**6. Build relationships with care coordinators:**
- Care coordinators decide which agency gets which client
- **Visit Ethos office:** Introduce yourself to care coordination team
- Attend Ethos provider meetings (quarterly or as offered)
- Stay visible

**7. Quality over volume:**
- Don't accept more clients than you can serve well
- **Ethos tracks:** Client satisfaction, complaints, incidents, readmissions
- Poor performance = fewer referrals (or termination from provider network)

**8. Understand care coordinator challenges:**
- Care coordinators manage 50-80 waiver participants each (overwhelmed)
- **Make their job easier:** Don't bring problems without solutions, be reliable, communicate clearly
- Agencies that reduce coordinator stress = preferred partners

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "MassHealth rates too low"**
**Solution:** True - MassHealth rates ($25-30/hour) lower than private pay ($30-40/hour). BUT: (1) Volume compensates (100+ Ethos clients = $2M+ revenue), (2) Payment reliability (no collection issues), (3) Client longevity (years vs. months), (4) Mix Ethos clients with higher-paying private clients.

**Challenge 2: "Ethos clients in unsafe neighborhoods"**
**Solution:** Some Boston neighborhoods (Dorchester, Roxbury, Mattapan) have safety concerns. Implement: (1) Caregiver safety training, (2) Buddy systems for high-risk areas, (3) Daytime-only visits if needed, (4) Cell phones + emergency protocols, (5) Partner with caregivers FROM those neighborhoods (local knowledge).

**Challenge 3: "Don't have multilingual caregivers"**
**Solution:** CRITICAL to hire Spanish, Haitian Creole, Portuguese, Vietnamese, Chinese speakers for Ethos success. Recruit: (1) Immigrant community centers, (2) Cultural churches/organizations, (3) Language-specific job boards, (4) Refugee resettlement agencies, (5) Community colleges ESL programs. Without multilingual staff, Ethos volume will be limited.

**Challenge 4: "MassHealth billing complicated"**
**Solution:** (1) Take MassHealth provider training (free courses available), (2) Hire billing specialist with MassHealth experience, (3) Use billing software with MassHealth integration (Wellsky, Homecare Homebase, etc.), (4) Join provider support groups (other agencies share billing tips), (5) Contact MassHealth provider relations (free support).

**Challenge 5: "Care coordinators don't respond to calls"**
**Solution:** Care coordinators manage 50-80 clients (overwhelmed). (1) Email preferred over phone (they can respond when available), (2) Be patient (may take 24-48 hours), (3) For urgent issues, call Ethos main line (617-522-7777) and ask for supervisor, (4) Don't bombard with non-urgent calls (save communications for important matters).
