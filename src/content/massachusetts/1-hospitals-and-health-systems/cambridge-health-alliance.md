## Overview

Cambridge Health Alliance (CHA) is a **community-focused health system** serving Cambridge, Somerville, and Everett with **3 hospitals** and extensive community health centers. As a **safety net hospital system**, CHA serves diverse, multilingual populations and is a critical referral source for agencies serving Metro Boston's urban communities.

**System Facilities:**
1. **Cambridge Hospital** - 165 beds
2. **Somerville Hospital** - 162 beds
3. **Everett Hospital** - 98 beds
4. **20+ primary care and specialty clinics** across Cambridge, Somerville, Everett

**Patient population:** 65+ languages spoken, diverse immigrant communities, significant MassHealth population

---

## Why CHA Matters

**Community mission:**
- Safety net hospital (serves regardless of ability to pay)
- **50%+ MassHealth patients** (higher than most MA hospitals)
- Diverse populations: Brazilian, Haitian, Somali, Chinese, Central American, Russian, Bengali
- Community health center model (primary care + hospital care integrated)

**For home care agencies:**
- **Multilingual capability essential:** CHA patients speak 65+ languages
- **Cultural competency critical:** Immigrant families, multi-generational households, diverse care preferences
- **MassHealth focus:** Most CHA-referred clients are MassHealth (not private pay)
- **Community relationships:** CHA values agencies that understand local communities
- **Less competition for culturally competent agencies:** If you have Portuguese/Haitian Creole/Spanish/Somali speakers, competitive advantage

**Why CHA ACO matters:**
- Community health center model = integrated care coordination
- Strong focus on social determinants of health (housing, food security, transportation)
- Home care as strategy to keep patients in community (vs. nursing home)

**How to partner:**
1. Contact CHA network development: 617-665-1000
2. Emphasize cultural competency + language skills
3. Demonstrate understanding of immigrant communities
4. Share quality metrics (outcomes for diverse populations)

## How to Get Started

### **Step 1: Assess Your Cultural Competency**

**Reality check:**
- CHA patients speak 65+ languages, represent 100+ countries
- If your agency doesn't have multilingual caregivers + cultural training, CHA may not be right fit
- **Essential languages:** Portuguese (Brazilian), Spanish, Haitian Creole
- **Helpful languages:** Somali, Chinese (Mandarin/Cantonese), Russian, Bengali, Arabic

**Decision:** Only pursue CHA if you can authentically serve diverse immigrant populations.

### **Step 2: Visit CHA Hospitals**

**Start with hospital closest to your service area:**
- Serve Cambridge → Cambridge Hospital
- Serve Somerville → Somerville Hospital
- Serve Everett/Chelsea/Revere → Everett Hospital

**Meeting process:**
1. Call hospital main number, ask for discharge planning
2. Request meeting with discharge planning manager
3. **Emphasize:**
   - Languages your caregivers speak
   - Cultural competency training
   - Experience serving immigrant communities
   - MassHealth enrollment (50%+ CHA patients are MassHealth)

### **Step 3: Partner with Local Community Organizations**

**CHA values agencies connected to local community:**
- Cambridge Community Development Department
- Somerville Council on Aging
- Everett Council on Aging
- Local immigrant-serving nonprofits (Brazilian Worker Center, Somali Development Center, etc.)

**Building community trust = CHA referrals.**

### **Step 4: Build Relationships with CHA Primary Care Clinics**

**CHA has 20+ primary care clinics** (separate from hospitals):
- Primary care physicians refer to home care (not just hospital discharge planners)
- Visit clinics serving seniors, introduce agency
- Leave materials in multiple languages

## Cost and ROI

**Cost:** 100% FREE

**ROI:** Moderate to High
- **Lead quality:** Good (immediate need, professionally assessed)
- **Conversion rate:** 60-75% (if you have right language/cultural capabilities)
- **Volume:** Moderate (10-25 referrals/month from CHA system if culturally competent)
- **Payment mix:** 50%+ MassHealth (lower reimbursement but reliable), 30% Medicare, 20% private pay/commercial

**Reality:**
- CHA is NOT high-margin referral source (MassHealth reimbursement lower than private pay)
- CHA IS high-mission referral source (serving vulnerable immigrant elders)
- Best fit for agencies with mission-driven values + cultural competency

## Best Practices

**1. Hire caregivers from CHA's communities:**
- Brazilian caregivers for Brazilian clients
- Haitian caregivers for Haitian clients
- Cultural + language match = better outcomes

**2. Understand immigrant family dynamics:**
- Multi-generational households (elder lives with adult children)
- Family decision-making (not just elder deciding)
- Cultural care preferences (food, hygiene, religious practices)

**3. Be flexible with MassHealth bureaucracy:**
- Many CHA patients have complex MassHealth eligibility (immigration status, renewals)
- Work with families to navigate MassHealth enrollment/authorization

**4. Build relationships with community health workers:**
- CHA employs community health workers (CHWs) who coordinate social services
- CHWs refer to home care agencies they trust

**5. Demonstrate commitment to health equity:**
- CHA values partners who share mission of serving vulnerable populations
- Don't cherry-pick wealthier Cambridge neighborhoods vs. lower-income Everett

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "MassHealth reimbursement too low"**
**Solution:** CHA is mission-driven partnership. If focused purely on profitability, CHA may not be right fit. Mix CHA (MassHealth) clients with private pay from other referral sources.

**Challenge 2: "Don't have Portuguese/Haitian Creole speakers"**
**Solution:** Recruit multilingual caregivers from local communities. Partner with immigrant employment agencies, Brazilian churches, Haitian community centers.

**Challenge 3: "Cultural differences causing care plan challenges"**
**Solution:** Cultural competency training for all staff. Hire care coordinators from immigrant communities who understand cultural dynamics.

**Challenge 4: "Competition from larger Boston agencies"**
**Solution:** Emphasize local presence + cultural competency. "We're based in Cambridge, our caregivers live in your neighborhood, we understand your community."

## Success Metrics

**Track CHA performance:**
- Referrals from CHA per month (target: 10-20)
- Conversion rate (target: 65%+)
- Client retention (CHA clients often long-term if culturally matched)
- Languages served (track: how many Portuguese/Spanish/Haitian Creole clients do you successfully serve?)
