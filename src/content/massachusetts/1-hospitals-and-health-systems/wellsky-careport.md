## Overview

WellSky CarePort (formerly CarePort Health before acquisition by WellSky, which was later acquired by Allscripts) is the leading care coordination network connecting acute and post-acute care providers. With over **2,000 hospitals** and **130,000 post-acute providers** on the platform, CarePort facilitates approximately **13 million annual hospital discharges** across the United States.

For Massachusetts home care agencies, CarePort is particularly significant because many major hospital systems nationwide—and potentially in Massachusetts—use the platform for discharge planning. The system is deeply integrated with hospital EHRs, giving discharge planners real-time visibility into provider capacity, quality metrics, and patient outcomes.

**Key differentiator:** CarePort's EHR integration means it's not just a referral marketplace—it's a care coordination tool that bridges acute and post-acute data, enabling seamless transitions and outcome tracking.

## Who Sends Referrals Through This Source

Referrals flow through CarePort from:

- **Hospital discharge planners and case managers** using CarePort embedded in their EHR workflow
- **Utilization review teams** coordinating appropriate post-acute placements
- **Care coordinators** within Accountable Care Organizations (ACOs) and health systems managing population health
- **Emergency department social workers** arranging follow-up care for discharged patients

CarePort is used by major academic medical centers, community hospitals, and health systems across the country. While specific Massachusetts hospital adoption varies, the platform's market-leading position means MA agencies may encounter it when partnering with regional and national hospital networks.

## Who Can Receive Referrals

CarePort supports all post-acute provider types:

- **Medicare-certified home health agencies** (skilled nursing, therapy, medical social work)
- **Private-pay home care agencies** (non-medical, companion care, personal care, homemaker services)
- **Hospice providers**
- **Skilled nursing facilities (SNFs)**
- **Long-term acute care hospitals (LTACHs)**
- **Inpatient/outpatient rehabilitation facilities**
- **Durable Medical Equipment (DME) suppliers**

**For private-pay home care agencies:** CarePort allows agencies to specify non-medical service offerings. Profiles can highlight companion care, personal care assistance, homemaker services, medication reminders, transportation, and other supportive services. This ensures discharge planners can find appropriate providers for patients not requiring skilled medical care.

## How It Works (From an Agency's Perspective)

Here's the typical CarePort workflow for a home care agency:

1. **Network enrollment:** Agency applies to join CarePort's provider network (process described below)

2. **Profile setup:** Agency creates comprehensive profile including:
   - Service types and specializations
   - Geographic coverage areas (cities, counties, ZIP codes)
   - Accepted insurance/payers (private pay, Medicare Advantage, MassHealth, commercial)
   - Real-time bed/capacity status
   - Quality metrics and outcome data (readmission rates, patient satisfaction)
   - Staff credentials and certifications
   - Languages spoken
   - Cultural competencies

3. **EHR integration:** CarePort embeds into hospital EHR systems (Epic, Cerner, Meditech, etc.), so discharge planners see provider options directly in their workflow

4. **Referral matching:** When a discharge planner searches for home care providers, CarePort uses algorithms to match based on:
   - Patient location (proximity to agency service area)
   - Care needs (ADL support, dementia care, post-surgical care, etc.)
   - Insurance/payer (CarePort filters providers by accepted insurance)
   - Availability (real-time capacity indicators)
   - Quality scores (outcomes, ratings, certifications)

5. **Referral notification:** Selected agencies receive referral via:
   - CarePort portal notifications
   - Email alerts
   - Fax (for agencies preferring traditional methods)
   - API webhooks (for integrated systems)

6. **Review and accept:** Agency reviews referral details in CarePort portal:
   - Patient demographics and clinical summary
   - Discharge diagnosis and care plan
   - Insurance authorization status
   - Preferred start date
   - Family contact information

7. **Coordinate care:** Agency and hospital use CarePort's communication tools to:
   - Confirm acceptance and start date
   - Share care plans and clinical documentation
   - Track transition progress
   - Report outcomes (reducing information silos)

8. **Outcome tracking:** CarePort tracks post-discharge outcomes including:
   - Hospital readmissions
   - ER visits
   - Patient satisfaction
   - Care plan adherence
   This data feeds back into provider quality scores, influencing future referrals

**Unique advantage:** CarePort's bi-directional data flow means agencies can send updates back to hospitals, demonstrating value and strengthening partnerships.

## How to Get Listed / Enrolled

Enrolling in WellSky CarePort requires a formal application process:

**Step 1: Contact WellSky CarePort Provider Relations**
- Visit https://careporthealth.com
- Look for "Become a Provider" or "Join Our Network" section
- Call or email provider relations team
- Note: CarePort targets established providers with proven track records

**Step 2: Submit application and documentation**

Required materials typically include:

- **Business information:**
  - Agency name, address, and contact information
  - Tax ID (EIN) and NPI number (if applicable)
  - State license number (for states requiring home care licensure)
  - Years in operation and ownership structure

- **Insurance and compliance:**
  - Certificate of insurance (general liability, professional liability, workers' comp)
  - Business Associate Agreement (BAA) for HIPAA compliance
  - Accreditation certificates (Joint Commission, ACHC, CHAP, HQAA)

- **Quality metrics and references:**
  - Patient satisfaction scores
  - Outcome data (if available)
  - Hospital references (discharge planners or case managers who can vouch for your agency)
  - Complaints/deficiencies history

**Step 3: Credentialing review**
- CarePort performs credentialing verification
- Background checks on agency and key personnel
- License and insurance verification
- Quality review and risk assessment

**Step 4: Contract negotiation**
- CarePort presents network participation agreement
- Review terms including data sharing, quality reporting requirements, and fees
- Negotiate if necessary (larger agencies may have more leverage)

**Step 5: Onboarding and training**
- Complete CarePort platform training (virtual or in-person)
- Set up portal access for intake coordinators and business development staff
- Configure profile with service offerings and capacity management
- Test referral workflow before going live

**Step 6: Go live**
- Begin receiving referrals from hospitals using CarePort
- Monitor performance metrics and adjust strategy

**Massachusetts-specific considerations:**

- **No state licensure required:** MA does not currently license home care agencies (only Medicare-certified home health), so there's no DPH license to provide—but you'll need to demonstrate operational legitimacy through other means (insurance, accreditation, references)
- **MassHealth provider enrollment:** If serving MassHealth patients, mention your provider enrollment status
- **Local hospital references:** Having references from MA discharge planners (Mass General Brigham, Boston Medical Center, etc.) strengthens your application

**Timeline:** Expect 4-8 weeks from application to go-live.

## Cost and Lead Quality

**Cost Structure:**

CarePort operates on an **enterprise subscription model**:

- **No per-lead fees:** Unlike some consumer lead platforms, CarePort does not charge per referral
- **Annual/monthly subscription:** Providers pay a recurring fee to access the network
- **Pricing tiers:** Cost varies based on:
  - Agency size (number of locations, patient volume)
  - Service types (home health vs. home care vs. multi-service)
  - Geographic coverage
  - Desired features (basic listings vs. advanced analytics)

- **Estimated range:** Small to mid-sized home care agencies may pay $5,000-$20,000+ annually (pricing is not publicly disclosed and varies by negotiation)

- **Value proposition:** For agencies receiving 10+ referrals/month, the ROI often justifies the subscription cost compared to pay-per-lead models

**Lead Quality:**

CarePort referrals are **exceptionally high quality** because:

- **Clinically appropriate:** Discharge planners use clinical data to match patients with right level of care
- **Insurance-verified:** Payer information is often pre-verified within hospital systems
- **Urgent need:** Patients are actively being discharged—timeline is immediate (hours to days)
- **Warm handoff:** Direct coordination between hospital and agency reduces friction
- **Outcome accountability:** CarePort tracks post-discharge results, creating incentive for good matches

**Typical referral characteristics:**

- **Acuity:** Mix of post-acute (recent surgery, hospitalization) and chronic disease management
- **Payer mix:** Medicare (traditional and Advantage), commercial insurance, private pay; some Medicaid
- **Volume:** Varies by hospital volume and agency performance—top agencies can receive 20-50+ referrals/month from high-volume hospitals
- **Conversion rate:** High (70-90%) because referrals are pre-qualified and patients have immediate need

**ROI considerations:**

- **High upfront cost** but no per-lead fees
- Best for agencies with capacity to handle volume and ability to respond quickly
- Smaller agencies may struggle to justify cost unless participating in high-volume hospital networks

## Pros and Cons for Massachusetts Home Care Agencies

**Pros:**

- **Massive network reach**—access to 2,000+ hospitals and 13M annual discharges
- **High-quality, pre-qualified referrals** from hospital discharge teams
- **EHR integration** creates seamless workflow for discharge planners (increases agency visibility)
- **No per-referral fees**—predictable subscription cost
- **Outcome tracking and quality metrics** help agencies demonstrate value
- **Bi-directional data sharing** enables proactive communication with hospitals
- **Market-leading platform**—used by major health systems nationwide
- **Strengthens hospital relationships** through data transparency and care coordination

**Cons:**

- **High cost**—annual subscription can be $10,000-$30,000+ depending on agency size
- **Enterprise-focused**—may not be cost-effective for small agencies with limited capacity
- **Credentialing requirements**—stringent quality and documentation standards
- **Variable MA hospital adoption**—not all MA hospitals use CarePort (some use Aidin, internal systems, or competing platforms)
- **Outcome pressure**—agencies are held accountable for readmissions and quality metrics
- **Requires dedicated resources**—staff must actively manage portal, respond quickly, and report outcomes
- **Contractual obligations**—network participation agreements may include exclusivity clauses or minimum performance standards

## How This Fits Mastering Home Care's Data Flow

CarePort is a **sophisticated, API-driven platform**, making it an excellent candidate for integration with Mastering Home Care.

**Typical Ingest Path:**

- **Best case (API integration):**
  - CarePort offers robust API for enterprise customers
  - Mastering Home Care connects via API to pull referral data in real-time
  - When CarePort assigns a referral, API webhook triggers intake creation in Mastering Home Care
  - Referral data (patient demographics, diagnosis codes, care plan, insurance, hospital contact, clinical summary) is automatically ingested and normalized
  - Mastering Home Care routes referral to appropriate branch based on service area and capacity
  - **Automation level:** Near-full automation—intake record created, staff notified, tasks generated

- **Standard case (portal + email notifications):**
  - CarePort sends email alerts when new referrals are assigned
  - Mastering Home Care's email monitoring system detects CarePort notification emails
  - Intake coordinator logs into CarePort portal to retrieve full referral details
  - Data is manually or semi-automatically entered into Mastering Home Care
  - **Automation level:** Medium—email alerts trigger workflow, but portal data entry may be manual

- **Traditional case (fax):**
  - Some hospitals still send CarePort referrals via fax for agencies without portal access
  - Mastering Home Care's fax ingestion channel receives referral documents
  - OCR and LLM parse fax content to extract patient data
  - Staff reviews and confirms accuracy
  - **Automation level:** Low to medium—depends on OCR accuracy

**Automation Possibilities:**

- **Automatic intake creation:** API integration allows instant creation of patient records from CarePort referrals with full clinical detail
- **Smart routing:** Mastering Home Care uses patient ZIP code, care needs, and payer type to assign to appropriate office, intake coordinator, or care manager
- **Authorization tracking:** Insurance details from CarePort can trigger automatic authorization request workflows
- **Task automation:** Generate tasks like "Schedule initial assessment within 48 hours," "Verify insurance authorization," "Coordinate with discharge planner"
- **Outcome reporting:** Mastering Home Care can push outcome data (visits completed, patient satisfaction, readmissions) back to CarePort via API, improving agency quality scores
- **Analytics and BI:** Track CarePort referral volume, conversion rates, payer mix, and revenue by hospital source

**Compliance and Constraints:**

- **BAA required:** CarePort handles PHI; agencies must sign Business Associate Agreement
- **API access:** Requires enterprise-level subscription and developer agreement
- **Data security:** All referral data must be encrypted in transit (TLS) and at rest
- **No data scraping:** Automated extraction must use official API—portal scraping violates ToS
- **Outcome reporting obligations:** CarePort may require agencies to report post-discharge outcomes; Mastering Home Care should facilitate this

**Integration Strategy:**

1. **Phase 1 (immediate):** Set up email monitoring for CarePort notifications; manually log referrals into Mastering Home Care
2. **Phase 2 (3-6 months):** Build semi-automated workflow—email parsing pre-populates intake forms
3. **Phase 3 (6-12 months):** Implement CarePort API integration for full automation (if referral volume and ROI justify investment)
4. **Phase 4 (12+ months):** Enable bi-directional data flow—push outcome data from Mastering Home Care back to CarePort to improve quality scores

**ROI calculation:** If an agency is paying $15,000/year for CarePort and receiving 30 referrals/month (360/year), that's ~$42/referral. If conversion rate is 75%, that's ~$56 per client acquisition—highly competitive for qualified hospital referrals.

## Best Practices to Maximize Referrals from This Source

**1. Maintain Real-Time Capacity Updates:**
- CarePort's algorithm prioritizes providers with available capacity
- Update your capacity status daily (or multiple times daily during high-volume periods)
- Use automated tools if possible—consider API integration to sync Mastering Home Care capacity data with CarePort

**2. Invest in Quality Metrics:**
- CarePort ranks providers by quality scores (readmissions, outcomes, satisfaction)
- Track and report positive outcomes (reduced ER visits, medication adherence, patient satisfaction)
- Request feedback from discharge planners after successful placements

**3. Respond Immediately:**
- Speed matters—discharge planners are moving quickly and may move to next provider if you don't respond within 1-2 hours
- Designate a dedicated CarePort intake coordinator during business hours
- Set up mobile alerts for high-priority referrals

**4. Build Relationships with Key Hospitals:**
- Identify which hospitals in your service area use CarePort
- Proactively reach out to discharge planners to introduce your agency and CarePort presence
- Offer to provide outcome reports and care coordination support
- Attend hospital discharge planning meetings if invited

**5. Optimize Your Profile:**
- Complete every field in CarePort profile (incomplete profiles rank lower)
- Use professional photos and logos
- Highlight specializations (dementia care, veterans, multilingual staff)
- List all languages spoken by staff (critical in diverse MA communities)
- Update service offerings as you expand capabilities

**6. Demonstrate Value Through Data:**
- Use Mastering Home Care's analytics to track outcomes and share with CarePort
- Report patient satisfaction scores
- Highlight low readmission rates
- Showcase care plan adherence and clinical improvements

**7. Participate in CarePort Quality Programs:**
- Engage with CarePort's quality improvement initiatives
- Attend provider training webinars
- Respond to satisfaction surveys
- Use feedback to improve operations

**8. Leverage Massachusetts Market Knowledge:**
- Emphasize local expertise in your profile: "Serving Boston for 20+ years"
- Highlight relationships with local resources (ASAPs, senior centers, MassOptions)
- Mention knowledge of MassHealth programs and local payers

**9. Monitor Competitor Activity:**
- Track which agencies are getting referrals from key hospitals
- Understand what makes top performers successful (response time, quality scores, specializations)
- Differentiate your agency where competitors are weak

**10. Calculate and Track ROI:**
- Use Mastering Home Care to measure CarePort referral volume, conversion rate, and revenue
- Compare cost per acquisition to other referral sources
- Adjust strategy if ROI declines (improve response time, quality scores, or service offerings)
