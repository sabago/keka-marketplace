## Overview

Tufts Medicine (formerly Wellforce) is a major Massachusetts health system serving **Metro Boston, Merrimack Valley, and North Shore** regions. The system includes **5 hospitals** with combined revenue of $3+ billion and generates thousands of home care referrals annually through discharge planning networks.

**System Hospitals:**
1. **Tufts Medical Center** (Boston) - 415 beds, academic medical center
2. **Lowell General Hospital** - 265 beds, Merrimack Valley anchor
3. **MelroseWakefield Hospital** - 234 beds, North Suburban Boston
4. **Lawrence Memorial Hospital** (Medford) - 99 beds, community hospital
5. **Lawrence General Hospital** - 189 beds, Merrimack Valley

**Total reach:** 1,200+ beds, 700,000+ patient visits annually

---

## Why Tufts Medicine Matters

**Geographic Coverage:**
- **Boston:** Tufts Medical Center (Chinatown/Downtown Crossing)
- **Merrimack Valley:** Lowell General, Lawrence General (Lowell, Lawrence, Haverhill region)
- **North Shore:** MelroseWakefield (Melrose, Wakefield, Stoneham, Malden)
- **Metro West:** Lawrence Memorial Hospital (Medford, Malden, Somerville border)

**Referral strengths:**
- Academic + community hospital mix (diverse patient populations)
- Tufts Medicine ACO (value-based care focus, readmission prevention)
- Strong geriatrics programs (aging population priority)
- Diverse communities (Spanish, Portuguese, Khmer, Vietnamese populations)

## Tufts Medicine ACO

**Tufts Medicine has HPC-certified ACO** managing ~500,000+ attributed patients.

**Why ACO matters:**
- Care transitions focus (preventing readmissions)
- Home care is key strategy for keeping patients healthy at home
- ACO care coordinators refer to agencies they trust

**How to connect with ACO:**
1. Contact Tufts Medicine network development team
2. Introduce agency + quality metrics (readmission rates, satisfaction scores)
3. Ask to be added to ACO's preferred provider list
4. Build relationships with ACO care coordinators at individual hospitals

## How to Get Started

### **Step 1: Choose Target Hospital(s)**

**If you serve:**
- Boston/Chinatown → Tufts Medical Center
- Lowell/Chelmsford/Billerica → Lowell General
- Melrose/Wakefield/Malden → MelroseWakefield
- Medford/Malden/Everett → Lawrence Memorial
- Lawrence/Methuen/Andover → Lawrence General

**Start with 1-2 Tufts Medicine hospitals** closest to your service area.

### **Step 2: Contact Discharge Planning**

**Process:**
1. Call hospital main number
2. Ask for "discharge planning department"
3. Request meeting with discharge planning manager or social worker
4. Visit in person (bring materials: brochure, business cards, service area map)

**What to emphasize:**
- Geographic coverage (which towns you serve)
- Languages spoken (Spanish, Portuguese, Khmer, Vietnamese essential for Tufts system)
- Responsiveness (same-day assessments, next-day care starts)
- Quality metrics (if you track readmission rates, share them)

### **Step 3: Build Relationships**

**Ongoing maintenance:**
- Quarterly in-person visits (check in, refresh business cards)
- Respond immediately to every referral call (even if you can't accept that patient, be helpful)
- Share outcomes (update discharge planner when their referred patient doing well)
- Attend Tufts Medicine provider events (if offered)

**Timeline to first referral:** 2-6 months typical

## Best Practices

**1. Prioritize language capabilities:**
- **Spanish** (essential for Lawrence General, helpful for all)
- **Portuguese** (Brazilian communities in Medford, Everett)
- **Khmer/Cambodian** (critical for Lowell General)
- **Vietnamese** (Lowell, Lawrence)

**2. Target community hospitals first:**
- MelroseWakefield, Lawrence Memorial, Lawrence General easier to penetrate than Tufts Medical Center
- Build track record at community hospitals, then approach academic center

**3. Emphasize Tufts Medicine ACO alignment:**
- Position agency as "readmission prevention partner"
- Track and share your readmission rates
- Communicate proactively when clients at risk

**4. Be patient with academic center (Tufts Medical Center):**
- Very competitive (30+ agencies)
- Takes longer to break in (6-12 months)
- Focus on differentiation (specialized services, multilingual, rapid response)

**5. Serve entire hospital service area:**
- Don't just serve 2-3 towns - cover Merrimack Valley, North Shore, Metro Boston regions
- Hospitals refer more to agencies with broad coverage

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "Tufts Medical Center too competitive"**
**Solution:** Start with Lowell General, MelroseWakefield, Lawrence General (less competitive), build Tufts Medicine system reputation, then approach Tufts Medical Center.

**Challenge 2: "Don't have Spanish/Khmer speakers"**
**Solution:** Hire multilingual caregivers (critical for Tufts system success) or focus on hospitals serving primarily English-speaking communities.

**Challenge 3: "Discharge planners don't return calls"**
**Solution:** In-person visits work 10x better. Show up with coffee, ask when's a good time to chat.
