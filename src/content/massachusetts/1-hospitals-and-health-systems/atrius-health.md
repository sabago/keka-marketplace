## Overview

Atrius Health is **Massachusetts' largest independent physician network**, serving 700,000+ patients annually through 30+ locations across Eastern Massachusetts. Formed from the merger of Harvard Vanguard Medical Associates, Dedham Medical Associates, and Granite Medical Group, Atrius represents a **high-volume referral opportunity** for home care agencies - particularly those serving Greater Boston, South Shore, and MetroWest communities.

**What makes Atrius different from hospitals:**
- **Primary care focus:** 700+ physicians (internal medicine, family medicine, pediatrics)
- **Specialty care:** Cardiology, orthopedics, OB/GYN, neurology, oncology, behavioral health
- **Outpatient model:** Most care delivered in community clinics (NOT hospital settings)
- **Population health:** Atrius manages health of entire patient populations (chronic disease, preventive care, care transitions)
- **ACO structure:** Financial incentives to keep patients healthy at home = HOME CARE partnerships critical

**For home care agencies:**
- **700,000 patients** = massive referral potential
- **Physicians make direct referrals** (not just discharge planners like hospitals)
- **Chronic disease management:** Atrius physicians actively coordinate home care for CHF, COPD, diabetes, dementia patients
- **Care coordinators** at every location (identify patients needing home support)
- **ACO incentives:** Atrius financially motivated to partner with quality home care agencies (reduce hospitalizations, improve outcomes)

## Why Atrius Health Matters

**The primary care referral moment:**

**Scenario 1: Physician office visit**
- Elderly patient (85) visits primary care doctor for routine checkup
- **Physician observes:** Patient declining (weight loss, poor hygiene, missed medications)
- **Physician to family:** "Your mother needs help at home. I'm going to refer you to home care agencies."
- **Care coordinator:** Calls patient's adult child same day with agency names

**Scenario 2: Chronic disease management**
- Atrius patient hospitalized for CHF exacerbation (congestive heart failure)
- **Discharged from hospital:** Hospital discharge planner arranges short-term skilled nursing
- **2 weeks later:** Patient sees Atrius cardiologist for follow-up
- **Cardiologist to family:** "Your father needs ongoing support - daily weight monitoring, medication reminders, symptom tracking. I recommend home care."
- Atrius care coordinator arranges home care agency assessment

**Scenario 3: Care coordinator outreach**
- Atrius care coordinator reviews high-risk patient list (recent ER visits, multiple chronic conditions)
- **Identifies patient:** 78-year-old woman, lives alone, diabetic, recent fall
- **Care coordinator calls patient:** "I'm concerned about your safety. Would home care help?"
- Refers to home care agency

**Scenario 4: Annual wellness visit**
- Medicare patient (70) comes for Annual Wellness Visit (preventive visit)
- **Physician screens for:** ADL needs, cognitive decline, fall risk, caregiver strain
- **Identifies decline:** Patient struggling with bathing, daughter (primary caregiver) burned out
- **Physician recommends:** Professional home care to supplement daughter's care
- Refers to agency

## Atrius Health ACO (Accountable Care Organization)

**What is Atrius ACO:**
- Atrius Health operates as HPC-certified Accountable Care Organization
- **Financial model:** Shared savings (Atrius keeps portion of Medicare savings if they improve quality + reduce costs)
- **Incentives:** Prevent hospitalizations, reduce ER visits, manage chronic disease at home
- **HOME CARE = KEY STRATEGY** for achieving ACO goals

**Why Atrius ACO matters for home care agencies:**

**1. Financial incentive to partner:**
- Traditional fee-for-service: Physicians paid per visit (NO incentive to coordinate home care)
- **ACO model:** Atrius financially benefits when patients stay healthy at home (STRONG incentive to partner with quality home care agencies)

**2. Quality focus:**
- ACO measured on: Readmission rates, patient satisfaction, preventable ER visits, medication adherence
- **Home care agencies help Atrius improve these metrics** (valuable partner)

**3. Care coordination:**
- Atrius ACO employs care coordinators, case managers, transitional care nurses
- **These staff actively refer patients** to home care agencies

**4. Preferred provider opportunities:**
- Atrius ACO seeks preferred home care partners (agencies meeting quality standards)
- **Preferred status = priority referrals**

**How to become Atrius ACO preferred partner:**
1. Contact Atrius Health Network Development: (617) 559-8000
2. Introduce agency, emphasize: Geographic coverage (Atrius service area), quality metrics (readmission rates, satisfaction scores), care coordination capabilities
3. Request preferred provider status
4. Demonstrate value: Share patient outcomes, communicate with Atrius care teams, respond rapidly

## How to Get Started with Atrius Health

### **Step 1: Identify Your Geographic Match**

**Atrius serves 30+ locations across Eastern MA:**
- Which Atrius locations are in YOUR service area?
- **Target locations where you have strong coverage** (don't claim coverage you can't deliver)

**Example:**
- Agency serving Greater Boston → Target Kenmore, Somerville, Brookline, Dorchester, Cambridge locations
- Agency serving South Shore → Target Dedham, Quincy, Weymouth, Braintree, Norwood locations
- Agency serving MetroWest → Target Watertown, Framingham, Natick, Wellesley locations

### **Step 2: Contact Atrius Locations Directly**

**Initial outreach (LOCATION-SPECIFIC approach works best):**

**Option A: Call specific location**
- **Find location phone:** atriushealth.org/locations
- **Call location:** Ask for "Care Coordination" or "Practice Manager"
- **Brief introduction:** "Hi, I'm [Name] from [Agency]. We're a local home care agency serving [town]. Many of your patients likely need home care support. May I introduce our agency as a resource?"

**Example:**
- Atrius Health Dedham: (781) 329-7600 → Ask for Care Coordination
- Atrius Health Quincy: (617) 774-1500 → Ask for Care Coordination

**Option B: System-wide contact**
- **Atrius Health main:** (617) 559-8000
- **Ask for:** "Network Development" or "Community Partnerships"
- **Introduce agency:** "We serve [region] and would like to be a home care partner for Atrius patients"

**Option C: Meet physicians at community events**
- Atrius sponsors health fairs, senior expos, community events
- **Attend:** Introduce yourself to Atrius physicians/staff
- Follow up with formal introduction

### **Step 3: Build Relationships with Care Coordinators**

**Care coordinators = PRIMARY REFERRAL SOURCE at Atrius:**
- **Who they are:** RNs, social workers embedded in Atrius practices
- **What they do:** Coordinate care for complex patients (chronic disease, post-hospital, high-risk)
- **Why they refer:** Identify patients needing home support, provide resource lists to families

**How to connect:**
1. Visit Atrius locations (bring materials)
2. Request meeting with care coordinator (15 minutes)
3. **Introduce agency:** Services, coverage, specializations
4. **Ask:** "When your patients need home care, may we be a resource?"
5. **Leave materials:** Brochures (50+ copies), business cards
6. **Stay visible:** Quarterly check-ins, refresh materials

**Key care coordinator locations:**
- **Kenmore (Boston):** Flagship Harvard Vanguard location (high volume)
- **Dedham:** Dedham Medical headquarters
- **Weymouth:** Granite Medical headquarters
- **Somerville, Quincy, Watertown:** High senior populations

### **Step 4: Target High-Referral Specialties**

**Atrius specialties that generate most home care referrals:**

**Cardiology:**
- CHF patients need home monitoring, medication management
- **Strategy:** Introduce agency to Atrius cardiologists (specialize in cardiac care)

**Orthopedics:**
- Post-surgical recovery (joint replacement, fracture)
- **Strategy:** Position as post-surgical care specialists

**Neurology:**
- Stroke, Parkinson's, dementia patients
- **Strategy:** Emphasize dementia care expertise

**Oncology:**
- Cancer patients need support during treatment, end-of-life care
- **Strategy:** Introduce to Atrius oncologists, position as oncology-trained caregivers

**Geriatric Medicine:**
- Atrius has geriatric specialists managing frail elderly
- **Strategy:** Build relationship with geriatricians (they refer frequently)

### **Step 5: Provide Value to Atrius**

**How to stand out:**

**A. Rapid response:**
- Atrius care coordinator calls: "Patient needs home care assessment today"
- **You respond:** Same-day assessment
- **Atrius remembers** agencies that deliver quickly

**B. Care coordination:**
- **Communicate with Atrius physicians:** Send visit summaries, alert to concerns
- Example: "Mrs. Smith's blood pressure elevated during our visits - recommend physician follow-up"
- **Atrius values** agencies that coordinate care (helps them manage patients)

**C. Reduce hospitalizations:**
- Atrius ACO measured on readmission rates
- **Your agency prevents readmissions:** Early symptom detection, medication adherence, fall prevention
- **Share outcomes:** "10 clients from Atrius last year - zero readmissions"

**D. Educational presentations:**
- Offer to present at Atrius locations: "Supporting Seniors at Home," "Caregiver Resources"
- **Benefit:** Visibility with physicians/staff, positions as expert

**E. Preferred provider metrics:**
- Track quality for Atrius referrals: Patient satisfaction scores, caregiver consistency, response time, readmission rates
- **Share data:** Demonstrates value to Atrius ACO

## Best Practices for Atrius Health Partnerships

**1. Location-by-location approach:**
- **Don't approach Atrius as monolith** (30 locations = 30 separate relationships)
- **Build relationships location-by-location** (visit practices, meet care coordinators, introduce to physicians)
- **Prioritize locations in your strongest service areas**

**2. Build care coordinator relationships:**
- **Care coordinators = referral gatekeepers** (most referrals come through them)
- Meet quarterly (coffee, lunch, check-in)
- Provide ongoing value (educational materials, rapid response, care updates)

**3. Communicate with physicians:**
- **Don't just take referrals silently** - send visit summaries to referring physicians
- Example: "Dr. Smith, thank you for referring Mrs. Jones. She's doing well with daily visits. We noticed blood pressure trending up - recommend follow-up."
- **Physicians remember agencies that communicate**

**4. ACO-aligned services:**
- **Understand Atrius ACO goals:** Reduce readmissions, improve chronic disease outcomes, enhance patient satisfaction
- **Position services as ACO solutions:** "We help prevent readmissions through daily monitoring"
- Share quality metrics that matter to ACO

**5. Specialize to stand out:**
- 20+ agencies competing for Atrius referrals
- **Differentiate:** "We specialize in cardiac care" or "Dementia care experts" or "Post-surgical orthopedic specialists"
- Care coordinators remember specialists (vs. generalists)

**6. Serve affluent communities:**
- Many Atrius patients are affluent (Brookline, Newton, Wellesley, Hingham)
- **Premium service positioning:** White-glove care, highly trained caregivers, excellent communication
- Higher rates viable ($40-50/hour in affluent suburbs)

**7. Long-term relationship building:**
- Atrius referrals are TRUST-based (physicians/coordinators refer to agencies they KNOW)
- **Takes 6-12 months** to build trust
- Stay visible (quarterly visits, events, communication)

## Atrius vs. Hospital Referrals

**Key differences:**

| Aspect | Hospital Referrals | Atrius Referrals |
|--------|-------------------|------------------|
| **Timing** | Immediate (discharge planning) | Ongoing (physician identifies need over time) |
| **Urgency** | High (discharge imminent) | Moderate (proactive care management) |
| **Decision-maker** | Discharge planner | Physician + care coordinator + family |
| **Care duration** | Often short-term (post-acute) | Mix of short + long-term (chronic disease management) |
| **Competition** | High (10-30 agencies at discharge) | Lower (3-8 agencies in coordinator's network) |
| **Payment mix** | Higher MassHealth percentage | Higher private pay percentage |
| **Conversion** | 50-65% | 60-75% |

**Strategic implication:**
- **Atrius complements hospital referrals** (different patient populations, different timing)
- **Best agencies partner with BOTH** (hospitals for post-acute, Atrius for chronic disease/preventive)

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "Too many Atrius locations to build relationships"**
**Solution:** (1) Start with 3-5 locations in your strongest service area, (2) Prioritize flagship locations (Kenmore, Dedham, Somerville = highest volume), (3) Expand gradually (add 2-3 locations/quarter), (4) System-wide partnership takes 18-24 months (be patient).

**Challenge 2: "Care coordinators don't remember us"**
**Solution:** (1) Visit MORE frequently (quarterly minimum), (2) Provide ongoing value (educational materials, rapid response, care updates), (3) Communicate patient outcomes (coordinators want to know their referrals worked out), (4) Build personal relationships (coffee meetings, not just office drop-ins).

**Challenge 3: "Atrius refers to multiple agencies (not exclusive)"**
**Solution:** TRUE - care coordinators provide 2-3 agency names to families (not exclusive referral). Differentiate: (1) Respond fastest, (2) Specialize (cardiac, dementia, orthopedic), (3) Communicate with physicians (stand out), (4) High-quality service (families tell coordinator: "The agency you recommended was amazing").

**Challenge 4: "Don't know which Atrius locations to target"**
**Solution:** (1) Geographic match (Atrius locations in YOUR service area), (2) Senior population (target locations in towns with high 65+ populations), (3) Flagship locations (Kenmore, Dedham, Somerville = highest volume), (4) Ask existing clients: "Do you see Atrius doctors? May I introduce myself to your physician's office?"

**Challenge 5: "ACO partnership seems complex"**
**Solution:** (1) Start simple (build location relationships first), (2) ACO partnership = advanced strategy (after you have 5-10 location relationships), (3) Contact Atrius Network Development (617-559-8000) when ready for system-wide discussion, (4) Don't let ACO complexity prevent location-level relationships (care coordinators refer regardless of ACO status).
