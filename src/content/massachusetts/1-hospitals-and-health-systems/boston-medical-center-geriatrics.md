## Overview

Boston Medical Center (BMC) is Boston's safety net hospital and the largest provider of trauma and emergency services in New England. As an academic medical center affiliated with Boston University, BMC serves a diverse, vulnerable population and operates the **oldest continuously operating home medical service in the United States** through its Geriatrics Department.

BMC's **Geriatrics Home Care Program** has been serving homebound elders since 1975, making BMC a leader in home-based geriatric care. The hospital also operates a Memory Disorders Clinic and comprehensive Geriatric Care Program that has earned U.S. News recognition.

**Key opportunity:** While BMC has its own home medical services, external home care agencies can partner with BMC for **non-medical companion care, personal care, and support services** that complement medical care—especially for BMC's underserved, multi-ethnic, multi-lingual patient population.

## Who Sends Referrals Through This Source

Referrals come from:

- **BMC Geriatrics physicians and nurses** providing in-home medical care
- **Social workers and discharge planners** coordinating post-discharge transitions
- **Case managers** supporting complex, chronically ill patients
- **Memory Disorders Clinic** staff coordinating dementia care
- **Primary care physicians** within BMC's Community Health Centers
- **Emergency department** social workers arranging support for frequent ED users

**Key BMC departments for referrals:**
- **BMC Geriatrics** (617-414-6649) - Primary referral source
- **Discharge Planning/Case Management** - Hospital-wide
- **Social Work Department** - Patient and family support
- **BMC Community Health Centers** - 15+ primary care sites across Boston

## Who Can Receive Referrals

BMC works with various home care providers:

- **Medicare-certified home health agencies** (BMC often provides its own via Geriatrics Home Care)
- **Private-pay home care agencies** - Companion care, personal care, homemaker services
- **Hospice providers**
- **Community-based organizations** providing culturally specific support services
- **Language-specific care providers** (Spanish, Haitian Creole, Cape Verdean Creole, Vietnamese, etc.)

**Private-pay home care opportunity:** BMC patients often need **non-medical supportive services** alongside medical care:
- Companion care for isolated seniors
- Meal preparation and nutrition support
- Light housekeeping and home safety
- Medication reminders (non-nursing)
- Transportation to appointments
- Cultural and language-specific care

**BMC patient demographics** (important for agency positioning):
- **Racially and ethnically diverse:** 40% Black, 25% Hispanic/Latino, 18% White, 8% Asian
- **Lower income:** Many patients on MassHealth, Medicare, or uninsured
- **Limited English proficiency:** Significant populations speaking Spanish, Haitian Creole, Cape Verdean Creole, Vietnamese
- **Complex needs:** Chronic diseases, social determinants of health challenges, housing instability

**Agency positioning:** Agencies with **multilingual staff, cultural competency, and flexible payment options** (sliding scale, MassHealth acceptance) will be most successful with BMC referrals.

## How It Works (From an Agency's Perspective)

BMC operates a **relationship-driven referral process**:

1. **Build relationships with key staff:**
   - Contact BMC Geriatrics Department (617-414-6649)
   - Reach out to Social Work and Discharge Planning departments
   - Introduce agency and service offerings
   - Emphasize multilingual capabilities and cultural competency

2. **Attend community meetings:**
   - BMC hosts community partner meetings and grand rounds
   - Participate in Boston Public Health Commission events (BMC is affiliated)
   - Join Boston Elder Health Network meetings

3. **Receive referrals:**
   - Phone calls from social workers or discharge planners
   - Faxed referral sheets with patient information
   - Email introductions connecting agency with patient/family
   - Patient/family may self-refer after receiving agency contact info

4. **Respond with cultural sensitivity:**
   - Many BMC patients face language barriers, financial constraints, and social isolation
   - Offer flexible payment options and sliding scale if possible
   - Connect families with resources (MassHealth enrollment, food pantries, transportation)

5. **Coordinate with BMC care teams:**
   - Communicate regularly with BMC Geriatrics physicians if patient is in home medical program
   - Report changes in patient status (falls, medication issues, declining function)
   - Collaborate on care plans to support hospital's goals (reducing ED visits, preventing readmissions)

6. **Provide outcome feedback:**
   - Follow up with referring staff after intake to confirm placement
   - Share success stories and patient outcomes
   - Build trust through reliable, compassionate service delivery

## How to Get Listed / Enrolled

BMC does not have a formal provider enrollment portal. Follow these relationship-building steps:

**Step 1: Contact BMC Geriatrics**
- Call (617) 414-6649 or visit https://www.bmc.org/geriatrics
- Ask to speak with care coordination staff or administrative lead
- Request meeting to introduce your agency

**Step 2: Connect with Social Work/Discharge Planning**
- Call BMC main line (617-638-8000) and ask for Social Work Department
- Request contact information for discharge planners
- Schedule introductory meetings with key staff

**Step 3: Prepare culturally-informed materials**
- Highlight multilingual staff (Spanish, Haitian Creole, Cape Verdean Creole, Vietnamese, Portuguese)
- Emphasize sliding scale or flexible payment options
- Showcase experience with diverse, underserved populations
- Provide references from other safety net hospitals or community health centers

**Step 4: Demonstrate mission alignment**
- BMC's mission is to serve vulnerable populations—align your agency with this value
- Offer to provide pro bono or reduced-cost services for extreme hardship cases
- Highlight any community partnerships (local churches, community centers, immigrant resource organizations)

**Step 5: Stay engaged:**
- Visit BMC quarterly to maintain relationships
- Attend BMC community events and health fairs
- Offer to present on home care topics at grand rounds or staff meetings

## Cost and Lead Quality

**Cost:**
- **FREE** - No enrollment fees or platform costs
- Investment is time (relationship-building) and marketing materials

**Lead Quality:**
- **Variable but high-need:** BMC patients have complex medical and social needs
- **Payer mix:** Heavy Medicaid (MassHealth), Medicare, some private pay, uninsured
- **Conversion challenges:** Financial constraints may affect ability to pay for private-pay services
- **Referral volume:** Moderate—agencies with cultural competency and flexible payment can receive 5-15 referrals/month
- **Long-term relationships:** BMC patients often need ongoing support, creating recurring revenue

**Typical patient profile:**
- Older adults (65+) with chronic conditions (diabetes, hypertension, heart disease)
- Limited English proficiency
- Social isolation and limited family support
- Financial constraints
- High risk for ED utilization and hospital readmissions

**Strategic value:** BMC referrals may not be the highest-revenue clients, but they:
- Build agency reputation for serving diverse populations
- Create pathways to community partnerships and grants
- Align with social mission and community impact goals
- Generate consistent volume in underserved neighborhoods

## Pros and Cons for Massachusetts Home Care Agencies

**Pros:**
- **Mission-driven partnership**—serve vulnerable populations with significant needs
- **Cultural diversity opportunity**—build multilingual, multicultural caregiver workforce
- **High patient need**—chronic conditions and social isolation create demand for services
- **Community reputation**—partnering with BMC enhances agency's community standing
- **Lower competition**—fewer agencies focus on safety net populations
- **Long-term relationships**—patients often need ongoing care for years
- **Academic affiliation**—potential for research partnerships, student placements, training opportunities

**Cons:**
- **Lower reimbursement**—heavy MassHealth and uninsured population limits private-pay revenue
- **Payment challenges**—some patients cannot afford services even at reduced rates
- **Complex social needs**—requires case management and resource navigation beyond typical home care
- **Higher no-show rates**—transportation challenges and chaotic life circumstances can affect reliability
- **Requires cultural competency**—agencies must invest in multilingual staff and training
- **Relationship-intensive**—building trust with BMC staff and patients takes significant time

## How This Fits Mastering Home Care's Data Flow

BMC referrals arrive via **traditional channels** (phone, fax, email):

**Typical Ingest Path:**

- **Phone referrals:**
  - Social worker calls agency intake line
  - Manual data entry into Mastering Home Care
  - **Automation opportunity:** Speech-to-text transcription to pre-populate intake forms

- **Fax referrals:**
  - Mastering Home Care's fax ingestion channel receives referral documents
  - OCR and LLM parse patient data
  - **Challenge:** BMC faxes may include handwritten notes—OCR accuracy may be lower

- **Email referrals:**
  - Email parser extracts patient information
  - **Automation level:** Medium—email parsing effective for structured formats

**Automation Possibilities:**

- **Language routing:** Detect patient language preference and route to bilingual care coordinators
- **Resource database integration:** Link to MassHealth, food pantries, transportation resources for holistic support
- **Care coordination tracking:** Document communication with BMC care teams
- **Outcome reporting:** Track ED visit reductions and hospital readmission prevention for value demonstration

**Compliance:**
- Standard HIPAA compliance for all referral communications
- Cultural sensitivity training for staff handling BMC referrals

## Best Practices to Maximize Referrals from This Source

**1. Invest in Multilingual Staff:**
- Hire caregivers fluent in Spanish, Haitian Creole, Cape Verdean Creole, Vietnamese, Portuguese
- List languages on all marketing materials

**2. Offer Flexible Payment Options:**
- Sliding scale based on income
- Accept MassHealth (if enrolled)
- Partner with community organizations for funding (Councils on Aging, charitable foundations)

**3. Build Cultural Competency:**
- Train staff on cultural humility and health equity
- Understand cultural norms around caregiving in Haitian, Latino, Cape Verdean, Asian communities
- Partner with community-based organizations trusted by these populations

**4. Position as Community Partner:**
- Attend community health fairs and events in Roxbury, Dorchester, Mattapan, East Boston
- Offer free health education workshops (falls prevention, medication safety)
- Sponsor local community events

**5. Demonstrate Mission Alignment:**
- Share your agency's commitment to serving vulnerable populations
- Provide pro bono hours for extreme hardship cases
- Highlight any charity care or community benefit programs

**6. Coordinate Closely with BMC Teams:**
- Communicate regularly with BMC Geriatrics physicians and nurses
- Attend interdisciplinary care meetings if invited
- Share patient status updates to support care coordination

**7. Track and Report Outcomes:**
- Measure ED visit reductions and hospital readmission prevention
- Document medication adherence improvements
- Report social isolation reduction and quality of life improvements
- Use Mastering Home Care analytics to generate outcome reports for BMC

**8. Network with Community Partners:**
- Build relationships with organizations serving BMC populations:
  - Boston Centers for Youth & Families senior centers
  - Mattapan Food and Fitness Coalition
  - Asian Community Development Corporation
  - El Comité de Salud
