## Overview

Southcoast Health is the **dominant healthcare system serving southeastern Massachusetts**, operating three acute care hospitals under a single license with 815 total beds. Formed in 1996 through the merger of St. Luke's Hospital (New Bedford), Charlton Memorial Hospital (Fall River), and Tobey Hospital (Wareham), Southcoast Health serves **719,000+ residents across 33 communities** spanning over 900 square miles.

**Southcoast Health hospitals:**
- **St. Luke's Hospital** (New Bedford) - 311 beds, Level II Trauma Center
- **Charlton Memorial Hospital** (Fall River) - 284 beds, cardiac center of excellence
- **Tobey Hospital** (Wareham) - 220 beds, bariatric surgery program
- **35+ ancillary facilities** across southeastern Massachusetts and Rhode Island

**Service area:** New Bedford, Fall River, Dartmouth, Wareham, Fairhaven, Westport, Somerset, Swansea, Seekonk, Acushnet, Freetown, Rochester, Marion, Mattapoisett, plus surrounding communities in southeastern Massachusetts and eastern Rhode Island

**Market share:** Regional dominance in southeastern Massachusetts (60%+ in New Bedford/Fall River corridor)

## Discharge Planning Process

**How Southcoast Health discharge works:**

### **Step 1: Patient Admission**
- Case manager assigned at admission (especially high-risk patients: elderly, complex conditions, social issues)
- **Discharge planning begins on Day 1** (not day of discharge)
- Case manager assesses: Living situation, support system, functional abilities, anticipated discharge needs

### **Step 2: Discharge Assessment**
- 24-48 hours before anticipated discharge, case manager finalizes plan
- **Assessment includes:** Home safety, ADL abilities, caregiver availability, insurance coverage
- **Home care needs identified:** Personal care, homemaking, medication management, safety supervision

### **Step 3: Home Care Referral**
- Case manager provides **list of approved home care agencies** to patient/family
- Patient/family chooses agency (patient choice required by law)
- **Case manager contacts chosen agency** (phone call or electronic referral)
- **Referral includes:** Patient demographics, diagnoses, medications, ADL limitations, insurance, anticipated discharge date

### **Step 4: Agency Response**
- **Response time expectation: 2-4 hours** (same-day response critical)
- Agency calls case manager to confirm referral, gather additional information
- **Agency contacts patient/family** to introduce services, schedule assessment
- Assessment ideally occurs **before discharge** or **day of discharge** (next-day acceptable)

### **Step 5: Care Initiation**
- Agency completes in-home assessment (functional abilities, home environment, care plan)
- **Care begins:** Typically discharge day or next day
- Agency provides feedback to case manager (care plan implemented, patient stable)

**Southcoast Health case management philosophy:**
- **Patient-centered:** Patient choice respected (case managers present options, don't mandate agency)
- **Care transitions focus:** Reducing readmissions, ensuring safe discharge, coordinating community resources
- **ACO alignment:** Southcoast Health participates in ACOs (focus on quality, care coordination, cost management)

## Best Practices for Southcoast Health Success

**1. Portuguese/Brazilian language capability = non-negotiable:**
- **Minimum:** 30-40% of caregivers Portuguese-speaking (reflects patient population)
- **Ideal:** Care coordinators, intake staff also Portuguese-speaking (first contact in patient's language)
- **Dialects matter:** Azorean Portuguese (most New Bedford/Fall River residents), Brazilian Portuguese (growing population), Cape Verdean Creole (distinct but Portuguese-based)
- **Written materials:** Brochures, care agreements, instructions in Portuguese
- **Cultural understanding:** Portuguese/Azorean culture (not just language) = family structures, food, Catholic traditions, communication styles

**2. Hire from the community:**
- **New Bedford/Fall River residents = best caregivers** (understand communities, culture, geography)
- **Recruit at Portuguese churches:** Catholic churches in New Bedford/Fall River = community hubs
- **Portuguese media:** Advertise caregiver positions in Portuguese Times newspaper, Portuguese radio stations
- **Word-of-mouth:** Portuguese community tight-knit (good caregivers know other potential caregivers)

**3. Understand working-class/gateway city dynamics:**
- **Financial constraints:** Many families have limited resources (balance quality care with affordability)
- **MassHealth volume:** Higher percentage than suburban areas (understand MassHealth eligibility, coverage)
- **Multi-generational households:** Grandparents living with adult children = complex family dynamics
- **Work schedules:** Many families work shift work (manufacturing, healthcare, service industry) = scheduling challenges
- **Pride and dignity:** Working-class families may resist "charity" (position as earned service, not handout)

**4. Three-hospital geographic strategy:**
- **Option A - Full coverage:** Serve all three hospital areas (New Bedford, Fall River, Wareham) = maximum referral potential
- **Option B - Two-hospital focus:** New Bedford + Fall River (largest volume, similar Portuguese demographics) OR New Bedford + Wareham (closer proximity)
- **Option C - Single-hospital specialization:** Focus on St. Luke's (New Bedford) = depth over breadth
- **Recommendation:** Full coverage ideal, but two-hospital focus acceptable (don't claim coverage you can't deliver)

**5. Build Portuguese community reputation:**
- **Community visibility:** Sponsor Portuguese festivals, health fairs, church events
- **Portuguese media:** Advertise in Portuguese Times, Portuguese radio (affordable, targeted)
- **Word-of-mouth marketing:** Portuguese community = tight-knit, referrals spread quickly (good or bad)
- **Partner with Portuguese organizations:** Portuguese Youth Cultural Organization, Portuguese-American organizations

**6. Specialize in common Southcoast Health discharges:**
- **Cardiac:** Charlton Memorial = cardiac center (CHF, post-cardiac surgery, cardiac rehab)
- **Trauma/orthopedic:** St. Luke's = trauma center (accident recovery, fractures, post-surgical orthopedic)
- **Bariatric:** Tobey = bariatric program (post-weight loss surgery care, dietary support)
- **Oncology:** All three hospitals (cancer support, end-of-life care)
- **Train caregivers:** Specialized training for common diagnoses = better care = more referrals

**7. Rapid response commitment:**
- **2-4 hour callback:** Case manager calls, return call within 2-4 hours (same day)
- **Same-day assessments:** Patient discharged today, assess today or tomorrow morning (not 2-3 days later)
- **Weekend coverage:** Many discharges occur Friday-Sunday (weekend intake capability = competitive advantage)
- **Emergency backup:** If primary caregiver unavailable, backup immediately (no gaps in care)

**8. Understand At Home relationship:**
- **At Home by Southcoast Health = partner, not competitor**
- Many patients receive both: At Home (nursing/therapy) + your agency (personal care)
- **Coordinate with At Home:** If patient has both services, communicate with VNA (medication changes, functional decline, safety concerns)
- **Referrals both directions:** At Home refers families to home care agencies, you refer families to At Home for skilled needs

## Common Challenges and Solutions

**Challenge 1: "We don't have Portuguese-speaking staff"**
**Solution:** (1) **Hire immediately** (non-negotiable for Southcoast Health success - advertise in Portuguese community, recruit at churches, partner with Portuguese organizations), (2) **Partner with another agency** (subcontract Portuguese-speaking caregivers until you hire your own), (3) **Use translation services** (phone interpreters for initial assessments - not ideal but better than nothing), (4) **Reconsider Southcoast Health** (if you can't provide Portuguese services, may be better to focus on non-Portuguese markets).

**Challenge 2: "Three hospitals too spread out to cover effectively"**
**Solution:** (1) **Start with one hospital** (St. Luke's = largest volume, establish presence first), (2) **Expand gradually** (once successful at St. Luke's, add Charlton or Tobey), (3) **Subcontract for distant areas** (partner with Wareham agency for Tobey referrals if you're New Bedford-based), (4) **Zone caregivers geographically** (New Bedford caregivers serve St. Luke's area, Fall River caregivers serve Charlton area).

**Challenge 3: "MassHealth rates too low to be profitable"**
**Solution:** Gateway cities = higher MassHealth volume (reality of market). (1) **Mixed payer strategy:** Accept MassHealth but also pursue private pay, Medicare Advantage (don't rely solely on MassHealth), (2) **Efficiency:** MassHealth requires operational efficiency (minimize administrative costs, optimize scheduling, reduce caregiver turnover), (3) **Volume:** MassHealth lower margin but higher volume (10 MassHealth clients = same profit as 5 private pay), (4) **Understand reimbursement:** Apply for MassHealth Personal Care Attendant program (PCA) = better rates than consumer-pay.

**Challenge 4: "Multi-generational households = complex family dynamics"**
**Solution:** Portuguese/working-class families often have multiple generations under one roof (grandparent, adult children, grandchildren). (1) **Identify decision-maker:** Who makes care decisions? (May be adult child, not elderly patient), (2) **Family meetings:** Include family in care planning (not just patient), (3) **Cultural sensitivity:** Understand family expectations (some families expect to provide some care, agency supplements), (4) **Respect hierarchy:** Respect family structure (elder has authority even if adult child manages logistics).

**Challenge 5: "Competition from Southcoast Health's own 'At Home' program"**
**Solution:** At Home = VNA services (skilled nursing, therapy), NOT personal care/homemaking (different services). (1) **Position as complementary:** "We work with At Home frequently - they provide nursing, we provide personal care," (2) **Explain difference:** Medicare covers At Home nursing, doesn't cover personal care (families need both), (3) **Build relationship with At Home:** Partner rather than compete (co-referrals, care coordination).

**Challenge 6: "Can't afford to market in Portuguese media"**
**Solution:** (1) **Community presence:** Sponsor Portuguese festival booths (affordable, high visibility), (2) **Church bulletins:** Advertise in Portuguese church bulletins (very affordable), (3) **Social media:** Portuguese community active on Facebook (create Portuguese-language Facebook page), (4) **Word-of-mouth:** Provide excellent care = Portuguese community spreads word (most cost-effective marketing).

**Challenge 7: "Gateway city demographics = challenging clients"**
**Solution:** Working-class gateway cities have different dynamics than affluent suburbs. (1) **Financial counseling:** Help families navigate MassHealth, understand payment options, (2) **Flexible scheduling:** Work schedules vary (shift work, multiple jobs) = accommodate family availability, (3) **Respect and dignity:** Never condescend (working-class families deeply value respect), (4) **Community understanding:** New Bedford/Fall River = proud communities with rich history (honor that).

## Cultural Competency: Portuguese/Brazilian/Cape Verdean Communities

**Understanding Portuguese immigration:**
- **Azorean origins:** Majority of Portuguese immigrants from Azores islands (9 islands, mid-Atlantic)
- **Whaling/fishing economy:** Portuguese came to New Bedford/Fall River for whaling (19th century), later fishing/maritime work
- **Textile mills:** Fall River textile mills employed generations of Portuguese families
- **Chain migration:** Families sponsored relatives (entire villages relocated to New Bedford/Fall River)

**Language considerations:**
- **Azorean Portuguese:** Distinct dialect (pronunciation, vocabulary differences from Continental Portuguese)
- **Brazilian Portuguese:** Growing Brazilian immigrant population (different accent, some vocabulary differences)
- **Cape Verdean Creole:** Portuguese-based but distinct language (Cape Verde islands, former Portuguese colony)
- **Language proficiency varies:** Older generation may speak little English, younger generation bilingual, middle-generation comfortable in both

**Family structures:**
- **Multi-generational households:** Common for 2-3 generations to live together
- **Strong family obligations:** Adult children expected to care for aging parents (accepting outside help = difficult decision)
- **Gender roles:** Traditional gender roles common (women primary caregivers, but changing in younger generations)
- **Respect for elders:** Deep cultural value (elders have authority, their wishes respected)

**Religious traditions:**
- **Catholic faith:** Overwhelming majority Catholic (influences end-of-life care, Sunday routines)
- **Parish identity:** Strong parish affiliation (Our Lady of Mount Carmel, Immaculate Conception parishes = community centers)
- **Feast days:** Religious feast days important (especially Holy Ghost feast, Feast of the Blessed Sacrament)
- **End-of-life:** Catholic traditions around death, dying (priests, sacraments, family presence)

**Food and diet:**
- **Portuguese cuisine:** Fish (cod, sardines), soups (kale soup, stone soup), bread (Portuguese sweet bread), rice, beans
- **Homemade meals:** Strong preference for homemade traditional foods (institutional food rejected)
- **Family recipes:** Food = cultural connection (caregivers who can prepare Portuguese meals = valued)
- **Dietary restrictions:** Catholic tradition (some observe no meat on Fridays)

**Communication styles:**
- **Direct but respectful:** Portuguese communicate directly but always with respect for elders
- **Emotional expressiveness:** Emotions expressed more openly than Anglo-American culture
- **Hospitality:** Guests always offered food, coffee (refusing = rude)
- **Stoicism about pain:** Older generation may minimize symptoms, downplay pain (cultural stoicism)

**Practical cultural competency tips:**
- **Greet in Portuguese:** "Bom dia" (good morning), "Boa tarde" (good afternoon) = builds rapport
- **Respect food traditions:** If family cooks Portuguese meals, praise the food (cultural pride)
- **Understand feast days:** Families may want to attend festas (August = Feast of the Blessed Sacrament in New Bedford - adjust schedules)
- **Church attendance:** Sunday mass important for many (plan care around church)
- **Large families:** Portuguese families often large (many siblings, cousins = many people involved in care decisions)
- **Privacy expectations:** Despite multi-generational households, privacy valued (knock, announce yourself, respect personal space)
