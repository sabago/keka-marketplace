## Overview

Lahey Health is a cornerstone of northeastern Massachusetts healthcare, anchored by its **flagship 329-bed Lahey Hospital & Medical Center in Burlington**. Now part of Beth Israel Lahey Health (BILH) following the 2019 merger, Lahey Health maintains its distinct regional identity while benefiting from system-wide resources.

**Why Lahey Health Matters for Home Care Agencies:**
- **Flagship tertiary hospital:** Lahey Hospital & Medical Center is a major surgical center with national rankings
- **High-acuity discharges:** Strong orthopedic, cardiac, and oncology programs generate significant post-acute needs
- **Geographic concentration:** Primary service area covers Burlington, North Shore, and Southern NH
- **FREE referrals:** No cost to establish relationships with discharge planning teams
- **Level I Trauma Center:** Only Level I trauma center north of Boston (high-volume, complex cases)
- **Academic affiliation:** Regional medical campus of UMass Chan Medical School
- **Quality reputation:** U.S. News & World Report Best Hospital 2025-2026, rated high performing in 18 procedures

For home care agencies serving Metro Boston's northern suburbs, North Shore, and Southern New Hampshire, **Lahey Hospital & Medical Center represents one of the highest-value hospital relationships** due to its surgical volume, complex case mix, and well-established discharge planning protocols.

Lahey Hospital & Medical Center's surgical reputation drives significant home care demand:

**Cardiac Surgery:**
- Coronary artery bypass grafting (CABG)
- Valve replacement/repair
- Complex cardiac procedures
- **Home care needs:** Cardiac monitoring, medication management, wound care, cardiac rehab coordination, fall prevention

- Total joint replacements (hip, knee, shoulder)
- Spine surgery
- Sports medicine procedures
- Orthopedic trauma reconstruction
- **Home care needs:** Physical therapy, occupational therapy, pain management, ADL assistance, fall prevention, mobility support
- **Typical length of care:** 3-8 weeks skilled therapy, often transitions to long-term personal care

- Post-surgical cancer care
- **Home care needs:** Wound management, symptom management, medication assistance, emotional support
- **Typical length of care:** Varies widely based on cancer type and treatment plan

As the only Level I trauma center north of Boston, Lahey Hospital treats severe injuries requiring extensive recovery:
- Motor vehicle accidents
- Falls from height
- Polytrauma patients
- **Home care needs:** Complex wound care, multiple therapy disciplines, adaptive equipment, extended recovery support
- **Referral potential:** Moderate volume, very high complexity cases

**Primary service area:**
- **Metro Boston North:** Burlington, Woburn, Wilmington, Reading, North Reading, Stoneham

- Agencies with coverage across northern Metro Boston and North Shore have access to multiple Lahey Health facilities

## Discharge Planning Process at Lahey Health

### **How Discharge Planning Works**

**Timeline:**
1. **Day 1-2:** Admission assessment by case management/social work
2. **Day 2-5:** Discharge planning begins (varies by expected length of stay)
3. **24-48 hours pre-discharge:** Finalize home care arrangements
4. **Discharge day:** Warm handoff to home care agency (ideally)

**Key personnel:**
- **Discharge planners:** Registered nurses and social workers assigned to specific units
- **Case managers:** Coordinate complex cases, especially those with multiple post-acute needs
- **Unit nurses:** Provide clinical updates and patient/family communication
- **Physicians:** Approve home care orders and prescriptions

### **How Referrals Are Made**

**Step 1: Patient/family identifies need or discharge planner assesses need**
- Discharge planner evaluates patient's post-discharge needs
- Discusses options with patient/family (home care, SNF, rehab facility)
- If home care is preferred, presents agency options

**Step 2: Patient/family selects home care agency**
- Discharge planner provides list of agencies (typically 3-5 options)
- Patient/family chooses based on: familiarity, recommendations, insurance, services offered
- **Patient choice is protected by federal law** - hospitals cannot mandate specific agencies

**Step 3: Discharge planner contacts selected agency**
- Phone call or electronic referral (some units use Epic referral system)
- Provides patient demographics, insurance, clinical summary, anticipated discharge date
- Requests agency availability and acceptance

**Step 4: Agency responds with intake assessment**
- Agency confirms service availability, insurance verification, scheduling
- Coordinates admission assessment timing (ideally within 24 hours of discharge)
- Communicates with discharge planner on care plan and equipment needs

**Step 5: Discharge and transition**
- Hospital provides discharge summary, medication list, physician orders
- Agency receives warm handoff (phone call with clinical update)
- Home care begins (ideally same day or next day)

### **What Discharge Planners Prioritize**

When selecting agencies to present to patients, discharge planners evaluate:

1. **Responsiveness:** Returns calls within 2 hours, accepts referrals promptly
2. **Reliability:** Shows up when scheduled, rarely cancels or delays
3. **Quality outcomes:** Low readmission rates, positive patient feedback
4. **Communication:** Regular updates, proactive problem-solving, collaborative approach
5. **Breadth of services:** Can handle diverse patient needs (skilled + non-skilled)
6. **Insurance acceptance:** Works with Medicare, MassHealth, commercial payers
7. **Geographic coverage:** Serves the hospital's full service area
8. **Specialty capabilities:** Can manage complex cases (wounds, dementia, bariatric, ventilators)

### **Step 3: Prepare Your Agency Introduction Packet**

**What to include:**
1. **One-page agency overview:**
   - Services offered (skilled nursing, PT/OT/ST, home health aides, personal care, respite)
   - Geographic coverage area (list specific towns)
   - Hours of operation (highlight 24/7 availability if applicable)
   - Languages spoken by staff
   - Specialty programs (dementia, orthopedic, cardiac, wound care)

2. **Insurance and payer information:**
   - Medicare-certified? (Yes/No)

3. **Quality metrics (if available):**

   - Patient satisfaction scores

4. **Referral contact information:**
   - 24/7 referral phone number
   - Fax number for referrals
   - Email for referrals
   - Online referral portal (if available)
   - Name and direct line of intake coordinator

5. **Staff credentials and specialties:**
   - RN clinical leadership
   - Specialized therapy staff (orthopedic PT, cardiac rehab)
   - Bilingual/multilingual staff
   - Dementia care training
   - Palliative care experience

### **Step 4: Make Initial Contact**

**Phone introduction script:**
> "Hello, my name is [Your Name] from [Agency Name]. We're a [Medicare-certified/licensed] home care agency serving [geographic area]. We provide [brief service description] and we'd like to establish a relationship with Lahey Hospital's discharge planning team. We have particular expertise in [specialty area] and are looking to support your patients transitioning home. Could you connect me with the discharge planning manager or let me know the best way to introduce our agency?"

**What to request:**
1. **Get on the referral list:** Ask to be added to the list of agencies discharge planners can offer to patients
2. **Understand referral process:** Ask how referrals are typically made (phone, fax, Epic)
3. **Identify key contacts:** Get names and direct phone/email for discharge planners by unit
4. **Schedule follow-up meeting:** Request 15-20 minute meeting (in-person or phone) with discharge planning leadership

### **Step 5: Build Unit-Specific Relationships**

**Target high-volume units first:**
- **Orthopedic units:** Hip/knee replacement patients have predictable home care needs
- **Cardiac surgery units:** Post-CABG patients require skilled nursing and cardiac monitoring
- **Medical-surgical units:** High volume, diverse patient population
- **Oncology units:** Cancer patients with complex symptom management needs

**Strategies for building relationships:**

**1. Regular check-ins (monthly):**
- Brief phone call or email to discharge planners
- Share updates on your agency (new services, staff additions, quality improvements)
- Ask about their current challenges or patient needs
- Offer solutions to common problems they're experiencing

**2. Responsiveness:**
- Return calls within 1-2 hours
- Accept referrals promptly (within 2 hours of initial contact)
- Provide quick insurance verification
- Be flexible with admission scheduling

**3. Reliability:**
- Show up when you say you will
- Don't cancel or reschedule unless absolutely necessary
- Follow through on commitments
- Provide 24/7 availability for urgent situations

**4. Communication and feedback loop:**
- Update discharge planners on patient progress
- Report any concerns or complications immediately
- Share positive outcomes and patient testimonials
- Notify promptly if patient needs rehospitalization (don't hide problems)

**5. Education and problem-solving:**
- Offer in-service training on home care topics (wound care, fall prevention, medication management)
- Provide resources to discharge planners (educational materials, community resources)
- Be a partner in solving discharge challenges, not just a vendor

**6. Face time:**
- Visit the hospital periodically (monthly or quarterly)
- Stop by discharge planning offices to check in
- Bring coffee/snacks (simple gesture, not inappropriate gifts)
- Attend hospital-sponsored events or care transition meetings if invited

### **Step 6: Handle the BILH At Home Competition**

**Acknowledge the internal agency:**
> "I know BILH has its own home care agency, and I respect the work they do. Our agency offers [specific differentiator] that may benefit certain patients, particularly those who [describe ideal patient fit]. We're here to complement your discharge options and provide choice for patients and families."

**Emphasize patient choice:**
- Remind discharge planners that patients have the legal right to choose their home care provider
- Position your agency as a valuable option for patients who: prefer a community-based agency, have used your services before, need specialized services you excel at, live in areas where you have stronger coverage

**Differentiate clearly:**
- "We specialize in serving [specific towns/regions]"
- "Our team includes native speakers of [languages]"
- "We have a dedicated dementia care program with certified staff"
- "We offer 24/7 RN availability and same-day urgent visits"
- "Our private-pay rates are competitive for patients needing care beyond what Medicare covers"

## Best Practices for Success with Lahey Health

### **1. Master the Intake Process**

**Respond quickly:**
- Answer referral line within 3 rings
- Return discharge planner calls within 1 hour
- Confirm acceptance within 2 hours of referral

**Verify insurance immediately:**
- Have direct lines to Medicare, MassHealth, commercial insurance verification
- Confirm coverage and communicate any limitations to discharge planner upfront
- Don't surprise the hospital or patient with coverage issues after discharge

**Schedule strategically:**
- Offer same-day admission if patient discharges early in day
- Be flexible with timing (morning vs. afternoon) based on patient/family preference
- Coordinate with equipment deliveries (hospital bed, oxygen, walker)

### **2. Communicate Proactively**

**Before admission:**
- Confirm appointment time with patient/family 24 hours in advance
- Call again 2 hours before arrival
- Notify discharge planner if any issues arise

**During care:**
- Update discharge planner if patient has complications or needs resources
- Share positive progress notes (builds confidence in your agency)
- Alert hospital immediately if rehospitalization is needed

**After discharge from home care:**
- Send brief outcome summary to referring discharge planner
- Highlight successful outcomes (patient met goals, returned to independence)
- Thank them for the referral (builds relationship)

### **3. Specialize and Differentiate**

**Develop expertise in Lahey's high-volume service lines:**
- **Orthopedic post-surgical care:** Train staff on joint replacement protocols, mobility progression, fall prevention
- **Cardiac recovery:** Cardiac monitoring, medication management, heart failure education
- **Wound care:** Surgical wound assessment, advanced wound care techniques
- **Geriatric syndromes:** Dementia care, fall prevention, polypharmacy management

**Market your specialization:**
- Create service-specific brochures (e.g., "Hip & Knee Replacement Home Recovery Program")
- Train staff on evidence-based protocols for these conditions
- Share outcomes data specific to these patient populations

### **4. Build Multi-Level Relationships**

**Don't rely on one contact:**
- Build relationships with multiple discharge planners across different units
- Know the discharge planning manager/director
- Develop rapport with unit nurses (they influence discharge planner recommendations)
- Connect with physician practices affiliated with Lahey (they may influence discharge preferences)

### **5. Track and Share Quality Outcomes**

**Metrics to track:**
- Hospital readmission rates (lower is better)
- Patient satisfaction scores
- Falls during home care
- Medication errors
- Emergency department visits during home care
- Successful discharge to independence (% of patients who complete care and remain at home)

**Share results with discharge planners:**
- Quarterly outcome reports
- Highlight improvements over time
- Benchmark against industry standards (if favorable)

### **6. Handle Challenges Professionally**

**Common challenges:**
- **Patient refuses service after admission:** Notify discharge planner immediately, offer to help problem-solve
- **Patient readmitted to hospital:** Communicate promptly, don't hide it, discuss what could be improved
- **Insurance denial:** Work with patient/family on appeal or alternative payment options, keep discharge planner informed
- **Staffing shortage:** Be honest about limitations, offer alternative solutions (e.g., delay start by 24 hours)

**Professional responses build trust.** Discharge planners value agencies that communicate problems transparently rather than disappearing when issues arise.

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: Competition from BILH At Home**

**Problem:** Internal BILH home care agency receives preferential referrals from some discharge planners.

**Solutions:**
- **Differentiate:** Emphasize what makes your agency unique (specialty services, geographic coverage, language capabilities, 24/7 availability)
- **Focus on patient choice:** Educate discharge planners that patients have the right to choose and may prefer a community-based agency
- **Build strong relationships:** Personal relationships with discharge planners can overcome internal preference
- **Target specific units:** Some units are more open to external agencies (especially if BILH At Home has capacity constraints)
- **Excel in quality:** Be the agency that never disappoints - reliability and outcomes win long-term

### **Challenge 2: Getting Past the Gatekeeper**

**Problem:** Difficult to reach discharge planning department or get added to referral list.

**Solutions:**
- **Try multiple entry points:** Main operator, social work department, patient relations, specific unit managers
- **Visit in person:** Show up at the hospital (non-intrusively) and ask to meet discharge planning leadership
- **Leverage connections:** If you know a physician, nurse, or administrator affiliated with Lahey, ask for an introduction
- **Be persistent but respectful:** Monthly check-ins until you establish contact
- **Attend community events:** Lahey Health hosts community health fairs and events where you can network

### **Challenge 3: Low Initial Referral Volume**

**Problem:** You're on the referral list but rarely get called.

**Solutions:**
- **Check in regularly:** Monthly calls to discharge planners to stay top-of-mind
- **Ask for feedback:** "We've received a few referrals but would love to serve more patients. What can we do to be a stronger partner?"
- **Target specific patient populations:** "I wanted to let you know we've expanded our Portuguese-speaking staff" or "We now have a certified dementia care specialist"
- **Improve responsiveness:** Even if volume is low, be exceptionally responsive to every referral (builds trust for increased volume)
- **Expand to multiple units:** Don't rely on one discharge planner - build relationships across cardiac, orthopedic, medical-surgical units

### **Challenge 4: Geographic Service Area Mismatch**

**Problem:** You don't serve all towns in Lahey's service area.

**Solutions:**
- **Be upfront about coverage:** Clearly communicate which towns you serve (saves time for discharge planner)
- **Focus on your zones:** Build extra-strong relationships for patients in your coverage area
- **Expand strategically:** Consider hiring staff or contracting with caregivers in high-referral towns you don't currently cover
- **Partner with other agencies:** Create referral partnerships with agencies that serve areas you don't (and vice versa)

### **Challenge 5: Insurance Network Gaps**

**Problem:** You're not contracted with certain commercial payers common at Lahey.

**Solutions:**
- **Pursue key contracts:** Research which commercial payers are most common at Lahey (Tufts Health Plan, Harvard Pilgrim, Blue Cross Blue Shield MA) and prioritize contracting
- **Offer private pay options:** Some patients will pay out-of-pocket if they prefer your agency
- **Single case agreements:** For high-value referrals, negotiate single case agreements with payers
- **Be transparent:** Let discharge planners know which insurances you accept upfront (saves everyone time)

### **Challenge 6: Rapid Discharge Timelines**

**Problem:** Hospital calls with referral expecting same-day admission (tight turnaround).

**Solutions:**
- **Maintain flexible capacity:** Keep scheduling buffer for urgent admits
- **Streamline intake process:** Electronic insurance verification, rapid staff assignment protocols
- **Communicate realistic timelines:** If you can't do same-day, offer next morning and explain why
- **Offer alternatives:** If you can't admit immediately, help discharge planner find an alternative (maintains goodwill)
