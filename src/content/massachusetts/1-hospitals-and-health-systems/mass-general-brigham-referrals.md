## Overview

Mass General Brigham (MGB) is the largest healthcare system in Massachusetts, comprising 13 hospitals including Massachusetts General Hospital, Brigham and Women's Hospital, and numerous community hospitals across the state. With over 80,000 employees and millions of patient encounters annually, MGB represents a significant referral opportunity for home care agencies.

MGB operates its own **integrated home care network** (MGB Home Care), which provides Medicare-certified home health and hospice services. However, the system also works with external home care agencies—particularly for private-pay services, non-medical care, geographic areas outside MGB Home Care's coverage, and specialty services.

**Key opportunity:** Building relationships with MGB discharge planners, case managers, and social workers across the system's 13 hospitals can generate a steady stream of high-acuity, well-insured referrals.

## Who Sends Referrals Through This Source

Referrals come from:

- **Discharge planners** coordinating transitions from hospital to home
- **Social workers** assessing post-discharge support needs
- **Case managers** managing complex patients and care coordination
- **Primary care physicians** within MGB's outpatient network
- **Geriatric specialists** at MGH Division of Palliative Care & Geriatric Medicine
- **Care coordinators** within MGB's Accountable Care Organization (the largest ACO in Massachusetts)

**MGB Hospitals (key referral sources):**
1. Massachusetts General Hospital (MGH) - Boston
2. Brigham and Women's Hospital (BWH) - Boston
3. Brigham and Women's Faulkner Hospital - Jamaica Plain
4. Newton-Wellesley Hospital - Newton
5. North Shore Medical Center (Salem and Lynn campuses)
6. Martha's Vineyard Hospital
7. Nantucket Cottage Hospital
8. Cooley Dickinson Hospital - Northampton
9. Wentworth-Douglass Hospital - Dover, NH
10. McLean Hospital (psychiatry) - Belmont
11. Mass Eye and Ear - Boston
12. Spaulding Rehabilitation Hospital - Charlestown
13. MGB Community Physicians (primary care network)

## Who Can Receive Referrals

MGB discharge teams refer patients to various home care providers:

- **Medicare-certified home health agencies** - For skilled nursing, physical therapy, occupational therapy, speech therapy, medical social work
- **Private-pay home care agencies** - For companion care, personal care, homemaker services, medication reminders, transportation
- **Hospice providers** - For end-of-life care
- **Specialty providers** - Infusion therapy, wound care, durable medical equipment (DME)

**Private-pay home care opportunity:** MGB discharge planners often work with external agencies when:
- Patient needs non-medical companion care or personal assistance
- Patient lives outside MGB Home Care's service area
- MGB Home Care is at capacity
- Family requests a specific agency
- Patient needs 24/7 live-in care (not provided by MGB Home Care)
- Cultural or language-specific care needs (e.g., multilingual caregivers)

**Important note:** MGB prioritizes its own home care service (MGB Home Care) for Medicare-certified home health, but this does NOT preclude external agencies from receiving referrals—especially for private-pay and non-medical services.

## How It Works (From an Agency's Perspective)

MGB's referral process is **relationship-driven** rather than platform-based:

1. **Build relationships with discharge planners:**
   - Identify key discharge planning departments at MGB hospitals
   - Request meetings with discharge planners, case managers, and social workers
   - Introduce your agency, service offerings, and coverage areas
   - Provide business cards, brochures, and intake contact information

2. **Get on preferred provider lists:**
   - Ask to be added to discharge planning resource directories
   - Provide required documentation (license if applicable, insurance certificates, references)
   - Some MGB hospitals maintain informal "preferred provider" lists based on past performance

3. **Receive referrals:**
   - Discharge planners call or fax referrals directly to your agency
   - Family members may be given your contact information to self-refer
   - Referral typically includes:
     - Patient name, DOB, and contact info
     - Discharge diagnosis and care needs
     - Insurance/payer (private pay, Medicare Advantage, commercial)
     - Requested services (companion care, personal care, etc.)
     - Preferred start date

4. **Respond quickly:**
   - Call patient and family within 2-4 hours of referral
   - Schedule initial assessment within 24-48 hours
   - Coordinate with MGB discharge team on care plan and transition timeline

5. **Provide feedback loop:**
   - Follow up with discharge planner after patient intake to confirm placement
   - Report outcomes (patient satisfaction, care plan adherence)
   - Build trust through reliable service delivery

**Key success factor:** MGB referrals are earned through **relationship-building and proven performance**, not automated platforms. Agencies must invest in face-to-face networking and demonstrate consistent quality.

## How to Get Listed / Enrolled

Unlike platform-based systems (Aidin, CarePort), MGB does not have a centralized provider enrollment portal for non-medical home care. Instead, follow these steps:

**Step 1: Research target hospitals**
- Identify which MGB hospitals serve your geographic area
- Focus on hospitals closest to your office(s) for faster response times
- Example: If based in Boston, prioritize MGH, BWH, and BWH Faulkner

**Step 2: Identify key contacts**
- Call hospital switchboard and ask for "Discharge Planning Department" or "Case Management"
- Request names and direct phone numbers of discharge planners and social workers
- LinkedIn can also help identify discharge planning staff

**Step 3: Schedule introductory meetings**
- Email or call discharge planners to request a brief meeting (15-30 minutes)
- Offer to bring lunch or coffee to make it convenient
- Be specific about your value proposition: "We provide private-pay companion care and personal care services throughout Metro Boston with 24/7 availability"

**Step 4: Prepare professional materials**
- **One-pager:** Agency overview, services offered, coverage area, contact info
- **Business cards:** With direct intake coordinator phone number and email
- **Brochure or service menu:** Highlight specializations (dementia care, post-surgical support, multilingual staff)
- **Insurance and accreditation:** Proof of liability insurance, any certifications (ACHC, Joint Commission if applicable)

**Step 5: Demonstrate value**
- Emphasize fast response times (within 2-4 hours)
- Highlight language capabilities (Spanish, Portuguese, Haitian Creole, Mandarin, etc. for Boston demographics)
- Mention any specialty training (Alzheimer's care, Parkinson's, diabetes management)
- Offer to provide regular feedback on patient outcomes

**Step 6: Stay top-of-mind**
- Visit discharge planning departments quarterly (bring donuts or bagels)
- Send holiday cards and thank-you notes
- Provide updates on new services or expanded coverage areas
- Offer to present at discharge planning team meetings

**Step 7: Deliver consistently**
- When you receive referrals, respond immediately and provide excellent care
- Follow up with discharge planners to confirm successful intake
- Request feedback and referrals to colleagues at other MGB hospitals

**Massachusetts-specific notes:**
- MGB operates across MA (Metro Boston, North Shore, Cape & Islands, Pioneer Valley) and southern NH—tailor your approach by region
- If serving MassHealth patients, mention your provider enrollment status
- MGB's ACO structure means care coordinators are highly focused on reducing readmissions—emphasize how your services support care transitions

## Cost and Lead Quality

**Cost:**
- **FREE** - No enrollment fees, platform subscriptions, or per-lead charges
- Only investment is time (relationship-building) and marketing materials (brochures, business cards)

**Lead Quality:**
- **Exceptionally high:** MGB patients tend to be well-insured, clinically complex, and have strong family support systems
- **Payer mix:** Medicare Advantage, commercial insurance (BlueCross, Harvard Pilgrim, Tufts), private pay; some MassHealth
- **Acuity:** Post-acute (surgery, hospitalization), chronic disease management, geriatric care
- **Volume:** Varies by relationship strength—agencies with strong ties to multiple MGB hospitals can receive 10-30+ referrals/month
- **Conversion rate:** High (70-90%) because patients have immediate need and trust hospital recommendations

**Typical patient profile:**
- Older adults (65+) discharged after hospitalization or surgery
- Families seeking companion care or personal care support to supplement family caregiving
- Patients needing medication reminders, meal preparation, light housekeeping, transportation
- Well-educated, financially stable families who value quality and professionalism

## Pros and Cons for Massachusetts Home Care Agencies

**Pros:**
- **Largest healthcare system in MA**—massive referral potential across 13 hospitals
- **High-quality referrals**—well-insured, clinically appropriate, immediate need
- **No platform fees**—relationship-based referrals are free
- **Geographic breadth**—MGB hospitals span Metro Boston, North Shore, Cape & Islands, Pioneer Valley
- **Trusted brand**—patients trust MGB recommendations, improving conversion rates
- **ACO incentives**—MGB's ACO structure creates focus on care transitions and reducing readmissions
- **Long-term partnerships**—once established, relationships generate consistent referral flow

**Cons:**
- **MGB Home Care competition**—MGB prioritizes its own home health service for Medicare patients
- **Relationship-dependent**—requires ongoing networking and face-to-face engagement
- **Time-intensive**—building relationships takes months of consistent effort
- **Decentralized process**—each hospital operates independently; no centralized enrollment
- **Variable adoption**—some discharge planners prefer MGB Home Care exclusively; others work with external agencies
- **No guaranteed volume**—referrals depend on individual discharge planner relationships and agency performance

## How This Fits Mastering Home Care's Data Flow

MGB referrals typically arrive via **traditional channels** (phone, fax, email), making them a good fit for Mastering Home Care's multi-channel ingestion capabilities.

**Typical Ingest Path:**

- **Phone referrals:**
  - Discharge planner calls agency intake line
  - Intake coordinator manually enters referral details into Mastering Home Care
  - **Automation level:** Low—manual data entry required
  - **Enhancement opportunity:** Record calls and use speech-to-text transcription to pre-populate intake forms

- **Fax referrals:**
  - Discharge planner faxes referral sheet or clinical summary
  - Mastering Home Care's fax ingestion channel receives document
  - OCR and LLM parse fax content to extract patient data (name, DOB, diagnosis, care needs, insurance)
  - Intake coordinator reviews and confirms accuracy
  - **Automation level:** Medium—OCR/LLM reduces manual data entry by 50-70%

- **Email referrals:**
  - Discharge planner emails referral details (plain text or PDF attachment)
  - Mastering Home Care's email monitoring system detects incoming referral
  - Email parser extracts key fields (patient name, phone, hospital, discharge date)
  - PDF attachments processed via OCR if present
  - **Automation level:** Medium to high—email parsing can be very accurate for structured formats

**Automation Possibilities:**

- **Smart routing:** Use hospital name and patient ZIP code to route referral to appropriate branch/office
- **Task generation:** Automatically create tasks: "Call patient within 2 hours," "Schedule assessment within 24 hours," "Follow up with MGB discharge planner"
- **Alert systems:** Send Slack/SMS alerts to intake team for high-priority MGB referrals
- **Outcome tracking:** Log referral source (which MGB hospital and discharge planner) for relationship management and ROI analysis
- **Follow-up automation:** Schedule automated reminder to thank discharge planner 1 week after intake

**Compliance and Constraints:**

- **HIPAA compliance:** All referral communications must comply with HIPAA—secure fax, encrypted email, access controls
- **No BAA required with MGB:** Unlike platforms like Aidin/CarePort, direct hospital-to-agency referrals don't require a formal BAA (but both parties must maintain HIPAA compliance)
- **Consent for data sharing:** Ensure patient consent is documented for sharing information between MGB and your agency

**Integration Strategy:**

1. **Phase 1 (immediate):** Set up dedicated phone line and fax number for MGB referrals; manually enter into Mastering Home Care
2. **Phase 2 (1-3 months):** Implement OCR for fax ingestion and email parsing for email referrals
3. **Phase 3 (3-6 months):** Build automated routing and task generation based on hospital source and patient location
4. **Phase 4 (6+ months):** Implement CRM features to track referral relationships and outcomes by hospital and discharge planner

## Best Practices to Maximize Referrals from This Source

**1. Map Your Target Hospitals:**
- Create a spreadsheet of all MGB hospitals within your service area
- Identify discharge planning contacts at each hospital
- Track relationship status and referral volume by hospital

**2. Prioritize High-Volume Hospitals:**
- MGH and BWH are the highest-volume hospitals in the system—focus here first
- Community hospitals (Newton-Wellesley, North Shore) may have less competition

**3. Build Personal Relationships:**
- Remember discharge planners' names and personal details (kids, hobbies, favorite coffee)
- Send personalized thank-you notes after successful placements
- Visit in person quarterly (don't just send marketing materials)

**4. Respond Faster Than Competitors:**
- MGB discharge planners are busy—speed matters
- Aim to call patient within 1-2 hours of referral
- Confirm acceptance with discharge planner same day

**5. Provide Outcome Reports:**
- After successful intake, email discharge planner a brief update: "Patient settled in well, family very satisfied, care plan in place"
- Highlight positive outcomes (reduced ER visits, medication adherence, family relief)
- Use Mastering Home Care analytics to generate outcome reports

**6. Offer Solutions for Difficult Cases:**
- Position your agency as the "go-to" for challenging placements:
  - Last-minute discharges (same-day or next-day start)
  - Difficult family dynamics
  - Specialized care needs (dementia, veterans, multilingual)
  - 24/7 live-in care

**7. Educate on Non-Medical Home Care:**
- Many discharge planners conflate home health (medical) with home care (non-medical)
- Clarify that your services complement MGB Home Care: "We provide companion care and personal care while your home health team handles medical needs"

**8. Leverage MGB's ACO Focus:**
- MGB's ACO is incentivized to reduce readmissions
- Emphasize how your care coordination and monitoring prevent hospital returns
- Offer to share post-discharge check-in data

**9. Network Across the System:**
- Ask successful contacts for introductions to colleagues at other MGB hospitals
- "I've been working with [Name] at MGH—would you be open to a brief introduction call?"

**10. Track ROI by Hospital:**
- Use Mastering Home Care to measure referral volume, conversion rate, and revenue by MGB hospital
- Focus energy on highest-ROI relationships
- Identify and troubleshoot low-performing relationships
