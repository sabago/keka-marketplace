## Overview

UMass Memorial Health is the **largest health system in Central Massachusetts**, serving Worcester and surrounding communities with **4 hospitals** and 1,400+ beds. As the dominant healthcare provider in the region, UMass Memorial is the **#1 priority hospital partnership** for agencies serving Central MA.

**System Hospitals:**
1. **UMass Memorial Medical Center** (Worcester) - 781 beds, Level 1 Trauma Center, academic medical center
2. **HealthAlliance-Clinton Hospital** (Clinton/Leominster) - 133 beds
3. **Harrington Hospital** (Southbridge) - 114 beds
4. **Marlborough Hospital** (Marlborough) - 67 beds

**Geographic reach:** Worcester County + MetroWest + North Central MA

---

## Why UMass Memorial Matters

**Market dominance:**
- **Only Level 1 Trauma Center in Central MA** (all major trauma cases come here)
- **Only academic medical center between Boston and Springfield**
- Affiliated with UMass Chan Medical School (teaching hospital)
- **40%+ market share** in Worcester County

**For home care agencies:**
- If you serve Central MA, UMass Memorial partnership is **essential**
- High discharge volume (largest hospital system in region)
- Less competition than Boston hospitals (10-15 agencies vs. 30+ in Boston)
- Strong geriatrics program + growing senior population

- Hospitals post discharge referrals, agencies respond in real-time

- Direct communication with discharge planners via platform

**Recommendation:**
- **If you serve Central MA seriously:** CarePort investment worthwhile for UMass Memorial access
- **If testing Central MA market:** Build relationships without CarePort first, add platform later if volume justifies

## How to Get Started

### **Step 1: Commit to Central MA Market**

**Reality check:**
- UMass Memorial dominates Central MA (you can't ignore them if you serve Worcester area)
- Geographic coverage essential (serve Worcester + surrounding 30-60 towns)
- Less competitive than Boston (easier to break in)

**Decision:** If you serve Central MA, UMass Memorial is your #1 hospital priority.

### **Step 2: Visit UMass Memorial Medical Center (Worcester)**

**Start with the anchor:**
1. Call 508-334-1000, ask for discharge planning department
2. Request meeting with discharge planning manager
3. Visit **both campuses** (University Campus + Memorial Campus - separate teams)
4. Bring materials: brochure, service area map showing Worcester County coverage, business cards

**What to emphasize:**
- **Worcester County coverage:** "We serve all of Worcester County" (not just Worcester city)
- **Central MA focus:** "We specialize in Central MA" (vs. Boston agencies spreading thin)
- **Rapid response:** Same-day assessments, next-day care starts
- **Complex care capability:** Trauma, transplant, oncology support

### **Step 3: Expand to Community Hospitals**

**Once established at UMass Memorial Medical Center:**
4. Visit HealthAlliance-Clinton (North Central MA)
5. Visit Harrington (South Central MA)
6. Visit Marlborough (MetroWest bridge)

**Community hospital strategy:**
- Easier to build relationships (smaller teams)
- Demonstrate track record: "We serve UMass Memorial patients"
- Less competition (rural/suburban)

### **Step 4: Consider WellSky CarePort**

**Evaluate CarePort investment:**
- Track UMass Memorial referral volume for 6-12 months
- If receiving 10+ UMass referrals/month → CarePort likely worthwhile
- If receiving <5 referrals/month → Hold off on CarePort, focus on relationships

## Best Practices

**1. Serve entire Worcester County:**
- Don't just serve Worcester city - cover Auburn, Shrewsbury, Grafton, Millbury, Leicester, etc.
- UMass Memorial refers to agencies with broadest coverage

**2. Be prepared for complex patients:**
- Level 1 Trauma Center = medically complex discharges
- Train caregivers on trauma recovery, wound care basics, post-transplant care
- Don't shy away from challenging cases

**3. Build relationships with BOTH UMass campuses:**
- University Campus and Memorial Campus have separate discharge planning teams
- Visit both, introduce to both teams

**4. Partner with Central MA ASAPs:**
- Elder Services of Worcester Area (Worcester)
- Montachusett Home Care (Fitchburg/Leominster)
- UMass Memorial patients often qualify for MassHealth Frail Elder Waiver

**5. Consider CarePort investment if serious about Central MA:**
- UMass Memorial is CarePort power user
- Platform access = competitive advantage
- Evaluate ROI after 6-12 months of relationship building

**6. Don't compete with Boston agencies on price:**
- Central MA cost of living lower than Boston
- Boston agencies may undercut you on price to enter Worcester market
- Compete on **local presence, responsiveness, Worcester knowledge**

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "Boston agencies entering Worcester market"**
**Solution:** Emphasize local advantage - "We're based in Worcester, respond faster, understand local community." Boston agencies often slower to respond to Central MA calls.

**Challenge 2: "CarePort subscription expensive"**
**Solution:** Build relationships first without CarePort. If UMass Memorial volume justifies (10+ referrals/month), invest in platform. Many successful agencies serve UMass Memorial without CarePort.

**Challenge 3: "Complex trauma/transplant patients intimidating"**
**Solution:** Partner with UMass Memorial discharge planners - they provide detailed care plans, medication lists, MD contacts. Don't be afraid to ask questions, request training.

**Challenge 4: "Worcester County is huge - can't cover everything"**
**Solution:** Focus on Worcester city + 15-20 closest towns initially. Expand coverage over time as capacity grows.
