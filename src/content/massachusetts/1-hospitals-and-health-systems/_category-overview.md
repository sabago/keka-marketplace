## Overview

Massachusetts is home to **70+ hospitals** and multiple major health systems that generate thousands of home care referrals annually. Hospital discharge planners, case managers, and social workers are tasked with ensuring safe transitions home and actively seek qualified home care agencies to support patients post-discharge.

**Why Hospitals Matter:**
- **High-quality referrals:** Patients have immediate, documented need for care
- **100% FREE to agencies:** No cost to build relationships with hospital staff
- **Conversion rates:** 50-70% for warm hospital referrals
- **Multiple touchpoints:** Discharge planning, geriatrics, rehab, specialty clinics
- **Relationship-driven:** Success depends on trust, responsiveness, quality outcomes

This overview covers strategies for building hospital relationships across Massachusetts. Individual hospital profiles provide specific contact information and enrollment steps.

## How Home Care Agencies Get Hospital Referrals

### **Step 1: Identify Target Hospitals**

**Prioritize by:**
- **Geography:** Hospitals within your service area (15-30 minute drive)
- **Volume:** Larger hospitals = more discharge volume
- **Specialty:** Focus on hospitals with geriatric units, rehab, cardiology, orthopedics
- **Payer mix:** Hospitals serving your target payer mix (MassHealth, Medicare, private pay)

**Start with 3-5 target hospitals** rather than trying to cover all 70+.

### **Step 2: Build Relationships with Key Staff**

**Primary contacts:**
- **Discharge planners / Case managers** (RN, LSW, MSW)
- **Social workers** (particularly geriatric social work)
- **Geriatrics program staff**
- **Rehab services coordinators**
- **Specialty clinic nurses** (cardiology, oncology, neurology)

**How to connect:**
- **In-person visits:** Schedule 15-minute introduction meetings
- **Bring materials:** Professional one-pagers, business cards, agency brochure
- **Emphasize responsiveness:** "We return calls within 1 hour"
- **Share outcomes:** Success stories (with permission), satisfaction data
- **Attend hospital events:** Provider fairs, discharge planning meetings, lunch-and-learns

### **Step 3: Demonstrate Quality & Responsiveness**

**Hospitals prioritize agencies that:**
- **Respond immediately:** Answer calls/emails within 1 hour during business hours
- **Accept complex patients:** Willing to serve challenging situations
- **Communicate proactively:** Update hospital on patient status, prevent readmissions
- **Have broad coverage:** Serve entire hospital service area, not just select towns
- **Accept multiple payers:** MassHealth, Medicare, private pay, all insurance
- **Provide 24/7 availability:** Can start care same-day or next-day
- **Document thoroughly:** Comprehensive care plans, visit notes, medication reconciliation

**Quality metrics hospitals track:**
- Readmission rates for your patients
- Family satisfaction
- Discharge planning delays caused by home care availability
- Responsiveness time
- Staffing reliability

### **Step 4: Stay Top-of-Mind**

**Ongoing relationship maintenance:**
- **Quarterly check-ins:** Stop by with treats, say hello, ask how you can improve
- **Thank discharge planners:** When referrals convert, circle back with gratitude
- **Share outcomes:** Update hospital on patient progress (with patient permission)
- **Be consistent:** Use same coordinator for each hospital relationship
- **Holiday appreciation:** Cards, small gifts (within hospital gift policies)
- **Participate in hospital initiatives:** Patient safety committees, community programs

## Massachusetts Hospital Discharge Planning Regulations & Standards

**Hospitals must:**
- Provide patients/families with **choice of post-acute providers** (not just one option)
- Offer list of providers participating in patient's insurance plan
- Cannot financially benefit from referrals (Stark Law, Anti-Kickback Statute compliance)
- Must assess patient needs and match to appropriate level of care

**For agencies:**
- You have the right to market to hospitals
- Hospitals cannot require exclusive relationships
- Focus on quality, not financial incentives
- Build relationships based on patient outcomes, not payments/gifts

## Hospital Referral Platforms & Technology

### **1. MassHIway Health Information Exchange (100% FREE)**
- **What it is:** Statewide HIE with real-time hospital admission/discharge notifications
- **How it works:** Register for Event Notification Services (ENS), get alerts when your patients are admitted/discharged from any MA hospital
- **Value:** Proactive outreach to families, competitive advantage
- **Cost:** 100% FREE through HAUS program
- **Signup:** masshiway.net

### **2. Aidin Platform (FREE basic plan)**
- **What it is:** Cloud-based discharge planning platform connecting hospitals and home care agencies
- **How it works:** Hospitals post discharge referrals, agencies respond in real-time
- **Value:** Access to hospitals using platform, faster response times
- **Cost:** FREE basic plan, paid tiers for premium features
- **Signup:** myaidin.com

### **3. WellSky CarePort (Enterprise subscription)**
- **What it is:** Leading care coordination network used by 2,000+ hospitals including 130+ in MA
- **How it works:** Real-time referral exchange, care transitions management
- **Value:** Access to major health systems using CarePort (UMass Memorial, community hospitals)
- **Cost:** Enterprise subscription ~$10,000-$30,000/year
- **Best for:** Larger agencies, those targeting CarePort-using hospitals

## Regional Strategies

### **Metro Boston** (highest hospital density)
- Focus on relationship quality over quantity
- Target hospitals aligned with your payer mix
- Compete on specialized services (dementia care, post-stroke, etc.)
- Attend MHA (Massachusetts Hospital Association) events

### **Central Massachusetts** (Worcester area)
- UMass Memorial dominates - make this a priority
- Strong community hospital presence (St. Vincent, Harrington, Milford Regional)
- Less competition than Metro Boston
- Build relationships with all hospitals in region

### **Western Massachusetts** (Springfield/Berkshires)
- Baystate Health is critical
- Rural coverage is advantage (many agencies won't travel to Berkshires)
- Focus on reliable coverage across entire region
- Partner with Cooley Dickinson (Northampton)

### **North Shore**
- Mass General Brigham (Salem Hospital, North Shore Medical Center)
- Beverly Hospital (Beth Israel Lahey)
- Competitive market - emphasize quality differentiators
- Strong relationships with individual discharge planners essential

### **South Shore / Cape Cod**
- South Shore Hospital (Weymouth) is anchor
- Cape Cod Hospital, Falmouth Hospital for Cape agencies
- Seasonal population surges in summer
- Emphasize year-round reliability

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "We already have preferred agencies"**
**Solution:**
- Ask to be added as backup/weekend coverage
- Emphasize specialized services others don't offer
- Share success stories and outcomes data
- Be patient - relationships take 6-12 months to develop

### **Challenge 2: Slow response from discharge planners**
**Solution:**
- Visit in-person rather than relying on email/phone
- Bring lunch, offer to present at team meeting
- Connect via LinkedIn, attend hospital events
- Persistence pays off - most discharge planners are overwhelmed

### **Challenge 3: Referrals but no conversions**
**Solution:**
- Follow up immediately (within 1 hour)
- Accept patients even if not ideal fit
- Communicate outcomes back to hospital
- Build trust over time with consistency

### **Challenge 4: Competition from hospital-owned home health**
**Solution:**
- Focus on non-medical services (companion care, homemaker, PCAs)
- Position as complement to home health, not competitor
- Emphasize flexible scheduling, customized care plans
- Build relationships with hospital home health for referrals post-discharge

## Success Metrics to Track

**Measure your hospital referral performance:**
- **Number of hospital relationships:** Target 5-10 active relationships
- **Referral volume per hospital:** Track which hospitals refer most
- **Conversion rate:** % of hospital referrals that become clients
- **Response time:** How quickly do you follow up on hospital calls?
- **Readmission rate:** Do your clients return to hospital within 30 days?
- **Revenue from hospital referrals:** Hospital channel ROI

**Benchmarks:**
- Well-established agency: 20-50% of new clients from hospital referrals
- New agency: Start with 1-2 hospital relationships, grow over 12-24 months
- Conversion rate: 50-70% for warm hospital referrals

## Best Practices Summary

1. **Start small:** Focus on 3-5 target hospitals, not all 70
2. **Be responsive:** Answer calls within 1 hour, start care same-day/next-day
3. **Build relationships:** In-person visits, consistency, follow-through
4. **Communicate outcomes:** Share patient progress with hospital (with permission)
5. **Accept diverse patients:** Be willing to serve complex situations
6. **Use technology:** MassHIway (free), Aidin (free), CarePort (if budget allows)
7. **Be patient:** Hospital relationships take 6-12 months to mature
8. **Track metrics:** Measure what works, double down on successful hospitals
9. **Maintain visibility:** Quarterly check-ins, holiday appreciation, event attendance
10. **Never give up:** Discharge planners change jobs - keep showing up
