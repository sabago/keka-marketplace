## Overview

Hospital discharge planning is one of the most valuable referral sources for Massachusetts home care agencies. With over 70 acute care hospitals across the state performing hundreds of thousands of discharges annually, the discharge planning process represents a massive opportunity for agencies that can build strong relationships with hospital partners.

This guide provides practical strategies for Massachusetts home care agencies to establish and maintain successful partnerships with hospital discharge planning departments. Unlike platform-specific guides (Aidin, CarePort), this resource covers **general best practices** that work across all hospital systems, whether they use digital platforms or traditional referral methods.

**Key insight:** Hospital discharge planners are under immense pressure to:
- Discharge patients safely and quickly (CMS discharge within 2 days of medical stability)
- Reduce hospital readmissions (ACO quality metrics and bundled payment models create financial incentives)
- Match patients with appropriate post-acute care providers
- Coordinate complex care transitions across multiple services

Home care agencies that make discharge planners' jobs **easier, faster, and more reliable** will earn consistent referral flow.

## Who Sends Referrals Through This Source

Hospital discharge referrals come from:

- **Discharge planners / Case managers** - Primary coordinators of post-acute care placement
- **Social workers** - Address psychosocial needs and connect patients with community resources
- **Utilization review nurses** - Ensure appropriate level of care and payer authorization
- **Care coordinators** - Particularly within ACOs focused on care transitions
- **Physicians and nurse practitioners** - May directly recommend home care services
- **Specialty coordinators** - Oncology, cardiology, geriatrics, orthopedics, etc.

**Massachusetts hospital landscape:**
- **70+ acute care hospitals** across the state
- **Major academic medical centers:** Mass General Brigham, Boston Medical Center, UMass Memorial, Tufts Medical Center, Beth Israel Lahey Health
- **Regional health systems:** Wellforce, Steward Health Care (in transition), Trinity Health of New England, Baystate Health
- **Community hospitals:** Dozens of smaller hospitals serving local populations
- **ACOs:** 16 HPC-certified ACOs creating care coordination focus

## Who Can Receive Referrals

Discharge planners refer patients to multiple post-acute provider types. As a home care agency, you're competing/collaborating with:

- **Medicare-certified home health agencies** (skilled nursing, therapy)
- **Private-pay home care agencies** (non-medical support)
- **Skilled nursing facilities / rehab centers**
- **Hospice providers**
- **Outpatient therapy clinics**
- **DME suppliers**

**Your competitive advantage (private-pay home care):**
- Patients who **don't qualify** for Medicare home health (not homebound, no skilled need)
- **Supplement** to home health (companion care alongside skilled nursing)
- **24/7 care** (live-in caregivers, which home health doesn't provide)
- **Immediate start** (same-day or next-day vs. home health authorization delays)
- **Cultural/language match** (multilingual caregivers)

## How It Works (From an Agency's Perspective)

**Phase 1: Identify Target Hospitals**

1. Map hospitals in your service area
2. Prioritize by:
   - Proximity to your office (faster response times)
   - Discharge volume (larger hospitals = more referrals)
   - Patient demographics (alignment with your specialties)
   - Existing relationships (build on warm connections)

3. Research each hospital:
   - ACO affiliation (care transitions focus)
   - EHR system (Epic, Cerner, etc.)
   - Use of platforms (Aidin, CarePort, internal systems)
   - Ownership/system affiliation

**Phase 2: Build Relationships**

1. **Identify decision-makers:**
   - Discharge planning manager/director
   - Individual discharge planners and case managers
   - Social work department lead
   - Care coordination team (if separate)

2. **Make initial contact:**
   - Call hospital switchboard: "May I speak with discharge planning, please?"
   - Email or LinkedIn outreach to department managers
   - Ask for brief meeting (15-30 minutes)

3. **Conduct introductory meetings:**
   - **Bring:** Business cards, one-pagers, service menu, insurance certificate
   - **Discuss:** Your services, coverage area, specializations, response times, multilingual capabilities
   - **Ask:** Their referral process, preferred communication methods, pain points
   - **Offer:** To make their job easier (fast responses, outcome reports, flexible service offerings)

4. **Get on preferred provider lists:**
   - Request to be added to resource binders, EHR directories, or internal lists
   - Provide updated contact information and service details

**Phase 3: Receive and Process Referrals**

1. **Referral arrives via:**
   - Phone call from discharge planner
   - Fax (still very common in healthcare)
   - Email with patient information
   - Platform notification (Aidin, CarePort if hospital uses)
   - Family self-referral (discharge planner gave your contact info)

2. **Respond immediately:**
   - Answer or return call within 1-2 hours
   - Confirm receipt of fax/email
   - Accept or decline referral quickly (with honest explanation if declining)

3. **Schedule assessment:**
   - Coordinate with patient/family for initial visit
   - Aim for within 24-48 hours of discharge
   - Communicate timeline back to discharge planner

4. **Complete intake and start services:**
   - Begin services smoothly and on time
   - Notify discharge planner of successful start

**Phase 4: Maintain Relationships**

1. **Follow up after each referral:**
   - "Thank you for the referral. Mrs. Smith is settled in well, and family is very satisfied."
   - Report positive outcomes: "No ER visits in first 30 days, medication adherence excellent"

2. **Stay top-of-mind:**
   - Visit quarterly (bring donuts, coffee, or lunch)
   - Send holiday cards and thank-you notes
   - Provide service updates (new specializations, expanded coverage, additional languages)

3. **Be a problem-solver:**
   - Accept difficult cases (last-minute discharges, complex family dynamics, specialized needs)
   - Go above and beyond to support discharge planners

4. **Track your performance:**
   - Monitor response times, acceptance rate, patient outcomes
   - Share success metrics with hospital partners

## How to Get Listed / Enrolled

**Step-by-Step Process:**

**1. Create Professional Marketing Materials**
- **One-pager:** Agency overview, services, coverage area, contact info (make it scannable - bullet points!)
- **Service menu:** Detailed list of services (companion care, personal care, homemaker, dementia care, post-surgical support, etc.)
- **Business cards:** Direct intake line and email
- **Insurance certificates:** Proof of liability insurance and workers' compensation
- **Accreditation:** If you have Joint Commission, ACHC, CHAP, or other certifications
- **Testimonials:** From families and other referral sources

**2. Research Hospital Contacts**
- Call hospital main line: "I'd like to speak with someone in discharge planning about becoming a resource for patient referrals"
- Ask for: Discharge planning manager, case management director, social work lead
- Use LinkedIn to identify names and roles

**3. Schedule Meetings**
- Email or call to request 15-30 minute meeting
- Be flexible with their schedule (they're busy!)
- Offer to bring coffee/lunch

**4. Prepare Your Pitch (5 minutes or less)**
- **Who we are:** "[Agency Name] is a [years]-year-old home care agency based in [city]"
- **What we do:** "We provide non-medical companion care and personal care services"
- **Coverage:** "We serve [geographic areas]"
- **Specializations:** "We specialize in dementia care, post-surgical support, and multilingual care"
- **Value prop:** "We respond within 2 hours and can start services same-day or next-day"
- **Ask:** "How can we support your discharge planning process?"

**5. Deliver on Promises**
- First impression is everything—respond fast, deliver quality, follow up
- Every referral is an audition for the next one

**6. Maintain Presence**
- Visit quarterly
- Send updates on new services or staffing
- Attend hospital community events if invited
- Offer to present at grand rounds or team meetings

**Massachusetts Hospital-Specific Tips:**

- **ACO awareness:** Many MA hospitals are part of ACOs with readmission reduction incentives—emphasize care coordination and post-discharge monitoring
- **MassHealth presence:** If you accept MassHealth, highlight this (many hospitals serve MassHealth populations)
- **Multilingual:** MA is diverse—list all languages your caregivers speak (Spanish, Portuguese, Haitian Creole, Cape Verdean Creole, Mandarin, Vietnamese, etc.)
- **Geriatric focus:** MA has aging population—mention geriatric training, dementia certifications

## Cost and Lead Quality

**Cost:**
- **FREE** - No platform fees, enrollment costs, or per-lead charges
- Only investment is time (relationship-building) and marketing materials

**Lead Quality:**
- **Extremely high:** Patients have immediate, clinically-verified need
- **Payer mix:** Medicare Advantage, commercial insurance, private pay; some MassHealth
- **Acuity:** Post-acute (surgery, hospitalization), chronic disease management
- **Volume potential:** Well-connected agencies report 20-50+ hospital referrals/month across multiple hospitals
- **Conversion rate:** 70-90% because need is urgent and patient trusts hospital recommendation

**ROI:**
- **Time investment:** 20-40 hours upfront to establish relationships at 5-10 hospitals
- **Ongoing maintenance:** 2-4 hours/month per hospital (visits, follow-ups)
- **Payoff:** Consistent, high-quality referral flow for years

## Pros and Cons for Massachusetts Home Care Agencies

**Pros:**
- **Highest-quality referrals**—immediate need, appropriate acuity, well-insured
- **FREE**—no platform fees or commissions
- **Volume potential**—70+ hospitals across MA
- **Long-term relationships**—once established, generates consistent flow
- **Professional credibility**—hospital partnerships enhance agency reputation
- **Market intelligence**—learn about competitors, trends, payer changes

**Cons:**
- **Time-intensive**—building relationships requires face-to-face effort
- **Slow to start**—may take 3-6 months to see consistent referral flow
- **Relationship-dependent**—discharge planner turnover can disrupt flow
- **Competitive**—other agencies are also courting hospital partners
- **Response speed matters**—requires dedicated intake staff and fast processes
- **Outcome accountability**—hospitals may track readmissions and patient complaints

## How This Fits Mastering Home Care's Data Flow

Hospital referrals arrive via **multiple traditional channels**:

**Ingest Paths:**

1. **Phone:** Discharge planner calls intake line → manual entry into Mastering Home Care → **Enhancement:** speech-to-text transcription

2. **Fax:** Hospital faxes referral sheet → Mastering Home Care fax ingestion → OCR and LLM parse data → staff reviews

3. **Email:** Discharge planner emails referral → email parser extracts key fields → auto-populates intake form

**Automation Opportunities:**

- **Unified intake:** Regardless of channel (phone/fax/email), data flows into same Mastering Home Care intake system
- **Hospital-specific routing:** Tag referral with hospital source → route to appropriate coordinator → generate hospital-specific follow-up tasks
- **Response time tracking:** Set SLA alerts: "Respond to [Hospital Name] referral within 2 hours"
- **Relationship management:** Track referral volume by hospital and discharge planner → identify top relationships and under-performers
- **Outcome reporting:** Generate automated follow-up emails to discharge planners with patient status updates

**Integration Strategy:**

1. **Phase 1:** Manual entry from all channels into Mastering Home Care
2. **Phase 2:** OCR for faxes, email parsing for emails
3. **Phase 3:** Hospital-specific routing rules and SLA automation
4. **Phase 4:** CRM integration for relationship tracking and outcome reporting

## Best Practices to Maximize Referrals from This Source

**1. Speed is Everything**
- Answer intake line within 2 rings during business hours
- Return missed calls within 30 minutes
- Respond to faxes/emails within 1-2 hours
- Set up forwarding to cell phones for after-hours emergencies

**2. Make It Easy for Discharge Planners**
- Provide ONE intake number (not multiple numbers to try)
- Offer online referral form (simple, one-page)
- Accept referrals in their preferred format (some love fax, some prefer email)
- Never say "we're at capacity" without offering alternative (overflow partner, waitlist)

**3. Build Trust Through Consistency**
- Always follow up after successful intake
- Report problems immediately if they arise
- Be honest about limitations ("We can't start until Tuesday, but let me find you a partner who can start tomorrow")
- Track your promises and deliver on every one

**4. Differentiate Your Agency**
- Specializations: "We specialize in dementia care with certified staff"
- Language: "We have Spanish, Portuguese, and Haitian Creole-speaking caregivers"
- Geography: "We're the only agency covering Cape Cod year-round"
- Speed: "We can start services same-day or next-day"
- Flexibility: "We offer 24/7 live-in care, which home health doesn't provide"

**5. Solve Their Pain Points**
- **Difficult discharges:** Take last-minute cases that others decline
- **Complex families:** Handle challenging family dynamics with grace
- **Weekend coverage:** Be available for Friday discharges that need weekend starts
- **Readmission prevention:** Provide proactive monitoring and communication

**6. Track Performance by Hospital**
- Use Mastering Home Care analytics to measure:
  - Referral volume by hospital
  - Response time by hospital
  - Conversion rate by hospital
  - Revenue by hospital source
- Double down on top-performing relationships
- Troubleshoot or deprioritize low-performing relationships

**7. Network Within Hospitals**
- Ask successful contacts for introductions: "Who else in the system should I connect with?"
- Attend hospital community events and open houses
- Offer to present at grand rounds or staff meetings

**8. Provide Value Beyond Referrals**
- Share industry trends and best practices
- Connect discharge planners with other resources (DME suppliers, transportation services, senior housing)
- Offer to co-present at community health fairs

**9. Maintain Presence Through Turnover**
- Discharge planners change jobs frequently—stay engaged with department, not just individuals
- When key contact leaves, immediately ask for introduction to replacement

**10. Celebrate Successes Together**
- Share patient success stories (with HIPAA compliance)
- Highlight outcome improvements (ED visit reductions, medication adherence, family satisfaction)
- Thank discharge planners publicly (e.g., agency newsletter feature)
