## Overview

Mass General Brigham (MGB) is the **largest healthcare system in Massachusetts**, operating **12 hospitals** with 6,000+ beds and generating billions in annual revenue. As the dominant health system in Greater Boston and beyond, MGB is the **#1 priority hospital partnership** for any agency serving Eastern Massachusetts.

**MGB Hospitals:**
1. **Massachusetts General Hospital** (Boston) - 999 beds, #1 ranked hospital nationally
2. **Brigham and Women's Hospital** (Boston) - 793 beds, top-ranked women's hospital
3. **McLean Hospital** (Belmont) - Psychiatric specialty hospital
4. **Newton-Wellesley Hospital** - 313 beds, community hospital
5. **North Shore Medical Center** (Salem/Lynn) - Combined 395 beds
6. **Cooley Dickinson Hospital** (Northampton) - 140 beds, Pioneer Valley
7. **Martha's Vineyard Hospital** - Island community hospital
8. **Nantucket Cottage Hospital** - Island community hospital
9. **Wentworth-Douglass Hospital** (Dover, NH) - Seacoast region
10. **Salem Hospital** - North Shore
11. **Union Hospital** (Lynn) - North Shore
12. **Faulkner Hospital** (Jamaica Plain) - Community hospital

**Additional:** 200+ outpatient clinics, physician practices across Massachusetts

**Market dominance:** 25%+ market share in Greater Boston, 50%+ in some suburban markets

## Why Mass General Brigham Matters

**The MGB advantage:**
- **Volume:** Largest discharge volume in Massachusetts (50,000+ discharges annually across system)
- **Academic excellence:** Nationally ranked hospitals = complex medical cases (high home care needs)
- **Geographic reach:** Boston to North Shore to Pioneer Valley to Cape & Islands
- **Affluent patient base:** Many MGB patients have private insurance, long-term care insurance, assets for private pay
- **Integrated care:** MGB ACO focuses on readmission prevention (home care critical strategy)

**For home care agencies:**
- **Essential partnership:** If you serve Greater Boston/Metro Boston, MGB relationship is mandatory
- **High-quality referrals:** Professionally assessed needs, immediate care requirements
- **Multiple entry points:** 12 hospitals + 200+ clinics = many relationship opportunities
- **Competitive:** 30-50+ agencies target MGB (must differentiate to succeed)

## MGB System Strategy for Home Care Agencies

**Challenge:** 12 hospitals + 200+ clinics = overwhelming (can't build relationships everywhere)

**Recommended approach:**

### **Phase 1: Choose 2-3 Target MGB Hospitals**

**Based on your service area:**
- **Serve Greater Boston:** Target MGH + Brigham (flagship hospitals, highest volume) OR Newton-Wellesley (easier entry, affluent suburbs)
- **Serve North Shore:** Target North Shore Medical Center (Salem + Lynn)
- **Serve MetroWest:** Target Newton-Wellesley + Faulkner
- **Serve Western MA:** Target Cooley Dickinson (Northampton)

**Don't try to cover all 12 hospitals initially** (spread too thin).

### **Phase 2: Build Depth at 2-3 Hospitals**

**Focus on relationship quality:**
- Visit discharge planning departments quarterly
- Respond to EVERY referral within 4 hours (not 24 hours - MGB expectations higher)
- Communicate outcomes: Update discharge planners when patients doing well
- Attend MGB provider events (if offered)

### **Phase 3: Expand to Additional MGB Hospitals**

**After 12-18 months:**
- Once established at 2-3 MGB hospitals, expand to additional hospitals
- **Leverage success:** "We currently serve [Hospital A] patients excellently - we'd love to support [Hospital B] as well"

## How to Get Started with Mass General Brigham

### **Step 1: Assess Your Readiness**

**Reality check:**
- **MGB is extremely competitive** (30-50+ agencies already established)
- **High expectations:** Rapid response, medical complexity, communication excellence
- **Geographic coverage:** Serve entire regions (not just 2-3 towns)
- **Resources:** Can you respond same-day? Handle complex medical? Communicate daily?

**If yes:** Proceed to Step 2
**If no:** Consider starting with less competitive hospitals (Steward, community hospitals) to build track record first

### **Step 2: Choose Target MGB Hospital(s)**

**Start with 1-2 hospitals:**
- Match your service area (Newton-Wellesley if you serve MetroWest, North Shore Medical if you serve North Shore, etc.)
- **Consider competition:** Newton-Wellesley, North Shore, Cooley Dickinson = less competitive than MGH/Brigham

### **Step 3: Research Target Hospital**

**Before contacting:**
- Study hospital website (specialties, patient populations, services)
- Google reviews (understand patient experience)
- Search "Home care [Hospital Name]" (see which agencies already working with them - competition research)

### **Step 4: Contact Discharge Planning**

**Process:**
1. Call hospital main number
2. Ask for "discharge planning department" or "care transitions"
3. Request meeting with discharge planning manager
4. **In-person visit preferred** (not just phone)

**What to bring:**
- Professional brochure
- Business cards (50+)
- Service area map (show towns you cover)
- Quality metrics (if available): Readmission rates, satisfaction scores, caregiver training protocols

**What to emphasize:**
- **Rapid response:** "We assess same-day, start care within 24 hours"
- **Medical complexity:** "We're comfortable with post-transplant, oncology, complex wound care"
- **Communication:** "We update discharge planners proactively - you'll never wonder what's happening with your patients"
- **Geographic coverage:** "We serve all of [region] - 50+ towns"
- **Specializations:** "We specialize in [dementia, complex medical, 24/7 care, multilingual]"

### **Step 5: Deliver Excellence on First Referral**

**MGB discharge planners remember:**
- Did agency respond within 4 hours? (Not 24 hours - MGB expects faster)
- Did agency accept patient or decline? (Agencies that say "yes" to challenging cases = valued)
- Did agency communicate well? (Updates, problem-solving, follow-through)
- Was patient/family happy? (Discharge planners hear feedback)

**First impression determines future referrals.**

### **Step 6: Build Long-Term Relationship**

**Ongoing:**
- **Quarterly in-person visits:** Refresh business cards, check in, ask "How can we serve your patients better?"
- **Respond to EVERY referral:** Even if you can't accept, respond quickly and professionally ("We're at capacity but here's another agency to try")
- **Share outcomes:** Email discharge planner: "Just wanted to update you - Mr. Smith is doing wonderfully, no readmissions, family very happy"
- **Attend MGB events:** Provider conferences, quality improvement meetings (if invited)

## Best Practices

**1. Start with less competitive MGB hospitals:**
- Newton-Wellesley, North Shore, Cooley Dickinson = easier entry than MGH/Brigham
- Build track record, then approach flagship hospitals

**2. Specialize, don't generalize:**
- MGH/Brigham discharge planners work with 50+ agencies
- **Stand out:** "We specialize in complex post-transplant care" or "We're dementia care experts with certified trainers"
- Generalists get lost in crowd

**3. Geographic coverage essential:**
- MGB refers regionally (MGH refers to agencies serving 30-60 towns, not 3-4 towns)
- **Expand coverage or miss referrals**

**4. Invest in medical training:**
- MGH/Brigham patients = medically complex
- Train caregivers on: Post-surgical care, wound care, medication management, medical equipment, disease-specific care (CHF, COPD, diabetes)
- **Medical capability = competitive advantage**

**5. Communication excellence:**
- MGB discharge planners overwhelmed (manage 20-30 discharges daily)
- **Make their lives easier:** Proactive updates, fast responses, problem-solving (not just problem-reporting)

**6. Consider CarePort investment:**
- If you're serious about MGB, CarePort is near-mandatory
- Evaluate after 6-12 months of relationship-building

**7. Build relationships with MGB-affiliated physicians:**
- 200+ MGB outpatient clinics
- Primary care physicians refer to home care (not just hospital discharge planners)
- Visit high-volume geriatrics practices affiliated with MGB

**8. Partner with MGB ACO care coordinators:**
- Separate from discharge planners
- Focus on readmission prevention (home care key strategy)
- Ask hospital contacts: "Who are the ACO care coordinators? I'd like to introduce our agency."

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "Too competitive - can't break into MGH/Brigham"**
**Solution:** Start with Newton-Wellesley, North Shore, or Cooley Dickinson (less competitive MGB hospitals). Build track record serving MGB patients. Then approach MGH/Brigham: "We successfully serve Newton-Wellesley patients - we'd love to support MGH as well."

**Challenge 2: "CarePort subscription too expensive"**
**Solution:** (1) Build relationships without CarePort first (many agencies succeed without it), (2) Evaluate ROI after 12 months: If getting 10+ MGB referrals/month, CarePort pays for itself, (3) Partner with CarePort-connected agency (subcontract).

**Challenge 3: "MGB patients too medically complex"**
**Solution:** Invest in caregiver medical training. Partner with hospice, VNA, skilled home health for complex cases. Don't shy away - complex cases = higher hours, higher revenue. Train team to handle complexity.

**Challenge 4: "Can't compete with large agencies on price"**
**Solution:** Don't compete on price with MGB patients (many have insurance, private pay, LTC insurance). Compete on: (1) Specialization, (2) Quality, (3) Responsiveness, (4) Communication. MGB discharge planners value quality over price (patient outcomes matter).

**Challenge 5: "Spread too thin across 12 MGB hospitals"**
**Solution:** Focus on 2-3 hospitals maximum initially. Deep relationships at 2 hospitals > superficial contact at 12. Expand AFTER establishing at first hospitals.
