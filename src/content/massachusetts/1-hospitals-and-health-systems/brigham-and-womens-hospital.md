## Overview

Brigham and Women's Hospital (BWH) is a **world-renowned academic medical center** and the second-largest hospital in the Mass General Brigham system. With 793 licensed beds, BWH generates over **20,000 annual discharges** requiring home care support, making it one of the most valuable hospital partnerships for Massachusetts home care agencies.

**Why Brigham and Women's Matters for Home Care Agencies:**
- **Volume:** 4,000+ patients annually need home care post-discharge
- **Women's health focus:** #1 women's hospital in the nation = unique patient population
- **Medical complexity:** Harvard Medical School teaching hospital with complex cases
- **Orthopedic excellence:** Top-ranked joint replacement program = high home care volume
- **Cancer center partnership:** Dana-Farber affiliation = oncology home care needs
- **Affluent demographics:** Boston/Brookline location = private pay capability
- **Technology platform:** Uses WellSky CarePort for discharge coordination

**Hospital Profile:**
- **Address:** 75 Francis Street, Boston, MA 02115
- **Main phone:** 617-732-5500
- **Discharge planning:** 617-732-5500 (ask for discharge planning department)
- **Beds:** 793 licensed beds
- **Annual admissions:** 48,000+ inpatient admissions
- **Annual surgeries:** 32,000+ surgical procedures
- **Emergency visits:** 55,000+ annually
- **Affiliation:** Mass General Brigham, Harvard Medical School

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from Brigham and Women's Hospital

**BWH Discharge Planning Infrastructure:**

Brigham and Women's operates a comprehensive discharge planning department with **40+ discharge planners** organized by clinical service line. The department emphasizes care transitions, readmission prevention, and coordinated post-acute care.

**Referral Process - Step by Step:**

### **Step 1: Initial Agency Introduction**

**Contact Process:**
1. Call BWH main line: 617-732-5500
2. Ask operator: "Please connect me to the Discharge Planning Department"
3. Request: "I'd like to schedule a brief meeting with the Discharge Planning Manager to introduce our home care agency"
4. Schedule 15-20 minute in-person meeting

**Optimal timing:** Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-3pm (avoid Monday mornings and Friday afternoons)

**What to bring to initial meeting:**
- **Professional brochure** (50+ copies for team distribution)
- **Business cards** (100+ cards with your direct line)
- **Service area map** (visual showing towns you cover)
- **One-page agency overview:**
  - Agency name, contact info, website
  - Services offered (companion, personal care, specialized care)
  - Service area (specific towns)
  - Specialties (orthopedic post-surgical, women's health, dementia, multilingual)
  - Insurance accepted
  - Response time commitments
  - Unique differentiators
- **Quality metrics** (if available):
  - Readmission rates
  - Patient satisfaction scores
  - Caregiver training certifications
  - References from other hospitals

**Key message points:**
- "We specialize in caring for Brigham patients - orthopedic post-surgical care is our expertise"
- "We respond same-day for assessments, can start care within 24 hours"
- "We serve [X] towns across Boston and suburbs - broad geographic coverage"
- "Our caregivers are trained in [post-surgical care, women's health support, cardiac recovery]"
- "We communicate proactively with your team - you'll always know how your patients are doing"

### **Step 4: Demonstrate Rapid Response and Reliability**

**BWH Expectations:**

**Response time:** SAME-DAY or next-morning assessment
- BWH discharge planners expect faster response than community hospitals
- Competitive agencies respond within 2-4 hours
- Slow responders (24-48 hours) lose referrals to faster agencies

**Care start timeline:** Within 24 hours of assessment
- BWH patients often discharge same-day or next-day after referral
- Agencies that can start care immediately get more referrals

**Availability:** 7 days/week including holidays
- BWH discharges patients every day (including weekends, holidays)
- Weekend availability = competitive advantage

**Best practices for responsiveness:**
- Designate "hospital liaison" staff member (checks voicemail every 2 hours)
- Use call forwarding to manager cell phone for hospital calls
- Block 2-3 daily assessment slots for hospital referrals
- Have standby caregivers available for immediate placements
- Return every BWH call even if you can't accept that particular case (maintain relationship)

### **Step 5: Specialize in High-Value BWH Service Lines**

**Differentiation strategy:** Don't be a generalist. Specialize in BWH's top service lines.

**Specialization Examples:**

**"Orthopedic Post-Surgical Care Specialists"**
- Train all caregivers in post-joint-replacement care
- Create standardized "Post-Hip Replacement" and "Post-Knee Replacement" care protocols
- Offer fixed-price care packages (first 2 weeks, weeks 3-6)
- Market specifically to BWH orthopedics department
- **Result:** Become "go-to agency" for BWH orthopedic discharges

**"Women's Health Home Care"**
- Recruit primarily female caregivers
- Train in post-mastectomy care, drain management, emotional support
- Partner with breast cancer support organizations
- Emphasize compassionate, gentle approach
- **Result:** Preferred agency for BWH women's health referrals

**"Complex Cardiac Care"**
- Train caregivers in vital sign monitoring, cardiac symptoms recognition
- Provide blood pressure cuffs, weight scales
- Create cardiac diet meal planning guides
- Offer medication management protocols
- **Result:** Trusted partner for BWH cardiac patients

### **Step 6: Communicate Quality Outcomes**

**What BWH tracks:**
- **30-day readmission rates** (by home care agency)
- Patient/family satisfaction
- Care quality and safety
- Agency responsiveness
- Communication quality

**How to demonstrate excellence:**

**Proactive outcome reporting:**
- Call discharge planner 3-5 days after care starts
- **Script:** "Hi [name], this is [your name] from [agency]. I wanted to update you on Mrs. Johnson who you referred last week. She's progressing beautifully - mobilizing well with her walker, pain controlled, family very happy with our caregiver. No concerns. Thank you for the referral."
- **Frequency:** Every BWH referral for first 6-12 months (builds trust)

**Problem escalation:**
- If patient declining or at readmission risk, call discharge planner BEFORE patient returns to ER
- **Script:** "I'm concerned about Mr. Smith - his pain isn't controlled and he's not mobilizing as expected. I think he needs medical evaluation. Wanted to give you heads-up."
- **Result:** BWH appreciates proactive communication, views you as partner

**Readmission analysis:**
- Track which BWH patients readmit within 30 days
- Analyze why (preventable vs. unpreventable)
- Implement improvements
- Share learnings with BWH: "We noticed X pattern and implemented Y solution to reduce readmissions"

### **Step 7: Maintain Consistent Visibility**

**Relationship maintenance schedule:**

**Months 1-6 (Building Phase):**
- In-person visits: Monthly
- Purpose: Refresh business cards, stay visible, build rapport
- Duration: 5-10 minutes (discharge planners are busy)
- Bring: Coffee, small treats (check BWH gift policy)

**Months 7-24 (Growth Phase):**
- In-person visits: Quarterly
- Purpose: Strengthen relationship, ask for feedback
- Message: "How can we serve your patients better?"

**Year 2+ (Mature Phase):**
- In-person visits: Quarterly or bi-annually
- Purpose: Maintain relationship, provide updates
- Ongoing: Respond to every referral promptly

**Best times to visit:**
- Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-3pm
- Avoid: Monday mornings (busiest), Friday afternoons (low staffing)
- Never: During shift change (7am, 3pm, 11pm)

## Tips to Get More Referrals from Brigham and Women's Hospital

### **1. Focus on Orthopedics First (Highest Volume)**

**Why orthopedics:**
- 5,000+ joint replacements annually = largest BWH home care referral source
- Predictable care needs (easier to staff than complex medical)
- Defined duration (2-8 weeks typically)
- Well-insured patients (elective surgery = good insurance)
- Lower complexity than cardiac/oncology

**How to become orthopedic specialist:**
- Train all caregivers in post-joint-replacement care
- Create "Post-Hip/Knee Replacement Care Package" (structured care plan, fixed pricing)
- Market specifically to BWH orthopedics discharge planning
- Develop relationships with BWH orthopedic surgeons (they influence discharge planning)
- Provide excellent care to first few orthopedic referrals (reputation builds quickly)

**ROI:** Orthopedic specialization can generate 50-70% of your total BWH referral volume.

### **2. Recruit and Train Female Caregivers for Women's Health**

**BWH unique characteristic:** 65% female patient population, #1 women's hospital nationally.

**Opportunity:** Women's health patients often prefer female caregivers, especially for:
- Post-mastectomy care
- Gynecologic surgery recovery
- Personal care after women's health procedures

**Strategy:**
- Recruit predominantly female caregivers
- Train in women's health support (post-mastectomy care, drain management, emotional support)
- Emphasize compassionate, gentle approach
- Market to BWH women's health service lines: "Our female caregivers specialize in supporting women through post-surgical recovery"

**Competitive advantage:** Many agencies don't specifically market women's health expertise.

### **3. Invest in WellSky CarePort (After Testing)**

**Decision framework:**

**Year 1 approach:**
- Build BWH relationships WITHOUT CarePort (test market)
- Track referral volume monthly
- Calculate revenue from BWH channel

**After 12 months, evaluate:**
- If receiving 5+ BWH referrals/month ($50k-$150k annual revenue) → CarePort investment justified
- If receiving <3 referrals/month → Continue relationship building, evaluate CarePort later

**CarePort ROI calculation:**
- Cost: $20,000/year (typical pricing)
- If CarePort increases BWH referrals by 30-50% (common) = additional $15k-$75k annual revenue
- Payback period: 3-9 months

### **4. Serve Broad Geographic Area**

**BWH reality:** Refers to 80+ towns across Greater Boston, MetroWest, South Shore.

**Common mistake:** Agency serves only 5-10 towns, misses 75% of BWH referrals.

**Solution:** Expand service area to 30+ towns minimum.

**Priority towns for BWH:**
- **Boston:** All neighborhoods (Mission Hill, Jamaica Plain, Roxbury, Dorchester, South End, Back Bay)
- **Immediate suburbs:** Brookline, Newton, Dedham, Needham, West Roxbury
- **Cambridge area:** Cambridge, Somerville, Watertown, Arlington, Belmont
- **MetroWest:** Framingham, Natick, Wellesley, Weston, Wayland
- **South Shore:** Quincy, Braintree, Weymouth, Milton, Hingham

**Strategy:** Hire caregivers in outer regions, don't require all caregivers to cover entire service area.

### **5. Accept Complex Patients Others Decline**

**BWH discharge planner insight:** They remember agencies that say "yes" to challenging cases.

**Patient types others often decline:**
- High fall risk patients
- Behavioral issues (agitation, aggression)
- Complex family dynamics
- Hoarding situations
- Limited English proficiency without family translators
- Very obese patients (mobility challenges)
- Complex medical equipment (wound vacs, oxygen, feeding tubes)

**Strategy:**
- Train team to handle complexity
- Price appropriately for challenging cases
- Use most experienced caregivers for complex patients
- Say "yes" when others say "no"
- Build reputation as agency that "handles anything"

**Result:** Preferred agency for complex discharges (less competition, higher revenue).

### **6. Respond FAST - Same Day Assessment**

**BWH expectation:** Assessment within 4-8 hours of referral, care start within 24 hours.

**Competitive reality:** Fastest agencies get the most referrals.

**Best practices:**
- Designate hospital liaison (checks calls every 2 hours)
- Block 2-3 daily assessment slots for hospital referrals
- Offer same-day assessment: "We can see the patient this afternoon at 2pm or 4pm - which works better?"
- Don't say: "We can assess in 48 hours" (too slow for BWH)

**Competitive advantage:** Being 30% faster than competitors can increase referral volume by 40-60%.

### **7. Communicate Outcomes Proactively**

**Standard agency:** Only contacts discharge planner if there's a problem.

**Excellent agency:** Updates discharge planner on successes.

**Implementation:**
- Call discharge planner 3-5 days after care starts for every BWH referral
- Share positive outcomes: "Mrs. Smith is doing wonderfully - mobilizing well, family happy, no concerns"
- Report patient progress: "She's already walking without walker around house - ahead of expected recovery"
- Thank for referral: "Thank you for trusting us with your patient"

**Frequency:** Every BWH referral for first 6-12 months (builds trust and top-of-mind awareness).

**Result:** Discharge planners remember agencies that close the feedback loop.

### **8. Create BWH-Specific Care Packages**

**Concept:** Pre-designed care packages for common BWH discharge types.

**Example 1: "Post-Joint Replacement Recovery Package"**
- Weeks 1-2: 4 hours/day (medication, meals, mobility assistance, safety)
- Weeks 3-4: 3 hours/day (continued support, increased independence)
- Weeks 5-6: 2 hours/day (transition to full independence)
- Fixed package price: $4,800-$7,200
- Caregiver trained in post-surgical orthopedic care

**Example 2: "Post-Cardiac Surgery Recovery Package"**
- Weeks 1-2: 6 hours/day (vital signs, medication, cardiac diet meals, activity monitoring)
- Weeks 3-6: 4 hours/day (progressive activity increase)
- Weeks 7-8: 3 hours/day (transition to independence)
- Includes: Daily weight/BP log, medication schedule, cardiac diet meal plan
- Fixed package price: $7,200-$12,800

**Example 3: "Women's Health Post-Surgical Support Package"**
- Weeks 1-2: 4 hours/day (wound care, drain management, medication, emotional support)
- Weeks 3-4: 3 hours/day (continued recovery support)
- Female caregiver, compassionate approach
- Fixed package price: $3,600-$5,800

**Benefits:**
- Easier for BWH discharge planners (standardized care plans)
- Transparent pricing for families
- Predictable staffing for agency
- Demonstrates expertise and specialization

### **9. Build Relationships with BWH Physicians**

**Opportunity:** BWH physicians influence discharge planning.

**Strategy:**
- Identify high-volume BWH orthopedic surgeons (they do hundreds of joint replacements annually)
- Ask BWH discharge planners: "Which surgeons have the highest volume? I'd love to introduce our agency to them."
- Attend BWH grand rounds (if permitted)
- Connect with physicians via LinkedIn, introduce agency
- Provide excellent care to their patients (surgeons hear feedback from patients)

**Result:** Surgeons request your agency for their patients' discharge planning.

### **10. Partner with Dana-Farber Cancer Institute**

**BWH connection:** BWH has close partnership with Dana-Farber Cancer Institute.

**Opportunity:** Dana-Farber patients often need home care during treatment.

**Strategy:**
- Build separate relationship with Dana-Farber social workers
- Position agency as "cancer care support specialists"
- Train caregivers in: Post-chemotherapy support, symptom management, medication assistance, emotional support, transportation to appointments
- Many Dana-Farber patients discharge from BWH → dual hospital relationship valuable

### **11. Offer Multilingual Capabilities**

**BWH patient diversity:** 25% speak languages other than English.

**High-demand languages:**
- Spanish (largest non-English population)
- Portuguese (Brazilian community)
- Russian
- Chinese (Mandarin, Cantonese)
- Arabic
- Hebrew

**Competitive advantage:** Agencies with multilingual caregivers get referrals others can't serve.

**Implementation:**
- Recruit multilingual caregivers
- Highlight language capabilities in all BWH materials
- Offer immediate language match: "We can provide Spanish-speaking caregiver same-day"

### **12. Track and Minimize Readmissions**

**BWH tracking:** Monitors 30-day readmission rates by home care agency.

**Your strategy:**
- Track every BWH patient who readmits within 30 days
- Analyze: Was it preventable? What could we have done differently?
- Implement improvements: Earlier medical escalation, better medication management, more frequent check-ins
- Communicate improvements to BWH: "We reviewed our readmission data and implemented X protocol to reduce readmissions"

**Result:** BWH trusts agencies that demonstrate quality improvement commitment.

**Medium Agency with CarePort Example (Year 2):**
- Investment: $7,000 + $20,000 CarePort = $27,000
- Revenue: $612,000
- Gross margin (30%): $183,600
- **Net ROI:** $156,600 profit / $27,000 investment = **580% ROI**
- **Payback period:** 2 months

### **ROI Verdict: EXCELLENT**

Brigham and Women's Hospital is a **high-return referral source** with exceptional ROI for agencies willing to invest in relationship building and specialization.

**Best for:**
- Agencies serving Greater Boston, MetroWest, South Shore
- Agencies capable of orthopedic and women's health specialization
- Agencies comfortable with medical complexity
- Patient agencies (12-18 month relationship building timeline)

**Not ideal for:**
- Agencies outside BWH service area
- Agencies unwilling to invest in medical training
- Agencies seeking immediate results (takes 6-12 months to build volume)

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "BWH is very competitive - hard to stand out among 40+ agencies"**

**Reality:** BWH discharge planners work with 40-50 home care agencies. Very competitive market.

**Solutions:**

**A) Specialize in orthopedics or women's health**
- Don't be generalist (forgotten quickly)
- **Orthopedic specialist:** "We focus exclusively on post-joint-replacement care - it's all we do"
- **Women's health specialist:** "Female caregivers trained specifically in women's health post-surgical support"
- Specialists remembered, generalists forgotten

**B) Be fastest responder**
- Commit to same-day assessment
- Respond to BWH calls within 1-2 hours (not 4-24 hours)
- Fastest agencies get more referrals (simple competitive advantage)

**C) Accept complex patients others decline**
- Build reputation as agency that "says yes" to challenging cases
- BWH discharge planners remember agencies that help with difficult discharges

**D) Communicate outcomes proactively**
- Update discharge planners on successes (not just problems)
- Close the feedback loop (discharge planners appreciate knowing outcomes)

### **Challenge 2: "BWH patients too medically complex for our caregivers"**

**Reality:** BWH is Harvard Medical School teaching hospital - patients more complex than community hospitals.

**Solutions:**

**A) Start with orthopedics (less complex)**
- Post-joint-replacement care = predictable, manageable complexity
- Build experience with orthopedic patients, then expand to cardiac/oncology

**B) Invest in medical training**
- Train caregivers in: Vital signs, medication assistance, post-surgical care, medical equipment
- Hire CNAs/HHAs (more training than PCAs)
- Partner with skilled home health for complex medical needs

**C) Use experienced caregivers for BWH patients**
- Don't send brand-new caregivers to complex BWH cases
- Match caregiver experience to patient complexity

**D) Charge appropriately**
- BWH patients often well-insured, private pay capability
- Price reflects complexity, training, expertise required

### **Challenge 3: "Can't afford WellSky CarePort subscription"**

**Reality:** CarePort $10k-$30k/year - significant investment for small agencies.

**Solutions:**

**A) Build relationships WITHOUT CarePort first (Year 1)**
- Test BWH market via phone/fax referrals
- Track referral volume and revenue
- Evaluate CarePort ROI after 12 months

**B) ROI analysis before investing:**
- If receiving 5+ BWH referrals/month ($60k-$150k annual revenue) → CarePort justified
- If receiving <3 referrals/month → Continue building relationships, evaluate later

**C) Partner with CarePort agency**
- Subcontract arrangement with CarePort-connected agency
- They receive referrals, subcontract to you
- Share margin

**D) Consider Mass General Brigham enterprise pricing**
- If targeting multiple Mass General Brigham hospitals (MGH, BWH, Newton-Wellesley), enterprise CarePort subscription covers all
- Better ROI than single-hospital subscription

### **Challenge 4: "Geographic coverage gaps - don't serve all BWH referral areas"**

**Reality:** BWH refers to 80+ towns. Most agencies serve 10-30 towns.

**Solutions:**

**A) Expand strategically to high-volume gap areas**
- Ask BWH discharge planners: "Which towns do you refer to most often?"
- Hire 1-2 caregivers in most common gap areas
- Example: If you serve Boston but not MetroWest, hire Framingham-based caregiver

**B) Be transparent about coverage limits**
- When BWH refers outside service area: "Thank you for thinking of us. We don't currently serve [town], but here's an agency that does: [referral]"
- Discharge planners appreciate honesty and helpfulness

**C) Accept select out-of-area cases**
- High-value patients (private pay, long-term potential)
- Pay caregiver mileage for out-of-area cases
- Make exceptions for excellent opportunities

**D) Partner with other agencies**
- Reciprocal referral arrangement with agencies serving areas you don't
- Example: You serve Boston, partner with MetroWest agency, cross-refer

### **Challenge 5: "Long sales cycle - taking 6+ months to get referrals"**

**Reality:** BWH relationships take 6-12 months to mature (normal for competitive teaching hospital).

**Solutions:**

**A) Adjust expectations**
- BWH is long-term investment (not quick win)
- Compare to building 5 community hospital relationships (similar timeline, similar volume)

**B) Focus on multiple hospitals simultaneously**
- Don't put all efforts into BWH only
- Build 5-8 hospital relationships concurrently
- Some convert faster than others

**C) Celebrate relationship milestones (not just referrals)**
- First meeting completed
- Business cards distributed across department
- First referral call (even if didn't convert)
- Track progress, stay motivated

**D) Generate other referral sources while building BWH**
- Council on Aging partnerships (faster to launch)
- Senior living communities (faster to launch)
- Physician practices (faster to launch)
- BWH = long-term asset, but don't wait 12 months for revenue

### **Challenge 6: "BWH discharge planners don't return calls"**

**Reality:** BWH discharge planners manage 15-25 discharges daily, receive 10-20 agency calls daily. Overwhelmed.

**Solutions:**

**A) In-person visits work much better than calls**
- Schedule face-to-face meetings (even 5-10 minutes)
- Show up quarterly to refresh relationship
- Human connection > voicemails

**B) Be helpful, not salesy**
- Don't call just to ask: "Do you have referrals?"
- Call to offer value: "How can we better serve your patients? Any feedback on our service?"

**C) Communicate outcomes (give them reason to call you back)**
- Update on patient successes
- Provide feedback on discharge process
- Demonstrate you're partner, not just vendor

**D) Persistence with patience**
- Takes 8-12 touchpoints to break through in competitive environment
- Keep showing up, stay positive, don't give up
