## Overview

Newton-Wellesley Hospital (NWH) is a **313-bed community hospital** and proud member of the Mass General Brigham health system. Located in affluent Newton, Massachusetts, NWH generates approximately **3,500+ annual discharges** requiring home care support, making it an **excellent referral source** for agencies serving MetroWest and Greater Boston.

**Why Newton-Wellesley Hospital Matters for Home Care Agencies:**
- **Less competitive than MGH/Brigham:** Easier entry point into Mass General Brigham system (15-20 agencies vs. 50+ at MGH)
- **Affluent patient population:** Newton, Wellesley, Needham = highest median incomes in MA ($150k-$200k+)
- **Private pay capable:** High percentage of patients with LTC insurance, assets, private pay capability
- **Community hospital culture:** Closer relationships with discharge planners than large teaching hospitals
- **Predictable case mix:** Strong maternity, orthopedics, general surgery programs = steady home care needs
- **Geographic concentration:** Serves compact MetroWest area (easier to cover than MGH's 100-town service area)
- **Mass General Brigham quality:** MGB resources and standards with community hospital accessibility

**Hospital Profile:**
- **Address:** 2014 Washington Street, Newton, MA 02462
- **Main phone:** 617-243-6000
- **Discharge planning:** 617-243-6000 (ask for discharge planning department)
- **Beds:** 313 licensed beds
- **Annual admissions:** 15,000+ inpatient admissions
- **Annual births:** 4,000+ deliveries (largest maternity program in MetroWest)
- **Emergency visits:** 45,000+ annually
- **Affiliation:** Mass General Brigham, Partners HealthCare

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from Newton-Wellesley Hospital

**NWH Discharge Planning Structure:**

Newton-Wellesley operates a **community-sized discharge planning department** (approximately 10-15 discharge planners and care coordinators) organized by clinical unit. This smaller team size creates **opportunity for closer relationships** compared to MGH/Brigham's 40-50 person teams.

**Referral Process - Step by Step:**

### **Step 1: Initial Agency Introduction**

**Contact Process:**
1. Call NWH main line: 617-243-6000
2. Ask operator: "Please connect me to the Discharge Planning Department"
3. Introduce yourself: "Hi, I'm [name] from [agency name]. We provide home care services in Newton, Wellesley, and surrounding MetroWest towns. I'd love to schedule a brief meeting to introduce our agency to your team."
4. Request: Meet with Discharge Planning Manager or lead discharge planner
5. Schedule: 15-20 minute in-person meeting

**Best timing for initial contact:**
- **Tuesday-Thursday, 10am-2pm** (avoid Mondays, Fridays, early mornings, late afternoons)
- **NOT during holiday weeks** (discharge planning overwhelmed)

**What to bring:**
- **Agency brochure** (30-50 copies - smaller team than MGH)
- **Business cards** (50+ cards with direct phone/cell number)
- **Service area map** showing MetroWest coverage
- **One-page overview:**
  - Services (companion, personal care, postpartum support, specialized care)
  - Service area (Newton, Wellesley, Needham, Natick, Weston, Dover, Dedham, Watertown, Waltham)
  - Specialties (postpartum care, orthopedic post-surgical, dementia, 24/7 care)
  - Response time (same-day assessment, 24-hour care start)
  - Insurance accepted (all commercial, Medicare, Medicaid, private pay)
  - What makes you different (specialization, quality, responsiveness)

**Key positioning for NWH:**
- "We specialize in serving Newton-Wellesley's affluent patient population with white-glove, personalized care"
- "Our postpartum care specialists support new mothers during the first weeks at home"
- "We understand the high expectations of Newton, Wellesley, and Needham families"
- "We respond same-day for assessments and can start care within 24 hours"
- "We serve the entire MetroWest corridor - Newton to Natick to Framingham"

### **Step 2: Understand NWH's Referral System**

**Technology:**
- Newton-Wellesley uses **WellSky CarePort** (same as MGH/Brigham - Mass General Brigham system-wide)
- CarePort gives real-time referral access
- **CarePort NOT required for NWH referrals** (community hospital = more phone/fax referrals than MGH)
- **However:** CarePort helpful if you're pursuing multiple Mass General Brigham hospitals

**Referral channels:**
1. **Phone call** (most common) - Discharge planner calls agency directly
2. **CarePort** (if agency subscribed)
3. **Fax** (sometimes used for detailed patient info)
4. **Email** (occasional)

**Timeline:**
- **Notice:** 24-48 hours before discharge (sometimes same-day for urgent)
- **Assessment window:** Same-day or next-day expected
- **Care start:** Within 24-48 hours of discharge

### **Step 3: Target High-Volume Units**

**Strategic approach:** Focus on 2-3 highest-volume units rather than trying to meet all discharge planners.

**Highest-Volume Units for Home Care:**

**1. Maternity/Postpartum (HIGHEST VOLUME, EASIEST ENTRY)**
- **Contact:** 617-243-6000, ask for "maternity discharge planning" or "women's health discharge planning"
- **Volume:** VERY HIGH (4,000+ deliveries annually, 20-30% need postpartum support = 800-1,200 postpartum referrals/year)
- **Patient needs:** Postpartum recovery, newborn care assistance, household support
- **Duration:** 1-4 weeks typically
- **Competition:** MODERATE (fewer agencies offer specialized postpartum care)
- **Entry barrier:** LOW (less complex than medical/surgical, easier to break in)

**Why postpartum is ideal NWH entry point:**
- Highest volume of any NWH service line
- Less complex than medical/surgical (easier for new agencies)
- Less competition (many agencies don't specialize in postpartum)
- Affluent patients can afford private pay ($25-$35/hour × 20-40 hours = $500-$1,400/week)
- Relationship building opportunity (families remember excellent postpartum care, may hire for elderly parents)

**How to position for postpartum:**
- "We specialize in postpartum and newborn care - supporting Newton and Wellesley families through the first weeks at home"
- "Our caregivers are trained in newborn care, breastfeeding support, postpartum recovery"
- "We understand the needs of first-time mothers and families with twins/multiples"
- Hire caregivers with postpartum/newborn experience
- Consider doula training or newborn care specialist certifications

**2. Orthopedics**
- **Contact:** 617-243-6000, ask for "orthopedics discharge planning"
- **Volume:** HIGH (500+ joint replacements annually)
- **Patient needs:** Post-surgical care, mobility assistance, wound care, medication management
- **Duration:** 2-8 weeks
- **Competition:** MODERATE-HIGH
- **Entry barrier:** MODERATE (need post-surgical care capabilities)

**3. Medical/Surgical Units**
- **Contact:** 617-243-6000, ask for "medical discharge planning" or "surgical discharge planning"
- **Volume:** MODERATE-HIGH (general medicine and surgery patients)
- **Patient needs:** Vary widely (post-surgical, chronic disease management, geriatric support)
- **Duration:** 2 weeks to ongoing
- **Competition:** MODERATE
- **Entry barrier:** MODERATE (need medical complexity capabilities)

### **Step 4: Demonstrate Understanding of Affluent Patient Expectations**

**NWH patient reality:** Newton, Wellesley, Needham families expect **white-glove service**, not basic care.

**What affluent patients expect:**
- **Professionalism:** Well-dressed caregivers, polite communication, respectful presence
- **Reliability:** On-time arrivals, consistent caregiver, no call-offs
- **Quality:** Trained, experienced, competent caregivers (not entry-level)
- **Communication:** Proactive updates, responsive to concerns, problem-solving
- **Personalization:** Customized care plans, flexibility, attention to preferences
- **Discretion:** Privacy, confidentiality, non-intrusive presence

**How to position your agency:**
- "We understand the expectations of Newton and Wellesley families"
- "Our caregivers are trained professionals who provide white-glove, personalized care"
- "We carefully match caregiver personality and skills to each family's preferences"
- "Many of our clients are private pay - they choose us for quality, not because insurance requires us"
- "We view ourselves as part of your care team, supporting your recovery with excellence"

**Operational excellence required:**
- Send your BEST caregivers to NWH referrals (not entry-level)
- Ensure caregivers dress professionally
- Train on etiquette, communication, professionalism
- Quality control: Supervisor check-ins, family satisfaction calls
- No tolerance for call-offs, late arrivals, unprofessionalism

### **Step 5: Offer Flexible Private Pay Options**

**NWH opportunity:** High percentage of patients can afford private pay.

**Why private pay matters:**
- Higher margins than insurance reimbursement
- More flexibility in services offered
- Less paperwork/authorization
- Families willing to pay for quality
- Can customize care beyond insurance limits

**Private pay strategies for NWH:**

**1. Transparent pricing:**
- Provide clear rate card: "$30/hour companion care, $35/hour personal care, $40/hour specialized care"
- Package pricing for common needs: "Post-surgical recovery package: 4 hours/day × 14 days = $1,960"
- No hidden fees, simple pricing

**2. Value-based positioning:**
- Don't compete on price (NWH families don't choose cheapest option)
- Compete on quality, reliability, personalization
- "Our rates reflect our caregiver quality, training, and service excellence"

**3. Flexible engagement:**
- Short-term care (1-2 weeks for post-surgical)
- Trial periods ("Try us for 1 week, see if we're the right fit")
- Easy increase/decrease hours based on needs

**4. Concierge touches:**
- "We'll coordinate with your other providers (PT, VNA, physician)"
- "We'll grocery shop, pick up prescriptions, handle household tasks"
- "We provide care plan updates to family members (especially helpful if adult children live out-of-state)"

### **Step 6: Respond Rapidly**

**NWH expectation:** Same-day or next-morning assessment.

**Competitive reality:** 15-20 agencies pursue NWH. Fastest responders get most referrals.

**Best practices:**
- Return NWH calls within 1-2 hours (not 4-24 hours)
- Offer specific assessment times: "We can assess at 2pm today or 9am tomorrow - which works better?"
- Block 1-2 daily assessment slots for hospital referrals
- Weekend availability (NWH discharges on weekends)
- Holiday availability

**Response time competitive advantage:**
- Being 2-3 hours faster than competitors can increase referral volume by 30-50%

### **Step 7: Build Personal Relationships (Community Hospital Advantage)**

**NWH advantage vs. MGH:** Smaller discharge planning team = easier to build close relationships.

**Relationship-building strategies:**

**1. Quarterly in-person visits**
- Stop by discharge planning office every 3 months
- Bring coffee, donuts, or treats (check hospital policy)
- Duration: 5-10 minutes (brief hello, refresh business cards)
- Ask: "How can we better serve your patients? Any feedback on our service?"

**2. Personalize relationships**
- Learn discharge planner names
- Remember personal details (birthdays, interests, family)
- Send holiday cards
- Genuine friendship, not just business

**3. Communicate outcomes**
- Call discharge planner 3-5 days after care starts: "Mrs. Smith is doing wonderfully - wanted to update you"
- Share successes: "The family sent us a beautiful thank-you note - I wanted to share it with you"
- Close feedback loop

**4. Be helpful beyond referrals**
- When you can't accept referral, suggest alternative agency
- Answer discharge planner questions about home care industry
- Problem-solve together
- Position as partner, not just vendor

### **Step 8: Leverage Mass General Brigham System Connections**

**Strategy:** If you establish at NWH, leverage for introductions to other Mass General Brigham hospitals.

**Approach after 12 months at NWH:**
1. Build strong relationship at NWH (12-18 months)
2. Accumulate success stories, testimonials
3. Approach MGH/Brigham discharge planning: "We successfully serve Newton-Wellesley patients - we'd love to support MGH/Brigham as well"
4. Use NWH as reference: "Contact [discharge planner name] at Newton-Wellesley - they can speak to our work"

**Mass General Brigham system benefit:**
- NWH = easier entry point than MGH/Brigham (less competition)
- Success at NWH = credibility for other MGB hospitals
- Potential CarePort investment justified if expanding across MGB system

- Investment: $5,000 + $2,000 postpartum training = $7,000 (Year 1)
- Revenue: $52,500 (Year 1)
- Gross margin (35%): $18,375
- **Net ROI:** $11,375 profit / $7,000 investment = **162% ROI**
- **Payback period:** 4-5 months

**Medium Agency Example:**
- Investment: $5,500 (Year 1)
- Revenue: $216,000 (Year 1)
- Gross margin (32%): $69,120
- **Net ROI:** $63,620 profit / $5,500 investment = **1,157% ROI**
- **Payback period:** 1-2 months

### **ROI Verdict: EXCELLENT**

Newton-Wellesley Hospital is a **high-ROI referral source** with several advantages:
- **Lower competition** than MGH/Brigham (easier entry)
- **Affluent market** (private pay capability, quality expectations)
- **Community hospital culture** (relationship-driven)
- **Mass General Brigham system** (access to MGB quality and resources)
- **Postpartum opportunity** (high volume, low competition)

**Best for:**
- Agencies serving MetroWest (Newton, Wellesley, Needham, Natick)
- Agencies targeting affluent market
- Agencies seeking easier entry into Mass General Brigham system
- Postpartum care specialists
- Agencies preferring relationship-driven sales vs. high-competition environments

**Not ideal for:**
- Agencies outside MetroWest service area
- Agencies competing primarily on low price (affluent market expects quality)

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "We're not sure we can serve affluent Newton/Wellesley market"**

**Reality:** Newton, Wellesley, Needham families have high expectations.

**Solutions:**

**A) Upgrade your service quality**
- Hire experienced, professional caregivers (not entry-level)
- Train on: Professional appearance, communication, etiquette
- Quality control: Supervisor visits, family satisfaction calls
- Invest in excellence

**B) Position as premium service**
- Market as "white-glove" or "personalized" care
- Price accordingly ($32-$40/hour)
- Attract families who value quality

**C) Learn from feedback**
- Ask families: "What would make our service even better?"
- Continuously improve based on feedback
- Demonstrate commitment to excellence

**D) Start small, build reputation**
- Begin with 3-5 NWH clients, deliver exceptional service
- Build testimonials and references
- Expand as reputation grows

### **Challenge 2: "Don't have postpartum care expertise"**

**Reality:** Postpartum is NWH's highest-volume referral source, but many agencies lack expertise.

**Solutions:**

**A) Invest in training**
- Newborn Care Specialist certification: $500-$1,000
- Postpartum doula training: $800-$1,500
- Online courses: Various options $100-$500
- **ROI:** Postpartum training pays for itself with 2-4 clients

**B) Hire experienced caregivers**
- Recruit mothers with newborn care experience
- Recruit nannies transitioning to elder care
- Recruit retired nurses/CNAs with OB experience

**C) Partner with postpartum experts**
- Network with doulas, lactation consultants
- Offer to refer for specialized needs
- Learn from their expertise

**D) Start simple, build complexity**
- Begin with basic household support (meals, laundry)
- Add newborn care assistance gradually
- Build expertise over time

### **Challenge 3: "Can't compete on price with larger agencies"**

**Reality:** NWH market can afford premium pricing.

**Solutions:**

**A) Don't compete on price**
- Affluent market doesn't choose cheapest option
- Compete on: Quality, professionalism, personalization, reliability
- "You get what you pay for"

**B) Value-based pricing**
- Charge $32-$40/hour (premium pricing)
- Justify with: Caregiver quality, training, service excellence
- Emphasize: "Our caregivers are selected for experience and expertise"

**C) Demonstrate value**
- Professional presentation
- Polished materials (brochure, business card, website)
- Quality references and testimonials
- Caregiver background checks, training documentation

**D) Package pricing**
- "Post-surgical recovery package: $4,800 for 2 weeks"
- Transparent, simple, professional
- Families appreciate predictability

### **Challenge 4: "Geographic coverage gaps in outer MetroWest"**

**Reality:** NWH refers to Natick, Wayland, Dedham, Waltham - not just Newton/Wellesley.

**Solutions:**

**A) Strategic expansion**
- Ask NWH discharge planners: "Which towns do you refer to most often?"
- Hire 1-2 caregivers in most common gap areas
- Example: If you serve Newton/Wellesley but not Natick, hire Natick-based caregiver

**B) Zone-based staffing**
- Don't require all caregivers to cover entire area
- Zone 1: Newton/Wellesley
- Zone 2: Needham/Dedham/Weston
- Zone 3: Natick/Wayland/Framingham

**C) Transparent about coverage**
- When NWH refers outside service area: "Thank you for thinking of us. We don't currently serve [town], but here's an agency that does: [referral]"
- Discharge planners appreciate honesty

**D) Accept select out-of-area cases**
- High-value clients (private pay, long-term potential)
- Pay caregiver mileage for out-of-area cases
- Make exceptions strategically

### **Challenge 5: "NWH discharge planners slow to refer to new agency"**

**Reality:** NWH discharge planners work with 15-20 established agencies. Takes time to break in.

**Solutions:**

**A) Patience (6-12 month timeline normal)**
- Relationship building takes time
- First referral: 3-6 months typically
- Consistent referrals: 9-18 months

**B) Persistent visibility**
- Quarterly in-person visits
- Holiday cards
- Proactive communication
- Stay top-of-mind

**C) Leverage initial referrals**
- Deliver exceptional service on first 2-3 referrals
- Ask families for feedback/testimonials
- Share successes with discharge planners
- Build reputation quickly

**D) Focus on less competitive service line**
- Postpartum = less competition than medical/surgical
- Easier entry point
- Build trust, then expand to other units
