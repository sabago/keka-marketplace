## Overview

Steward Health Care operates **9 community hospitals** across Massachusetts, serving diverse urban and suburban populations in Greater Boston, South Shore, North Shore, and Merrimack Valley regions. With 1,200+ beds and millions of patient visits annually, Steward represents a significant hospital referral opportunity for home care agencies.

**Steward MA Hospitals:**
1. **St. Elizabeth's Medical Center** (Brighton) - 330 beds
2. **Good Samaritan Medical Center** (Brockton) - 190 beds
3. **Holy Family Hospital** (Methuen/Haverhill) - 341 beds
4. **Carney Hospital** (Dorchester) - 159 beds
5. **Norwood Hospital** (Norwood) - 188 beds
6. **Morton Hospital** (Taunton) - 149 beds
7. **St. Anne's Hospital** (Fall River) - 160 beds
8. **Nashoba Valley Medical Center** (Ayer) - 42 beds
9. **Good Samaritan Medical Center** (satellite campuses)

**Geographic reach:** Metro Boston, South Shore, North Shore, Merrimack Valley, Southeast MA

---

## Why Steward Matters

**Community hospital network:**
- All Steward hospitals are **community hospitals** (not academic medical centers)
- Serve working-class, diverse populations (Spanish, Portuguese, Haitian Creole communities)
- Higher MassHealth population than many MA hospitals
- Strong emergency services + medical/surgical care

**For home care agencies:**
- **Less competition than academic centers:** Community hospitals have 8-15 agencies vs. 30+ at Mass General/Brigham
- **Easier relationship building:** Smaller discharge planning teams, more accessible
- **Geographic diversity:** Steward hospitals span Metro Boston to Fall River (broad agency reach)
- **Cultural competency valued:** Multilingual capabilities essential for Steward patient populations
- **MassHealth enrollment critical:** 40-50%+ Steward patients are MassHealth

## Steward Network Strategy

**Steward hospitals operate independently:**
- Each hospital has own discharge planning team
- NOT centralized referral system (unlike Mass General Brigham)
- **Agencies must build relationships hospital-by-hospital**

**Network advantages:**
- Reputation with one Steward hospital helps with others
- Demonstrate: "We serve St. Elizabeth's patients successfully" → credibility for Carney, Good Samaritan, etc.
- Share quality metrics across Steward hospitals

**Geographic strategy:**
- Don't try to cover all 9 Steward hospitals unless you serve statewide
- **Focus on 2-3 Steward hospitals in your service area:**
  - Serve Metro Boston → St. Elizabeth's (Brighton) + Carney (Dorchester)
  - Serve South Shore → Good Samaritan (Brockton) + Norwood Hospital
  - Serve Merrimack Valley → Holy Family (Methuen/Haverhill)
  - Serve Southeast MA → Morton (Taunton) + St. Anne's (Fall River)

## How to Get Started

### **Step 1: Choose Target Steward Hospital(s)**

**Match service area:**
- Serve Brighton/Allston → St. Elizabeth's
- Serve Brockton/South Shore → Good Samaritan
- Serve Merrimack Valley → Holy Family
- Serve Dorchester → Carney
- Serve Norwood/Route 1 corridor → Norwood Hospital
- Serve Taunton/Southeast MA → Morton Hospital
- Serve Fall River → St. Anne's
- Serve North Central MA → Nashoba Valley

**Start with 1-2 Steward hospitals** closest to your agency base.

### **Step 2: Contact Discharge Planning**

**Process:**
1. Call hospital main number (listed above)
2. Ask for "discharge planning department" or "case management"
3. Request meeting with discharge planning manager
4. Visit in person (bring brochure, business cards, service area map)

**What to emphasize:**
- **Multilingual capability:** Steward hospitals serve diverse populations (Spanish, Portuguese, Haitian Creole critical)
- **MassHealth enrollment:** Most Steward hospitals have high MassHealth populations (confirm you accept MassHealth)
- **Geographic coverage:** Show which towns you serve in hospital's region
- **Cultural competency:** Emphasize understanding of immigrant communities, multi-generational households

### **Step 3: Build Relationship Over Time**

**Community hospital advantages:**
- **Smaller teams = easier access:** Steward discharge planners more accessible than Mass General/Brigham
- **Relationship-driven:** Get to know discharge planners by name, visit quarterly
- **Responsiveness matters:** Return calls same-day, respond to referrals within 24 hours

**Timeline:**
- First referral: 2-6 months (faster than academic centers)
- Established relationship: 6-12 months
- Consistent monthly referrals: 12-18 months

### **Step 4: Partner with Local ASAPs**

**Steward hospitals + ASAPs = powerful combination:**
- Many Steward patients are MassHealth-eligible (qualify for Frail Elder Waiver through ASAPs)
- Build relationships with ASAPs serving Steward hospital areas:
  - St. Elizabeth's → Boston ASAPs (Elder Services of Greater Boston, Ethos)
  - Good Samaritan → Old Colony Elder Services (South Shore)
  - Holy Family → Elder Services of Merrimack Valley
  - Carney → Boston ASAPs
  - Norwood → South Shore Elder Services
  - Morton → Greater Attleboro-Taunton Elder Services
  - St. Anne's → Greater Fall River Elder Services
  - Nashoba Valley → Montachusett Home Care

## Best Practices

**1. Prioritize multilingual capability:**
- **Spanish:** Essential for Holy Family, St. Anne's, Good Samaritan, Carney
- **Portuguese:** Critical for St. Anne's (Fall River), helpful for Good Samaritan (Brockton)
- **Haitian Creole:** Essential for Carney (Dorchester), Good Samaritan (Brockton)
- **Russian, Chinese:** Helpful for St. Elizabeth's (Brighton)

**2. MassHealth enrollment required:**
- 40-50%+ Steward patients are MassHealth
- If not enrolled as MassHealth provider, you'll miss majority of Steward referrals
- Complete MassHealth Home Health Agency Provider Enrollment Application (PCA/homemaker waiver)

**3. Build hospital-by-hospital relationships:**
- Steward has NO centralized referral system
- Each hospital operates independently
- Visit each Steward hospital individually, build separate relationships

**4. Emphasize community hospital advantages:**
- "We specialize in community-focused care" (vs. academic medical center specialists)
- "We understand the local community" (Brighton, Brockton, Fall River, etc.)
- Position as local agency vs. large Boston agencies

**5. Understand immigrant family dynamics:**
- Multi-generational households common (elder lives with adult children)
- Family decision-making (not just elder deciding about care)
- Cultural care preferences (food, hygiene, religious practices)
- Train caregivers on cultural competency

**6. Start with smaller Steward hospitals:**
- Nashoba Valley, Morton, Norwood = easier entry points (less competition)
- Build track record, then approach St. Elizabeth's, Good Samaritan, Holy Family (larger hospitals)

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "Steward has financial difficulties"**
**Solution:** Steward's national financial issues don't affect hospital discharge planning partnerships (hospitals still discharge patients, still need home care). Focus on relationship with individual hospitals, not corporate ownership.

**Challenge 2: "MassHealth reimbursement too low"**
**Solution:** Steward hospitals have high MassHealth populations (40-50%+ patients). If not prepared to accept MassHealth, Steward may not be right fit. Mix Steward (MassHealth) referrals with private pay from other sources (COAs, consumer platforms).

**Challenge 3: "Don't have multilingual caregivers"**
**Solution:** Recruit from local immigrant communities. Partner with Brazilian churches (Portuguese), Haitian community centers (Creole), Hispanic organizations (Spanish). Multilingual capability essential for Steward success.

**Challenge 4: "Too many Steward hospitals to cover"**
**Solution:** Don't try to cover all 9 hospitals. Focus on 2-3 Steward hospitals in YOUR service area. Quality over quantity.

**Challenge 5: "Steward patients in unsafe neighborhoods"**
**Solution:** Implement caregiver safety protocols (buddy systems, cell phones, emergency contacts, daytime-only visits if needed). Train all caregivers on urban safety.
