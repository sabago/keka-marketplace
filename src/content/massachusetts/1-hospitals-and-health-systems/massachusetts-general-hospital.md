## Overview

Massachusetts General Hospital (MGH) is the **#1 ranked hospital in the United States** and the flagship of the Mass General Brigham health system. With 999 licensed beds, MGH is the largest hospital in New England and generates over **25,000 annual discharges** requiring home care support.

**Why MGH Matters for Home Care Agencies:**
- **Volume:** Largest single-hospital discharge volume in Massachusetts (5,000+ patients annually needing home care)
- **Medical complexity:** National and international referral center = complex cases requiring specialized home care
- **Patient demographics:** Affluent population with private insurance, long-term care insurance, and private pay capability
- **Academic excellence:** Harvard Medical School teaching hospital = comprehensive discharge planning protocols
- **Technology platform:** Uses WellSky CarePort for discharge coordination
- **Geographic reach:** Serves all of Massachusetts + New England + 50 states (specialty care referrals)

**Hospital Profile:**
- **Address:** 55 Fruit Street, Boston, MA 02114
- **Main phone:** 617-726-2000
- **Discharge planning:** 617-726-2000 (ask for discharge planning department)
- **Provider enrollment:** Contact discharge planning for agency registration
- **Beds:** 999 licensed beds (operates at 95%+ occupancy year-round)
- **Annual admissions:** 50,000+ inpatient admissions
- **Emergency visits:** 100,000+ annually

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from Massachusetts General Hospital

**MGH Discharge Planning Infrastructure:**

MGH operates one of the most sophisticated discharge planning departments in the United States, with **50+ discharge planners** (RNs, social workers, case managers) organized by clinical service line.

**Referral Process - Step by Step:**

### **Step 1: Initial Agency Registration**

**Process:**
1. Call MGH main line: 617-726-2000
2. Ask to be transferred to "Discharge Planning Department"
3. Request meeting with Discharge Planning Manager or Supervisor
4. Schedule 15-20 minute introduction meeting

**What to bring:**
- Professional agency brochure (50+ copies for team distribution)
- Business cards (100+ cards)
- Service area map showing towns covered
- One-page agency overview (services, specialties, differentiators, contact information)
- Quality metrics (if available): Readmission rates, satisfaction scores, caregiver training certifications
- References from other hospitals (if applicable)

**Information to provide:**
- Agency name, address, phone, website
- Service area (specific towns/regions covered)
- Services offered (companion care, personal care, homemaker, specialized care)
- Specialties (dementia, Parkinson's, post-surgical, multilingual, 24/7 care)
- Insurance accepted (Medicare Advantage, commercial, Medicaid, private pay)
- Response time commitments (same-day assessment, 24-hour care start)
- On-call availability (24/7 contact number)
- Unique differentiators (what makes your agency different)

### **Step 2: Understand MGH's Technology Platform - WellSky CarePort**

**Critical:** MGH uses **WellSky CarePort** for discharge coordination.

**What is CarePort:**
- Cloud-based platform connecting hospitals and post-acute care providers
- Hospitals post discharge referrals; agencies respond in real-time
- Secure messaging, patient records access, care coordination tools

**Do you NEED CarePort to get MGH referrals?**
- **No** - MGH will call non-CarePort agencies by phone/fax
- **BUT** - CarePort agencies have significant competitive advantage (see referrals instantly, faster response times, easier for MGH staff)

**CarePort Investment Decision:**
- **Cost:** $10,000-$30,000/year (enterprise subscription)
- **ROI calculation:** If you receive 10+ MGH referrals per month, CarePort pays for itself
- **Alternative:** Build relationships for 6-12 months via phone/fax, then evaluate CarePort investment based on actual referral volume

**How to get CarePort:**
- Contact WellSky: wellsky.com/careport
- Request demo and pricing
- Typical setup: 4-6 weeks from contract to go-live

### **Step 3: Build Relationships with Service Line-Specific Discharge Planners**

**MGH organizes discharge planning by clinical department:**

**Strategy:** Don't try to meet all 50+ discharge planners. Focus on 3-5 high-volume service lines matching your agency's strengths.

**High-Volume Service Lines for Home Care:**

1. **Geriatrics/Medicine**
   - Contact: Call 617-726-2000, ask for "Geriatrics Discharge Planning"
   - Patient volume: VERY HIGH (largest source of home care referrals)
   - Typical patients: 75+ years old, multiple chronic conditions, ADL impairments
   - Agency strengths needed: Comprehensive personal care, medication management, fall prevention, dementia capability

2. **Orthopedics**
   - Contact: 617-726-2000, ask for "Orthopedics Discharge Planning"
   - Patient volume: HIGH
   - Typical patients: Hip/knee replacement, spinal surgery, fracture care
   - Agency strengths needed: Post-surgical care, mobility assistance, wound care, pain management

3. **Cardiology/Cardiac Surgery**
   - Contact: 617-726-2000, ask for "Cardiology Discharge Planning"
   - Patient volume: HIGH
   - Typical patients: Post-CABG, valve replacement, heart failure
   - Agency strengths needed: Vital sign monitoring, medication compliance, weight tracking, low-sodium meal prep

4. **Neurosciences**
   - Contact: 617-726-2000, ask for "Neurosciences Discharge Planning"
   - Patient volume: MODERATE-HIGH
   - Typical patients: Post-stroke, brain surgery, movement disorders
   - Agency strengths needed: Mobility assistance, cognitive support, fall prevention, specialized dementia/Parkinson's training

5. **Cancer Center (oncology)**
   - Contact: 617-726-2000, ask for "Oncology Discharge Planning"
   - Patient volume: MODERATE
   - Typical patients: Post-chemotherapy, palliative care, symptom management
   - Agency strengths needed: Medication support, symptom monitoring, emotional support, end-of-life care experience

6. **Transplant Services**
   - Contact: 617-726-2000, ask for "Transplant Discharge Planning"
   - Patient volume: MODERATE (but high-acuity, high-value)
   - Typical patients: Post-organ transplant (kidney, liver, heart, lung)
   - Agency strengths needed: Medication adherence, infection prevention, vital sign monitoring, transportation coordination

### **Step 4: Demonstrate Rapid Response Capability**

**MGH Expectation: SAME-DAY RESPONSE**

**Reality check:** MGH discharge planners work with 50+ agencies. The agencies that respond fastest get the most referrals.

**Best practices:**
- **Phone calls:** Return within 1 hour (not 4 hours, not 24 hours)
- **Assessment scheduling:** Offer same-day or next-morning assessment (not "we can see them in 48 hours")
- **Care start:** Be prepared to start care within 24 hours of assessment
- **Weekend/holiday availability:** MGH discharges patients 7 days/week including holidays

**Competitive advantage strategies:**
- Dedicate one staff member as "hospital liaison" (checks voicemail every 2 hours)
- Use call forwarding to owner/manager cell phone for urgent hospital calls
- Block 2-3 assessment slots daily for hospital referrals (don't overbook)
- Have standby caregivers available for immediate placements

### **Step 5: Communicate Exceptional Quality**

**What MGH discharge planners track:**
- **Readmission rates:** Do your patients return to MGH within 30 days?
- **Family satisfaction:** Do families complain or compliment?
- **Care quality:** Are patients safe, clean, well-cared-for?
- **Reliability:** Do you deliver what you promise?

**How to demonstrate quality:**
- **Proactive communication:** Call discharge planner 3-5 days after care starts: "Just wanted to update you - Mrs. Johnson is doing wonderfully, family is very happy, no concerns."
- **Problem-solving:** If patient declining or at risk for readmission, call discharge planner BEFORE patient returns to ER
- **Outcomes tracking:** Keep spreadsheet of MGH referrals, track which patients readmit, analyze why, improve processes
- **Testimonials:** Ask satisfied families to provide feedback; share with discharge planners (with permission)

### **Step 6: Maintain Consistent Visibility**

**Relationship maintenance schedule:**
- **Monthly (Months 1-6):** In-person check-ins, bring business cards, say hello
- **Quarterly (Year 1+):** In-person visits to refresh relationship
- **Weekly:** Respond to referral calls (ongoing)
- **Annually:** Provider appreciation (holiday cards, small gifts within hospital policy)

**In-person visit best practices:**
- **Timing:** Mid-morning (9-11am) or mid-afternoon (2-4pm) - avoid shift changes, lunch hours
- **Duration:** 5-10 minutes maximum (discharge planners are BUSY)
- **Bring:** Coffee, donuts, or small treats (check hospital policy - some limit gifts)
- **Message:** "Just stopping by to say hello, refresh our business cards, and let you know we're here to support your patients. How can we serve you better?"

## Tips to Get More Referrals from Massachusetts General Hospital

### **1. Specialize, Don't Generalize**

**Problem:** 50+ agencies tell MGH discharge planners "We do everything for everyone."

**Solution:** Differentiate with specialization.

**Specialization examples:**
- "We specialize in complex post-transplant care - our caregivers are trained in immunosuppression medication protocols."
- "We're dementia care experts - our entire team is Teepa Snow certified."
- "We focus on complex medical - ventilators, feeding tubes, wound vacs - the cases other agencies decline."
- "We specialize in multilingual care - 80% of our caregivers speak Spanish, Portuguese, Russian, or Chinese."
- "We're 24/7 care specialists - we handle live-in and overnight cases other agencies can't staff."

**Result:** Discharge planners remember specialists, forget generalists.

### **2. Serve a BROAD Geographic Area**

**Reality:** MGH refers patients to 100+ towns across Massachusetts and New England.

**Common mistake:** Agency only covers 5-10 towns, misses 80% of MGH referrals.

**Best practice:** Expand service area to 30-60 towns minimum.

**Geographic priorities for MGH:**
- All of Boston and immediate suburbs (required)
- North Shore: Salem, Beverly, Peabody, Lynn, Danvers, Marblehead, Gloucester
- South Shore: Quincy, Weymouth, Braintree, Milton, Hingham, Scituate
- Metro West: Newton, Wellesley, Needham, Framingham, Natick, Waltham
- Cambridge/Somerville area

**Strategy:** Hire caregivers in outer regions, don't require all caregivers to cover all towns.

### **3. Accept Complex, Challenging Patients**

**Insight:** MGH discharge planners remember agencies that say "yes" vs. agencies that say "no."

**Patient types other agencies often decline:**
- Hoarding situations
- Difficult family dynamics
- High fall risk
- Complex medication regimens (12+ medications)
- Medical equipment (feeding tubes, oxygen, wound vacs)
- Behavioral issues (agitation, aggression)
- Limited English proficiency + no family translators

**Strategy:** Train your team to handle complexity. Say yes when others say no. Build reputation as the agency that "handles anything."

**Risk management:** Start complex cases with your best, most experienced caregivers. Assess thoroughly. Charge appropriately for complexity.

### **4. Invest in Medical Training**

**MGH patient reality:** Medically complex (post-transplant, post-CABG, advanced cancer, stroke, complex wound care).

**Competitive advantage:** Agencies with medically trained caregivers get more MGH referrals.

**Training recommendations:**
- Basic medical care training (vital signs, medication assistance, medical equipment)
- Disease-specific training (CHF, COPD, diabetes, stroke, dementia)
- Post-surgical care protocols
- Wound care basics
- Infection prevention
- Emergency recognition (when to call 911, when to call doctor)

**Certifications to pursue:**
- HHA (Home Health Aide) certification for caregivers
- CNA (Certified Nursing Assistant)
- Dementia care certifications (Teepa Snow, Best Friends)
- First Aid/CPR for all caregivers

### **5. Use WellSky CarePort Platform**

**Reality:** MGH strongly prefers CarePort-connected agencies (easier workflow).

**Decision framework:**

**Year 1:** Build relationships without CarePort (test MGH referral volume)
- Track: Number of MGH referrals per month
- Calculate: Estimated annual MGH revenue

**After 12 months:** Evaluate CarePort ROI
- If getting 5+ MGH referrals/month ($75k-$200k annual revenue) → CarePort investment justified
- If getting <3 referrals/month → Continue building relationships, evaluate CarePort again in 12 months

**Alternative:** Partner with CarePort-connected agency (subcontract arrangement)

### **6. Communicate Outcomes Proactively**

**Standard agency:** Only contacts discharge planner if there's a problem.

**Excellent agency:** Proactively updates discharge planner on positive outcomes.

**Script:**
"Hi [Discharge Planner Name], this is [Your Name] from [Agency]. I wanted to update you on Mrs. Smith who you referred to us last week. She's doing wonderfully - family is very happy with care, she's gaining strength, no safety concerns. I know you're busy so just a quick update. Thank you again for the referral."

**Frequency:** Update discharge planner 3-5 days after care starts for every new MGH referral (first 6-12 months of relationship building).

**Result:** Discharge planner remembers your agency positively, increases referrals.

### **7. Build Relationships with MGH Outpatient Clinics**

**Opportunity:** MGH operates 200+ outpatient clinics across Massachusetts.

**Referral potential:** Primary care physicians, geriatricians, and specialists refer patients needing home care (not just hospital discharge planners).

**High-volume clinics to target:**
- MGH Geriatrics (multiple locations)
- MGH Primary Care (multiple locations)
- MGH Cancer Center outpatient clinics
- MGH Cardiology clinics

**Process:**
1. Identify high-volume MGH outpatient clinics in your service area
2. Call clinic, ask to speak with practice manager or nurse manager
3. Request brief meeting to introduce agency
4. Leave materials (brochures, business cards, service overview)
5. Follow up quarterly

### **8. Partner with MGH Affiliated Physicians**

**Reality:** MGH physicians have private practices affiliated with MGH network.

**Opportunity:** These physicians refer patients to home care.

**Strategy:**
1. Search "MGH affiliated physicians [specialty]" + your target towns
2. Example: "MGH geriatrics Newton", "MGH cardiology Wellesley"
3. Contact affiliated practices using same relationship-building approach
4. Leverage MGH brand: "We specialize in caring for MGH patients post-discharge."

### **9. Join MGH ACO Preferred Provider Network**

**MGH ACO:** Mass General Brigham ACO (3+ million attributed patients)

**ACO focus:** Readmission prevention, care coordination, value-based care

**Home care role:** Critical to keeping patients healthy at home

**How to join:**
1. Contact MGH network development: 617-726-2000, ask for "ACO network development" or "population health partnerships"
2. Present agency capabilities, emphasize quality outcomes
3. Request addition to ACO preferred provider list
4. Maintain relationship with ACO care coordinators (separate from discharge planners)

### **10. Attend MGH Provider Events**

**Opportunity:** MGH occasionally hosts provider fairs, lunch-and-learns, quality improvement meetings.

**How to find out:**
- Ask discharge planners: "Does MGH host any provider events where we could meet your team?"

### **11. Handle International Patients with Cultural Competence**

**Agency advantages:**

- Flexibility with temporary care arrangements (2-4 weeks)
- Concierge-level service

**How to position:** "We specialize in supporting MGH's international patient families - multilingual staff, cultural awareness, white-glove service."

### **12. Develop "MGH Post-Discharge" Care Packages**

**Concept:** Pre-designed care packages for common MGH discharge types.

**Examples:**

**"Post-Joint Replacement Package"**
- First 2 weeks: 4 hours/day (medication, meals, mobility assistance)
- Weeks 3-6: 3 hours/day (transition to independence)
- Caregiver trained in post-surgical care, PT exercise support
- Fixed package pricing

**"Post-Cardiac Surgery Package"**
- First 2 weeks: 6 hours/day (vital sign monitoring, medication, cardiac diet meals)
- Weeks 3-8: 4 hours/day (progressive activity increase)
- Caregiver trained in cardiac care
- Weight/BP tracking log provided

**Value for MGH discharge planners:**
- Simplified referral process
- Predictable care plans
- Easier to explain to families

**Value for families:**
- Clear expectations
- Fixed pricing

## Common Challenges & Solutions

### **Challenge 1: "Too competitive - we can't break into MGH"**

**Reality:** 50+ agencies actively pursuing MGH relationships. Very competitive environment.

**Solutions:**

**A) Start with less competitive Mass General Brigham hospitals**
- Newton-Wellesley Hospital (less competitive than MGH)
- North Shore Medical Center (less competitive than MGH)
- Build track record serving Mass General Brigham patients
- Then approach MGH: "We successfully serve Newton-Wellesley patients - we'd love to support MGH as well"

**B) Differentiate through specialization**
- Don't be another generalist agency
- Specialize: Transplant care, dementia, complex medical, multilingual, 24/7 care
- Position as expert, not generalist

**C) Be the agency that says "yes"**
- Accept challenging patients other agencies decline
- Build reputation as reliable partner for complex cases

**D) Out-service competitors**
- Respond faster (same-day vs. next-day)
- Communicate better (proactive updates vs. reactive)
- Follow through consistently (do what you say you'll do)

### **Challenge 2: "MGH patients are too medically complex for our caregivers"**

**Reality:** MGH patients are more medically complex than community hospital patients (national referral center, complex cases).

**Solutions:**

**A) Invest in medical training**
- Train caregivers on: Vital signs, medication assistance, medical equipment, disease-specific care
- Hire experienced caregivers (CNAs, HHAs) for MGH cases
- Partner with skilled home health agencies (refer for skilled needs, keep non-skilled care)

**B) Start with less complex service lines**
- Orthopedics (post-joint replacement) = less complex than transplant
- Geriatrics (ADL support) = familiar care needs
- Build experience, then expand to more complex cases

**C) Use your most experienced caregivers for MGH cases**
- Don't send brand-new caregivers to complex MGH patients
- Match caregiver experience to patient complexity

**D) Charge appropriately for complexity**
- MGH patients often have insurance, LTC insurance, private pay capability
- Price reflects complexity, training, expertise required

### **Challenge 3: "Can't afford WellSky CarePort subscription"**

**Reality:** CarePort costs $10k-$30k/year - significant investment for small agencies.

**Solutions:**

**A) Build relationships WITHOUT CarePort first**
- Many agencies succeed with MGH via phone/fax referrals
- CarePort is competitive advantage, not requirement
- Test MGH referral volume for 12 months, then evaluate CarePort

**B) ROI analysis after Year 1**
- If you receive 5+ MGH referrals/month ($75k-$200k annual revenue) → CarePort justified
- If you receive <3 referrals/month → Focus on relationship building, evaluate CarePort later

**C) Partner with CarePort-connected agency**
- Subcontract arrangement: CarePort agency receives referral, subcontracts to you
- Share referral fee/margin
- Allows you to serve MGH patients without CarePort investment

**D) Pursue CarePort enterprise pricing**
- If multiple Mass General Brigham hospitals important to you, enterprise pricing covers all hospitals
- Negotiate multi-year discount

### **Challenge 4: "Geographic coverage gaps - we don't serve all MGH referral areas"**

**Reality:** MGH refers to 100+ towns. Most agencies serve 10-30 towns.

**Solutions:**

**A) Expand strategically**
- Identify which MGH referral towns you're missing most often
- Hire 1-2 caregivers in high-volume gap areas
- Example: If you serve Metro Boston but not North Shore, hire 2 North Shore caregivers

**B) Be transparent**
- When MGH refers outside your service area, respond: "Thank you for thinking of us. We don't currently serve [town], but here's an agency that does: [provide referral]"
- Discharge planners appreciate honesty and helpfulness

**C) Partner with other agencies**
- Reciprocal referral arrangements with agencies serving areas you don't
- Example: You serve Metro Boston, partner with North Shore agency, cross-refer

**D) Accept select out-of-area cases**
- High-value patients (private pay, long-term care)
- Pay caregiver mileage, make exceptions for excellent opportunities

### **Challenge 5: "MGH discharge planners don't return our calls"**

**Reality:** MGH discharge planners manage 20-30 discharges daily, receive 10-20 agency calls daily. Overwhelmed.

**Solutions:**

**A) In-person visits work better than calls**
- Schedule face-to-face meetings (even 5 minutes)
- Show up in person quarterly to refresh relationship
- Human connection > voicemails

**B) Be helpful, not salesy**
- Don't call just to ask "Do you have any referrals?"
- Call to offer value: "Just checking in - how can we better serve your patients?"
- Problem-solve, don't just ask for business

**C) Communicate outcomes**
- Update discharge planners on patient successes
- "Mrs. Smith is doing wonderfully - thank you for the referral"
- Discharge planners remember agencies that close the loop

**D) Persistence with patience**
- It takes 8-12 touch points to break through
- Keep showing up, stay positive, don't give up

### **Challenge 6: "Long sales cycle - taking 6+ months to get first referral"**

**Reality:** MGH relationships take 6-12 months to mature (very normal for competitive hospital).

**Solutions:**

**A) Adjust expectations**
- MGH is long-term investment (not quick win)
- Compare to building relationships with 3-5 community hospitals vs. 1 MGH (similar timeline, similar volume)

**B) Focus on multiple hospitals simultaneously**
- Don't put all eggs in MGH basket
- Build relationships with 5-8 hospitals at once
- Some will convert faster than others

**C) Track relationship milestones**
- First meeting completed
- Business cards distributed
- Follow-up visit #2 completed
- First referral call (even if you couldn't accept)
- Celebrate progress, not just final outcome

**D) Generate other referral sources while building MGH**
- Council on Aging partnerships (faster to launch)
- Senior living communities (faster to launch)
- Physician practices (faster to launch)
- MGH is long-term asset, but don't wait 12 months for revenue
