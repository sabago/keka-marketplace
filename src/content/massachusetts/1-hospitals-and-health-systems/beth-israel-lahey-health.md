## Overview

Beth Israel Lahey Health (BILH) is the **second-largest health system in Massachusetts** with 13 hospitals and approximately $5.4 billion in net patient revenue. Formed in 2019 from the merger of Beth Israel Deaconess Medical Center and Lahey Health, BILH serves patients across Metro Boston, North Shore, and Merrimack Valley regions.

**Why BILH Matters for Home Care Agencies:**
- **Scale:** 13 hospitals + community health centers = thousands of annual discharges
- **Geographic coverage:** Broad service area from Boston to North Shore to Merrimack Valley
- **FREE referrals:** No cost to build relationships
- **Quality focus:** System-wide emphasis on care transitions and readmission reduction
- **Academic + community:** Mix of teaching hospitals and community hospitals

## Who Sends Referrals

**Primary referral contacts:**
- **Discharge planners** (RNs, social workers) at each hospital
- **Case managers** for complex patients
- **Geriatric social workers** (BIDMC has robust geriatrics program)
- **Specialty clinic nurses** (especially orthopedics at New England Baptist)
- **Rehab coordinators** (inpatient rehab units)

**System-level contacts:**
- BILH has system-wide care transitions initiatives
- Some hospitals participate in shared discharge planning platforms
- ACO relationships (Beth Israel Lahey ACO)

## Who Can Receive Referrals

**Eligible agencies:**
- Licensed home care agencies in Massachusetts (no state license required, but professional operation expected)
- Must serve geographic area of specific hospital
- Must accept hospital's payer mix (Medicare, MassHealth, commercial insurance, private pay)
- Preference given to agencies with:
  - Track record of quality outcomes
  - Low readmission rates
  - 24/7 availability
  - Broad service area coverage
  - Specialized services (e.g., dementia care, orthopedic post-surgical)

**No formal enrollment required** - BILH hospitals do not have exclusive preferred provider networks. Success depends on relationship quality with individual discharge planners.

## How It Works

### **Step 1: Build Relationships with Target BILH Hospitals**

**Start with 2-3 BILH hospitals closest to your service area:**
- If you serve Metro Boston → BIDMC, Mount Auburn, Winchester
- If you serve North Shore → Beverly, Addison Gilbert, Anna Jaques
- If you serve Merrimack Valley → Lawrence Memorial, Anna Jaques
- If you serve MetroWest → Lahey Burlington, Winchester, BIDMC-Needham
- If you serve South Shore → BIDMC-Plymouth

**Initial outreach:**
1. Call hospital main number, ask for "discharge planning department"
2. Request 15-minute introduction meeting with discharge planning manager
3. Bring professional materials: agency brochure, service area map, contact sheet, one-pager

**In-person visits work best** - discharge planners are overwhelmed with emails/calls.

### **Step 2: Demonstrate Value**

**What BILH discharge planners care about:**
- **Responsiveness:** Can you return calls within 1 hour? Start care same-day or next-day?
- **Geographic coverage:** Do you serve entire hospital catchment area?
- **Payer acceptance:** Will you accept MassHealth, Medicare, all insurance?
- **Quality:** Can you provide references, success stories, satisfaction data?
- **Reliability:** Do you have consistent caregiver availability (not chronic call-offs)?
- **Communication:** Will you keep hospital updated on patient status?
- **Readmission prevention:** Do you proactively communicate with MD if patient declines?

**Differentiation strategies:**
- Specialized services (dementia care, Parkinson's, post-stroke, orthopedic post-surgical)
- Multilingual caregivers (especially for BIDMC, Mount Auburn - diverse populations)
- Weekend/evening availability
- Willingness to accept complex patients others decline

### **Step 3: Receive Referrals**

**How referrals happen:**
- Discharge planner calls your agency: "We have a patient discharging tomorrow, can you do an assessment?"
- Typical timeline: 24-48 hours notice (sometimes same-day for urgent discharges)
- Discharge planner provides: Patient name, diagnosis, insurance, care needs, family contact
- You respond: "Yes, we can do that. I'll send our RN supervisor for assessment today/tomorrow."

**Assessment process:**
- Meet patient/family at hospital or home (before discharge or day-of)
- Complete care plan assessment
- Provide quote to family
- Coordinate start date with discharge planner

**Post-discharge:**
- Update discharge planner when care starts
- Communicate any concerns (patient unstable, family issues, readmission risk)
- Thank discharge planner for referral

### **Step 4: Nurture Relationships**

**Ongoing relationship maintenance:**
- Quarterly in-person check-ins (bring coffee, donuts, say hello)
- Share outcomes: "The patient you referred last month is doing great - family is thrilled"
- Respond immediately to every call (even if you can't accept that particular referral, be helpful)
- Attend hospital provider fairs, discharge planning team meetings
- Holiday appreciation (cards, small gifts within hospital policy limits)

## How to Get Started

### **Immediate Actions (Week 1):**

1. **Identify your target BILH hospital(s):**
   - Which BILH hospital is closest to your service area?
   - Which hospital's patient population matches your agency's strengths?

2. **Research discharge planning contacts:**
   - Call hospital main number: "(Hospital name) main number, please"
   - Ask operator: "Can you connect me to discharge planning?"
   - Introduce yourself: "Hi, I'm (name) from (agency). We provide home care in (towns). I'd love to introduce our agency to your team. Is there a good time to stop by for a brief meeting?"

3. **Prepare professional materials:**
   - Agency one-pager (services, service area, contact info, differentiators)
   - Business cards
   - Service area map (highlight which towns you cover)
   - Optional: Success stories, testimonials, outcomes data

### **Short-term Actions (Months 1-3):**

4. **Schedule introduction meetings:**
   - Target: 2-3 BILH hospitals
   - Meeting length: 15-20 minutes
   - Bring: Materials, positive energy, genuine desire to help patients

5. **Follow up consistently:**
   - After meeting, send thank-you email
   - Check in monthly for first 3 months
   - Offer to help even if no referrals yet (goodwill building)

6. **Join BILH provider events (if offered):**
   - Some BILH hospitals host provider fairs, lunch-and-learns, networking events
   - Attend to meet discharge planners and other hospital staff

### **Long-term Strategy (Months 4-12):**

7. **Be patient - relationships take 6-12 months:**
   - First referral may not come for 3-6 months
   - Keep showing up, stay positive, demonstrate consistency
   - Discharge planners need to trust you before referring

8. **Expand to additional BILH hospitals:**
   - Once successful at 1-2 BILH hospitals, add more
   - System reputation helps (if Lahey Burlington trusts you, BIDMC may too)

## Cost and ROI Analysis

**Cost:** **100% FREE**
- No enrollment fees, network fees, or subscription costs
- Only investment: staff time for relationship-building (estimate 2-4 hours/month per hospital)

**ROI:** **High**
- **Lead quality:** Excellent - patients have immediate documented need
- **Conversion rate:** 50-70% for warm hospital referrals (vs. 20-30% for consumer leads)
- **Lead value:** $4,000-$30,000+ lifetime value per client depending on care duration
- **Scalability:** Once relationship established, referrals can be consistent monthly flow
- **Cost per acquisition:** ~$0 (just time investment)

**Expected timeline to first referral:**
- **Months 1-3:** Introduction, relationship building (0-2 referrals)
- **Months 4-6:** Trust building (1-5 referrals)
- **Months 7-12:** Established relationship (3-10 referrals/month from each hospital)
- **Year 2+:** Mature partnership (5-20 referrals/month from each hospital)

## Best Practices for BILH

**1. Prioritize New England Baptist Hospital (if you serve orthopedics):**
- Specialty orthopedic hospital = HIGH VOLUME hip/knee replacement discharges
- Predictable care needs (post-surgical, ADL assistance, med management)
- Well-insured patient population
- Easier to convert than complex medical cases

**2. Emphasize multilingual capabilities at BIDMC and Mount Auburn:**
- Diverse patient populations (Spanish, Portuguese, Russian, Chinese, Haitian Creole, Arabic)
- Agencies with multilingual caregivers have significant competitive advantage

**3. Target community hospitals for easier entry:**
- BIDMC-Needham, BIDMC-Milton, BIDMC-Plymouth, Beverly, Winchester are less competitive than BIDMC Boston
- Smaller discharge planning teams = easier to build relationships
- Community hospitals appreciate local agencies vs. large regional competitors

**4. Leverage Beth Israel Lahey ACO relationship (if applicable):**
- Beth Israel Lahey ACO has 440,000 attributed patients
- ACO focused on care transitions and readmission reduction
- If you can demonstrate low readmission rates, position yourself as ACO-aligned partner

**5. Be prepared for quick turnarounds:**
- BILH hospitals often need same-day or next-day care starts
- Have standby assessment staff available
- Prioritize responsiveness over perfect fit

**6. Track readmissions religiously:**
- If your BILH-referred patient returns to hospital within 30 days, investigate why
- Communicate proactively with discharge planner if patient declining
- Hospitals track readmissions by home care agency

## Common Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge 1: BIDMC Boston is very competitive - hard to break in**
**Solution:** Start with smaller BILH hospitals (BIDMC-Needham, BIDMC-Plymouth, Beverly, Winchester). Once you have success stories from other BILH hospitals, approach BIDMC Boston.

**Challenge 2: Discharge planners don't return calls/emails**
**Solution:** In-person visits work 10x better. Show up with coffee, ask when's a good time to chat. Be friendly, brief, helpful.

**Challenge 3: "We already have preferred agencies"**
**Solution:** Ask to be backup/overflow option. Offer specialized services (weekends, multilingual, dementia care). Be patient - agencies lose contracts, discharge planners change preferences.

**Challenge 4: Geographic coverage gaps**
**Solution:** Be honest about coverage area. If you don't serve a town, say so. Better to decline respectfully than accept and fail to deliver.

## Success Metrics to Track

**Measure your BILH performance:**
- **Number of BILH hospitals with active relationships:** Target 3-5
- **Referrals per hospital per month:** Mature relationship = 5-10/month
- **Conversion rate:** % of BILH referrals that become clients (target: 60%+)
- **Readmission rate:** % of BILH-referred clients readmitted within 30 days (target: <15%)
- **Revenue from BILH referrals:** Track separately from other channels
- **Response time:** How quickly do you follow up on BILH calls? (target: <1 hour)

**Celebrate milestones:**
- First BILH referral
- 10th BILH referral
- First month with 10+ BILH referrals
- Expansion to 3rd, 5th BILH hospital
