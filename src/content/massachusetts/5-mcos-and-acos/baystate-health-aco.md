## Overview

Baystate Health operates **Western Massachusetts' dominant Accountable Care Organization**, managing care for 220,000+ attributed patients across Hampden, Hampshire, Franklin, and Berkshire counties. As the largest health system in Western MA with a Level 1 Trauma Center, academic medical center, and regional hospital network, Baystate ACO represents the **primary ACO partnership** for home care agencies serving Springfield and Western Massachusetts.

**What makes Baystate Health ACO significant:**
- **220,000+ attributed patients** across Medicare Shared Savings Program, commercial ACO contracts, and MassHealth ACO programs
- **Western MA regional dominance** - 70%+ market share in Hampden County (Springfield metro)
- **5 hospitals** - Baystate Medical Center (flagship, 716 beds, Level 1 Trauma Center), Baystate Franklin (Greenfield), Baystate Wing (Palmer), Baystate Noble (Westfield), Baystate Children's Hospital (pediatric)
- **1,400+ affiliated physicians** - Baystate Medical Practices (600+ employed physicians) + community physicians
- **Medicare Shared Savings Program** - Track 2 (upside + limited downside risk)
- **Commercial ACO contracts** - Health New England (HNE partnership), Blue Cross Blue Shield, Tufts Health Plan
- **MassHealth ACO** - Accountable Care Partnership Plan (ACPP) with HNE

**For Western Massachusetts home care agencies:**
- **"One stop shop" for Western MA** - Baystate ACO accesses Medicare + commercial + MassHealth populations through single partnership
- **Geographic monopoly** - Baystate dominant in Springfield/Western MA (unlike Boston with competing systems)
- **Health New England integration** - Baystate affiliated with HNE (largest Western MA MCO) = combined Medicare ACO + MassHealth MCO referrals
- **Academic medical center** - Tufts University affiliation, teaching hospital, complex patient population
- **Care management infrastructure** - 150+ RN care managers, social workers, Community Health Workers
- **Technology platform** - Baystate uses Epic EHR with care coordination capabilities
- **Quality focus** - Baystate invests heavily in ACO performance (quality outcomes, readmission prevention)

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from Baystate ACO

**Baystate's integrated care management model:**

### **Pathway 1: Baystate Care Transitions Program (45% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **Patient discharged** from Baystate hospital
2. **Transitional Care Nurse (TCN) assesses** within 24 hours of admission
   - **TCNs embedded in hospitals** (physical presence for real-time coordination)
   - Reviews medical record, speaks with hospital team
   - Assesses readmission risk (LACE score, social determinants, family support)
3. **TCN identifies home care need** - "Patient lives alone, high fall risk, CHF exacerbation, needs post-discharge support"
4. **TCN contacts in-network agency** - Phone call or electronic referral via Epic Care Link
5. **Agency responds** - Within 4 hours, schedules assessment within 24 hours of discharge
6. **Care begins** - 6-12 weeks post-discharge support
7. **Ongoing coordination** - Agency provides weekly updates to TCN

**Target population:**
- CHF, COPD exacerbations (high readmission risk)
- Falls, fractures (especially hip fractures)
- Post-surgical (orthopedic, cardiac, neurosurgery, trauma)
- Stroke, neurological events
- Diabetes complications
- Pneumonia, infections (sepsis, UTI, cellulitis)
- Members living alone with limited family support

**Volume:** 200-350 referrals per month system-wide

**Key differentiator:**
**Baystate TCNs are hospital-based AND community-based** - TCNs follow patients after discharge (phone calls, home visits) creating **longitudinal relationship** vs. one-time discharge handoff. **Agencies that communicate well with TCNs get repeat referrals.**

### **Pathway 2: ACO Complex Care Management (30% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **Baystate identifies high-risk ACO patients** via predictive analytics
   - **Epic Healthy Planet** platform analyzes claims data
   - Identifies patients at risk: frequent hospitalizations, ER visits, multiple chronic conditions, history of readmissions
2. **ACO Care Manager (RN or social worker) reaches out** - Phone assessment, comprehensive needs assessment
3. **Care Manager determines home care needed** - "Patient needs ADL support + medication management + chronic disease monitoring"
4. **Refers to in-network agency** - Typically longer-term care (12-24 months) vs. short-term post-discharge
5. **Interdisciplinary care team meetings** - Monthly meetings with agency, care manager, PCP, social worker, pharmacist

**Target population:**
- "Super-utilizers" (top 5% of ACO population - frequent hospitalizers)
- Complex chronic conditions (CHF, COPD, diabetes, dementia)
- Frail elderly (75+) with declining function
- Medication non-adherence
- Social isolation, limited family support

**Volume:** 130-220 referrals per month

**Why this pathway matters:**
- **Long-term relationships** (12-24+ months vs. 6-8 weeks post-discharge)
- **Higher intensity** (often 15-30 hours/week home care)
- **Care team collaboration** (monthly meetings with ACO team)
- **Performance tracking** (Baystate monitors outcomes closely - quality accountability)

### **Pathway 3: Baystate Medical Practices (Primary Care) (15% of referrals)**

**Baystate Medical Practices:**
- **600+ employed physicians** across primary care and specialties
- **Tight EHR integration** (Epic system-wide) - PCPs refer to ACO Care Managers electronically
- 40+ practice locations across Western MA

**How it works:**
1. **Baystate PCP identifies patient decline** during office visit
   - Missed appointments, medication non-adherence, functional decline, family caregiver burnout
2. **PCP submits Epic referral** to ACO Care Management
3. **Care Manager assesses patient** - Phone or home visit assessment
4. **Care Manager refers to home care agency**
5. **Agency coordinates** with PCP and care manager

**Target population:**
- Early decline detection (preventing hospitalization)
- Chronic disease management
- Family caregiver support/respite
- Post-hospitalization ongoing support

**Volume:** 65-110 referrals per month

**Key advantage:**
**Epic EHR integration** creates seamless PCP → Care Manager → Home Care Agency workflow (vs. manual phone/fax referrals)

### **Pathway 4: Health New England (HNE) Partnership (8% of referrals)**

**Baystate-HNE strategic partnership:**
- **HNE is Baystate's primary payer partner** (90,000+ HNE MassHealth members + 65,000+ HNE Medicare Advantage members)
- **Integrated care management:** Baystate Care Managers coordinate with HNE Care Managers
- **MassHealth ACO (ACPP):** Joint Baystate-HNE Accountable Care Partnership Plan for Medicaid members

**How it generates home care referrals:**
1. **HNE Care Manager identifies member needing home care** (HNE member hospitalized at Baystate OR community-based outreach)
2. **HNE Care Manager coordinates with Baystate Care Manager**
3. **Joint referral to home care agency** serving both Baystate ACO and HNE MCO

**Why this matters:**
- **Single agency can serve both Baystate ACO and HNE MCO patients** (efficiency)
- **Coordinated referrals** (less duplication, better communication)
- **Combined volume** (Baystate ACO + HNE MCO = 200,000+ members)

**Volume:** 35-60 referrals per month (Baystate-HNE coordinated referrals)

### **Pathway 5: Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF) Diversion (2% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **Patient could go to SNF but prefers home** with intensive support
2. **Baystate Care Manager evaluates** - "Can patient safely return home with daily home care?"
3. **Refers to agency for intensive home support** - 2-3 visits daily, 7 days/week initially
4. **Cost savings for ACO:** $8,000-$15,000/month SNF cost vs. $4,000-$7,000/month intensive home care

**Baystate incentive:**
- **SNF = expensive + quality risk** (SNF-related readmissions count against Baystate)
- **Home care = lower cost + patient preference + equal/better outcomes**
- **Baystate actively seeks agencies capable of SNF diversion**

**Volume:** 10-20 referrals per month

## Network Enrollment and Credentialing Process

### **Baystate Preferred Provider Network**

**Baystate operates structured ACO network** (more formal than Steward, less rigorous than Mass General Brigham/BILH):

### **Step 1: Initial Contact**

**Contact Baystate ACO Network Development:**
- **Phone:** 413-794-5515
- **Email:** ACOProviderRelations@baystatehealth.org
- **Subject:** "Home Care Agency - Baystate ACO Network Application"

**Introduce your agency:**
- Agency name, location, years in operation
- Service area (which Western MA communities served?)
- Services offered (personal care, skilled nursing, homemaking, specializations)
- **Languages:** Spanish CRITICAL (Springfield 45% Latino) - how many Spanish-speaking caregivers?
- Member capacity
- Accreditation (Joint Commission, CHAP, ACHC)
- Specializations (CHF, COPD, post-surgical, dementia, dual eligibles)
- Quality metrics (readmission rates, patient satisfaction)
- References (hospitals, ASAPs, other ACOs)

**Request application:**
Network Development will send credentialing packet (15-20 pages)

### **Step 2: Complete Application**

**Required documentation:**

**Business/legal:**
- Articles of incorporation, EIN, MA DPH license, Certificate of Good Standing, W-9

**Insurance:**
- General liability ($1-2 million per occurrence / $3 million aggregate)
- Professional liability ($1 million / $3 million)
- Workers compensation, auto insurance
- Certificates naming Baystate as additional insured

**Accreditation (preferred):**
- Joint Commission, CHAP, or ACHC (not required but strengthens application)

**Agency policies:**
- Care planning, caregiver training/supervision, background checks (CORI/SORI), quality assurance, incident reporting, emergency procedures, infection control, medication administration, HIPAA

**Caregiver qualifications:**
- Training curriculum, certifications (CNA, HHA, PCA), background checks, language capabilities (Spanish ESSENTIAL), supervision protocols

**Service description:**
- Personal care, homemaking, companion care, skilled nursing (if licensed)
- Specializations: Dementia care, CHF/COPD management, post-surgical, dual eligibles, behavioral health, end-of-life, live-in care

**Geographic coverage:**
- List all Western MA towns served
- **Rural capabilities:** Can you serve Franklin County (Greenfield, Orange)? Hampshire County (Amherst, Northampton)?

**Language capabilities:**
- **Spanish** (ESSENTIAL - 25% of Western MA population Latino)
- Portuguese, Polish (Chicopee, Holyoke)
- English

**Quality metrics:**
- Patient satisfaction, caregiver retention, hospital readmission rates, falls/medication errors/incidents, complaint rates

**Financial information:**
- Agency financial statements

**References:**
- 3-5 professional references

### **Step 3: Application Review**

**Timeline:** 60-90 days

**Review process:**
- Network Development and Clinical Quality teams review
- Verifies licensure, insurance, accreditation
- Reviews policies and quality metrics
- Checks references

**Site visit (possible):**
- For new agencies without established track record
- Assess: Office operations, staff qualifications, technology, quality processes, Spanish-speaking staff capabilities

**Decision:**
- Approved - contract offer
- Conditional - address specific items
- Denied - quality concerns or insufficient capacity (rare - Baystate needs Western MA agencies)

### **Step 4: Contract Terms**

**Reimbursement rates:**
- **Personal care:** $27-$33/hour
- **Homemaking:** $25-$31/hour
- **Companion care:** $24-$29/hour
- **Live-in care:** $225-$300/day
- **Skilled nursing:** $58-$75/hour (if contracted)

**Rate negotiation:**
- Spanish-speaking premium (+$2-3/hour)
- Rural service premium (+$2-4/hour for Franklin/Berkshire counties)
- CHF/COPD specialization (+$2-3/hour)
- Dual eligible expertise (+$3-5/hour)
- Performance-based increases (after demonstrating quality)

**Authorization requirements:**
- Prior authorization required for all services
- Initial authorization: Care Manager approves hours/week, duration
- Re-authorization: Agency requests continuation with progress notes
- Turnaround: 2 business days

**Service level expectations:**
- Assessment: Within 24-48 hours of referral
- Care initiation: Within 48-72 hours
- Communication: Weekly updates to Care Managers/TCNs
- Immediate alerts: Same-day notification for hospitalizations, ER visits, safety concerns
- Documentation: Monthly care summaries

**Quality standards:**
- Patient satisfaction: 90%+ target
- 30-day hospital readmission: <15% target
- ER utilization: <100 visits per 1,000 patients annually
- Caregiver consistency: Minimize turnover
- Safety: <2% incident rate

**Billing/payment:**
- Electronic claims via Baystate portal or paper
- Payment: Clean claims paid within 30-45 days
- Direct deposit (ACH)

**Contract term:**
- Initial: 2 years
- Auto-renews unless terminated with 90 days notice
- Annual performance review

### **Step 5: Epic Care Link Integration**

**What is Epic Care Link:**
- Baystate uses **Epic EHR system** with Care Link functionality
- Care Link = referral and care coordination platform for external partners
- Real-time patient information sharing between Baystate and home care agencies

**Enrollment:**
- Baystate offers Epic Care Link access to network agencies (optional but recommended)
- **Cost:** FREE for network agencies
- **Benefits:**
  - Electronic referrals from TCNs, Care Managers, PCPs
  - Access to patient medical records, medications, care plans, discharge summaries
  - Direct communication with Baystate care team
  - Real-time ADT alerts (admissions, discharges, transfers)

**Implementation:**
- 2-3 hours training (Baystate provides)
- Assign staff to check Care Link daily
- **Agencies using Care Link receive 30-40% more referrals** (care managers prefer electronic workflow)

### **Step 6: Launch and Relationship Building**

**Announce network status:**
- Email Baystate Care Managers and TCNs
- Update website: "Proud Baystate Health ACO Preferred Provider"

**Meet care managers in-person:**
- Visit Baystate hospitals (Medical Center in Springfield, Franklin, Wing, Noble)
- Request meetings with TCN teams, ACO Care Management
- Quarterly in-person check-ins

**Attend Baystate provider meetings:**
- Quarterly ACO provider forums
- ATTEND - builds relationships, demonstrates commitment

**First referrals:**
- Respond FAST (4-hour response, same-day assessment)
- Document excellent outcomes
- Request feedback

**1. Use Epic Care Link platform actively (competitive advantage):**
- Agencies using Care Link receive 30-40% more referrals

- High prevalence in Western MA (smoking rates, industrial history, poverty)
- Baystate penalized for readmissions (financial incentive to partner with quality agencies)
- Train caregivers in disease-specific care
- Track readmissions, report to Baystate quarterly

**4. Build tight relationships with Transitional Care Nurses:**
- TCNs generate 45% of referrals
- TCNs follow patients post-discharge (not just one-time handoff)
- Respond within 4 hours to every TCN referral
- Provide weekly updates (proactive communication)
- Success = repeat referrals from same TCN

**5. Serve rural Western MA (competitive advantage):**
- Franklin County (Greenfield, Orange) and Hampshire County (Amherst, Northampton) underserved
- Fewer agencies willing to serve rural areas
- Baystate values rural coverage
- Negotiate rural premium (justify higher rates for drive time)

**6. Coordinate with Health New England (HNE):**
- Baystate-HNE strategic partnership
- Single agency can serve both Baystate ACO and HNE MCO (efficiency)
- Credential with both Baystate and HNE for combined volume
- Coordinated referrals = better communication, less duplication

**7. Position for SNF diversion opportunities:**
- Baystate actively seeks agencies capable of intensive home care (vs. SNF)
- Market intensive capabilities: "We provide 2-3 visits daily, 7 days/week"
- Cost savings pitch: Show Baystate saves $5,000-$10,000/month vs. SNF
- Accept complex cases (demonstrate capability)

**8. Participate in care team meetings:**
- ACO Care Management hosts monthly interdisciplinary meetings for complex patients
- Attend (builds relationships, demonstrates commitment)
- Provide patient updates, collaborate on care plans

**9. Demonstrate quality with Western MA-specific data:**
- Track Baystate patients separately
- Quarterly reports: "Last quarter: 45 Baystate patients, 6% readmission rate, 93% satisfaction"
- Western MA-specific outcomes (vs. generic data)

**10. Respond faster than competitors:**
- 4-hour response to ALL Baystate referrals
- Same-day assessments when possible
