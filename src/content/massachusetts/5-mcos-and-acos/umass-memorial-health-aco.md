## Overview

UMass Memorial Health operates the **dominant Accountable Care Organization in Central Massachusetts**, managing care for 200,000+ attributed patients across Worcester County and surrounding regions. As the largest health system in Central and Western MA, UMass Memorial ACO represents a **critical partnership opportunity** for home care agencies seeking consistent referrals from a value-based care leader with significant market share.

**What makes UMass Memorial ACO significant:**
- **200,000+ attributed patients** across Medicare ACO, commercial ACO, and MassHealth ACPP partnerships
- **Central MA market dominance:** 60%+ market share in Worcester area (largest provider)
- **WellSky CarePort platform:** Technology-enabled care coordination ($10k-$30k/year subscription = competitive advantage for agencies)
- **Tufts Health Together partnership:** MassHealth Accountable Care Partnership Plan (Medicaid ACO)
- **Academic medical center + community hospitals:** Level 1 Trauma Center + regional hospitals

**For home care agencies:**
- **Geographic focus:** Ideal for agencies serving Worcester, Leominster, Marlborough, Clinton, Fitchburg
- **Technology advantage:** Agencies subscribing to WellSky CarePort get preferential access to referrals
- **MassHealth opportunity:** Tufts Health Together ACO = Medicaid patient population (underserved market)
- **Quality-based partnership:** Performance monitoring, outcome tracking, preferred provider potential
- **Care coordination:** 844-549-0213 (dedicated care coordination line)

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Use This as a Referral Source

**Why UMass Memorial ACO matters for Central MA agencies:**

### **Central MA Market Dominance:**
- UMass Memorial = **60%+ market share** in Worcester area
- Most hospitalized patients in Central MA → UMass Memorial hospitals
- **ACO relationship = access to majority of Central MA patient population**

### **Key Referral Pathways:**

**1. Post-Acute Care Transitions (highest volume)**
- Patient discharged from UMass Memorial hospital
- **Care Transition Team assesses:** High-risk for readmission? Complex medical needs? Social barriers?
- **Refers to home care agency:** 2-6 weeks intensive post-discharge support
- **Agency provides:** Medication management, symptom monitoring, physician follow-up coordination, fall prevention

**Volume:** 30-50% of discharged patients need home care support (Level 1 Trauma Center = complex patients)

**2. Chronic Disease Management Program**
- High-utilizing patients (CHF, COPD, diabetes with frequent ER visits, hospitalizations)
- **UMass Memorial Care Management enrolls:** Comprehensive care plan, home care coordination
- **Refers to home care agency:** Long-term disease management (6-18 months)
- **Agency provides:** Medication adherence, vital sign monitoring, lifestyle support, symptom tracking, caregiver respite

**Volume:** 5-10% of ACO population enrolled in intensive care management (10,000-20,000 patients)

**3. MassHealth ACPP (Tufts Health Together Partnership)**
- **Medicaid ACO patients:** Lower-income populations, complex social needs
- **Barriers:** Housing instability, transportation challenges, food insecurity, language barriers
- **Home care role:** Wraparound support (personal care + social support + care coordination)
- **Payment:** MassHealth (Medicaid rates) + potential Community Partner Organization (CPO) flexible services funding

**Why this matters:** Underserved population, less competition from agencies (many focus on Medicare/private pay)

**4. Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF) Diversion**
- Patient could go to SNF but prefers home with intensive support
- **Care Transition Team evaluates:** Can patient safely return home with daily home care?
- **Refers to agency:** Intensive home support (2-3 visits daily initially)
- **Cost savings for ACO:** $8,000-$15,000/month SNF cost vs. $3,000-$6,000/month intensive home care

**5. Complex Care Management (High Utilizers)**
- Patients with 3+ hospitalizations/year, frequent ER visits
- **UMass Memorial identifies via analytics:** Predictive modeling for high-risk patients
- **Proactive home care referral:** BEFORE next crisis (preventive approach)
- **Agency provides:** Frequent monitoring (daily/weekly depending on needs), early intervention

**Expected patient characteristics:**
- **Moderate to high acuity:** Level 1 Trauma Center patients = complex medical conditions
- **Diverse:** Language capabilities important (Spanish, Portuguese, Vietnamese especially)
- **Mix of payers:** 50% Medicare, 25% private pay, 15% MassHealth, 10% commercial insurance
- **Care duration:** Mix of short-term (6-8 weeks post-discharge) + long-term (6-24 months chronic disease)

## Network Enrollment and Credentialing Process

### **Step 1: Initial Contact**

**Contact UMass Memorial Care Coordination:**
- **Phone:** 844-549-0213 (dedicated care coordination line)
- **Main switchboard:** 508-334-1000 (ask for "Network Development" or "ACO Partnerships")
- **Introduce your agency:** "We're a home care agency serving Central MA and would like to partner with UMass Memorial ACO for care transitions"

**Be prepared to discuss:**
- Service area (which towns in Worcester County do you serve?)
- Capabilities (services offered, languages spoken, complex medical case experience)
- Quality metrics (readmission rates, patient satisfaction, outcomes data)
- References (other hospitals/ACOs you work with)
- Technology (EHR system, ability to use care coordination platforms)

### **Step 2: Complete Network Application**

**Application requirements:**
- **Licensure:** Massachusetts home care agency license (if applicable)
- **Insurance:** General liability ($2-3 million), professional liability, workers compensation
- **Accreditation (preferred):** Joint Commission, CHAP, or comparable
- **Quality metrics:** Readmission rates, ER utilization, patient satisfaction scores
- **Caregiver qualifications:** Training protocols, certifications, background checks
- **Technology capabilities:** EHR system, care coordination platform capabilities
- **Language capabilities:** CRITICAL for UMass Memorial (Spanish, Portuguese, Vietnamese, Arabic, Albanian)
- **References:** 3+ hospital or provider references

**Timeline:** 2-4 months (faster than some ACOs - Central MA needs capacity)

### **Step 3: WellSky CarePort Platform Decision**

**What is WellSky CarePort:**
- Care coordination platform used by UMass Memorial ACO
- Real-time patient tracking (admissions, discharges, ER visits, transitions)
- Direct communication with UMass Memorial care coordinators
- Access to patient medical records, care plans, discharge summaries
- Referral management (receive/manage referrals electronically)

**The competitive advantage:**
- Agencies subscribing to WellSky CarePort receive **priority referrals** from UMass Memorial
- Why? Care coordinators can see which agencies are in CarePort system (easier to refer, better communication)
- **Cost:** $10,000-$30,000/year (depending on agency size, functionality level)

**Should you subscribe?**
- **Yes, if:** UMass Memorial is major referral source (or you want it to be), serving Central MA primarily
- **No, if:** Limited UMass Memorial volume expected, can't justify $10k-$30k/year investment, serve multiple regions (need different platforms)
- **Alternative:** Can participate without CarePort (phone/fax referrals) but lower priority, less volume

**How to subscribe:**
- Contact WellSky: https://www.wellsky.com/careport/
- Mention UMass Memorial ACO partnership (CarePort used by 700+ hospitals/ACOs nationwide)
- Implementation: 1-2 months (staff training, workflow integration)

### **Step 4: Sign Network Participation Agreement**

**Agreement terms:**
- **Service Level Agreements (SLAs):**
  - Assessment within 24-48 hours of referral
  - Care initiation within 48-72 hours
  - Same-day communication for urgent status changes
  - Weekly updates to care coordinators (more frequent for high-risk patients)
- **Quality standards:**
  - 30-day readmission rate target: <15%
  - ER visit rate target: <100 visits per 1,000 patients annually
  - Patient satisfaction target: 90%+ ("satisfied" or "very satisfied")
- **Data sharing:**
  - UMass Memorial provides: Patient data via CarePort (or fax), care plans, medical records
  - Agency provides: Visit notes, patient status updates, outcome data (monthly/quarterly reporting)
- **Payment terms:** Traditional fee-for-service (hourly billing to insurance/patient/family)
- **Term:** 1-2 years (renewable based on performance)

### **Step 5: Care Coordinator Integration**

**Meet the Care Transition Team:**
- Each UMass Memorial hospital has dedicated care coordinators (RNs, social workers)
- **Schedule introductions:** Visit hospitals, meet care coordinators in-person
- Provide direct contact information (cell phone, email for fast communication)
- Attend quarterly network meetings (care coordinator training sessions)

**Communication protocols:**
- **Referral receipt:** Confirm within 2-4 hours (via CarePort or phone)
- **Assessment scheduling:** Within 24 hours of referral
- **Care initiation:** Within 48-72 hours of discharge
- **Ongoing updates:** Weekly status emails/calls to care coordinators
- **Immediate alerts:** Same-day notification for concerning changes (symptoms worsening, medication issues, hospitalization)

### **Step 6: Ongoing Performance Monitoring**

**UMass Memorial tracks:**
- Number of referrals sent to your agency
- Referral acceptance rate (how often do you accept vs. decline referrals?)
- Assessment turnaround time
- Readmission rates for your patients
- ER visit rates
- Patient satisfaction scores
- Care coordinator feedback (responsiveness, communication quality)

**Quarterly reviews:**
- Performance scorecard provided by UMass Memorial
- Meeting with ACO leadership (if performance concerns OR high performance recognition)
- Opportunities for improvement discussed
- Network status maintained OR performance improvement plan

## Care Coordination and Referral Pathways

### **UMass Memorial Care Coordination Structure:**

**Care Transition Team (hospital-based):**
- **RN Care Coordinators:** Embedded in each hospital (UMMC, Harrington, Marlborough, Clinton, Wing)
- **Social Workers:** Address social determinants (housing, transportation, food insecurity)
- **Discharge Planners:** Coordinate equipment, medications, follow-up appointments

**Population Health Team (community-based):**
- **Case Managers:** Manage high-risk, high-utilizing patients (frequent hospitalizations)
- **Chronic Disease Coordinators:** CHF, COPD, diabetes-specific programs
- **MassHealth ACPP Coordinators:** Manage Tufts Health Together Medicaid ACO patients

**Contact:** 844-549-0213 (dedicated care coordination line - all teams accessible)

### **How Referrals Flow:**

**Scenario 1: Post-Discharge Referral (WellSky CarePort agencies)**
1. **Patient discharged** from UMass Memorial hospital
2. **Care Coordinator enters referral in CarePort:** Patient name, diagnosis, care plan, contact info
3. **Agency receives CarePort notification:** Email/platform alert (real-time)
4. **Agency accepts referral in CarePort:** Click "Accept" (care coordinator sees acceptance immediately)
5. **Agency contacts patient:** Schedule assessment within 24 hours
6. **Assessment completed:** Document in agency EHR, update CarePort status
7. **Care initiated:** Begin home care within 48-72 hours
8. **Ongoing updates:** Update patient status in CarePort weekly (care coordinator monitors)

**Scenario 2: Post-Discharge Referral (Non-CarePort agencies)**
1. **Patient discharged** from UMass Memorial hospital
2. **Care Coordinator calls/faxes agency:** Provides patient information, discharge summary
3. **Agency confirms receipt:** Return call within 4 hours
4. **Rest of process same:** Assessment, care initiation, updates via phone/fax

**Why CarePort agencies get priority:**
- Electronic referrals = faster (seconds vs. hours)
- Care coordinators prefer CarePort (see real-time status updates vs. waiting for phone calls)
- Better data tracking (care coordinators can report on CarePort agency performance easily)

**Scenario 3: MassHealth ACPP Referral (Tufts Health Together)**
1. **Medicaid patient identified:** High-risk, complex social needs
2. **Care Coordinator assesses:** Needs home care + Community Partner services (food, housing support, transportation)
3. **Refers to home care agency + CPO:** Coordinated services
4. **Agency provides:** Personal care, ADL support, medication management
5. **CPO provides:** Flexible services funding (food delivery, transportation vouchers, housing assistance)
6. **Coordination:** Monthly care team meetings (agency + CPO + care coordinator + physician)

**Communication expectations:**
- **Response time:** 2-4 hours for referral acceptance, 24 hours for assessment scheduling
- **Weekly updates:** Phone call or CarePort update to care coordinator
- **Immediate alerts:** Same-day notification for hospitalizations, ER visits, significant status changes
- **Monthly summaries:** Formal care summary at month-end (for chronic disease management patients)

## Quality Metrics and Performance Expectations

**UMass Memorial ACO tracks agency performance:**

### **Primary Metrics:**

**1. 30-Day Hospital Readmission Rate**
- **Target:** <15% (UMass Memorial ACO financial penalty if system-wide readmission rate >15%)
- **Measured:** % of patients readmitted to any hospital within 30 days of UMass Memorial discharge
- **Benchmark:** National average 20-25%, high-performing agencies <10%

**2. Emergency Department Utilization**
- **Target:** <100 ER visits per 1,000 patients annually
- **Measured:** ER visits by patients receiving your home care services
- **Focus:** Preventable ER visits (UTIs, dehydration, medication issues, minor falls)

**3. Patient Satisfaction**
- **Target:** 90%+ satisfaction ("satisfied" or "very satisfied")
- **Measured:** Post-care surveys, CAHPS scores
- **Key drivers:** Caregiver consistency, communication, competence, compassion

**4. Care Coordinator Satisfaction**
- **Target:** 90%+ satisfaction from UMass Memorial care coordinators
- **Measured:** Quarterly surveys, informal feedback
- **Key drivers:** Responsiveness, communication quality, clinical competence, problem-solving

**5. Communication Timeliness**
- **Target:** Same-day response to urgent matters, 24-hour response to routine
- **Measured:** Time stamps on CarePort responses (or phone call logs)

### **Performance Reporting:**

**Quarterly scorecard:**
- Number of referrals received from UMass Memorial
- Referral acceptance rate
- Active patients currently receiving care
- 30-day readmission rate
- ER visit rate
- Patient satisfaction scores
- Care coordinator feedback summary

**Annual review:**
- Comprehensive performance analysis
- Network status maintained OR improvement plan OR potential network removal
- Recognition for high performers (referral priority, preferred provider designation)

## Common Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge 1: "WellSky CarePort too expensive ($10k-$30k/year)"**
**Solution:** (1) Calculate ROI - if CarePort generates 50% more referrals, what's revenue impact? (Often $300k-$1M+ additional revenue), (2) Payback typically 2-4 months (not 12 months), (3) Alternative: Start without CarePort, build UMass Memorial relationship for 6-12 months, then subscribe once volume justifies, (4) Negotiate with WellSky (sometimes discounts for new customers or multi-year commitments), (5) Consider if Central MA is primary market (worth investment) vs. one of many regions (maybe not worth it).

**Challenge 2: "Can't serve entire Central MA region (too large geographic area)"**
**Solution:** (1) Focus on Worcester + 1-2 adjacent hospitals (don't need all 5 hospitals immediately), (2) Start with hospitals closest to your office (Worcester + Marlborough OR Worcester + Clinton), (3) Expand gradually as volume grows, (4) UMass Memorial understands geographic constraints (not required to serve all hospitals for partnership), (5) Even Worcester-only coverage = significant volume (781-bed flagship hospital).

**Challenge 3: "MassHealth rates too low (can't afford to serve ACPP patients)"**
**Solution:** (1) Understand total payment model - MassHealth ACPP includes flexible services funding through CPOs (supplements home care payments), (2) Selective acceptance (serve MassHealth patients who are good fit for your agency, decline very complex cases), (3) Mix of payers (80% Medicare/private pay, 20% MassHealth = sustainable), (4) MassHealth patients = less competition (many agencies avoid = opportunity for those who serve), (5) Build relationships with CPOs (Community Healthlink, Seven Hills) - coordinated care = better outcomes + support.

**Challenge 4: "Don't speak Spanish/Portuguese/Vietnamese (language barrier)"**
**Solution:** (1) Hire multilingual caregivers (Worcester has large Spanish, Portuguese, Vietnamese communities - recruit from these communities), (2) Translation services (phone interpretation services like LanguageLine), (3) Partner with agencies serving specific populations (refer patients you can't serve linguistically), (4) Start with English-speaking patients, gradually expand language capabilities, (5) Language diversity = competitive advantage (UMass Memorial highly values multilingual agencies).

**Challenge 5: "UMass Memorial has affiliated VNA (UMass Memorial HomeCare)"**
**Solution:** TRUE - UMass Memorial owns system VNA (skilled nursing, therapy). (1) Different services: System VNA = skilled care (nursing, PT, OT), Your agency = personal care, companionship (complementary, not competitive), (2) Many patients need BOTH (skilled care + personal care), (3) System VNA can't serve all patients (capacity constraints), (4) UMass Memorial refers external agencies for non-skilled care, (5) Position as partner: "We work alongside UMass Memorial HomeCare - complementary services."

**Challenge 6: "Competing with 15-20 other agencies for UMass Memorial referrals"**
**Solution:** Differentiate: (1) Subscribe to CarePort (priority status), (2) Serve broader geography (5 hospitals vs. 1-2), (3) Language capabilities (Spanish, Portuguese, Vietnamese = advantage), (4) Respond faster (2-hour response vs. 24 hours), (5) Specialize (complex medical cases, post-surgical, cardiac), (6) Demonstrate outcomes (present data quarterly), (7) Build personal relationships (visit hospitals, meet care coordinators in-person), (8) Consistent quality (care coordinators refer to agencies they trust - earn trust through performance).

**Challenge 7: "Referral volume slow to build (only 5-10 referrals/month in Year 1)"**
**Solution:** NORMAL for ACO partnerships (12-24 months to mature). Meanwhile: (1) Continue building relationships with individual discharge planners (supplement ACO referrals), (2) Demonstrate quality with every referral (care coordinators increase referrals to high performers), (3) Expand to other hospitals (don't rely solely on UMass Memorial), (4) Be patient - ACO partnerships are long-term strategy (Year 3+ volume justifies early investment), (5) Track metrics showing improvement (show trajectory even if absolute numbers still building).
