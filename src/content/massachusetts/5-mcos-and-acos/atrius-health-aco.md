## Overview

Atrius Health operates one of Massachusetts' **most successful Accountable Care Organizations (ACOs)**, managing care for 700,000+ attributed patients across Eastern Massachusetts. As an HPC-certified ACO participating in Medicare Shared Savings Programs and commercial ACO contracts, Atrius has **strong financial incentives** to partner with high-quality home care agencies that help keep patients healthy at home.

**What makes Atrius ACO unique:**
- **Physician-led ACO:** Unlike hospital-led ACOs, Atrius is primarily a physician network (700+ doctors)
- **Value-based care pioneer:** Operating in risk-based contracts since 2009 (15+ years ACO experience)
- **Population health focus:** Managing entire populations (not just post-discharge patients)
- **Home care = strategic priority:** Atrius ACO views home care as ESSENTIAL to achieving quality/cost goals

**For home care agencies:**
- **Preferred provider opportunities:** Atrius seeks quality home care partners (preferred status = priority referrals)
- **Care coordination support:** Atrius employs 200+ care coordinators, case managers, transitional care nurses (active referral network)
- **Alternative payment models:** Bundled payments, care management fees, shared savings opportunities
- **Quality focus:** Agencies demonstrating quality outcomes get more referrals
- **System-wide partnership potential:** 30+ Atrius locations across Eastern MA (massive volume)

## Understanding Atrius Health ACO Model

### **What is an ACO?**

**Traditional fee-for-service medicine:**
- Physicians paid per visit/procedure (MORE care = MORE revenue)
- **NO financial incentive** to keep patients healthy at home
- **NO incentive** to coordinate with home care agencies

**Accountable Care Organization (ACO):**
- Physicians manage health of patient population (attributed patients)
- **Shared savings model:** If Atrius keeps patients healthier at LOWER cost, Atrius shares savings with Medicare/insurers
- **Financial incentives:** Prevent hospitalizations, reduce ER visits, manage chronic disease proactively
- **HOME CARE = KEY STRATEGY** for achieving ACO goals (keep patients healthy at home)

### **Atrius ACO Structure:**

**Attributed patients:**
- 700,000+ patients managed by Atrius ACO
- **Medicare ACO:** 100,000+ Medicare beneficiaries
- **Commercial ACO contracts:** Major insurers (Blue Cross Blue Shield, Tufts, Harvard Pilgrim)

**ACO leadership:**
- Chief Medical Officer
- VP of Population Health
- Network Development team
- Care Management leadership (200+ care coordinators, case managers)

**Performance-based payments:**
- **Quality metrics:** 30+ measures (patient satisfaction, chronic disease outcomes, preventive care, care transitions)
- **Cost targets:** Medicare sets spending benchmarks
- **Shared savings:** If Atrius meets quality + reduces costs, receives shared savings payments ($10M-$30M+ annually)

**Risk model:**
- Atrius participates in UPSIDE + DOWNSIDE risk
- **Upside:** Share savings if performance good
- **Downside:** Pay penalties if costs exceed benchmarks + quality low
- **HIGH STAKES** = strong incentive to partner with quality home care

## Why Home Care Matters to Atrius ACO

**ACO metrics directly impacted by home care:**

### **1. Hospital Readmissions (30-day readmission rate)**

**ACO measured on:** % of patients readmitted to hospital within 30 days of discharge

**How home care helps:**
- Daily monitoring (catch problems early before ER visit)
- Medication management (prevent medication errors → readmissions)
- Symptom tracking (alert physicians to concerning trends)
- Fall prevention (reduce fall-related hospitalizations)

**Example:**
- CHF patient discharged from hospital
- **Without home care:** 25% chance of readmission within 30 days
- **With home care:** 10% readmission rate (daily weight monitoring, medication adherence, early symptom detection)
- **Atrius ACO saves:** $15,000 per avoided readmission (share savings with Medicare)

### **2. Emergency Department Utilization**

**ACO measured on:** ER visits per 1,000 attributed patients

**How home care helps:**
- 24/7 caregiver availability (prevent unnecessary ER trips)
- Physician communication (call doctor vs. going to ER)
- Symptom management (treat at home when appropriate)

**Example:**
- Elderly patient with UTI symptoms (painful urination, confusion)
- **Without home care:** Family panics, goes to ER ($2,000 ER visit)
- **With home care:** Caregiver alerts physician, patient seen same day in office ($200 office visit)
- **Atrius ACO saves:** $1,800

### **3. Chronic Disease Outcomes**

**ACO measured on:** Diabetes control, blood pressure control, CHF management, COPD exacerbations

**How home care helps:**
- Medication adherence (diabetes medications, blood pressure meds)
- Diet compliance (heart-healthy meals for CHF patients)
- Daily monitoring (blood pressure checks, weight tracking, blood glucose)
- Lifestyle support (exercise, fall prevention)

**Example:**
- Diabetic patient with poor glucose control (HbA1c 9.5%)
- **Atrius physician prescribes:** Insulin, diet changes, monitoring
- **Home care supports:** Daily insulin administration, diabetic meals, glucose monitoring
- **Result:** HbA1c drops to 7.2% (improved ACO diabetes metrics)

### **4. Patient Satisfaction**

**ACO measured on:** CAHPS survey scores (patient satisfaction with care)

**How home care helps:**
- Patients feel supported (not alone at home)
- Better communication (caregivers relay concerns to physicians)
- Improved quality of life (safety, companionship, ADL support)

**Example:**
- Frail elderly patient struggling at home, unhappy with care coordination
- **Home care provides:** Daily visits, medication help, companionship, communication with physician
- **Patient survey:** "I feel well-cared-for by my Atrius team" (improved satisfaction scores)

### **5. Care Transitions**

**ACO measured on:** Quality of hospital-to-home transitions

**How home care helps:**
- Smooth discharge transitions (home care starts immediately)
- Medication reconciliation (ensure discharge medications correct)
- Physician follow-up coordination (ensure post-discharge visit scheduled)
- Early problem detection (prevent readmissions)

## Atrius ACO Preferred Provider Program

**Atrius seeks preferred home care partners:**

### **What is "Preferred Provider"?**

- **Preferred status:** Home care agencies meeting Atrius quality standards
- **Benefits:** Priority referrals, care coordination support, data sharing, alternative payment opportunities
- **Requirements:** Geographic coverage, quality metrics, responsiveness, care coordination capabilities

### **How to Become Preferred Provider:**

**Step 1: Contact Atrius Network Development**
- **Phone:** (617) 559-8000
- **Ask for:** "Network Development" or "ACO Partnerships"
- **Introduce agency:** "We serve [region] and would like to be a preferred home care partner for Atrius ACO"

**Step 2: Complete agency assessment**
- Atrius evaluates: Service area, services offered, specializations, quality metrics, references
- **Be prepared to share:**
  - Geographic coverage (which Atrius locations can you serve?)
  - Quality data (readmission rates, patient satisfaction scores, caregiver retention)
  - Care coordination capabilities (how do you communicate with physicians?)
  - Response time (same-day assessments? 24-hour start?)
  - Specializations (cardiac care, dementia, post-surgical, chronic disease)

**Step 3: Sign partnership agreement**
- **Business Associate Agreement (BAA):** HIPAA-compliant data sharing
- **Service Level Agreements (SLAs):** Response time expectations, quality standards
- **Payment terms:** Fee-for-service, bundled payments, or care management fees

**Step 4: Integrate into Atrius care coordination**
- **Access to Atrius care management team:** Phone numbers, email contacts
- **Referral process:** How Atrius refers patients to your agency
- **Data sharing:** How you communicate patient status back to Atrius
- **Quality reporting:** What metrics Atrius tracks for your agency

**Step 5: Demonstrate value**
- **Track outcomes:** Readmission rates, ER utilization, patient satisfaction for Atrius patients
- **Share data quarterly:** Report quality metrics back to Atrius ACO leadership
- **Continuous improvement:** Atrius monitors preferred providers (maintain quality to keep status)

## Alternative Payment Models with Atrius ACO

**Beyond traditional fee-for-service:**

### **Model 1: Fee-for-Service (Standard)**

**How it works:**
- Agency bills hourly rate ($30-$50/hour)
- Patient/family pays out-of-pocket OR insurance covers (if applicable)
- **No financial relationship** between agency + Atrius

**Pros:** Simple, familiar model
**Cons:** No alignment of financial incentives

### **Model 2: Care Management Fees**

**How it works:**
- Atrius ACO pays agency **care coordination fee** for managing high-risk patients
- Example: $300/month per patient for care management (ON TOP OF hourly care)
- Agency provides: Daily monitoring, physician communication, symptom tracking, care coordination

**Why Atrius pays:**
- Prevents $15,000 hospitalization by spending $300/month on proactive care management
- **ROI for Atrius:** $3,600/year care management fees vs. $15,000+ saved from avoided hospitalization

**How to negotiate:**
- After demonstrating quality outcomes with Atrius ACO
- Propose: "We'd like to discuss care management fees for high-risk patients"
- Atrius evaluates: Do your outcomes justify additional payment?

### **Model 3: Bundled Payments**

**How it works:**
- Atrius pays agency **fixed amount** for episode of care (e.g., 90 days post-hospital discharge)
- Example: $5,000 for 90-day post-discharge bundle (agency provides all needed care within budget)
- **Risk-sharing:** If patient needs more care than expected, agency absorbs cost. If patient needs less, agency keeps savings.

**Why Atrius uses bundles:**
- Predictable costs (Atrius knows exactly what post-discharge care will cost)
- Aligns incentives (agency motivated to provide RIGHT amount of care - not too much, not too little)

**How to participate:**
- Advanced partnership model (after 12+ months of proven performance)
- Negotiate: Bundle rate, episode length, included services
- Atrius tracks: Readmission rates, patient satisfaction (bundle success metrics)

### **Model 4: Shared Savings**

**How it works:**
- Atrius ACO shares portion of Medicare savings with agency (if agency helps achieve ACO goals)
- Example: Agency helps Atrius reduce readmissions by 20% → Atrius receives $10M shared savings from Medicare → Atrius shares $500k with partner agencies

**Requirements:**
- Multi-year partnership
- Demonstrated impact on ACO metrics (readmissions, ER visits, chronic disease outcomes)
- Data-driven performance reporting

**Rare but emerging model** (few ACOs share savings with home care agencies yet)

## Care Coordination with Atrius ACO

**How Atrius coordinates care:**

### **Atrius Care Management Team:**

**Care Coordinators (RNs, Social Workers):**
- Embedded in Atrius clinics (30+ locations)
- **Identify high-risk patients:** Recent hospitalizations, multiple chronic conditions, living alone
- **Refer to home care agencies:** Provide 2-3 agency names to families
- **Track outcomes:** Follow up with families to ensure care working

**Transitional Care Nurses:**
- Call patients within 48 hours of hospital discharge
- **Assess home care needs:** "Do you have help at home? Are you managing medications?"
- **Refer to agencies if needed**

**Case Managers:**
- Manage complex patients (multiple hospitalizations, expensive utilization)
- **Coordinate multi-service care plans:** Home care + DME + transportation + physician visits
- **Partner with agencies:** Regular communication about patient status

**Population Health Team:**
- Analyze data to identify high-risk patients (predictive analytics)
- **Proactive outreach:** Contact patients BEFORE crisis (prevent hospitalization)
- **Refer to home care for preventive support**

### **How to Integrate with Atrius Care Team:**

**Communication expectations:**
1. **Rapid response:** Return care coordinator calls within 4 hours
2. **Assessment turnaround:** Complete assessments within 24-48 hours
3. **Care start:** Begin services within 48-72 hours of referral
4. **Ongoing updates:** Alert care coordinator to concerning changes (new symptoms, medication issues, fall risks)
5. **Discharge communication:** Notify Atrius when services end (patient improved, moved to facility, hospitalized, passed away)

**Communication methods:**
- **Phone:** Care coordinator direct lines
- **Fax:** Care summaries to physician offices
- **Email:** HIPAA-compliant email (if Atrius provides)
- **EHR access (rare):** Some agencies get read-only access to Atrius EHR

## Best Practices for Atrius ACO Partnership

**1. Demonstrate quality with data:**
- **Track Atrius ACO referrals separately:** Readmission rates, ER visits, patient satisfaction
- **Report quarterly:** Share outcomes with Atrius ACO leadership
- **Continuous improvement:** If metrics below expectations, implement changes

**2. Integrate into care coordination workflows:**
- **Know care coordinators:** Build relationships at 10-15 Atrius locations
- **Rapid response:** Same-day assessments for urgent referrals
- **Proactive communication:** Alert coordinators to patient concerns BEFORE crises

**3. Align with ACO goals:**
- **Understand Atrius priorities:** Readmission prevention, chronic disease management, patient satisfaction
- **Position services as ACO solutions:** "We help you achieve quality metrics"
- **Speak ACO language:** Use metrics, outcomes data, value-based care terminology

**4. Specialize in high-risk populations:**
- **ACO focuses on:** CHF, COPD, diabetes, dementia, post-discharge
- **Train caregivers:** Cardiac care, respiratory care, diabetes management, dementia care
- **Position as specialist:** "We excel at managing CHF patients at home"

**5. Invest in care coordination technology:**
- **HIPAA-compliant communication:** Secure email, fax, phone systems
- **Documentation systems:** Track patient status, care plans, physician communications
- **Outcome reporting:** Systems to track readmissions, ER visits, satisfaction

**6. Build system-wide relationships:**
- **Don't rely on one location:** Partner with 10-15 Atrius locations
- **Network Development contact:** Build relationship with ACO leadership (not just individual coordinators)
- **Long-term commitment:** ACO partnerships take 18-24 months to mature

**7. Negotiate alternative payment models:**
- **After demonstrating quality:** Propose care management fees or bundled payments
- **Show ROI:** "We prevented 10 readmissions last quarter - $150k savings for ACO"
- **Start small:** Pilot with 10-20 patients before system-wide expansion

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "ACO partnership seems overwhelming"**
**Solution:** (1) Start simple - build relationships with individual Atrius locations first (referrals happen without formal ACO contract), (2) ACO preferred provider status = advanced strategy (after 6-12 months of location relationships), (3) Contact Network Development when ready (they guide process), (4) Focus on location care coordinators initially (easier entry point).

**Challenge 2: "Atrius wants quality data we don't track"**
**Solution:** (1) Start tracking NOW - readmission rates, ER visits, patient satisfaction (even if manual), (2) Invest in systems (care coordination software, outcome tracking tools), (3) Request guidance from Atrius (what metrics matter most?), (4) Demonstrate improvement over time (Atrius values trajectory, not just current state).

**Challenge 3: "Can't serve all 30 Atrius locations (too broad geography)"**
**Solution:** (1) Focus on subset of locations in YOUR service area (Atrius understands geographic constraints), (2) Target 5-15 locations where you have strong coverage, (3) System-wide preferred provider status NOT required for high referral volume (location relationships sufficient), (4) Expand coverage area gradually if ACO partnership justifies.

**Challenge 4: "Alternative payment models feel risky"**
**Solution:** (1) Start with fee-for-service (traditional model, no risk), (2) Negotiate care management fees BEFORE bundled payments (less risk), (3) Pilot alternative models with 10-20 patients (test before full commitment), (4) Consult financial advisor before risk-based contracts, (5) Many successful agencies never move beyond fee-for-service (ACO partnership valuable regardless).

**Challenge 5: "Atrius refers to multiple agencies"**
**Solution:** TRUE - care coordinators provide 2-3 agency names (not exclusive). Differentiate: (1) Respond faster than competitors, (2) Demonstrate better outcomes (track data), (3) Communicate more effectively (care coordinators remember responsive agencies), (4) Specialize (cardiac, dementia, post-surgical), (5) Build personal relationships (coordinators refer to agencies they TRUST).
