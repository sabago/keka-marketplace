## Overview

MassHealth contracts with **5 Managed Care Organizations (MCOs)** to provide coordinated care to MassHealth members. MCOs manage medical, behavioral health, and long-term services for enrolled members.

**The 5 MassHealth MCOs:**
1. **Boston Medical Center HealthNet Plan**
2. **Fallon Community Health Plan**
3. **Health New England**
4. **Neighborhood Health Plan**
5. **Network Health** (Tufts Health Public Plans)

## Who Sends Referrals

- MCO care coordinators
- Network PCPs
- Hospital discharge planners coordinating with MCO teams

## Who Can Receive Referrals

- Medicare-certified home health
- Private-pay home care (when members have supplemental resources)
- MassHealth-enrolled home care providers

## How It Works

1. **Enroll with each MCO individually** (separate credentialing processes)
2. **Enroll as MassHealth provider** (prerequisite)
3. **Receive referrals** from MCO care coordinators
4. **Coordinate care** with MCO teams
5. **Bill MassHealth** through MCO systems

## Cost and Lead Quality

**Cost:** FREE enrollment; MassHealth rates (lower than private-pay)

**Lead Quality:** High-need, vulnerable populations; long-term relationships

## Best Practices

1. **Start with 1-2 MCOs** (don't try to contract with all 5 simultaneously)
2. **Focus on largest:** Network Health (Tufts) and BMC HealthNet Plan
3. **Build care coordinator relationships**
4. **Understand MassHealth regulations** and billing procedures
