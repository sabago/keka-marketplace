## Overview

Health New England (HNE) is **Western Massachusetts' dominant health plan**, serving 180,000+ members through MassHealth MCO, Medicare Advantage, and commercial insurance. Based in Springfield with deep roots in Hampden, Hampshire, Franklin, and Berkshire counties, HNE represents the **primary MCO partnership** for home care agencies serving Western Massachusetts.

**What makes Health New England unique:**
- **Western MA regional dominance** - 60%+ market share in Hampden County (Springfield, Chicopee, Holyoke, Westfield)
- **MassHealth MCO** - 90,000+ MassHealth members (Standard, CarePlus, Family Assistance)
- **Medicare Advantage** - 65,000+ Medicare members (including dual eligible Special Needs Plan)
- **Commercial insurance** - 25,000+ employer-based members
- **Springfield-based** - Affiliated with Baystate Health (largest health system in Western MA)
- **50+ year history** - Founded 1985, deep Western MA community ties
- **Non-profit community health plan** - Mission-driven, community-focused

**For Western Massachusetts home care agencies:**
- **Regional monopoly advantage** - HNE is THE dominant payer in Springfield/Western MA (vs. Boston with 6 competing MCOs)
- **Single credentialing for multiple payers** - MassHealth + Medicare + commercial access through one network
- **Baystate Health integration** - Preferred referrals from Baystate hospitals (flagship hospital has 716 beds)
- **Underserved population** - Western MA lower-income, higher-need, Spanish-speaking (45% of Springfield is Latino)
- **Less competitive** - Fewer home care agencies in Western MA vs. Boston (easier to establish relationships)
- **Care management infrastructure** - 120+ RN care managers, social workers, Community Health Workers

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from Health New England

**HNE Care Management Structure:**

### **Pathway 1: Baystate Health Hospital Care Transitions (40% of referrals)**

**Baystate Health = HNE's primary hospital partner:**
- Baystate Medical Center (Springfield) - 716 beds, flagship, Level 1 Trauma Center
- Baystate Franklin Medical Center (Greenfield) - 90 beds
- Baystate Wing Hospital (Palmer) - 74 beds
- Baystate Noble Hospital (Westfield) - 97 beds

**How it works:**
1. **HNE member hospitalized** at Baystate hospital
2. **Baystate sends ADT alert** to HNE (real-time notification)
3. **HNE Transitional Care Nurse (TCN) assesses** within 24 hours
   - Embedded at Baystate hospitals (physical presence for better coordination)
   - Reviews medical record, speaks with hospital care team
   - Assesses readmission risk, home care needs
4. **TCN identifies home care need** - "Member lives alone, high fall risk, complex medications, CHF exacerbation"
5. **TCN contacts in-network agency** - Phone or electronic referral
6. **Agency responds** - Within 4 hours, assessment within 24 hours of discharge
7. **Care begins** - 6-12 weeks post-discharge support

**Target population:**
- CHF, COPD exacerbations (frequent in Western MA due to smoking prevalence)
- Falls, fractures
- Diabetes complications
- Post-surgical
- Stroke (Baystate Comprehensive Stroke Center)
- Members with limited family support

**Volume:** 150-250 referrals per month system-wide

**Key advantage:**
**Tight HNE-Baystate integration** (HNE affiliated with Baystate) = smoother referrals, better coordination than non-affiliated hospitals

### **Pathway 2: Senior Care Options (SCO) Complex Care Management (35% of referrals)**

**HNE Senior Care Options = Dual Eligible Special Needs Plan:**
- 15,000+ dual eligible members (MassHealth + Medicare)
- Most complex population (medical + social needs)
- Fully integrated Medicare + Medicaid benefits

**How it generates referrals:**
1. **SCO member identified as high-risk** via data analytics
   - Recent hospitalizations, ER visits
   - Multiple chronic conditions
   - Functional decline, cognitive impairment
   - Social determinants (poverty, housing instability, isolation)
2. **SCO Care Manager (RN) completes comprehensive assessment**
   - Medical, functional, cognitive, social, home safety assessment
3. **Interdisciplinary care team develops plan** (RN, PCP, social worker, pharmacist, CHW)
4. **Home care identified as need** - "Member needs ADL support, medication management, chronic disease monitoring"
5. **Care Manager refers to preferred agency** - Agencies experienced with dual eligibles prioritized
6. **Agency provides long-term care** - 12-36+ months (not just post-discharge)

**Why SCO matters:**
- **15,000+ dual eligibles** = highest-need, highest-utilization population
- **Long-term relationships** (vs. short-term transitions)
- **Higher reimbursement** (Medicare + MassHealth combined)
- **Wraparound services** (home care coordinates with meals, transportation, housing, behavioral health)

**Volume:** 130-220 referrals per month

**Target population:**
- Frail elderly (75+) with multiple conditions
- Disabled adults under 65 (complex medical/social needs)
- Dementia/cognitive impairment
- Behavioral health (depression, anxiety, substance use history)
- Social isolation, no family support
- Poverty, housing instability

### **Pathway 3: MassHealth MCO Care Management (15% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **MassHealth member shows signs of high risk**
   - PCP reports declining function
   - Claims data shows increased ER visits
   - CHW home visit identifies needs
2. **HNE Care Manager reaches out** - Phone assessment
3. **Care Manager determines home care needed** - "Member struggling with ADLs, medication adherence, home safety"
4. **Refers to in-network agency** - Electronic or phone referral
5. **Agency assesses and begins care**

**Target population:**
- Working-age adults with disabilities
- Chronic disease management (diabetes, COPD, CHF)
- Behavioral health stabilization
- Family caregiver burnout

**Volume:** 50-100 referrals per month

### **Pathway 4: Community Health Worker (CHW) Home Visits (8% of referrals)**

**HNE's CHW program:**
- **40+ Community Health Workers** conducting home visits to high-risk members
- **Bilingual CHWs:** Spanish-speaking CHWs for Latino members (CRITICAL in Springfield area)
- **Address social determinants:** Housing, food, transportation, utilities, social isolation

**How it generates referrals:**
1. **CHW visits member at home** (addressing food insecurity, housing concerns)
2. **CHW observes needs** - "Home cluttered, member struggling with bathing, missed medications, refrigerator empty"
3. **CHW reports to Care Manager** - "This member needs home care"
4. **Care Manager authorizes services**
5. **Refers to agency** - Often coordinated with CHW services

**Why this matters:**
- **Proactive identification** before crisis
- **Trust-based referrals** (CHWs have member relationships)
- **Holistic support** (home care + food + housing)

**Volume:** 25-50 referrals per month

### **Pathway 5: Primary Care Integration (2% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **Member sees PCP** (Baystate Medical Practices or community health center)
2. **PCP identifies home care need**
3. **PCP refers to HNE Care Manager**
4. **Care Manager assesses and authorizes**
5. **Refers to agency**

**Volume:** 10-20 referrals per month

## Network Enrollment and Credentialing Process

### **Step 1: Initial Contact**

**Contact HNE Provider Relations:**
- **Phone:** 800-310-2835
- **Email:** provider.relations@hne.com
- **Subject:** "Home Care Agency Network Application"

**Introduce your agency:**
- Agency name, location, years in operation
- Service area (which Western MA communities served?)
- Services offered (personal care, skilled nursing, homemaking, specializations)
- **Languages:** Spanish CRITICAL - how many Spanish-speaking caregivers?
- Member capacity
- Accreditation (Joint Commission, CHAP, ACHC)
- Specializations (dementia, CHF/COPD, behavioral health, dual eligibles)
- References

**Request application:**
Provider Relations will send credentialing packet (15-20 pages)

### **Step 2: Complete Credentialing Application**

**Required documentation:**

**Business and legal:**
- Articles of incorporation
- Federal Tax ID (EIN)
- MA DPH home health license (if applicable)
- Certificate of Good Standing
- W-9

**Insurance:**
- General liability ($1 million per occurrence / $3 million aggregate minimum)
- Professional liability ($1 million / $3 million)
- Workers compensation
- Auto insurance (if caregivers transport)
- Certificates naming HNE as additional insured

**Accreditation (preferred):**
- Joint Commission, CHAP, or ACHC
- Not required but strengthens application

**Agency policies:**
- Care planning and assessment
- Caregiver training and supervision
- Background checks (CORI, SORI, national criminal)
- Quality assurance program
- Incident reporting
- Emergency procedures
- Infection control
- Medication administration (if applicable)
- HIPAA privacy and security

**Caregiver qualifications:**
- Training curriculum (initial + ongoing)
- Certifications (CNA, HHA, PCA)
- Background check process
- **Language capabilities:** Number of Spanish-speaking caregivers (ESSENTIAL)
- Caregiver-to-client ratios
- Supervision protocols

**Service description:**
- Personal care (ADL support)
- Homemaking (meal prep, housekeeping, laundry)
- Companion care (socialization, medication reminders)
- Skilled nursing (if licensed)
- Specialized services:
  - Dementia/Alzheimer's care
  - CHF/COPD disease management
  - Post-surgical care
  - Dual eligible support (SCO specialization)
  - Behavioral health (substance use history common in Western MA)
  - End-of-life care
  - 24-hour live-in care

**Geographic coverage:**
- List all Western MA towns served
- **Rural capabilities IMPORTANT:** Can you serve Franklin County (Greenfield, Orange)? Berkshire County (Pittsfield, North Adams)?
- Response time commitments

**Language capabilities (CRITICAL for HNE):**
- **Spanish** (ABSOLUTELY ESSENTIAL - 35-40% of members)
  - Number of Spanish-speaking caregivers?
  - Fluency level (conversational vs. professional fluency)?
- Polish (Chicopee, Holyoke communities)
- Portuguese
- English

**Quality metrics:**
- Patient satisfaction scores
- Caregiver retention rates
- Hospital readmission rates (if tracked)
- Falls, medication errors, incidents
- Complaint rates

**Financial information:**
- Agency financial statements (demonstrates stability)

**References:**
- 3-5 professional references (hospitals, ASAPs, health plans, community organizations)

### **Step 3: Application Review and Site Visit**

**Timeline:** 45-90 days

**Application review:**
- Provider Relations team reviews
- Verifies licensure, insurance, accreditation
- Reviews policies and quality metrics
- Checks references

**Site visit (likely for new agencies):**
- **Who visits:** Provider Relations staff, Clinical Quality representative
- **What they assess:** Office operations, staff qualifications, technology, quality processes
- **Prepare:** Clean office, staff available, sample documentation, outcome data, introduce Spanish-speaking staff

**Decision:**
- Approved - contract offer
- Conditional - address specific items
- Denied - rare (HNE needs Western MA capacity)

### **Step 4: Contract Negotiation**

**HNE Provider Contract:**

**Reimbursement rates:**
- **Personal care:** $27-$33/hour (negotiable)
- **Homemaking:** $25-$31/hour
- **Companion care:** $24-$29/hour

**Rate negotiation strategies:**
- **Spanish-speaking premium:** Justify +$2-3/hour for bilingual caregivers (HIGH VALUE to HNE)

- **Rural service premium:** Serving Franklin/Berkshire counties involves drive time - justify +$2-4/hour
- **Baystate coordination:** If strong Baystate relationship, leverage for higher rates
- **Quality performance:** Request performance-based increases after demonstrating outcomes

**Authorization requirements:**
- Prior authorization required for all services
- Initial authorization: Care Manager approves hours/week and duration
- Re-authorization: Agency requests continuation with progress notes
- Turnaround: 2 business days
- Denial appeals process

**Service level expectations:**
- Assessment: Within 24-48 hours of referral
- Care initiation: Within 48-72 hours of assessment
- Communication: Weekly updates to Care Managers
- Immediate alerts: Same-day notification for hospitalizations, ER visits, safety concerns
- Documentation: Monthly/quarterly care summaries

**Quality standards:**
- Patient satisfaction: 90%+ target
- Caregiver consistency: Minimize turnover
- Safety: <2% incident rate
- Hospital readmissions: <15% 30-day readmission
- Member complaints: <1% rate

**Billing and payment:**
- Claims submission: Electronic via HNE portal (preferred) or paper
- Payment timeline: Clean claims paid within 30-45 days
- Payment method: Direct deposit (ACH)

**Contract term:**
- Initial term: 2 years
- Renewal: Auto-renews unless terminated with 90 days notice
- Performance review: Annual review

**Termination clauses:**
- Either party can terminate with 90 days notice
- Immediate termination for cause
- Member continuity provisions

**Review and sign:**
- Review carefully (attorney review recommended)
- Ensure rates profitable
- Negotiate if needed
- Sign and return

**Solution:** **This is NON-NEGOTIABLE investment for Western MA.** (1) Recruit aggressively from Springfield Latino communities (North End, Six Corners, South End), (2) Pay premium ($2-4/hour more for bilingual caregivers), (3) Partner with Latino community organizations for recruitment (Nueva Esperanza, Square One, Centro Presente), (4) Start small (hire 3-5 Spanish-speaking caregivers initially), (5) Retention investment (support bilingual caregivers with culturally-appropriate supervision, career advancement), (6) Advertise in Spanish-language media, (7) Without Spanish capability, HNE volume will be severely limited.

**Challenge 2: "Western MA lower reimbursement rates than Boston ($28/hour vs. $32/hour)"**
**Solution:** TRUE - Western MA rates slightly lower due to lower cost of living. (1) Lower overhead (Springfield office rent $1,200/month vs. Boston $4,000/month), (2) Lower caregiver wages (Springfield caregivers accept $16-18/hour vs. Boston $20-22/hour), (3) Net margin similar despite lower rates, (4) Negotiate rates with HNE (more flexible than some MCOs), (5) Mix payer sources (HNE + private pay), (6) Volume opportunity compensates (HNE dominates Western MA = high volume vs. competing for scraps in Boston).

**Challenge 3: "Rural service challenging (long drives to Franklin/Berkshire counties)"**
**Solution:** (1) Negotiate rural premium (justify higher rates), (2) Cluster visits (schedule multiple Franklin County clients same day), (3) Recruit local caregivers (hire from Greenfield, Pittsfield vs. Springfield-based driving out), (4) Selective acceptance (serve rural strategically), (5) Rural = competitive advantage (fewer agencies = more referrals for those who serve).

**Challenge 4: "Members have complex social needs (poverty, housing, food insecurity)"**
**Solution:** Western MA poverty rates high - members have social determinants challenges. (1) Coordinate with HNE CHWs (they address housing, food, transportation), (2) Partner with community organizations (food pantries, housing assistance, transportation programs), (3) Trauma-informed care training (many members experienced trauma), (4) Don't take on social work role alone (leverage HNE resources), (5) Holistic perspective (you're stability, connection, advocacy for vulnerable members).

**Challenge 5: "Behavioral health prevalence overwhelming (40% of members)"**
**Solution:** Opioid crisis hit Western MA hard - behavioral health common. (1) Training investment (mental health first aid, de-escalation, trauma-informed care, substance use recovery support), (2) Start selectively (accept behavioral health cases within your capabilities, decline very complex initially), (3) Coordinate with HNE behavioral health team, (4) Grow gradually (build expertise over time), (5) Specialization = competitive advantage (fewer agencies comfortable with behavioral health = more referrals for those who are).

**Challenge 6: "In-network but low referral volume"**
**Solution:** (1) Spanish capability (ensure you have Spanish-speaking caregivers), (2) Build Care Manager relationships (email, meetings, provider events), (3) Baystate relationships (visit hospitals, meet discharge planners), (4) Geographic coverage (confirm service area accurate in HNE system), (5) Respond fast (4-hour response), (6) SCO focus (dual eligible population = highest volume).

**Challenge 7: "Competing with established Western MA agencies"**
**Solution:** Differentiate: (1) Spanish language, (2) SCO/dual eligible specialization, (3) Rural coverage, (4) CHF/COPD expertise, (5) Behavioral health capability, (6) Speed (4-hour response), (7) Quality (demonstrate with data), (8) Communication (weekly proactive updates), (9) Baystate relationships, (10) **Western MA = less competitive than Boston** (50-75 agencies vs. 200+ in Boston - easier to become known).
