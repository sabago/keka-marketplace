## Overview

WellSense Health Plan is a **MassHealth Managed Care Organization (MCO)** serving 100,000+ MassHealth members statewide. As one of six MassHealth MCOs, WellSense represents a **growing referral opportunity** for home care agencies - particularly those seeking to establish early relationships with an expanding health plan.

**WellSense Health Plan:**
- **MassHealth MCO:** Serving MassHealth Standard, CommonHealth, Family Assistance members
- **Membership:** 100,000+ MassHealth members (growing)
- **Service area:** Statewide Massachusetts coverage
- **Ownership:** Independent nonprofit health plan
- **Official website:** wellsense.org

**For home care agencies:**
- **Growing network:** WellSense actively recruiting provider partners (opportunity to establish relationships)
- **Statewide presence:** Geographic flexibility (not limited to Boston or one region)
- **Care coordination:** WellSense employs care coordinators who refer to home care agencies
- **In-network opportunities:** Credentialing available for all qualified Massachusetts home care agencies
- **Less competition:** Newer MCO = fewer established home care providers (vs. saturated networks at older MCOs)

## Why WellSense Health Plan Matters

**The growing MCO opportunity:**

**Scenario 1: Hospital discharge coordination**
- WellSense member hospitalized (hip fracture, surgery)
- **Hospital discharge planner notifies WellSense care coordinator**
- **WellSense coordinator assesses:** "Member lives alone, needs post-surgical support at home"
- **Coordinator refers:** Contacts in-network home care agencies (provides 2-3 names to family)

**Scenario 2: PCP identifies declining member**
- WellSense member sees primary care doctor (routine checkup)
- **Physician observes:** Patient declining (weight loss, missed medications, poor hygiene)
- **Physician to WellSense care coordinator:** "This patient needs home care support"
- **Coordinator arranges:** Assessment with in-network agency, authorizes services

**Scenario 3: Proactive high-risk member outreach**
- WellSense analyzes member data (identifies high-risk: recent ER visits, multiple chronic conditions, living alone)
- **Care coordinator calls member:** "We're concerned about your health - would home care help manage your conditions?"
- **Member agrees:** Coordinator refers to in-network home care agency

**Scenario 4: Community health worker home visit**
- WellSense community health worker visits member at home (addressing social determinants of health)
- **CHW observes:** Home unsafe (clutter, fall hazards), member struggling with daily tasks
- **CHW reports to care coordinator:** "This member needs home care services"
- **Coordinator authorizes:** Home care agency assessment and services

## Care Coordination Model at WellSense

**How WellSense identifies members needing home care:**

### **Care Coordinators**

**Who they are:**
- RNs, social workers, care managers employed by WellSense
- **Embedded statewide:** Regional care coordination teams

**What they do:**
- **Assess member needs:** Identify high-risk members (chronic disease, recent hospitalizations, complex care needs)
- **Coordinate services:** Arrange home care, DME, transportation, specialty care
- **Authorize home care:** Determine medical necessity, approve hours/services
- **Monitor outcomes:** Track member progress, adjust care plans as needed

**How they refer to home care:**
- **In-network priority:** Care coordinators refer to in-network agencies FIRST
- **Referral process:** Call or email agency → provide member information → request assessment
- **Authorization:** Issue prior authorization for services (hours/week, duration)

### **Community Health Workers (CHWs)**

**Who they are:**
- Frontline workers from communities WellSense serves
- **Cultural/linguistic match:** Spanish-speaking CHWs for Hispanic members, Haitian Creole-speaking for Haitian members

**What they do:**
- **Home visits:** Address social determinants of health (housing, food insecurity, access to care)
- **Identify needs:** Observe living conditions, member functioning, family support
- **Connect to resources:** Link members to community services, home care agencies

**How CHWs generate home care referrals:**
- **Report to care coordinators:** "Member needs home care support"
- **Care coordinator follows up:** Assesses medical necessity, refers to agency

### **Transitional Care Program**

**Hospital-to-home coordination:**
- **WellSense notified of hospitalizations:** Real-time data from hospitals
- **Transitional care nurses:** Call members within 48 hours of discharge
- **Assess home care needs:** "Do you have help at home? Are you managing medications?"
- **Refer to home care if needed:** Connect members with in-network agencies

## How to Become WellSense In-Network Provider

**Being "in-network" = PRIORITY REFERRALS:**

### **Step 1: Visit WellSense Provider Website**

**Provider portal:**
- **Website:** wellsense.org/providers
- **Navigation:** Providers → MA → Join Our Network OR Become a Provider

**Download application:**
- WellSense credentialing application (10-20 pages typical)

### **Step 2: Complete Credentialing Application**

**Required documentation:**
- **Agency license:** DPH home health agency license (required for all MA agencies)
- **Business documents:** Articles of incorporation, EIN, business license
- **Liability insurance:** General + professional liability (typical minimums: $1M/$3M)
- **Agency policies/procedures:**
  - Care planning processes
  - Caregiver supervision and training
  - Quality assurance program
  - Emergency procedures
  - Incident reporting
  - Infection control
- **Criminal background check policies:** CORI/SORI for all caregivers and staff
- **References:** Professional references (hospitals, ASAPs, community organizations)
- **Service description:**
  - Personal care (ADL assistance)
  - Homemaking (meal prep, light housekeeping, laundry)
  - Companion care (socialization, supervision, safety)
  - Skilled nursing (if applicable)
  - Specialized services (dementia care, post-surgical, chronic disease)
- **Geographic coverage:**
  - Which Massachusetts cities/towns do you serve?
  - Response time expectations (24-48 hours for assessments?)
- **Languages available:**
  - Spanish, Portuguese, Haitian Creole, Chinese, Vietnamese, etc.
  - List caregivers' languages fluently spoken

### **Step 3: Submit Application**

**Submission:**
- **Mail or email:** Follow WellSense credentialing instructions
- **Provider Services contact:** (877) 957-1300

**Timeline:**
- **60-90 days typical** (credentialing takes time - be patient)
- **Follow up monthly:** Call WellSense credentialing to check status

### **Step 4: Contract Negotiation**

**WellSense offers contract:**
- **Review carefully:**
  - **Rates:** Hourly reimbursement for personal care, homemaking, companion care ($25-$30/hour typical for MCOs)
  - **Authorization requirements:** How to request and obtain prior authorization
  - **Service expectations:** Response time, quality standards, care coordination requirements
  - **Billing procedures:** Claims submission, payment timelines
  - **Termination clauses:** How either party can terminate contract

**Negotiate if needed:**
- Rates negotiable (especially if you offer specialized services - dementia care, multilingual capabilities, 24/7 live-in)
- Service expectations negotiable (response times, coverage areas)

**Sign contract:**
- Once signed, you're in WellSense provider network

### **Step 5: Set Up Operations**

**Systems integration:**
- **Authorization system:** How to request prior authorizations (online portal, phone, fax)
- **Billing system:** Set up claims submission (electronic or paper)
- **Care coordination contacts:** Get care coordinator phone/email contacts (regional teams)

**Staff training:**
- Train administrative staff on WellSense authorization and billing processes
- Train care coordinators on WellSense care coordination expectations

## Expected Referral Volume from WellSense

**Volume depends on:**
1. **In-network status:** Yes or no? (CRITICAL - out-of-network agencies rarely get referrals)
2. **Geographic coverage:** Broad statewide coverage or limited?
3. **Languages:** Spanish, Portuguese, Haitian Creole = higher volume
4. **Care coordinator relationships:** Know coordinators personally? (Visit WellSense offices, attend provider meetings)
5. **Response time:** Same-day assessments or 3-day wait?
6. **Quality outcomes:** Do coordinators hear positive feedback from members?

**Expected monthly referrals:**
- **Minimal relationship** (in-network, passive): 5-12 referrals/month
- **Building relationship** (in-network, quarterly coordinator contact): 12-25 referrals/month
- **Established partnership** (strong coordinator relationships, rapid response, quality outcomes): 25-50 referrals/month

**Conversion rate:** 70-80% (WellSense-referred members, professionally assessed, MassHealth-covered)

**Client characteristics:**
- **Acuity:** Mix of low-acuity (ADL support) + high-acuity (chronic disease, post-surgical)
- **Care duration:** Mix of short-term (3-6 months) + long-term (1-3+ years)
- **Languages:** 30-40% Spanish-speaking, 10-15% Portuguese/Haitian Creole, 50-60% English

## WellSense Reimbursement Rates

**Typical MCO rates for home care:**
- **Personal care:** $26-$30/hour (negotiated in contract)
- **Homemaking:** $24-$28/hour
- **Companion care:** $22-$26/hour
- **Live-in care:** $200-$250/day (if contracted)

**Rate considerations:**
- **Rates lower than private pay:** Private pay $45-$55/hour vs. MCO $26-$30/hour
- **BUT volume consistent:** MCO provides steady referrals
- **Negotiate rates:** Specialized services (dementia, multilingual, 24/7) may justify higher rates

**Financial viability:**
- **Margin analysis:** Ensure WellSense rates profitable (caregiver pay + overhead + margin)
- **Mix is key:** 30-40% MCO clients + 60-70% private pay = balanced business
- **Don't accept contract if unprofitable:** Better to focus on private pay than lose money on MCO

## Best Practices for WellSense Partnership

**1. Early relationship building (competitive advantage):**
- **WellSense is growing:** Newer MCO actively building provider network
- **Opportunity:** Establish relationship NOW (before network saturated with competitors)
- **Strategy:** Be among first 5-10 home care agencies WellSense care coordinators know well (top-of-mind)

**2. Build care coordinator relationships:**
- **Being in-network ≠ automatic referrals:** Coordinators refer to agencies they KNOW
- **Meet coordinators:**
  - Visit WellSense regional offices (request meetings with care management teams)
  - Attend WellSense provider meetings (quarterly or as offered)
  - Quarterly check-ins (phone, email, coffee meetings)
- **Stay visible:** Respond rapidly to referrals, communicate member outcomes, demonstrate quality

**3. Multilingual capability ESSENTIAL:**
- **WellSense members are diverse:** 40%+ non-English speakers
- **Spanish CRITICAL:** 25-30% of members Spanish-speaking
- **Portuguese, Haitian Creole:** High-need languages
- **Without multilingual caregivers, WellSense volume LIMITED**

**4. Rapid response to referrals:**
- **WellSense referrals often urgent:** Hospital discharge imminent, member in crisis
- **Same-day response:** Call family within 4 hours of referral
- **24-48 hour assessment:** Complete assessment within 1-2 days
- **48-72 hour service start:** Begin services quickly
- **Care coordinators remember responsive agencies** (more referrals to agencies that deliver fast)

**5. Master WellSense authorization process:**
- **Each MCO has different processes:** WellSense has specific authorization requirements
- **Learn the system:**
  - Attend WellSense provider trainings (authorization, billing, care coordination)
  - Use WellSense provider portal (online authorization system)
  - Call provider services with questions: (877) 957-1300
- **Dedicate staff:** Assign one administrative staff to master WellSense processes (billing, authorization, care coordination)

**6. Communicate member outcomes:**
- **Care coordinators want to know:** Are members doing well with your agency?
- **Proactive communication:**
  - Alert coordinator to member improvements: "Mrs. Smith's mobility improving, fewer falls"
  - Alert to concerns: "Mr. Jones' CHF symptoms worsening - recommend physician follow-up"
  - Report when services end: "Ms. Lee graduated from home care - no longer needs services"
- **Coordinators remember agencies that communicate** (more referrals to communicative partners)

**7. Statewide coverage advantage:**
- **WellSense operates statewide:** Members in Boston, Worcester, Springfield, Cape Cod
- **If you serve multiple regions:** Emphasize broad coverage (competitive advantage)
- **If you serve one region:** Partner with WellSense coordinators in YOUR region (Boston agencies → Boston WellSense team; Worcester agencies → Worcester WellSense team)

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "WellSense credentialing taking long"**
**Solution:** (1) Typical timeline 60-90 days (be patient), (2) Follow up monthly (call credentialing: "What's the status?"), (3) Ensure application complete (incomplete applications = delays), (4) Apply to multiple MCOs simultaneously (don't wait for WellSense to complete before applying to Fallon, BMC HealthNet, etc.).

**Challenge 2: "In-network but not getting referrals"**
**Solution:** (1) Build relationships with care coordinators (being in directory ≠ referrals - coordinators refer to agencies they KNOW), (2) Visit WellSense offices (meet care management teams), (3) Attend WellSense provider meetings (visibility), (4) Languages (if you don't have Spanish/Portuguese speakers, referrals LIMITED for diverse populations), (5) Response time (respond same-day - coordinators remember responsive agencies).

**Challenge 3: "WellSense authorization process confusing"**
**Solution:** (1) Attend WellSense provider trainings (offered quarterly), (2) Call provider services (877-957-1300) - ask questions (they WANT you to understand), (3) Use online provider portal (easier than phone/fax), (4) Dedicate staff (one person masters WellSense processes), (5) Network with other agencies (share tips at provider meetings).

**Challenge 4: "WellSense rates too low"**
**Solution:** (1) Negotiate during contracting (especially for specialized services), (2) Operate efficiently (optimize scheduling, reduce drive time, minimize overhead), (3) Mix WellSense + private pay (30% WellSense + 70% private pay = balanced), (4) Consider declining contract if unprofitable (not all agencies should accept MCO - know your margins).

**Challenge 5: "Don't know WellSense care coordinators"**
**Solution:** (1) Request meeting with care management leadership (call provider services: "We'd like to meet care coordination team"), (2) Attend WellSense provider meetings (meet coordinators in group setting), (3) Regional approach (WellSense has regional teams - connect with team serving YOUR area), (4) Quarterly check-ins (don't wait for referrals - proactively stay visible).
