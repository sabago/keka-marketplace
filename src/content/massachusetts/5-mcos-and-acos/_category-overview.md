## Overview

Massachusetts Managed Care Organizations (MCOs) and Accountable Care Organizations (ACOs) represent value-based care models focused on care coordination, quality outcomes, and cost management. These organizations are active referral sources for home care agencies, particularly for post-hospital care transitions and chronic disease management.

**Why MCOs & ACOs Matter:**
- **FREE to partner:** No enrollment fees for network participation
- **Care transitions focus:** ACOs prioritize preventing readmissions (home care is key strategy)
- **Care coordination:** MCO/ACO care managers actively refer to home care agencies
- **Value-based care:** Organizations incentivized to keep patients healthy at home
- **Large patient populations:** MA has 16 HPC-certified ACOs + 5 MassHealth MCOs serving 2+ million residents

---

## MassHealth Managed Care Organizations (MCOs)

### **What Are MCOs?**
Managed Care Organizations are private health plans contracted by MassHealth to provide care for MassHealth members. **5 MCOs serve Massachusetts:**

1. **BMC HealthNet Plan** - Affiliated with Boston Medical Center, serves Boston area + statewide
2. **Fallon Health** - Central MA focus (Worcester area)
3. **Health New England** - Western MA (Springfield, Berkshires)
4. **Neighborhood Health Plan** - Boston/Metro Boston
5. **WellSense Health Plan** - Statewide coverage

### **How MCOs Work:**
- MassHealth members choose (or are assigned to) an MCO
- MCO provides comprehensive medical care + coordinates social services
- MCO care managers assess member needs, refer to home care when appropriate
- Home care agencies must be credentialed with MCO to serve members

### **Why Agencies Care:**
- **MassHealth payment through MCO:** Guaranteed state reimbursement
- **Care manager referrals:** MCO care teams actively coordinate home care
- **Long-term relationships:** MassHealth members often need ongoing care
- **Readmission prevention:** MCOs incentivized to keep members out of hospital (home care is strategy)

### **How to Partner:**
1. **Credential with each MCO separately** (each has own provider enrollment process)
2. **Build relationships with MCO care management teams**
3. **Attend MCO provider meetings, trainings**
4. **Demonstrate quality outcomes** (low readmission rates, member satisfaction)

**Expected referral volume:** 5-20 referrals/year per MCO (varies by agency capacity, geographic coverage)

## Massachusetts Accountable Care Organizations (ACOs)

### **What Are ACOs?**
Accountable Care Organizations are networks of doctors, hospitals, and healthcare providers who coordinate care for a defined patient population. ACOs take financial responsibility for total cost of care + quality outcomes.

**Massachusetts has 16 HPC-certified ACOs:**

**Major ACOs:**
1. **Mass General Brigham ACO** - Largest (3M+ attributed patients)
2. **Beth Israel Lahey Health ACO** (BILH ACO) - 440,000+ attributed patients
3. **Atrius Health ACO** - Large physician organization
4. **Steward Health Care Network ACO** - Community hospital system
5. **Cambridge Health Alliance ACO** - Community-focused
6. **Tufts Medicine ACO** - Academic medical center + community hospitals
7. **UMass Memorial ACO** - Central MA
8. **Baystate Health ACO** - Western MA
9. **Wellforce ACO / Tufts Medicine** (combined)
10. **Reliant Medical Group ACO** - Central MA physician group
11. **Berkshire Health Systems ACO** - Western MA
12. **Community Care Cooperative (C3)** - Physician-led
13. **BMC Health System ACO** - Boston Medical Center-affiliated
14. **Southcoast Health Network ACO** - South Shore/Cape region
15. **Merrimack Valley ACO** - North Shore/Merrimack Valley
16. **Pioneer Valley ACO** - Springfield area

### **How ACOs Work:**
- ACOs manage total cost + quality for attributed patients (Medicare, commercial, MassHealth)
- Financial incentives for reducing hospital readmissions, ER visits, nursing home admissions
- **Home care is critical ACO strategy** (keep patients healthy at home, prevent institutionalization)
- ACO care coordinators refer to home care agencies for post-discharge support, chronic disease management

### **Why Agencies Care:**
- **Care transitions focus:** ACOs desperately need home care agencies to prevent readmissions
- **Warm referrals:** ACO care managers refer directly to agencies they trust
- **Quality partnerships:** ACOs track which agencies have best outcomes
- **Volume potential:** Large ACOs manage hundreds of thousands of patients

### **How to Partner:**
1. **Contact ACO network team** (provider relations, network development)
2. **Introduce your agency's services + quality metrics**
3. **Request to be added to ACO's preferred provider list**
4. **Build relationships with ACO care coordinators, hospital discharge planners within ACO**
5. **Participate in ACO quality improvement initiatives**

**Expected referral volume:** 10-40 referrals/year per ACO partnership (varies significantly by ACO size + your geographic coverage)

## Key Differences: MCOs vs. ACOs

| Aspect | MCOs | ACOs |
|--------|------|------|
| **Payer** | MassHealth (Medicaid) | Medicare, Commercial, MassHealth |
| **Members** | Low-income MassHealth enrollees | All insurance types |
| **How agencies paid** | Through MCO (MassHealth rates) | Varies (Medicare, insurance, private pay) |
| **Partnership** | Credential with MCO | Partner with ACO network team |
| **Focus** | Managed care for MassHealth | Value-based care, reducing costs |
| **Number in MA** | 5 MCOs | 16 HPC-certified ACOs |

## How Home Care Agencies Get MCO/ACO Referrals

### **Step 1: Credential with MCOs (for MassHealth members)**

**Process:**
1. Visit MCO provider websites (each MCO has provider enrollment portal)
2. Complete provider application (agency info, services, insurance certificates, references)
3. Submit required documentation (license, liability insurance, W-9, background check policies)
4. Wait for credentialing approval (4-8 weeks typical)
5. Sign provider agreement
6. Receive MCO provider ID number

**Resources:**
- [BMC HealthNet Plan Providers](https://www.bmchealthnet.org/providers)
- [Fallon Health Provider Network](https://www.fal lonhealth.org/providers)
- [Health New England Providers](https://www.hne.com/providers)
- [Neighborhood Health Plan Providers](https://www.nhp.org/providers)
- [WellSense Provider Resources](https://www.wellsense.org/providers)

### **Step 2: Partner with ACOs (for care coordination)**

**Process:**
1. **Identify ACOs operating in your service area** (use list above)
2. **Research ACO structure** (visit website, understand care model)
3. **Contact ACO network development team:**
   - Call ACO main number, ask for "provider network team" or "care coordination"
   - Email: Look for provider relations contact on ACO website
4. **Request introduction meeting:**
   - Introduce agency services
   - Emphasize quality metrics (readmission rates, satisfaction scores)
   - Highlight geographic coverage overlapping with ACO service area
5. **Ask to be added to ACO's preferred provider list:**
   - Some ACOs maintain formal lists
   - Others rely on informal relationships with specific agencies
6. **Build relationships with ACO care coordinators:**
   - These are the people who make referrals day-to-day
   - Attend ACO provider meetings, networking events
   - Be responsive when ACO calls with urgent discharge needs

**Timeline:** 1-3 months from initial contact to first referral (relationship-driven)

### **Step 3: Demonstrate Value**

**What MCOs/ACOs care about:**
- **Low readmission rates:** Your clients don't bounce back to hospital within 30 days
- **Patient satisfaction:** Families happy with your services
- **Communication:** You keep ACO care coordinators updated on patient status
- **Responsiveness:** You accept referrals quickly, start care same-day/next-day when possible
- **Reliability:** Consistent caregiver availability, don't drop clients unexpectedly
- **Data:** You can provide outcomes data (satisfaction scores, readmission rates, service utilization)

**How to prove it:**
- Track your readmission rate (% of clients readmitted within 30 days)
- Survey client satisfaction regularly
- Share quarterly reports with MCO/ACO showing your performance
- Respond immediately to every MCO/ACO referral (even if you can't accept, be helpful)

## Cost and ROI Analysis

**Cost:** **100% FREE**
- No enrollment fees for MCO credentialing
- No fees to partner with ACOs
- Only investment: staff time for applications + relationship building (4-8 hours/MCO, 2-4 hours/month per ACO)

**ROI:** **Moderate to High**

**MCOs (MassHealth):**
- **Lead quality:** Good (care manager-referred, immediate need)
- **Conversion rate:** 50-70%
- **Payment:** MassHealth rates (guaranteed but lower than private pay)
- **Volume:** 5-20 referrals/year per MCO
- **Best for:** Agencies serving MassHealth populations, urban/diverse communities

**ACOs:**
- **Lead quality:** Excellent (care coordinator-referred, post-hospital, urgent need)
- **Conversion rate:** 60-80%
- **Payment:** Varies (Medicare, commercial insurance, private pay)
- **Volume:** 10-40 referrals/year per ACO
- **Best for:** Agencies near major hospitals, focused on care transitions

**Timeline to first referral:**
- **MCOs:** 1-3 months after credentialing approval
- **ACOs:** 2-4 months after initial partnership meeting (relationship-driven)

## Best Practices for MCO/ACO Partnerships

**1. Start with largest MCOs/ACOs in your service area:**
- MCOs: If you serve Metro Boston → BMC HealthNet, Neighborhood Health Plan
- MCOs: If you serve Central MA → Fallon Health
- MCOs: If you serve Western MA → Health New England
- ACOs: Identify which hospitals in your area belong to which ACOs (e.g., if you're near MGH, focus on Mass General Brigham ACO)

**2. Focus on care transitions (post-hospital discharge):**
- ACOs are desperate to reduce readmissions (financial penalties for high readmission rates)
- Position your agency as "readmission prevention partner"
- Offer rapid response (same-day assessments, next-day care starts)

**3. Track and report your readmission rates:**
- Calculate: (# of your clients readmitted within 30 days) / (total clients)
- Target: <15% readmission rate (industry average ~20%)
- Share your rate with MCO/ACO care managers quarterly

**4. Build relationships with care coordinators (not just executives):**
- ACO executives approve partnerships, but care coordinators make daily referrals
- Visit care coordination teams, introduce yourself, leave business cards
- Respond immediately when care coordinators call

**5. Attend MCO/ACO provider meetings:**
- Most MCOs/ACOs host quarterly provider meetings, trainings, networking events
- Show up, participate, ask questions
- Meet other providers, learn about ACO initiatives

**6. Be flexible with payer mix:**
- ACO patients have various insurance (Medicare, commercial, private pay)
- Don't turn down referrals because payer isn't ideal
- Building trust > maximizing reimbursement per client

**7. Communicate proactively:**
- If client's condition changes, notify ACO care coordinator immediately
- If client at risk for readmission (not taking meds, falling frequently), alert ACO
- ACOs appreciate agencies who partner in care coordination (not just provide services)

## Common Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge 1: "I'm credentialed with MCO but haven't received referrals"**
**Solution:**
- Credentialing ≠ automatic referrals
- Build relationships with MCO care management teams
- Attend MCO provider events, introduce yourself
- Ask MCO: "How can we receive member referrals?"

**Challenge 2: "ACO said they're interested but no referrals yet"**
**Solution:**
- ACO partnerships are relationship-driven (slow build)
- Focus on specific hospitals within ACO (build relationships with discharge planners at ACO hospitals)
- Be patient - first referral may take 3-6 months after initial meeting

**Challenge 3: "ACO patient readmitted to hospital - ACO blames our agency"**
**Solution:**
- Document everything (care plan, visit notes, communication with family/MD)
- Investigate: Why did patient return to hospital? Was it preventable?
- Communicate findings to ACO, show what you did to prevent readmission
- Use as learning opportunity - how can you improve?

**Challenge 4: "MassHealth MCO rates too low - not profitable"**
**Solution:**
- Mix MassHealth clients with private pay (diversify payer mix)
- Focus on efficiency (group clients geographically, optimize scheduling)
- Consider: MassHealth clients are long-term, predictable revenue (vs. unpredictable private pay)

## Success Metrics to Track

**Measure your MCO/ACO performance:**
- **Number of MCOs credentialed:** Target 3-5 MCOs (all in your service area)
- **Number of ACO partnerships:** Target 2-4 ACOs
- **Referrals per MCO/ACO per year:** Track volume by organization
- **Conversion rate:** % of MCO/ACO referrals that become clients
- **Readmission rate for MCO/ACO referrals:** Target <15%
- **Revenue from MCO/ACO channel:** What % of total revenue?

**Benchmarks:**
- Established agency: 20-40% of revenue from MCO/ACO partnerships
- New agency: Start with 1-2 MCOs + 1 ACO (Year 1)
- Growth trajectory: Credential with all relevant MCOs by Year 2, expand to 3-4 ACOs
