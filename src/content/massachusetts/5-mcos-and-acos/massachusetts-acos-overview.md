## Overview

Massachusetts has **16 HPC-certified Accountable Care Organizations** for 2024-2025, covering millions of residents. ACOs coordinate care across providers to improve quality and reduce costs, with strong focus on **care transitions and reducing hospital readmissions**—creating opportunities for home care agencies.

**Key ACOs:**
- Mass General Brigham (largest)
- Beth Israel Lahey Health Performance Network
- Atrius Health
- BMC Health System
- UMass Memorial Health
- Tufts Medicine
- Baycare Health Partners
- Steward Health Care Network
- Plus 8 others

## Who Sends Referrals

- ACO care coordinators
- Network hospitals and PCPs
- Discharge planners within ACO systems

## Who Can Receive Referrals

All post-acute providers supporting ACO quality metrics:
- Home health agencies
- Home care agencies
- Skilled nursing facilities
- Outpatient services

**ACO value proposition for home care:** Preventing readmissions and supporting successful care transitions.

## How It Works

1. **Identify ACOs in your service area**
2. **Contact ACO network development teams**
3. **Position your agency** as partner in reducing readmissions
4. **Demonstrate value** through outcome data (reduced ED visits, hospital stays)
5. **Receive referrals** from ACO hospitals and care coordinators

## Cost and Lead Quality

**Cost:** FREE partnerships

**Lead Quality:** Excellent—ACO-attributed patients with care coordination support

## Best Practices

1. **Understand ACO quality metrics:** Readmissions, care transitions, patient satisfaction
2. **Track and report outcomes:** Show how your services prevent hospitalizations
3. **Build hospital relationships within ACO systems**
4. **Participate in ACO care coordination meetings**
5. **Position as value-based care partner**
