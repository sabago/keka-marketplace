## Overview

Beth Israel Lahey Health (BILH) operates one of Massachusetts' **largest and most sophisticated Accountable Care Organizations**, managing care for over 440,000 attributed patients through the Beth Israel Deaconess Care Organization (BIDCO). With 13 hospitals, 4,000+ physicians, and an integrated delivery network spanning Greater Boston to New Hampshire, BILH ACO represents a **major partnership opportunity** for home care agencies seeking consistent, high-quality referrals from a value-based care leader.

**What makes BILH ACO significant:**
- **440,000+ attributed patients** across Medicare, commercial, and MassHealth ACO programs
- **Beth Israel Deaconess Care Organization (BIDCO):** HPC-certified ACO with 15+ years value-based care experience
- **Preferred Home Care Network:** Structured program for quality agencies (CMS 3+ star requirement)
- **Technology integration:** PatientPing platform for real-time care coordination
- **System VNA:** Beth Israel Lahey Health at Home (understand competitive landscape)

**For home care agencies:**
- **Preferred provider status:** Agencies meeting quality standards receive priority referrals
- **RN Care Manager coordination:** Direct access to hospital-based care managers (617-754-1120)
- **Care transition programs:** Post-discharge support for high-risk patients
- **Quality-based relationships:** BILH tracks readmission rates, outcomes, response times
- **Alternative payment opportunities:** Care management fees, bundled payment pilots

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Use This as a Referral Source

**Traditional hospital discharge vs. ACO partnership:**

**Hospital discharge model (transactional):**
- Discharge planner calls agency for one patient
- Limited information sharing
- No ongoing relationship
- Volume depends on individual discharge planners

**BILH ACO model (strategic partnership):**
- **Preferred provider network:** Quality agencies receive priority referrals across 13 hospitals
- **Care coordinator relationships:** Direct access to RN care managers (not just discharge planners)
- **Data sharing:** PatientPing platform provides real-time patient information
- **Performance monitoring:** BILH tracks your readmission rates, response times, outcomes
- **Volume building:** Consistent referrals as quality proven (not dependent on individual relationships)

### **Key Referral Pathways:**

**1. Post-Acute Care Transitions (highest volume)**
- Patient discharged from BILH hospital
- **RN Care Manager assesses:** High-risk for readmission? Lives alone? Complex medications?
- **Refers to Preferred Home Care Network:** Care manager contacts agencies directly
- **Agency provides:** 2-6 weeks intensive support (medication management, physician follow-up, symptom monitoring)

**2. Chronic Disease Management Program**
- High-utilizing patients (CHF, COPD, diabetes with frequent hospitalizations)
- **BILH Care Management Team enrolls patient:** Comprehensive care plan, home care support
- **Refers to home care agency:** Long-term disease management (6-12+ months)
- **Agency provides:** Medication adherence, vital sign monitoring, lifestyle support, symptom tracking

**3. Frail Elder Support Program**
- Elderly patients (75+) with multiple conditions, declining function
- **BILH identifies through predictive analytics:** High risk of hospitalization, functional decline
- **Proactive referral to home care:** BEFORE crisis (preventive approach)
- **Agency provides:** Personal care, fall prevention, nutrition support, social engagement

**4. Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF) Diversion**
- Patient could go to SNF but prefers home with support
- **BILH Care Manager evaluates:** Can patient safely return home with home care?
- **Refers to agency for intensive home support:** Daily visits, therapy coordination
- **Payment:** Mix of insurance + possible BILH care coordination fees

**Expected patient characteristics:**
- **Higher acuity:** BILH ACO refers complex medical cases (multiple conditions, recent hospitalizations)
- **Care duration:** Mix of short-term (4-8 weeks post-discharge) + long-term chronic disease management (6-24 months)
- **Payment mix:** 40% Medicare, 30% private pay, 20% commercial insurance, 10% MassHealth

## Network Enrollment and Credentialing Process

**BILH Preferred Home Care Network requirements:**

### **Step 1: Initial Contact**

**Contact BILH Network Development:**
- **Phone:** 617-754-1120
- **Email:** BIDCOSNFCareManagers@bidmc.harvard.edu
- **Ask for:** "Home Care Network Development" or "ACO Partnerships"

**Introduce your agency:**
- "We're a home care agency serving [region] and would like to join the BILH Preferred Home Care Network"
- Be prepared to discuss: Service area, specializations, quality metrics, references

### **Step 2: Complete Network Application**

**Application requirements:**
- **Licensure:** Massachusetts home care agency license (if applicable)
- **Insurance:** General liability ($2-3 million), professional liability, workers compensation
- **Accreditation (preferred):** Joint Commission, CHAP, or equivalent
- **CMS Star Rating:** **3+ stars required** (BILH quality standard)
- **Quality metrics:** Readmission rates, patient satisfaction scores, caregiver retention data
- **Technology capabilities:** EHR system, secure communication, PatientPing integration capability
- **References:** 3+ hospital or ACO references

**Timeline:** 3-6 months (rigorous vetting process)

### **Step 3: Quality Assessment**

**BILH evaluates:**
- **Outcomes data:** What are your readmission rates? ER visit rates? Patient satisfaction scores?
- **Caregiver qualifications:** Training protocols, certifications (CNA, HHA, PCA), background checks
- **Operational capabilities:** Response time commitments, 24/7 availability, language capabilities
- **Financial stability:** Agency financials reviewed (ability to sustain partnership)
- **Site visit:** BILH team may visit your office (meet staff, review operations)

**What BILH looks for:**
- Agencies demonstrating quality outcomes (not just low price)
- Responsive communication (same-day response to referrals)
- Specialization in complex medical cases (CHF, COPD, post-surgical, dementia)
- Technology-enabled (able to integrate with BILH systems)
- Long-term partnership commitment (not just transactional)

### **Step 4: Sign Network Participation Agreement**

**Agreement terms:**
- **Service Level Agreements (SLAs):**
  - Assessment within 24-48 hours of referral
  - Care initiation within 48-72 hours
  - Same-day communication for urgent changes
- **Quality standards:**
  - 30-day readmission rate target: <15%
  - Patient satisfaction target: 90%+ satisfaction scores
  - Care plan adherence: 95%+
- **Data sharing:**
  - BILH provides: Patient medical data via PatientPing, care plans, recent hospitalizations
  - Agency provides: Visit notes, patient status updates, outcome data
- **PatientPing integration:** Required platform for real-time care alerts
- **Payment terms:** Traditional fee-for-service + potential care coordination fees
- **Term:** 2-3 years (renewable based on performance)

### **Step 5: PatientPing Platform Integration**

**What is PatientPing:**
- Real-time care coordination platform
- Alerts when BILH patient admitted to hospital, discharged, visits ER
- Access to patient medical records, diagnoses, medications
- Communication portal with BILH care managers

**Agency requirements:**
- **Staff training:** Care coordinators trained on PatientPing (2-3 hours)
- **Workflow integration:** Check PatientPing daily for patient alerts
- **Documentation:** Update patient status in platform
- **Cost:** Typically covered by BILH for network agencies

**Why it matters:**
- **Real-time information:** Know immediately when patient hospitalized (proactive communication)
- **Better care planning:** Access to complete medical history (not just discharge summary)
- **Improved coordination:** Direct messaging with BILH care managers

### **Step 6: Ongoing Performance Monitoring**

**BILH tracks network agency performance:**
- **Monthly scorecard:** Readmission rates, ER visits, patient satisfaction
- **Quarterly reviews:** Performance meetings with BILH ACO leadership
- **Annual recredentialing:** Network status reviewed annually (maintain quality standards)

**Consequences:**
- **High performance:** More referrals, preferred status maintained, potential financial bonuses
- **Poor performance:** Performance improvement plan, reduced referrals, potential network removal

## Care Coordination and Referral Pathways

### **BILH RN Care Manager Team**

**Who they are:**
- **200+ RN care managers** embedded across 13 BILH hospitals
- **Transitional Care Nurses:** Call patients within 48 hours of discharge
- **Case Managers:** Manage high-risk, high-utilizing patients
- **Population Health Coordinators:** Proactive outreach to at-risk patients

**What they do:**
- Identify patients needing home care support
- Refer to Preferred Home Care Network agencies
- Coordinate care plans (home care + physicians + specialists + therapists)
- Track patient outcomes (readmissions, ER visits, satisfaction)

**Direct contact:**
- **Main line:** 617-754-1120
- **Email:** BIDCOSNFCareManagers@bidmc.harvard.edu
- **Hospital-specific care managers:** Each hospital has dedicated team

### **How Referrals Flow:**

**Scenario 1: Post-Discharge Referral**
1. **Patient discharged** from BILH hospital (e.g., CHF exacerbation)
2. **RN Care Manager assesses:** High risk for readmission (lives alone, complex medications)
3. **Care Manager contacts agency:** Phone call or PatientPing referral (same day)
4. **Agency receives referral:** Patient name, diagnosis, care plan, discharge summary
5. **Agency responds:** Confirm receipt within 4 hours, schedule assessment within 24 hours
6. **Assessment completed:** Agency conducts home assessment, develops care plan
7. **Care initiated:** Home care begins within 48-72 hours of discharge
8. **Ongoing communication:** Agency provides weekly updates to RN Care Manager (more frequent if issues)

**Scenario 2: Proactive Chronic Disease Management**
1. **BILH identifies high-risk patient** via predictive analytics (frequent ER visits, uncontrolled diabetes)
2. **Population Health Coordinator reaches out:** Enrolls patient in care management program
3. **Coordinator refers to home care agency:** Long-term disease management support needed
4. **Agency assesses:** Comprehensive home assessment, identify barriers (medication nonadherence, food insecurity, social isolation)
5. **Care plan developed:** Agency collaborates with BILH care team (6-12 month plan)
6. **Ongoing coordination:** Monthly care team meetings (agency + RN care manager + physician + social worker)

**Scenario 3: Emergency Department Referral**
1. **Patient visits BILH ER** (fall, medication issue, symptom concern)
2. **ER physician treats and discharges** (no hospitalization needed)
3. **Care Manager notified via PatientPing:** Patient had ER visit
4. **Care Manager calls patient:** "Do you have support at home? Do you need help?"
5. **If yes, refers to home care agency:** Prevent future ER visits with home support
6. **Agency provides:** Short-term support (2-4 weeks) during vulnerable period

### **Communication Expectations:**

**Response time SLAs:**
- **Urgent referrals:** 2-4 hour response (same day)
- **Routine referrals:** 24 hour response
- **Assessment scheduling:** Within 24-48 hours
- **Care initiation:** Within 48-72 hours

**Ongoing communication:**
- **Weekly updates:** Email or phone update to RN Care Manager (patient status, progress, concerns)
- **Immediate alerts:** Call care manager same-day for concerning changes (symptoms worsening, medication issues, fall, hospitalization)
- **Monthly summaries:** Formal care summary at end of month
- **PatientPing updates:** Document patient status in platform (daily/weekly depending on acuity)

**Communication methods:**
- **Phone:** Direct lines to hospital-based care managers
- **Email:** HIPAA-compliant email (BIDCOSNFCareManagers@bidmc.harvard.edu)
- **Fax:** Care summaries to physician offices
- **PatientPing platform:** Secure messaging, documentation

## Quality Metrics and Performance Expectations

**BILH tracks agency performance rigorously:**

### **Primary Quality Metrics:**

**1. 30-Day Hospital Readmission Rate**
- **Definition:** % of BILH patients readmitted to any hospital within 30 days of discharge
- **BILH target:** <15% readmission rate
- **National average:** 20-25% (agencies below 15% = high performers)
- **Why it matters:** Readmissions cost BILH $15,000-$30,000 per patient (ACO financially penalized)

**How agencies can improve:**
- Intensive post-discharge monitoring (daily contact first week)
- Medication adherence support (pill boxes, reminders, education)
- Early symptom detection (catch problems before ER visit needed)
- Physician follow-up coordination (ensure appointments scheduled + attended)
- Caregiver education (warning signs, when to call doctor vs. ER)

**2. Emergency Department Utilization**
- **Definition:** ER visits per 1,000 BILH ACO patients receiving home care
- **BILH target:** <100 ER visits per 1,000 patients annually
- **Why it matters:** Preventable ER visits cost $1,000-$3,000 (ACO goal is reduce unnecessary utilization)

**How agencies can improve:**
- 24/7 caregiver availability (family can call agency vs. going to ER)
- Direct communication with physicians (call doctor for guidance vs. ER default)
- Patient/family education (when ER appropriate vs. when can wait for office visit)

**3. Patient Satisfaction**
- **Definition:** Patient satisfaction scores via surveys
- **BILH target:** 90%+ satisfaction ("satisfied" or "very satisfied")
- **Measured via:** Post-care surveys, CAHPS scores, online reviews

**Key satisfaction drivers:**
- Caregiver consistency (same caregivers, not rotating staff)
- Communication responsiveness (return calls promptly, proactive updates)
- Care quality (competent, compassionate caregivers)
- Coordination (seamless coordination with physicians, smooth transitions)

**4. Care Plan Adherence**
- **Definition:** Did agency follow BILH care plan recommendations?
- **BILH target:** 95%+ adherence
- **Monitored via:** Documentation review, care manager feedback

**Examples:**
- Care plan says "monitor weight daily" → Agency documents daily weights
- Care plan says "ensure follow-up appointment scheduled" → Agency confirms appointment made
- Care plan says "medication reconciliation" → Agency reviews all medications with patient/physician

**5. Communication Timeliness**
- **Definition:** How quickly does agency respond to referrals, provide updates, alert to changes?
- **BILH target:** Same-day response to urgent matters, 24-hour response to routine
- **Tracked via:** Time stamps on referrals, care manager feedback

**6. Caregiver Qualifications**
- **Definition:** % of caregivers with appropriate training, certifications, background checks
- **BILH requirement:** 100% compliance
- **Monitored via:** Annual credentialing review, incident reports

### **Performance Reporting:**

**Monthly scorecard (provided by BILH):**
- Number of referrals received from BILH
- Number of patients currently receiving care
- 30-day readmission rate
- ER visit rate
- Patient satisfaction scores
- Communication response times

**Quarterly performance review:**
- Meeting with BILH ACO leadership (Network Development, Quality team)
- Review scorecard metrics
- Discuss challenges, barriers, opportunities for improvement
- Action plan for any performance gaps

**Annual recredentialing:**
- Comprehensive review of quality metrics, outcomes data
- Site visit (if performance concerns)
- Network status renewed OR performance improvement plan OR network removal

## Tips to Get More Referrals from This Source

**1. Master PatientPing platform:**
- Check daily for patient alerts (hospitalizations, discharges, ER visits)
- Proactive communication when patients hospitalized ("We're aware Mrs. Smith was admitted - we're ready to support upon discharge")
- Document patient status updates in platform (care managers review)
- **Agencies active on PatientPing get more referrals** (visibility + responsiveness)

**2. Build relationships with hospital-based RN Care Managers:**
- Identify care managers at each BILH hospital you serve
- **In-person meetings:** Visit hospitals quarterly, meet care managers face-to-face
- Provide direct contact information (cell phone, email for fast communication)
- **Responsiveness = trust:** Care managers refer to agencies they trust (earned through consistent responsiveness)

**3. Demonstrate quality with data:**
- Track BILH patient outcomes separately (readmissions, ER visits, satisfaction)
- **Share success stories:** "Last quarter, we served 45 BILH patients with 8% readmission rate (below 15% target)"
- Present data at quarterly performance reviews (show continuous improvement)
- **Data-driven agencies get more referrals** (BILH rewards outcomes)

**4. Specialize in high-risk populations:**
- **BILH prioritizes:** CHF, COPD, diabetes, dementia, post-surgical, frail elderly
- Train caregivers in specialty areas (cardiac care, respiratory care, diabetes management)
- Market specialization: "We excel at managing CHF patients at home - our caregivers are trained in daily weights, symptom monitoring, medication adherence"
- **Specialists get more referrals** (care managers refer complex cases to agencies with expertise)

**5. Expand geographic coverage:**
- BILH has 13 hospitals across Greater Boston, North Shore, MetroWest, South Shore
- **Broader coverage = more referrals:** Serve 5-10 hospitals vs. 1-2
- Consider expanding service area if BILH partnership justifies investment

**6. Invest in care coordination capabilities:**
- Hire dedicated care coordinator (liaison with BILH care managers)
- Technology infrastructure (EHR, PatientPing, outcome tracking)
- **Agencies with strong care coordination get more referrals** (care managers value seamless coordination)

**7. Respond faster than competitors:**
- **4-hour response time** to all BILH referrals (vs. 24 hours)
- Same-day assessments when possible
- **Speed = competitive advantage:** Care managers refer to agencies that respond fastest

**8. Communicate proactively:**
- Weekly updates to care managers (even when no issues: "All patients doing well, no concerns")
- Immediate alerts for concerning changes (same-day phone call)
- **Proactive communication = trust:** Care managers feel confident referring (know you'll alert them to problems)

**9. Attend BILH ACO meetings:**
- Quarterly quality improvement meetings
- Case review meetings for complex patients
- Network provider meetings
- **Attendance = visibility:** Build relationships, demonstrate commitment

**10. Request feedback regularly:**
- Ask care managers: "How can we serve your patients better?"
- Request feedback after each referral (what went well, what could improve)
- **Continuous improvement mindset = more referrals** (care managers value agencies committed to improvement)

## Common Challenges & Solutions

**Challenge 1: "PatientPing platform overwhelming"**
**Solution:** (1) Start with basic functionality (alerts only) before advanced features, (2) Assign one staff member as PatientPing champion (train, then train others), (3) BILH provides training support (request additional training if needed), (4) Check platform daily for 15 minutes (becomes routine), (5) Focus on high-value features first (patient alerts, care manager messaging).

**Challenge 2: "Can't meet CMS 3+ star rating requirement"**
**Solution:** CMS star rating applies to Medicare-certified home health agencies (skilled services). If you're non-medical home care (personal care, homemaking): (1) Explain to BILH you're not Medicare-certified (different model), (2) Provide alternative quality metrics (patient satisfaction, caregiver retention, references), (3) Many non-medical agencies in BILH network (not all have CMS ratings). If you ARE Medicare-certified but below 3 stars: (1) Implement quality improvement plan, (2) Request BILH guidance, (3) Work toward 3+ stars over 12-18 months.

**Challenge 3: "BILH also owns Beth Israel Lahey Health at Home (system VNA)"**
**Solution:** TRUE - BILH has affiliated VNA (skilled nursing, therapy). (1) Different services: BILH at Home = skilled care (nursing, PT, OT), Your agency = personal care, homemaking, companionship (complementary, not competitive), (2) Many patients need BOTH (skilled care + personal care), (3) Position as partner: "We work alongside BILH at Home - they provide skilled care, we provide daily living support," (4) BILH refers to external agencies for non-skilled care (system VNA can't serve all patients).

**Challenge 4: "Credentialing process taking 4-6 months"**
**Solution:** NORMAL for ACO credentialing (more rigorous than hospital). (1) Complete application thoroughly (missing information delays process), (2) Follow up monthly with Network Development (check status), (3) Meanwhile: Build relationships with individual BILH hospital discharge planners (get referrals during credentialing), (4) Be patient - BILH partnership worth wait (high volume, quality referrals).

**Challenge 5: "Readmission rate above 15% target"**
**Solution:** (1) Analyze why: Medication nonadherence? Missed follow-up appointments? Symptom escalation not caught early? (2) Implement interventions: Better medication management, appointment coordination, earlier escalation protocols, (3) Train caregivers: Warning signs for CHF, COPD, diabetes complications, (4) Request BILH support: Care managers may have resources, training materials, (5) Track improvement monthly (show trajectory even if not at target yet), (6) Consider patient selection: Are you accepting patients too complex for your capabilities? Focus on patients you can successfully manage.

**Challenge 6: "Competing with multiple agencies for BILH referrals"**
**Solution:** BILH network includes 20-30+ agencies (not exclusive). Differentiate: (1) Respond faster (4-hour response vs. 24 hours), (2) Communicate better (weekly proactive updates vs. only when problems), (3) Demonstrate outcomes (present data showing low readmission rates), (4) Specialize (cardiac care, dementia, post-surgical expertise), (5) Build personal relationships (care managers refer to agencies they trust - earn trust through consistency), (6) Geographic coverage (serve more hospitals than competitors).

**Challenge 7: "Care management fees not offered by BILH"**
**Solution:** Traditional fee-for-service is standard (90%+ of BILH home care referrals). Care management fees and bundled payments are EMERGING models (not yet widespread). (1) Focus on fee-for-service initially (reliable, familiar model), (2) After demonstrating quality (12-24 months), propose care management fees: "We prevented 20 readmissions last year - $300k savings for BILH. Can we discuss care coordination fees?" (3) Start with pilot (10-20 patients), (4) Be patient - alternative payment models are long-term opportunities (not Year 1 expectation).
