## Overview

Fallon Community Health Plan is a **dual-model health plan** operating as both a MassHealth MCO and Medicare Advantage/ACO in Central Massachusetts. With 300,000+ members across MassHealth, Medicare, and commercial populations, Fallon represents a **comprehensive partnership opportunity** for home care agencies in Worcester County and surrounding regions.

**What makes Fallon Community Health Plan unique:**
- **Regional dominance in Central MA** - 50%+ market share in Worcester area across all payer types
- **MassHealth MCO** - 150,000+ MassHealth members (Standard, CommonHealth, CarePlus, Family Assistance)
- **Medicare Advantage** - 100,000+ Medicare members (including Special Needs Plans for dual eligibles)
- **Commercial insurance** - 50,000+ commercial members
- **Medicare Shared Savings Program ACO** - Value-based care model for Medicare population
- **50+ year history** - Founded 1977, deep roots in Central MA community
- **Non-profit community health plan** - Mission-driven vs. investor-owned

**For Central Massachusetts home care agencies:**
- **"One stop shop" partnership** - single credentialing process accesses MassHealth + Medicare + commercial members
- **Central MA focus** - Worcester, Leominster, Fitchburg, Milford, Southbridge (not spread thin across state)
- **Established care management** - 200+ RN care managers, social workers, Community Health Workers
- **Provider-friendly reputation** - Known for accessible care managers, responsive authorization, fair rates
- **Technology platform** - Fallon Care Connect for real-time care coordination
- **Quality-based relationships** - Preferred Provider Program for high-performing agencies

**Provider contact:**

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from Fallon

**Fallon's integrated care management model generates home care referrals across multiple pathways:**

### **Pathway 1: Care Transitions Program (35% of home care referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **Fallon member hospitalized** at any hospital (UMass Memorial, Saint Vincent, Harrington, Milford Regional, etc.)
2. **Hospital sends ADT alert** to Fallon (real-time admission/discharge notification)
3. **Fallon Transitional Care Coordinator assesses** within 24 hours
   - Reviews medical record (diagnoses, procedures, medications, discharge plan)
   - Calls hospital care coordinator (discusses home care needs)
   - Assesses readmission risk (LACE score, history, social support)
4. **Coordinator identifies home care need** - "Member lives alone, high fall risk, complex medications, needs post-discharge support"
5. **Coordinator refers to in-network agency** - Phone or electronic referral via Care Connect
6. **Agency responds** - Accepts referral within 4 hours, schedules assessment within 24 hours of discharge
7. **Care begins** - Agency provides 6-12 weeks intensive post-discharge support

**Target population:**
- CHF, COPD exacerbations (frequent readmission risk)
- Falls, fractures (hip, wrist, spine)
- Pneumonia, UTI, sepsis
- Post-surgical (orthopedic, cardiac, abdominal)
- Stroke, neurological events
- Members living alone with limited family support

**Volume:** 200-350 referrals per month system-wide

**Key differentiator:**
Fallon Transitional Care Coordinators are **embedded in major hospitals** (UMass Memorial, Saint Vincent) - physical presence enables faster, better-coordinated referrals than remote care managers

### **Pathway 2: NaviCare Complex Care Management (30% of referrals)**

**What is NaviCare:**
Fallon's **Dual Eligible Special Needs Plan** (D-SNP) for members with both MassHealth and Medicare. NaviCare integrates Medicare and Medicaid benefits with intensive care management.

**How it generates referrals:**
1. **NaviCare member identified as high-risk** via predictive analytics
   - Recent hospitalizations, ER visits
   - Multiple chronic conditions
   - Functional decline
   - Social determinants (housing instability, food insecurity, isolation)
2. **NaviCare Care Manager (RN) completes comprehensive assessment**
   - Medical needs, functional status, cognitive status, social support, home safety
3. **Interdisciplinary care team develops plan** (care manager, PCP, social worker, pharmacist, community health worker)
4. **Home care identified as need** - "Member needs ADL support, medication management, safety monitoring"
5. **Care Manager refers to preferred agency** - Agencies with experience serving dual eligibles prioritized
6. **Agency provides long-term care** - Often 12-36+ months (not just post-discharge)

**Why NaviCare matters:**
- **20,000+ dual eligible members** = most complex, highest-need population
- **Long-term relationships** (vs. short-term post-discharge)
- **Higher reimbursement** (Medicare + MassHealth combined funding)
- **Wraparound services** - Home care coordinates with meals, transportation, housing, behavioral health

**Volume:** 180-300 referrals per month

**Target population:**
- Frail elderly (75+) with multiple chronic conditions
- Disabled adults (under 65) with complex medical/social needs
- Dementia/cognitive impairment
- Behavioral health conditions (depression, anxiety, psychosis)
- Social isolation, limited family support
- Housing instability, food insecurity

### **Pathway 3: Medicare Advantage Care Management (20% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **Medicare Advantage member (non-dual)** shows signs of decline
   - PCP reports declining function, missed appointments, medication nonadherence
   - Claims data shows increased ER visits, hospitalizations
   - Health Risk Assessment identifies functional limitations
2. **Fallon Care Manager (RN) reaches out** to member proactively
   - Phone assessment of needs, barriers, resources
3. **Care Manager determines home care needed** - "You're struggling with bathing, medications, meal prep - home care can help"
4. **Refers to in-network agency** - Provides 2-3 agency options to member/family
5. **Member chooses agency** - Agency conducts assessment
6. **Fallon authorizes services** - Typically 8-20 hours/week for 3-12 months

**Target population:**
- Early decline detection (prevent progression to nursing facility)
- Chronic disease management (CHF, COPD, diabetes)
- Post-hospitalization (Medicare Advantage members)
- Family caregiver support/respite

**Volume:** 120-200 referrals per month

### **Pathway 4: Community Health Worker Referrals (10% of referrals)**

**Fallon's CHW program:**
- **50+ Community Health Workers** conducting home visits to high-risk MassHealth and NaviCare members
- **CHWs address social determinants:** Housing, food, transportation, utilities, social isolation
- **CHWs identify home care needs** during visits

**How it generates referrals:**
1. **CHW visits member at home** (addressing food insecurity, housing concerns)
2. **CHW observes** - "Member's home cluttered and unsafe, member struggling with ADLs, missed medications visible, member reports difficulty managing"
3. **CHW reports to Care Manager** - "This member needs home care assessment"
4. **Care Manager follows up** - Assesses medical necessity
5. **Refers to home care agency** - Often paired with CHW services (coordinated approach)

**Why this matters:**
- **Proactive identification** - catches needs before crisis
- **Trust-based referrals** - CHWs have relationships with members (warm handoff to agency)
- **Holistic support** - Home care coordinates with food, housing, transportation services

**Volume:** 60-100 referrals per month

### **Pathway 5: Primary Care Referrals (5% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **Fallon member sees PCP** at Reliant Medical Group (Fallon's affiliated physician practice - 500+ physicians)
2. **PCP identifies home care need** - "Patient declining, needs ADL support"
3. **PCP submits referral** to Fallon Care Management via EHR
4. **Fallon Care Manager assesses and authorizes** - Refers to agency

**Reliant Medical Group partnership:**
Fallon owns Reliant Medical Group (largest physician group in Central MA). **Tight integration** between primary care and Fallon care management = smoother referrals, better coordination.

**Volume:** 30-50 referrals per month

## Network Enrollment and Credentialing Process

**How to become Fallon In-Network Home Care Provider:**

### **Step 1: Initial Contact**

**Contact Fallon Provider Relations:**
- **Phone:** 800-868-5200
- **Email:** providerrelations@fallonhealth.org
- **Subject:** "Home Care Agency Network Application"

**Introduce your agency:**
- Agency name, location, years in operation
- Service area (which Central MA towns do you serve?)
- Services offered (personal care, skilled nursing, homemaking, specialized services)
- Languages spoken by caregivers (Spanish CRITICAL for Worcester area)
- Member capacity (how many clients can you serve?)
- Accreditation (Joint Commission, CHAP, ACHC)
- Specializations (dementia, CHF, COPD, post-surgical, behavioral health, dual eligibles)
- References (hospitals, ASAPs, other health plans)

**Request application:**
Provider Relations will send credentialing application (15-25 pages)

### **Step 2: Complete Credentialing Application**

**Required documentation:**

**Business and legal:**
- Articles of incorporation
- Federal Tax ID (EIN)
- MA DPH home health agency license (if applicable)
- Certificate of Good Standing
- W-9

**Insurance:**
- General liability ($1 million per occurrence / $3 million aggregate minimum)
- Professional liability ($1 million / $3 million)
- Workers compensation (statutory limits)
- Auto insurance (if caregivers transport members)
- Certificates naming Fallon as additional insured

**Accreditation (strongly preferred):**
- Joint Commission, CHAP, or ACHC accreditation
- Not required but significantly strengthens application

**Agency policies:**
- Care planning and assessment
- Caregiver training and supervision
- Background checks (CORI, SORI, national criminal)
- Quality assurance program
- Incident reporting
- Emergency procedures
- Infection control
- Medication administration (if applicable)
- HIPAA privacy and security

**Caregiver qualifications:**
- Training curriculum (initial + ongoing)
- Certifications (CNA, HHA, PCA)
- Background check process
- Language capabilities (list caregivers by language proficiency)
- Caregiver-to-client ratios
- Supervision protocols

**Service description:**
- Personal care (ADL support)
- Homemaking (meal prep, housekeeping, laundry)
- Companion care (socialization, supervision, medication reminders)
- Skilled nursing (if licensed)
- Specialized services:
  - Dementia/Alzheimer's care
  - CHF/COPD disease management
  - Post-surgical care
  - Dual eligible support (NaviCare specialization)
  - Behavioral health
  - End-of-life care
  - 24-hour live-in care

**Geographic coverage:**
- List all Worcester County and surrounding towns served
- Response time commitments (24-48 hours for assessments?)
- Rural capabilities (can you serve Orange, Athol, Winchendon?)

**Language capabilities:**
- **Spanish** (ESSENTIAL - 18% of Fallon MassHealth members)
- Portuguese, Vietnamese, Chinese, Albanian
- Number of caregivers fluent in each language

**Quality metrics:**
- Patient satisfaction scores
- Caregiver retention rates
- Hospital readmission rates (if tracked)
- Falls, medication errors, incidents
- Complaint rates

**Financial information:**
- Agency financial statements (demonstrates financial stability)

**References:**
- 3-5 professional references (hospitals, ASAPs, health plans, community organizations)

### **Step 3: Application Review and Site Visit**

**Timeline:** 45-90 days (faster than some MCOs - Fallon known for efficient credentialing)

**Application review:**
- Provider Relations team reviews application
- Verifies licensure, insurance, accreditation
- Reviews policies and quality metrics
- Checks references

**Site visit (possible):**
For new agencies or those without established track record, Fallon may conduct site visit:
- **Who visits:** Provider Relations staff, Clinical Quality representative
- **What they assess:** Office operations, staff qualifications, technology systems, quality processes
- **Prepare:** Clean office, staff available, sample care plans and documentation, outcome data

**Decision:**
- **Approved** - contract offer extended
- **Conditional** - address specific items (additional insurance, policy updates)
- **Denied** - quality concerns or market saturation (rare in Central MA)

### **Step 4: Contract Negotiation**

**Fallon Provider Contract:**

**Reimbursement rates:**
- **Personal care:** $28-$34/hour (negotiable)
- **Homemaking:** $26-$32/hour
- **Companion care:** $25-$30/hour
- **Live-in care:** $230-$300/day (24-hour care)
- **Skilled nursing:** $60-$80/hour (if contracted)

**Rate negotiation strategies:**
- **Fallon open to negotiation** (more flexible than some MCOs)
- **Spanish-speaking premium:** Justify +$2-3/hour for bilingual caregivers
- **Dual eligible expertise premium:** NaviCare members complex - justify +$3-5/hour
- **Rural service premium:** Serving remote towns (Athol, Orange, Winchendon) involves more drive time - justify +$2-4/hour
- **Quality performance:** Request performance-based increases after 1-2 years demonstrating outcomes

**Authorization requirements:**
- **Prior authorization required** for all services
- **Initial authorization:** Care Manager approves hours/week and duration
- **Re-authorization:** Agency requests continuation with progress notes
- **Turnaround:** Fallon commits to 2 business day decision
- **Denial appeals:** Process for appealing denied authorizations

**Service level expectations:**
- **Assessment:** Within 24-48 hours of referral
- **Care initiation:** Within 48-72 hours of assessment
- **Communication:** Weekly updates to Care Managers (more frequent for high-risk members)
- **Immediate alerts:** Same-day notification for hospitalizations, ER visits, safety concerns, significant changes
- **Documentation:** Monthly/quarterly care summaries

**Quality standards:**
- **Patient satisfaction:** 90%+ target
- **Caregiver consistency:** Minimize turnover on cases
- **Safety:** <2% incident rate
- **Hospital readmissions:** <15% 30-day readmission target
- **Member complaints:** <1% complaint rate

**Billing and payment:**
- **Claims submission:** Electronic via Fallon provider portal (preferred) or paper
- **Payment timeline:** Clean claims paid within 30-45 days
- **Payment method:** Direct deposit (ACH)

**Contract term:**
- **Initial term:** 2 years
- **Renewal:** Auto-renews unless terminated with 90 days notice
- **Performance review:** Annual review to maintain network status

**Termination clauses:**
- Either party can terminate with 90 days notice
- Immediate termination for cause (fraud, quality violations, licensure issues)
- Member continuity provisions (60-90 days for existing members if contract terminates)

**Review and sign:**
- Review contract carefully (attorney review recommended)
- Ensure rates profitable (calculate margins: reimbursement - caregiver pay - overhead = 15-25% margin target)
- Negotiate if needed
- Sign and return

### **Step 5: Operational Setup**

**Provider portal access:**
- **Request credentials** for Fallon provider portal
- **Portal functions:**
  - Submit authorization requests
  - Submit claims
  - Check authorization status
  - Check claim status and payment
  - Verify member eligibility
  - Download resources (policies, forms, training materials)
- **Train staff:** Administrative staff learn portal (plan 3-5 hours training)

**Fallon Care Connect platform:**
- **What it is:** Real-time care coordination platform for ADT alerts, referrals, care plans
- **Enrollment:** Fallon offers Care Connect access to network agencies (optional but recommended)
- **Cost:** FREE for network agencies (Fallon covers subscription)
- **Benefits:**
  - Real-time alerts when members hospitalized/discharged
  - Electronic referrals from Care Managers (faster than phone/fax)
  - Access to member care plans and medical information
  - Communication portal with Care Managers
- **Implementation:** 1-2 hours training, assign staff to check daily

**Care Manager contacts:**
- Request regional Care Manager contact list
- Fallon has care managers assigned by geography (Worcester, Fitchburg/Leominster, Milford, Southbridge)
- Introduce yourself via email to care managers in regions you serve

**Billing system setup:**
- Configure billing software for Fallon claims
- Set up electronic claims submission
- Train billing staff on Fallon requirements

**Staff training:**
- **Care coordinators:** Train on Fallon authorization process, care coordination expectations
- **Caregivers:** Train on Fallon member populations (MassHealth, Medicare, dual eligibles), cultural competence
- **Administrative staff:** Train on portal, billing, authorization

### **Step 6: Launch and Relationship Building**

**Announce network status:**
- Email Fallon Care Managers: "We're excited to be Fallon in-network - ready for referrals in [regions]"
- Update website: "Proud Fallon Community Health Plan In-Network Provider"

**Meet Care Managers in-person:**
- Visit Fallon headquarters (Worcester - 10 Chestnut St)
- Request meetings with regional care management teams
- Quarterly in-person check-ins

**Attend Fallon provider meetings:**
- Quarterly provider forums (network updates, training, networking)
- ATTEND - builds relationships

**First referrals:**
- Respond FAST to first 5-10 referrals (4-hour response, same-day assessment if possible)
- Document excellent outcomes
- Request feedback
- First impressions matter - earn trust with initial referrals

## Common Challenges and Solutions

**Challenge 1: "Credentialing slower than expected (60-90 days)"**
**Solution:** Typical MCO timeline. Meanwhile: (1) Apply to other MCOs simultaneously (BMC HealthNet, Health New England), (2) Build hospital relationships for direct referrals, (3) Follow up monthly with Fallon Provider Relations, (4) Be patient - Fallon partnership worth wait.

**Challenge 2: "In-network but low referral volume"**
**Solution:** (1) Build Care Manager relationships (email, meetings, provider events), (2) Use Care Connect daily (active agencies get more referrals), (3) Languages (ensure Spanish capability), (4) Geographic coverage (confirm service area accurate in Fallon system), (5) Respond fast (4-hour response), (6) NaviCare focus (dual eligible population = highest volume opportunity).

**Challenge 3: "NaviCare members too complex (medical + social needs overwhelming)"**
**Solution:** TRUE - dual eligibles are complex. (1) Start selectively (accept NaviCare referrals that fit your capabilities, decline very complex initially), (2) Training (invest in caregiver training for chronic disease, behavioral health, medication management), (3) Coordinate with Fallon CHWs and Care Managers (you're not alone - leverage Fallon's wraparound services), (4) Grow gradually (serve 5-10 NaviCare members initially, expand as expertise builds), (5) Higher reimbursement + longer relationships = worth investment in capability.

**Challenge 4: "Rural service area challenging (long drive times to Athol, Orange, Winchendon)"**
**Solution:** (1) Negotiate rural premium with Fallon (justify higher rates for drive time), (2) Cluster visits (schedule multiple clients in same town on same day), (3) Recruit local caregivers (hire caregivers living in rural towns vs. Worcester-based driving out), (4) Selective acceptance (serve rural areas strategically vs. accepting every rural referral), (5) Rural = competitive advantage (fewer agencies willing to serve = more referrals for those who do).

**Challenge 5: "Reliant Medical Group already has preferred agencies (hard to break in)"**
**Solution:** (1) Reliant works with multiple agencies (not exclusive relationships), (2) Quality + responsiveness = path to more referrals (start with few referrals, demonstrate excellence, earn trust over time), (3) Meet Reliant care coordinators in-person (visit offices, introduce agency), (4) Specialize (if most agencies generalists, your dementia or CHF specialization differentiates), (5) Don't rely solely on Reliant (also build relationships with Fallon Care Managers for non-Reliant member referrals).

**Challenge 6: "Authorization process confusing"**
**Solution:** (1) Attend Fallon provider training (offered quarterly), (2) Use provider portal (easier than phone/fax), (3) Call Provider Services (800-868-5200) with questions, (4) Dedicate staff (one person masters Fallon processes), (5) Fallon known as provider-friendly (more accessible than some MCOs - leverage this).

**Challenge 7: "Competing with 100+ other Fallon network agencies"**
**Solution:** Differentiate: (1) NaviCare specialization, (2) Spanish language, (3) Rural coverage, (4) CHF/COPD expertise, (5) Speed (4-hour response), (6) Quality (demonstrate with data), (7) Communication (weekly proactive updates), (8) Relationships (Care Managers refer to agencies they know and trust - invest in personal connections).

**Challenge 8: "Fallon rates lower than preferred ($30/hour vs. $50/hour private pay)"**
**Solution:** (1) Negotiate (Fallon more flexible than some MCOs), (2) Mix payer sources (30-40% Fallon + 60-70% private pay), (3) Operational efficiency, (4) Calculate margins (ensure profitable), (5) NaviCare rates slightly higher (Medicare + MassHealth combined), (6) Performance-based increases (request after demonstrating quality).
