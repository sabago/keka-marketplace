## Overview

Reliant Medical Group is **Central Massachusetts' largest physician organization** operating as an Accountable Care Organization managing care for 400,000+ patients. As an Optum-affiliated, physician-led multispecialty practice with 500+ physicians across 30+ locations, Reliant ACO represents a **physician-centric partnership opportunity** for home care agencies serving Worcester County and surrounding regions.

**What makes Reliant Medical Group ACO unique:**
- **Physician-led ACO** (owned by Optum/UnitedHealth Group) - decisions driven by physicians vs. hospital system
- **500+ physicians** - Primary care, 30+ specialties across Worcester County, MetroWest, Southern NH
- **400,000+ patients** - Medicare ACO (MSSP), commercial ACO contracts, Optum health plans
- **Central MA concentrated** - Worcester County primary service area (90% of patients)
- **Fallon Community Health Plan affiliation** - Owned by same parent company (Fallon Health acquired by Optum 2018)
- **Advanced primary care model** - Team-based care, embedded care managers, proactive population health
- **Technology infrastructure** - Epic EHR, care coordination platforms, data analytics
- **Quality performance** - Consistently high ACO quality scores (top quartile MSSP performance)

**For Central Massachusetts home care agencies:**
- **Primary care-driven referrals** - Reliant PCPs identify home care needs during office visits (proactive vs. reactive hospital discharge model)
- **Embedded care managers** - Care managers at practice locations (vs. hospital-based) = longitudinal patient relationships
- **Worcester County focus** - 90% of patients in Worcester County = geographic concentration (vs. statewide systems)
- **Fallon Health integration** - Combined Reliant ACO + Fallon MCO/Medicare credentialing = efficiency
- **Quality-based relationships** - Reliant tracks home care agency performance rigorously (quality outcomes matter)
- **Long-term care focus** - Reliant emphasizes chronic disease management, prevention (12-24 month home care relationships common)

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from Reliant ACO

**Reliant's physician-centric, proactive care model:**

Unlike hospital systems where referrals flow from discharge planners AFTER hospitalization, **Reliant emphasizes PROACTIVE primary care-driven home care referrals BEFORE crisis**. This creates different referral dynamics.

### **Pathway 1: Embedded Care Manager Referrals (50% of referrals)**

**Reliant's care management model:**
- **100+ care managers (RNs, social workers, LPNs)** embedded at Reliant practice locations
- Care managers co-located with PCPs (vs. hospital-based or centralized call center)
- **Longitudinal patient relationships** - care managers know patients over months/years (vs. one-time hospital discharge interaction)

**How it works:**
1. **PCP identifies patient need** during office visit
   - "Patient declining functionally - struggling with ADLs, medications, home safety"
   - "Patient recently hospitalized - needs post-discharge support"
   - "Family caregiver overwhelmed - needs respite, professional support"
2. **PCP refers to embedded care manager** (down the hall, same practice location)
3. **Care manager completes comprehensive assessment** - Phone or home visit
   - Medical needs, functional status, cognitive status, medication adherence, social support, home safety, caregiver stress
4. **Care manager determines home care needed**
5. **Care manager refers to home care agency** - Phone call or electronic referral
6. **Agency coordinates closely with care manager** - Weekly updates, care plan collaboration, monthly care team meetings

**Target population:**
- Frail elderly (75+) with declining function
- Chronic disease management (CHF, COPD, diabetes, dementia)
- Post-hospitalization ongoing support (beyond acute post-discharge period)
- Family caregiver support/respite
- Medication non-adherence
- Social isolation
- Early decline detection (BEFORE crisis)

**Volume:** 120-220 referrals per month system-wide

**Key differentiator:**
**Reliant care managers focus on PRIMARY CARE PANEL** (longitudinal relationships) vs. hospital care managers focused on DISCHARGE EVENT. **Home care referrals = long-term chronic disease management** (12-24 months common) vs. short-term post-discharge (6-8 weeks).

### **Pathway 2: Post-Hospitalization Transitions (30% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **Reliant patient hospitalized** at any hospital (UMass Memorial, Saint Vincent, Milford Regional, community hospitals)
2. **Hospital notifies Reliant** via ADT alert (admission/discharge/transfer)
3. **Reliant Transitional Care Coordinator assesses** within 24-48 hours post-discharge
   - **Centralized transitions team** (vs. embedded at each practice)
   - Phone call to patient, reviews hospital discharge summary, coordinates with PCP and embedded care manager
4. **Transitional Care Coordinator identifies home care need**
5. **Refers to home care agency** - 6-12 weeks post-discharge support
6. **Hand-off to embedded care manager** - After acute post-discharge period, embedded care manager takes over for ongoing chronic disease management

**Target population:**
- CHF, COPD exacerbations
- Falls, fractures
- Post-surgical
- Stroke, neurological events
- High readmission risk

**Volume:** 70-130 referrals per month

**Key insight:**
Reliant's transition model = **TWO-PHASE approach**. Phase 1 (0-12 weeks post-discharge): Transitional Care Coordinator + home care agency. Phase 2 (12+ weeks): Embedded care manager + home care agency for long-term chronic disease management. **Agencies that demonstrate quality in Phase 1 get Phase 2 long-term referrals.**

### **Pathway 3: High-Risk Patient Identification (15% of referrals)**

**Reliant's population health analytics:**
- **Epic Healthy Planet platform** analyzes patient data
- Identifies high-risk patients BEFORE crisis:
  - Rising ER utilization, missed PCP appointments, medication non-adherence, declining function (captured via annual wellness visits)
  - Claims data shows increasing costs, specialist visits, imaging

**How it works:**
1. **Analytics identify high-risk patient**
2. **PCP and care manager alerted** - "This patient at high risk for hospitalization"
3. **Care manager reaches out proactively** - Phone assessment, home visit
4. **Care manager determines home care needed** - "Patient needs ADL support + medication management to prevent hospitalization"
5. **Refers to home care agency**

**Target population:**
- "Pre-super-utilizers" (rising risk trajectory BEFORE becoming frequent hospitalizers)
- Frail elderly with early decline
- Complex chronic conditions
- Social isolation, limited family support

**Volume:** 35-60 referrals per month

**Why this matters:**
**PROACTIVE referrals = prevention** vs. REACTIVE referrals = damage control. **Agencies serving Reliant's proactive referrals = longer relationships** (patient stable, receiving preventive care for 12-24+ months vs. crisis-driven 6-8 week post-discharge).

### **Pathway 4: Specialist Referrals (4% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **Reliant specialist identifies need** (cardiologist seeing CHF patient, neurologist seeing stroke patient, orthopedic surgeon post-op)
2. **Specialist refers to PCP care manager** (Epic message to embedded care manager)
3. **Care manager assesses and refers to home care**

**Volume:** 10-20 referrals per month

**Variability:** Depends on individual specialists (some actively refer, others less engaged)

### **Pathway 5: Fallon Community Health Plan Integration (1% of referrals)**

**Reliant-Fallon common ownership:**
- **Optum owns both Reliant and Fallon** (acquired Fallon 2018)
- **Coordinated care management** between Reliant ACO and Fallon MCO/Medicare

**How it generates referrals:**
1. **Fallon member who is also Reliant patient** identified as needing home care
2. **Fallon Care Manager coordinates with Reliant Care Manager**
3. **Joint referral to home care agency** serving both Reliant ACO and Fallon

**Volume:** 5-10 referrals per month (small percentage but seamless coordination)

## Network Enrollment and Credentialing

### **Reliant Preferred Provider Network**

**Reliant operates structured ACO network** with quality standards and performance monitoring:

### **Step 1: Initial Contact**

**Contact Reliant Network Development:**
- **Phone:** 508-595-2020
- **Email:** network.development@reliantmedicalgroup.org
- **Subject:** "Home Care Agency - Reliant ACO Network Application"

**Introduce your agency:**
- Agency name, location, years in operation
- **Service area:** Which Worcester County towns served? (Reliant concentrated in Worcester County)
- Services offered (personal care, skilled nursing, homemaking, specializations)
- Languages (Spanish important for Worcester area)
- Member capacity
- Accreditation (Joint Commission, CHAP, ACHC)
- Specializations (chronic disease management, CHF, COPD, diabetes, dementia, post-hospitalization)
- **Quality metrics:** Readmission rates, patient satisfaction, outcomes data (CRITICAL for Reliant - physician-led ACO values data)
- References (hospitals, ASAPs, other ACOs, physician practices)

**Request application:**
Network Development will send credentialing packet (15-25 pages)

### **Step 2: Complete Application**

**Required documentation:**

**Business/legal:**
- Articles of incorporation, EIN, MA DPH license (if applicable), Certificate of Good Standing, W-9

**Insurance:**
- General liability ($1-2 million per occurrence / $3 million aggregate)
- Professional liability ($1 million / $3 million)
- Workers compensation, auto insurance
- Certificates naming Reliant as additional insured

**Accreditation (strongly preferred):**
- Joint Commission, CHAP, or ACHC

**Agency policies:**
- Care planning, caregiver training/supervision, background checks (CORI/SORI), quality assurance, incident reporting, emergency procedures, infection control, medication administration, HIPAA

**Caregiver qualifications:**
- Training curriculum (initial + ongoing), certifications (CNA, HHA, PCA), background checks, language capabilities, supervision protocols

**Service description:**
- Personal care, homemaking, companion care, skilled nursing (if licensed)
- **Chronic disease management emphasis:** CHF, COPD, diabetes, dementia (Reliant's focus areas)
- Post-surgical, post-hospitalization, medication management, fall prevention, end-of-life, live-in care

**Geographic coverage:**
- Worcester County towns served (complete list)
- Response time commitments

**Language capabilities:**
- Spanish (Worcester 20% Latino) - number of Spanish-speaking caregivers?
- Portuguese, Vietnamese, English

**Quality metrics (CRITICAL for Reliant):**
- **30-day hospital readmission rate** (for patients you serve)
- **ER utilization rate**
- **Patient satisfaction scores** (CAHPS or internal surveys)
- **Caregiver retention rates**
- **Falls, medication errors, incidents** (safety data)
- **Complaint rates and resolution**
- **Reliant wants EVIDENCE-BASED quality** (not just claims - show data)

**Financial information:**
- Agency financial statements

**References:**
- 3-5 professional references (physician practices preferred - Reliant values physician input)

### **Step 3: Application Review**

**Timeline:** 45-90 days (rigorous physician-led review)

**Review process:**
- **Physician quality committee reviews** (physicians decide - not just administrative staff)
- Network Development and Clinical Quality teams review
- Verifies licensure, insurance, accreditation
- **Reviews quality metrics closely** (readmission rates, satisfaction, outcomes)
- Checks references (calls physician practices)

**Site visit (likely):**
- Reliant conducts site visit for most new agencies
- Assess: Office operations, staff qualifications, technology, quality processes, data tracking capabilities

**Decision:**
- Approved - contract offer
- Conditional - address specific items (often: need better quality data tracking systems)
- Denied - quality concerns or insufficient data (Reliant has high standards)

### **Step 4: Contract Terms**

**Reimbursement rates:**
- **Personal care:** $28-$35/hour (negotiable - Reliant pays for quality)
- **Homemaking:** $26-$33/hour
- **Companion care:** $25-$31/hour
- **Live-in care:** $230-$320/day
- **Skilled nursing:** $60-$85/hour (if contracted)

**Rate negotiation:**
- **Quality premium:** Reliant open to higher rates for agencies demonstrating low readmissions, high satisfaction (+$3-5/hour)
- Chronic disease specialization (+$2-4/hour)
- Medication management expertise (+$2-3/hour)
- Performance-based increases (Reliant reviews annually, rewards quality with rate increases)

**Authorization requirements:**
- Prior authorization required
- Initial authorization: Care Manager approves hours/week, duration
- Re-authorization: Agency requests continuation with progress notes
- Turnaround: 2-3 business days

**Service level expectations:**
- Assessment: Within 24-48 hours
- Care initiation: Within 48-72 hours
- Communication: **Weekly updates to care managers** (Reliant emphasizes care manager coordination)
- Immediate alerts: Same-day notification for hospitalizations, ER visits, safety concerns, significant status changes
- **Monthly care team meetings:** Participate in interdisciplinary meetings for complex patients (care manager, PCP, agency, social worker, pharmacist)
- Documentation: Monthly care summaries

**Quality standards (RIGOROUS):**
- **30-day hospital readmission:** <12% target (Reliant's internal target stricter than MSSP <15%)
- **ER utilization:** <80 visits per 1,000 patients annually (preventable ER visits)
- **Patient satisfaction:** 92%+ target ("satisfied" or "very satisfied")
- **Caregiver consistency:** <15% caregiver turnover on Reliant cases
- **Communication timeliness:** 100% same-day response to urgent care manager requests
- **Care plan adherence:** 95%+ adherence to Reliant care plans

**Performance reporting:**
- **Quarterly scorecard:** Reliant provides performance data (readmissions, ER visits, satisfaction)
- **Quarterly performance review meetings:** Discuss metrics, opportunities for improvement
- **Annual recredentialing:** Network status reviewed annually based on quality performance

**Billing/payment:**
- Electronic claims via Reliant portal or paper
- Payment: Clean claims paid within 30-45 days
- Direct deposit (ACH)

**Contract term:**
- Initial: 2 years
- Auto-renews unless terminated with 90 days notice
- Annual performance review (maintain network status)

### **Step 5: Epic Care Link Access**

**Reliant uses Epic EHR:**
- **Epic Care Link** = referral and care coordination platform for external partners
- Reliant offers Care Link access to network agencies (optional but highly recommended)
- **Cost:** FREE for network agencies
- **Benefits:**
  - Electronic referrals from care managers, PCPs
  - Access to patient records (medications, diagnoses, care plans, recent visits)
  - Direct messaging with care managers
  - Real-time updates on patient status

**Implementation:**
- 2-3 hours training (Reliant provides)
- Assign staff to check Care Link daily
- **Agencies using Care Link receive 40-50% more referrals** (care managers prefer electronic workflow, better communication)

### **Step 6: Launch**

**Announce network status:**
- Email Reliant Care Managers (Reliant will provide contact list)
- Update website

**Meet care managers:**
- Visit Reliant practice locations (Worcester Main Street flagship, Shrewsbury, Leominster, Marlborough)
- Request meetings with embedded care managers
- Quarterly in-person check-ins

**Attend Reliant provider meetings:**
- Quarterly ACO provider forums

**First referrals:**
- Respond FAST (4-hour response)
- Document outcomes meticulously (Reliant tracks closely)
- Request feedback

## Tips to Get More Referrals from Reliant ACO

**1. Demonstrate quality with rigorous data (THE differentiator for physician-led ACO):**
- Track Reliant patients separately (readmissions, ER visits, satisfaction, falls, medication errors)
- **Quarterly data reports to Reliant:** "Last quarter: 32 Reliant patients, 4% readmission rate (below 12% target), 96% satisfaction, ZERO falls"
- **Reliant physicians value EVIDENCE** - data-driven agencies get significantly more referrals
- Use outcomes to negotiate higher rates ("Our 4% readmission rate saves Reliant $X annually")

**2. Use Epic Care Link actively (essential for high volume):**
- Agencies using Care Link receive 40-50% more referrals
- Check daily, accept referrals within 2 hours, update patient status 2-3 times/week
- Care managers prefer electronic workflow

**3. Build relationships with embedded care managers (not just corporate):**
- **100+ embedded care managers** at practice locations = decentralized referral sources
- Visit practice locations (Worcester, Shrewsbury, Leominster, Marlborough)
- Meet embedded care managers in-person
- Provide direct contact info (cell phone, email)
- **Embedded care managers have autonomy** - they choose which agencies to refer to (based on past responsiveness, quality)

**4. Emphasize long-term chronic disease management (Reliant's focus):**
- **Reliant emphasizes prevention + chronic disease management** (vs. acute post-discharge only)
- Train caregivers in chronic disease-specific care:
  - CHF: Daily weights, symptom monitoring, medication adherence, low-sodium diet, exercise
  - COPD: Inhaler technique, symptom recognition, smoking cessation, pulmonary rehab
  - Diabetes: Blood sugar monitoring, medication management, foot care, nutrition
  - Dementia: Safety, routine, behavioral management, caregiver education
- Market specialization: "We excel at long-term CHF management - our caregivers trained in disease-specific care"

**5. Participate actively in care team meetings:**
- Reliant hosts monthly interdisciplinary care team meetings for complex patients
- **Attend meetings** (builds relationships, demonstrates commitment, quality collaboration)
- Provide patient updates, contribute to care planning

**6. Coordinate closely with care managers (communication is key):**
- **Weekly proactive updates** via email/Care Link (don't wait for care managers to call you)
- Immediate alerts for hospitalizations, ER visits, safety concerns (same-day phone call)
- **Care managers value agencies that communicate proactively** (reduces their workload, increases trust)

**7. Serve Worcester County comprehensively (geographic breadth):**
- Reliant has 30+ locations across Worcester County, MetroWest
- Broader coverage = more referrals (vs. Worcester-only)
- Consider expanding to Leominster, Marlborough, Shrewsbury if not currently served

**8. Position as medication management expert:**
- **Medication non-adherence = major Reliant concern** (causes hospitalizations, poor disease control)
- Train caregivers in medication management:
  - Pill organization, reminders, technique (inhalers, injections)
  - Medication reconciliation (compare home meds to discharge summary)
  - Side effect monitoring, escalation when needed
- Market capability: "We specialize in medication adherence - our caregivers trained in medication management"

**9. Respond faster than competitors:**
- 4-hour response to ALL Reliant referrals
- Same-day assessments when possible
- **Speed + quality data = winning combination for Reliant**

**10. Leverage Fallon Community Health Plan relationship:**
- Credential with both Reliant ACO and Fallon MCO/Medicare
- Combined volume opportunity (Reliant ACO patients + Fallon members)
- **Optum common ownership** creates coordination opportunities
