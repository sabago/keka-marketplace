## Overview

Mass General Brigham (MGB) Accountable Care Organization is the **largest ACO in Massachusetts**, managing care for 3+ million attributed patients across Greater Boston, MetroWest, North Shore, and beyond. As part of the Mass General Brigham health system (12 hospitals, 200+ clinics), the MGB ACO represents a **critical partnership opportunity** for home care agencies seeking value-based care contracts and hospital discharge referrals.

**What is an ACO:**
- Accountable Care Organization = healthcare providers coordinating patient care
- **Financial incentives for quality + cost containment** (shared savings model)
- Focus on preventing hospital readmissions, managing chronic disease, care transitions
- **Home care = key strategy** for keeping patients healthy at home (vs. expensive hospitalizations)

**MGB ACO scale:**
- **3+ million attributed patients** (largest ACO in MA)
- **12 hospitals:** Mass General, Brigham and Women's, Newton-Wellesley, North Shore Medical, Cooley Dickinson, McLean, Faulkner, plus others
- **200+ outpatient clinics, physician practices**
- **HPC-certified:** Massachusetts Health Policy Commission certified ACO (meets quality standards)
- **$15+ billion** in annual system revenue

## Why MGB ACO Matters for Home Care Agencies

**The value-based care shift:**
- Traditional healthcare: Hospitals paid per service (fee-for-service) = no incentive to prevent readmissions
- **ACO model:** Hospitals financially rewarded for keeping patients HEALTHY (shared savings if costs down + quality up)
- **Home care role:** Patients recovering at home with professional support = fewer ER visits, fewer readmissions, lower costs
- **ACOs NEED home care agencies** to succeed in value-based contracts

**For home care agencies:**
- **Access to MGB patient population:** 3+ million patients = massive referral potential
- **Care transition programs:** MGB ACO identifies high-risk patients post-discharge → refers to home care for support
- **Preferred provider opportunities:** ACOs create "preferred provider networks" (agencies meeting quality standards get priority referrals)
- **Data sharing:** ACO shares patient data (diagnoses, medications, care plans) → better care coordination
- **Payment models:** Some ACOs exploring alternative payment (bundled payments, pay-for-performance vs. fee-for-service)

## How MGB ACO Differs from Hospital Referrals

**Traditional hospital discharge planning:**
- Patient discharged → discharge planner calls agency → agency provides care → agency bills insurance/patient
- **Transactional** (one-time referral, no ongoing relationship)

**MGB ACO model:**
- **Proactive identification:** ACO care coordinators identify high-risk patients BEFORE discharge
- **Ongoing partnership:** Agency becomes part of ACO care team (regular communication, shared goals)
- **Data integration:** Agency accesses ACO patient portal (medications, diagnoses, recent hospitalizations)
- **Quality monitoring:** ACO tracks agency performance (readmission rates, patient satisfaction, care plan adherence)
- **Preferred provider status:** High-performing agencies get more referrals

**Benefits for agencies:**
- **Higher volume:** ACO relationship = consistent referral flow (not just occasional discharge referral)
- **Better information:** Access to patient medical data (better care planning)
- **Long-term partnership:** ACO invests in relationships with preferred agencies (vs. transactional hospital referrals)
- **Quality focus:** ACOs value OUTCOMES (not just price) = agencies with good quality can command fair rates

## MGB ACO Care Transition Programs

**MGB operates several programs generating home care referrals:**

### **1. Post-Discharge Support Program**

**How it works:**
- **High-risk patient** discharged from MGB hospital (recent surgery, CHF exacerbation, fall, multiple conditions)
- **ACO care coordinator assesses:** Does patient need home support to prevent readmission?
- **If yes:** Care coordinator refers to home care agency for 2-4 weeks intensive support
- **Goals:** Medication adherence, follow-up appointments, monitoring vitals, preventing readmission

**Agency role:**
- Provide 10-20 hours/week support during critical post-discharge period
- **Daily communication** with ACO care coordinator (patient status updates)
- Identify warning signs (worsening symptoms) → escalate to ACO team
- Ensure patient attends follow-up appointments

**Payment:**
- Traditional insurance billing (Medicare, MassHealth, private pay)
- Some ACOs exploring **bundled payments** (single payment for episode of care - hospital + home care + physician)

### **2. Chronic Disease Management Program**

**How it works:**
- **Patient with chronic conditions** (CHF, COPD, diabetes, multiple comorbidities)
- Frequent ER visits, hospitalizations (high cost, poor outcomes)
- **ACO enrolls in care management:** Nurse care coordinator, care plan, home care support
- **Home care agency provides:** Medication management, vital sign monitoring, symptom tracking, caregiver support

**Agency role:**
- Long-term engagement (6-12+ months)
- **Weekly/biweekly** communication with ACO nurse care coordinator
- Track patient metrics (weight, blood pressure, blood sugar, symptoms)
- Prevent disease exacerbations (catch problems early)

**Payment:**
- Traditional insurance + possible care management fees
- Some ACOs pay **monthly care coordination fees** to agencies (supplementing per-visit billing)

### **3. Transitional Care for Frail Elderly**

**How it works:**
- **Frail elder** (age 75+, multiple conditions, recent hospitalization)
- High risk of readmission, decline, nursing home placement
- **ACO provides:** Comprehensive assessment, care plan, home care + other services coordination

**Agency role:**
- Personal care, homemaking, companionship
- **Coordinate with ACO team:** Physical therapy, occupational therapy, nursing, social work
- Monitor for decline (report to ACO if patient deteriorating)

**Payment:**
- Mix of insurance + patient private pay + possible ACO care coordination payments

## How to Partner with MGB ACO

### **Step 1: Understand MGB ACO Structure**

**MGB ACO is NOT a single entity:**
- **12 hospitals each have care transition teams** (separate contacts)
- **ACO leadership coordinates** (system-wide strategy)
- **200+ physician practices participate** (some have own care coordinators)

**Implication:** You'll build relationships at MULTIPLE levels:
- System ACO leadership (strategic partnership)
- Hospital care transition teams (day-to-day referrals)
- Physician practices (primary care referrals)

### **Step 2: Contact MGB ACO Network Development**

**Initial contact:**
- **Phone:** 617-278-1000 (MGB main number)
- **Ask for:** "ACO Network Development" or "Community Partnerships"
- **Introduce agency:** "We're a home care agency serving [region], we'd like to explore partnership with MGB ACO for care transitions"

**Be prepared to discuss:**
- **Service area:** Which MGB hospitals/regions do you cover?
- **Capabilities:** What services? Languages? 24/7 availability? Complex medical cases?
- **Quality metrics:** Do you track readmission rates? Patient satisfaction? Outcomes?
- **Technology:** Can you integrate with ACO patient portal? Accept electronic referrals?
- **References:** Other ACOs/hospitals you work with?

### **Step 3: Complete ACO Credentialing**

**ACO credentialing more rigorous than typical hospital:**
- Home care agency license (if required)
- Liability insurance ($2-5 million - higher than typical)
- **Quality data:** Readmission rates, satisfaction scores, CAHPS data (if available)
- **Caregiver qualifications:** Training protocols, certifications, background checks
- **Technology capabilities:** EHR integration, secure messaging, data sharing
- **Site visit:** ACO may visit your agency (assess operations, meet team)

**Timeline:** 2-6 months (longer than hospital credentialing)

### **Step 4: Sign ACO Participation Agreement**

**Agreement terms:**
- **Service requirements:** Response times (often tighter than hospital requirements), communication protocols
- **Quality standards:** Readmission rate targets, satisfaction benchmarks, care plan adherence
- **Data sharing:** ACO provides patient data, you provide visit notes/outcomes
- **Payment terms:** May include traditional fee-for-service + potential shared savings or care coordination fees
- **Term:** 1-3 years typical (longer commitment than hospital agreements)

### **Step 5: Integrate into ACO Workflows**

**Ongoing:**
- **Technology integration:** Access ACO patient portal, electronic referrals
- **Attend ACO meetings:** Quarterly quality improvement meetings, case reviews
- **Report outcomes:** Monthly/quarterly reporting (readmissions, patient status, challenges)
- **Continuous improvement:** ACO provides feedback, you adapt practices

## MGB ACO Quality Expectations

**ACOs track agency performance closely:**

**Metrics monitored:**
1. **30-day readmission rate:** % of patients readmitted within 30 days (target: <15%)
2. **ER visit rate:** % of patients with ER visits during home care (target: <10%)
3. **Patient satisfaction:** CAHPS scores, surveys (target: 4.5+ stars)
4. **Care plan adherence:** Did agency follow ACO care plan? (target: 95%+)
5. **Communication timeliness:** How quickly did agency respond to referrals? Update ACO on changes? (target: same-day)
6. **Medication adherence:** Are patients taking meds as prescribed? (target: 90%+)
7. **Follow-up appointment adherence:** Did patients attend post-discharge appointments? (target: 85%+)

**Consequences:**
- **High performance:** More referrals, preferred provider status, possible financial bonuses
- **Poor performance:** Fewer referrals, required improvement plans, potential removal from network

**ACOs are SERIOUS about quality** (financial incentives tied to outcomes).

## Payment Models in MGB ACO

**Traditional fee-for-service:**
- Agency bills patient's insurance (Medicare, MassHealth, commercial) per visit/hour
- **Most common** (90%+ of ACO home care still fee-for-service)

**Bundled payments (emerging):**
- Single payment for entire "episode of care" (e.g., hip replacement: hospital + rehab + home care)
- **Agency receives portion** of bundle (negotiated with ACO)
- **Risk:** If patient readmitted, agency shares cost (incentive to prevent readmissions)

**Care coordination fees (emerging):**
- **Monthly fee** per ACO patient (e.g., $200/month for care coordination + home care visits)
- Supplements traditional billing
- **Covers:** Extra communication time, care team meetings, proactive monitoring

**Shared savings (rare for agencies):**
- If ACO achieves cost savings + quality benchmarks, partners share savings
- **Very rare** for home care agencies (mostly for physician groups)

**Most agencies in MGB ACO = traditional fee-for-service** (but be aware of alternative models emerging).

## Best Practices for MGB ACO Partnership

**1. Invest in technology:**
- ACOs require **electronic communication** (portals, secure messaging, electronic referrals)
- Paper-based agencies struggle with ACO requirements
- Budget for: EHR system, patient portal access, secure email

**2. Track quality metrics:**
- **Know your readmission rate, satisfaction scores, outcomes**
- ACOs ask for data - have it ready
- Use data to improve (if readmission rate high, identify why + fix)

**3. Communicate obsessively:**
- ACO care coordinators want **daily/weekly updates** (not just monthly)
- Proactive: "Mrs. Smith doing great, no concerns" (not just problems)
- **Fast response:** Same-day communication when issues arise

**4. Embrace care team model:**
- ACO = **team approach** (you're one member of team: physician, nurse care coordinator, social worker, therapist, YOU)
- Attend team meetings (even if virtual)
- Collaborate, don't work in silo

**5. Focus on high-risk patients:**
- ACOs prioritize **high-risk patients** (frequent flyers, complex medical, frail elderly)
- **Specialize** in complex care (CHF, COPD, diabetes, dementia, post-surgical)
- Agencies good with challenging cases = valued by ACOs

**6. Prevent readmissions:**
- **This is THE metric** ACOs care about most
- Train caregivers on: Medication adherence, symptom monitoring, when to escalate, preventing falls
- **Proactive:** Catch problems BEFORE ER visit needed

**7. Be patient:**
- ACO partnerships take TIME (6-24 months to fully integrate)
- Referral volume builds slowly (not immediate like hospital discharge)
- **Long-term value:** Mature ACO relationships = consistent high volume

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "ACO credentialing too complex"**
**Solution:** True - ACO credentialing more rigorous than hospitals. (1) Start with hospital relationships first (build track record), (2) Hire consultant to help with credentialing (health system consultants specialize in this), (3) Be patient (2-6 months normal), (4) Worth it (MGB ACO = massive long-term opportunity).

**Challenge 2: "Don't have quality data ACO requesting"**
**Solution:** (1) Start tracking NOW (readmission rates, satisfaction scores), (2) Use EHR reports (most systems generate these), (3) Survey clients monthly (satisfaction data), (4) Partner with quality improvement consultant, (5) If no data, be honest: "We're implementing tracking systems, will have data in 6 months."

**Challenge 3: "Technology integration expensive"**
**Solution:** (1) Many EHR systems have ACO modules (often included), (2) MGB may provide portal access (ask), (3) Phased approach (start with email, upgrade to full integration later), (4) Calculate ROI (if MGB ACO generates $500k/year revenue, $10k/year technology worth it).

**Challenge 4: "ACO referral volume slow to build"**
**Solution:** NORMAL. ACO relationships take 12-24 months to mature. Meanwhile: (1) Continue building hospital discharge relationships (immediate volume), (2) Demonstrate quality (ACOs reward with more referrals), (3) Be patient (long-term game). Don't expect 50 ACO referrals/month in Year 1.

**Challenge 5: "Can't meet ACO quality benchmarks"**
**Solution:** If readmission rates high: (1) Analyze why (caregiver training issue? Communication gap? Patient selection?), (2) Implement improvements (better medication management, earlier escalation, caregiver education), (3) Ask ACO for support (ACOs want you to succeed - may provide training resources), (4) If persistent quality issues, ACO may not be right fit yet (focus on fundamentals first).
