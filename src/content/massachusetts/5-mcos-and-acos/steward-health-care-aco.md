## Overview

Steward Health Care operates one of **Massachusetts' largest statewide Accountable Care Organizations**, managing care for 350,000+ attributed patients across 10 hospitals spanning Greater Boston, MetroWest, Cape Cod, and Southeastern MA. As a private, for-profit integrated health system, Steward ACO represents a **major but complex partnership opportunity** for home care agencies seeking geographic reach and volume across multiple regions.

**What makes Steward Health Care ACO significant:**
- **350,000+ attributed patients** across Medicare Shared Savings Program, commercial ACO contracts, and population health programs
- **10 hospitals statewide** - Greater Boston (5), MetroWest (2), Cape Cod (1), Southeastern MA (2)
- **Geographic reach** - Broadest ACO footprint in Massachusetts (vs. region-focused ACOs)
- **Medicare Shared Savings Program** - Upside/downside risk (financial incentives for quality + cost reduction)
- **Commercial ACO contracts** - Blue Cross Blue Shield, Tufts Health Plan, Harvard Pilgrim (150,000+ commercial lives)
- **Physician network** - 3,500+ affiliated physicians across primary care and specialties

**For Massachusetts home care agencies:**
- **Statewide footprint opportunity** - Single ACO relationship accesses 10 hospitals (vs. building relationships with 10 separate systems)
- **Volume potential** - 350,000+ attributed lives = significant referral volume for agencies with capacity
- **Care transitions focus** - Steward penalized for readmissions ($8-12M annually) = strong incentive to partner with quality home care
- **Decentralized model** - Each hospital has care management team (build relationships at hospital level, not just corporate)
- **Performance-based opportunities** - Steward shares savings with high-performing post-acute partners

**Important context:**
- **Financial challenges** - Steward has faced financial difficulties (2024 bankruptcy filing) creating operational uncertainties
- **Hospital transitions** - Several Steward hospitals sold or under sale agreements (ownership changes affect relationships)
- **Payment reliability** - Some partners report payment delays (evaluate financial risk before deep investment)
- **Opportunity amidst change** - Steward still operating, seeing patients, generating referrals (but monitor situation closely)

- **Hospital-specific care managers:** Contact individual hospitals (recommended approach)

## How Massachusetts Home Care Agencies Can Receive Referrals from Steward ACO

**Steward's decentralized care management model:**

Unlike Mass General Brigham or BILH with centralized ACO leadership, **Steward operates with hospital-based care management teams**. Each hospital has its own care coordinators, discharge planners, and case managers who generate home care referrals. **Strategy: Build relationships at individual hospital level vs. relying solely on corporate ACO team.**

### **Pathway 1: Hospital Discharge Planning (60% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **Patient discharged** from Steward hospital (St. Elizabeth's, Good Samaritan, Holy Family, etc.)
2. **Hospital discharge planner assesses** post-discharge needs
   - High readmission risk? (CHF, COPD, falls, complex medications)
   - Lives alone? Limited family support?
   - Functional limitations requiring ADL support?
3. **Discharge planner contacts home care agency**
   - **Traditional referral model** (phone/fax vs. sophisticated technology platform)
   - Provides patient name, diagnosis, contact info, discharge summary
4. **Agency responds** - Within 4-24 hours, schedules assessment
5. **Care begins** - Agency provides 4-12 weeks post-discharge support

**Target population:**
- CHF, COPD exacerbations (Steward penalized for readmissions)
- Falls, fractures (orthopedic surgery)
- Post-surgical (cardiac, abdominal, orthopedic)
- Diabetes complications
- Stroke, neurological events

**Volume by hospital:**
- **St. Elizabeth's (Brighton):** 30-50 referrals/month (largest volume)
- **Good Samaritan (Brockton):** 25-45 referrals/month
- **Holy Family (Methuen/Haverhill):** 25-40 referrals/month
- **Saint Anne's (Fall River):** 20-35 referrals/month
- **St. Luke's (New Bedford):** 18-30 referrals/month
- **Carney (Dorchester):** 15-25 referrals/month
- **Morton (Taunton):** 12-20 referrals/month

**Key insight:**
Steward discharge planners have **MORE autonomy** than planners at systems with centralized preferred provider networks. **Building personal relationships with individual discharge planners is CRITICAL** (vs. just credentialing with corporate ACO office).

### **Pathway 2: ACO Care Management Program (25% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **Steward identifies high-risk ACO patients** via data analytics
   - Frequent hospitalizations, ER visits
   - Multiple chronic conditions
   - Prior readmissions
2. **ACO Care Manager (RN or social worker) reaches out** - Phone assessment of needs, barriers
3. **Care Manager determines home care needed** - "Patient needs ADL support + medication management + chronic disease monitoring"
4. **Refers to home care agency** - Typically longer-term care (6-18 months) vs. short-term post-discharge
5. **Agency coordinates** with ACO Care Manager on ongoing basis

**Target population:**
- "Super-utilizers" (frequent ER visits, hospitalizations)
- Complex chronic conditions (CHF, COPD, diabetes)
- Medication non-adherence
- Frail elderly with declining function

**Volume:** 80-150 referrals per month across Steward ACO (distributed among agencies)

**Key differentiator:**
ACO Care Managers manage **highest-risk patients** (top 5-10% of ACO population). **These referrals = long-term relationships** (12-24 months) vs. short post-discharge (6-8 weeks).

### **Pathway 3: Primary Care Physician Integration (10% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **ACO-affiliated physician** identifies patient decline (missed appointments, medication non-adherence, functional decline)
2. **PCP refers** to Steward ACO Care Manager or directly to home care agency
3. **Agency assesses and begins care**

**Volume:** 35-60 referrals per month

**Variability:** Depends on individual physician practices (some PCPs actively refer to home care, others rarely do)

### **Pathway 4: Skilled Nursing Facility (SNF) Diversion (5% of referrals)**

**How it works:**
1. **Patient could go to SNF but prefers home** with intensive support
2. **Steward Care Manager evaluates** - "Can patient safely return home with daily home care?"
3. **Refers to agency for intensive home support** - 2-3 visits daily initially
4. **Cost savings for ACO:** $8,000-$15,000/month SNF cost vs. $3,000-$6,000/month intensive home care

**Volume:** 20-40 referrals per month

**Financial incentive:** Steward STRONGLY incentivized to divert from SNF (SNF = expensive, home care = lower cost with equal or better outcomes)

## Network Enrollment and Credentialing

### **Steward's Fragmented Approach**

**Unlike centralized ACOs** (Mass General Brigham, BILH with comprehensive network development):
- **Steward does NOT have rigorous centralized credentialing** for home care agencies
- **Individual hospitals maintain their own referral relationships**
- **Discharge planners refer to agencies they know and trust** (vs. mandatory preferred provider list)

**Implication for agencies:**
- **Build relationships directly with hospitals** (St. Elizabeth's, Good Samaritan, Holy Family, etc.)
- **Corporate ACO enrollment is OPTIONAL** (helpful but not required for referrals)
- **Hospital-by-hospital approach** = more work but also more flexibility

### **Option 1: Hospital-Direct Relationships (RECOMMENDED PRIMARY STRATEGY)**

**How to build:**
1. **Identify Steward hospitals in your service area**
   - Which Steward hospitals serve regions you cover?
   - Example: If serving Brighton/Brookline → St. Elizabeth's. Brockton/South Shore → Good Samaritan.
2. **Contact hospital directly:**
   - **Call hospital main number:** Ask for "Discharge Planning" or "Case Management"
   - **Request meeting with discharge planning manager**
3. **Introduce your agency:**
   - In-person visit (bring one-pager about your agency, business cards)
   - Explain: Service area, services offered, quality metrics, responsiveness, specializations
   - **Provide direct contact info** (cell phone, email for fast communication)
4. **Build relationships with individual discharge planners:**
   - Each hospital has 5-15 discharge planners (RNs, social workers)
   - **Discharge planners are gatekeepers** for home care referrals
   - **Be responsive:** When they call with referral, respond within 2-4 hours (earn trust through reliability)
   - **Communicate outcomes:** Update discharge planners on patient progress, readmission prevention
5. **Maintain relationships:**
   - Quarterly in-person visits to hospital (stay visible)
   - Holiday gifts/thank you notes (build personal relationships)
   - **Responsiveness = repeat referrals:** Discharge planners remember agencies that respond fast and communicate well

**Timeline:** 2-4 months to build initial relationships, 12-24 months to become top-of-mind referral

**No formal credentialing required** (some hospitals may request: license, insurance certificates, references - provide informally)

### **Option 2: Corporate ACO Network Enrollment (SUPPLEMENTAL STRATEGY)**

**Value:** Enhances visibility, may generate ACO Care Management referrals (25% of referrals), positions for performance-based contracts

**How to enroll:**
1. **Contact Steward Provider Relations:**
   - Email: provider.relations@steward.org
   - Phone: 617-419-4700
   - Subject: "Home Care Agency ACO Network Enrollment"
2. **Provide agency information:**
   - Agency name, location, EIN
   - Service area (geographic coverage)
   - Services offered (personal care, skilled nursing, homemaking, specializations)
   - Licenses, insurance certificates
   - Quality metrics (readmission rates, patient satisfaction)
   - References (hospitals, ASAPs, other ACOs)
3. **Sign participation agreement** (if offered):
   - Service level expectations (response times, communication, documentation)
   - Quality standards (readmission targets, satisfaction targets)
   - Payment terms (fee-for-service standard, potential performance bonuses)
4. **Timeline:** 2-4 months (less rigorous than Mass General Brigham, BILH)

**NOTE:** Corporate enrollment does NOT guarantee referrals from hospital discharge planners. **You still need hospital-level relationships.**

## Tips to Get More Referrals from Steward ACO

**1. Prioritize hospital-level relationships over corporate ACO (Steward-specific strategy):**
- **Steward is decentralized** - individual hospitals have autonomy
- **Discharge planners control referrals** (not corporate ACO office)
- **Visit hospitals in-person quarterly** (stay visible)
- **Build personal relationships** with discharge planners (coffee meetings, holiday thank-yous)
- **Responsiveness = trust = repeat referrals**

**2. Respond lightning-fast to discharge planner calls:**
- **2-4 hour response** to every referral (same-day phone call to patient/family)
- **Same-day assessments** when possible (vs. 24-48 hours)
- **Steward discharge planners have autonomy** - they choose agencies based on past responsiveness
- **Speed = #1 competitive advantage** at Steward (vs. quality metrics at some other ACOs)

**3. Focus on CHF and COPD readmission prevention:**
- **Steward penalized $8-12M annually for readmissions** (Medicare penalties)
- **CHF and COPD = highest readmission rates** (30-day readmission 25-30%)
- **Train caregivers** in disease-specific monitoring:
  - CHF: Daily weights, symptom recognition, medication adherence, low-sodium diet
  - COPD: Inhaler technique, symptom escalation, smoking cessation, pulmonary rehab
- **Track readmissions** for Steward patients separately
- **Report to discharge planners:** "Last quarter: 28 Steward patients, 6% readmission rate (below 15% target)"
- **Discharge planners prioritize agencies that prevent readmissions**

**4. Serve multiple Steward hospitals (geographic diversification):**
- **Don't rely on one hospital** (especially given Steward financial uncertainty)
- **Serve 3-5 Steward hospitals** = diversified volume
- **Example:** Brighton/Brookline agency serves St. Elizabeth's + Carney. South Shore agency serves Good Samaritan + Morton.
- **Broader coverage = more referrals + risk mitigation**

**5. Communicate proactively with discharge planners:**
- **Weekly email updates** to discharge planners on patients they referred
- **Immediate alerts** for hospitalizations, ER visits (same-day phone call)
- **Success stories:** "Mrs. Rodriguez was readmitted 3 times before our care - ZERO readmissions in 6 months since"
- **Discharge planners value communication** (reduces their worry about patients)

**6. Position for SNF diversion opportunities:**
- **Steward STRONGLY incentivized** to avoid SNF (expensive, penalties)
- **Market intensive home care capabilities:** "We provide 2-3 visits daily for patients who could go to SNF"
- **Cost comparison:** Show Steward saves $5,000-$10,000/month with intensive home care vs. SNF
- **Accept complex cases:** Demonstrate you can manage patients others would send to SNF

**7. Expand service area to include Southeastern MA/Cape Cod (less competition):**
- **Fall River, New Bedford, Taunton, Cape area** = underserved
- **Fewer agencies** willing to serve these regions (vs. oversaturated Greater Boston)
- **Steward has 3 hospitals** in this region (Saint Anne's, St. Luke's, Morton)
- **Less competition = easier to become preferred partner**

**8. Monitor Steward financial situation (risk management):**
- **Steward has financial challenges** (bankruptcy filing, hospital sales)
- **Payment delays** reported by some partners
- **Risk mitigation strategies:**
  - **Diversify:** Don't make Steward >30-40% of revenue
  - **Monitor payment:** If delays exceed 60 days, consider reducing Steward volume
  - **Strong contracts:** Ensure clear payment terms, escalation process for delays
  - **Bankruptcy protections:** Consult attorney on protecting receivables if financial situation worsens
- **Continue serving Steward** (still operating, seeing patients) but **manage risk**

**9. Demonstrate quality with data:**
- Track Steward patients separately (readmissions, ER visits, satisfaction)
- **Quarterly reports** to discharge planning managers at each hospital
- **Hospital-specific data:** "At Good Samaritan last quarter: 32 referrals, 8% readmission rate, 92% satisfaction"
- **Data-driven agencies get more referrals**

**10. Build relationships with ACO Care Managers (for long-term referrals):**
- **ACO Care Managers** (vs. discharge planners) generate 25% of referrals
- **Longer-term relationships:** 12-24 months vs. 6-8 weeks post-discharge
- **Contact:** 617-419-4700 (ask for ACO Care Management)
- **Introduce agency:** "We specialize in high-risk, complex patients with chronic conditions"
- **ACO Care Managers value:** Quality outcomes, communication, care coordination

## Common Challenges and Solutions

**Challenge 1: "Steward financial uncertainty (bankruptcy, hospital sales)"**
**Solution:** (1) **Continue serving Steward** (still operating, seeing patients, generating referrals), (2) **Diversify revenue** (don't exceed 30-40% Steward), (3) **Monitor payments** (if delays exceed 60 days, reduce volume), (4) **Strong contracts** (clear payment terms, attorney review), (5) **Monitor news** (stay informed on hospital sales, financial developments), (6) **Risk-adjusted approach** (serve Steward but manage downside).

**Challenge 2: "Payment delays (some partners report 60-90 day payment vs. 30-45 days)"**
**Solution:** (1) **Cash flow planning** (ensure reserves to weather payment delays), (2) **Submit clean claims** (minimize denials/delays with complete documentation), (3) **Follow up proactively** (call billing department weekly if payment overdue), (4) **Escalate if needed** (contact hospital CFO or corporate if delays persist), (5) **Consider payment terms** in volume decisions (if delays worsen, reduce Steward volume).

**Challenge 3: "No centralized preferred provider list (hard to get 'in' with Steward)"**
**Solution:** **This is actually an OPPORTUNITY** (no gatekeepers). (1) **Hospital-by-hospital approach** (build direct relationships with discharge planners), (2) **Responsiveness** (fastest agencies win - no credential barrier), (3) **Personal relationships** (discharge planners have autonomy - invest in relationships), (4) **Quality outcomes** (demonstrate readmission prevention), (5) **Easier than Mass General Brigham/BILH credentialing** (no 6-month formal process).

**Challenge 4: "Decentralized model requires building multiple hospital relationships (more work)"**
**Solution:** (1) **Start with 1-2 hospitals** (build expertise before expanding), (2) **Hire dedicated business development staff** (1 person focuses on hospital relationships), (3) **Leverage success** (use outcomes from Hospital A when approaching Hospital B), (4) **Quarterly hospital visits** (4 times/year per hospital = manageable), (5) **ROI justifies effort** (revenue potential significant).

**Challenge 5: "Hospital ownership changes creating relationship disruptions"**
**Solution:** TRUE - several Steward hospitals sold/under sale. (1) **Build personal relationships with discharge planners** (even if hospital ownership changes, discharge planners often stay), (2) **Quality outcomes travel** (new owners value quality partners), (3) **Proactive communication** when ownership changes (introduce yourself to new leadership), (4) **Diversify** across multiple hospitals (not reliant on one).

**Challenge 6: "Competing with agencies that have long-standing Steward relationships"**
**Solution:** Differentiate: (1) **Speed** (4-hour response vs. 24 hours), (2) **Readmission prevention** (demonstrate with data), (3) **Communication** (weekly proactive updates vs. reactive), (4) **Specializations** (CHF, COPD, post-surgical), (5) **SNF diversion** (intensive home care capabilities), (6) **Personal relationships** (discharge planners choose agencies they trust - earn trust through consistency).

**Challenge 7: "Limited technology infrastructure (no Care Connect-type platform)"**
**Solution:** TRUE - Steward lacks sophisticated care coordination technology of Mass General Brigham/BILH. (1) **Old-school relationship building** (phone calls, in-person visits vs. electronic referrals), (2) **Direct communication** with discharge planners (cell phones, emails), (3) **Lower tech barrier** = easier for smaller agencies (don't need expensive care coordination platforms), (4) **Personal touch** can be competitive advantage.
