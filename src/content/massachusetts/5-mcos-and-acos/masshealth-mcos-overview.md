## Overview

MassHealth (Massachusetts Medicaid) contracts with **six Managed Care Organizations (MCOs)** to provide care for 1+ million MassHealth members statewide. For home care agencies serving MassHealth clients, **MCO partnerships are ESSENTIAL** - MCOs manage member care, coordinate services, and can provide consistent referrals to home care agencies in their provider networks.

**MassHealth MCOs (6 plans):**
1. **Boston Medical Center HealthNet Plan** (BMC HealthNet)
2. **Fallon Community Health Plan** (Fallon 365 Care)
3. **Health New England** (HNE)
4. **Neighborhood Health Plan** (NHP)
5. **Tufts Health Together** (Tufts Health Public Plans)
6. **WellSense Health Plan**

**For home care agencies:**
- **1+ million MassHealth members** enrolled in MCOs (massive market)
- **MCO referral pathways:** Care coordinators, case managers, provider networks
- **Credentialing opportunity:** Become in-network provider (priority referrals)
- **Care coordination support:** MCOs employ care coordinators who actively refer to home care
- **Quality incentives:** MCOs measured on care coordination, outcomes (partner with agencies that deliver quality)

## Understanding MassHealth MCO Model

### **What are MassHealth MCOs?**

**Traditional Medicaid (fee-for-service):**
- State pays providers directly for each service
- Patients choose any MassHealth-enrolled provider
- **NO care coordination** (fragmented care)

**Managed Care Organizations (MCOs):**
- MassHealth pays MCOs **fixed monthly fee (capitation)** per member
- MCO responsible for managing ALL care for attributed members
- **Financial incentive:** Keep members healthy at LOWER cost (MCO keeps savings if costs below capitation payment)
- **Care coordination:** MCOs employ care coordinators, case managers to coordinate services
- **Provider networks:** MCOs contract with physicians, hospitals, home care agencies

### **Who enrolls in MCOs?**

**Required MCO enrollment:**
- MassHealth Standard members (adults 21-64, children, pregnant women)
- MassHealth CommonHealth members
- MassHealth Family Assistance members

**Optional enrollment:**
- MassHealth CarePlus members (elderly/disabled) - CAN choose MCO or stay fee-for-service

**Excluded from MCOs:**
- Medicare-Medicaid dual-eligible seniors (enrolled in SCO plans instead)
- Long-term care facility residents
- Some specialized populations

**Total MCO enrollment:** 1+ million MassHealth members statewide

## Why MCO Partnerships Matter for Home Care Agencies

**The MCO care coordination moment:**

**Scenario 1: Hospital discharge**
- MassHealth MCO member hospitalized (CHF exacerbation)
- **MCO care coordinator notified** (hospital discharge planning contacts MCO)
- **Care coordinator assesses:** "Member lives alone, needs help at home"
- **Care coordinator refers:** Contacts in-network home care agency (arranges services)

**Scenario 2: Community health worker identifies need**
- MCO community health worker visits member at home (routine check-in)
- **Observes:** Home cluttered, member struggling with medications, missing meals
- **CHW reports to care coordinator:** "Member needs home care"
- **Care coordinator refers:** Provides family with 2-3 in-network agency names

**Scenario 3: PCP referral**
- MCO member sees primary care physician (routine visit)
- **Physician observes:** Patient declining, daughter (caregiver) burned out
- **Physician to care coordinator:** "This patient needs home care support"
- **Care coordinator arranges:** Assessment with in-network agency

**Scenario 4: High-risk member proactive outreach**
- MCO analyzes data (predictive analytics identify high-risk members)
- **Care coordinator calls member:** "We're concerned about your health - would home care help?"
- **Member agrees:** Care coordinator arranges home care services

## How to Become MCO In-Network Provider

**Being "in-network" = PRIORITY REFERRALS:**

### **Benefits of In-Network Status:**

**1. Priority referrals:**
- MCO care coordinators refer to in-network agencies FIRST (contractual obligation)
- **Out-of-network agencies:** Only used if no in-network agencies available (rare)

**2. Streamlined authorization:**
- In-network = pre-approved rates, simplified authorization process
- **Out-of-network:** Must negotiate rates each time, longer authorization delays

**3. Direct payment:**
- MCO pays agency directly (billing simplified)
- **Out-of-network:** Payment delays, may require member to pay upfront + seek reimbursement

**4. Care coordination access:**
- In-network agencies get care coordinator contact info (direct referrals)
- **Out-of-network:** Must rely on families finding agency (no MCO support)

### **How to Apply (General Process - varies by MCO):**

**Step 1: Determine which MCOs to target**
- Which MCOs serve YOUR geographic area? (All 6 are statewide, but each has strongest regions)
- **Example:**
  - Boston agencies → BMC HealthNet, Neighborhood Health Plan, Tufts
  - Worcester agencies → Fallon 365 Care, BMC HealthNet
  - Springfield agencies → Health New England, BMC HealthNet

**Step 2: Visit MCO provider website**
- Each MCO has "Join Our Network" or "Provider Credentialing" section
- **Download application** (typically 10-25 pages)

**Step 3: Complete credentialing application**

**Required documentation (typical):**
- Agency license (DPH home health license)
- Business documents (articles of incorporation, EIN, business license)
- Liability insurance (general + professional liability)
- Agency policies/procedures (care plans, supervision, quality assurance)
- Criminal background check policies (caregivers, staff)
- References (professional, community)
- Service description (personal care, homemaking, companion, skilled services)
- Geographic coverage (which towns/cities do you serve?)
- Languages available (Spanish, Portuguese, Chinese, etc.)

**Step 4: Submit application**
- **Timeline:** 60-120 days typical (credentialing takes time)
- **Follow up:** Call MCO credentialing department monthly (stay top-of-mind)

**Step 5: Contract negotiation**
- MCO offers contract (rates, terms, service expectations)
- **Review carefully:** Rates (hourly), authorization requirements, quality expectations, termination clauses
- **Negotiate if needed:** Rates negotiable (especially if you offer specialized services)

**Step 6: Sign contract → In-network**
- Once signed, you're in MCO provider directory
- **Care coordinators can now refer to you**

**Step 7: Build relationships with care coordinators**
- **Don't assume referrals happen automatically** (being in-network ≠ automatic referrals)
- **Meet care coordinators:** Visit MCO offices, attend provider meetings, introduce your agency
- **Stay visible:** Quarterly check-ins, respond rapidly to referrals, demonstrate quality

## MassHealth Reimbursement Rates

**MCO rates for home care (typical):**
- **Personal care:** $25-$30/hour (varies by MCO, negotiated in contract)
- **Homemaking:** $22-$28/hour
- **Companion care:** $20-$26/hour

**Rate considerations:**
- **Rates LOWER than private pay** ($45-$55/hour private pay vs. $25-$30/hour MCO)
- **BUT volume high** (1+ million MCO members = consistent referrals)
- **Margin tighter** (must operate efficiently to be profitable on MCO rates)

**Financial viability:**
- **Mix is key:** 30-40% MCO clients + 60-70% private pay/Medicare = balanced business
- **Don't rely 100% on MCO** (rates too low for sole revenue source)
- **MCO provides:** Consistent volume, diversification, community mission

## Best Practices for MCO Partnerships

**1. Credential with multiple MCOs:**
- **Don't rely on one MCO** (diversify - all 6 if possible)
- **Each MCO serves different populations:** BMC HealthNet (urban), Fallon (Central MA), HNE (Western MA)
- **Credentialing is time-consuming but worthwhile** (6-12 months to complete all 6)

**2. Build care coordinator relationships:**
- **Being in-network ≠ automatic referrals** (care coordinators refer to agencies they KNOW)
- **Meet coordinators:** Visit MCO offices, attend provider meetings
- **Stay visible:** Quarterly check-ins, rapid response, quality outcomes

**3. Multilingual capability ESSENTIAL:**
- **MCO populations are diverse:** 40-60% non-English speakers
- **Spanish CRITICAL:** 25-35% of MCO members Spanish-speaking
- **Portuguese, Haitian Creole, Vietnamese, Chinese:** High-need languages
- **Without multilingual caregivers, MCO volume LIMITED**

**4. Understand MCO authorization requirements:**
- **Each MCO has different authorization process:** Pre-authorization, ongoing authorization, documentation requirements
- **Learn MCO processes:** Billing, authorization, care coordination (varies by MCO)
- **Dedicate staff:** MCO billing/authorization can be complex (need trained staff)

**5. Track quality outcomes:**
- **MCOs measure care coordination quality:** Member satisfaction, readmissions, care plan adherence
- **Demonstrate value:** Share outcomes with MCO care coordinators
- **Quality agencies get more referrals**

**6. Respond rapidly to referrals:**
- **MCO referrals often urgent:** Hospital discharge imminent, member in crisis
- **Same-day response critical:** Care coordinators remember responsive agencies
- **24-48 hour service start:** MCOs expect rapid service initiation

**7. Operate efficiently (margins are tight):**
- **MCO rates lower than private pay:** Must control costs (caregiver efficiency, scheduling optimization, minimize drive time)
- **Technology helps:** Scheduling software, EVV (electronic visit verification), billing automation
- **Don't accept MCO contracts if unprofitable** (better to focus on private pay than lose money on MCO)

## Common Challenges

**Challenge 1: "MCO credentialing takes forever"**
**Solution:** TRUE - 60-120 days typical. (1) Apply to all 6 MCOs simultaneously (don't wait for one to complete before starting next), (2) Follow up monthly (call credentialing department: "What's the status?"), (3) Ensure application complete (incomplete applications = delays), (4) Be patient (long-term investment).

**Challenge 2: "MCO rates too low to be profitable"**
**Solution:** (1) Negotiate rates during contracting (especially if you offer specialized services like dementia care), (2) Operate efficiently (optimize scheduling, reduce drive time, minimize administrative overhead), (3) Mix MCO + private pay (30-40% MCO max), (4) Consider declining MCO contracts if truly unprofitable (not all agencies should accept MCO - know your margins).

**Challenge 3: "In-network but not getting referrals"**
**Solution:** (1) Build relationships with care coordinators (being in directory ≠ referrals - coordinators refer to agencies they KNOW), (2) Visit MCO offices (meet care management teams), (3) Attend MCO provider meetings (visibility), (4) Languages (if you don't have Spanish/Portuguese/Haitian Creole speakers, MCO referrals LIMITED for diverse populations), (5) Response time (respond same-day to referrals - coordinators remember responsive agencies).

**Challenge 4: "MCO authorization process confusing"**
**Solution:** (1) Attend MCO provider trainings (all MCOs offer authorization training), (2) Dedicate staff (assign one person to learn MCO processes), (3) Call MCO provider services (ask questions - they WANT you to understand process), (4) Use MCO online portals (most MCOs have provider portals for authorization/billing), (5) Join MCO provider forums (network with other agencies - share tips).

**Challenge 5: "Can't serve all 6 MCOs (too complex)"**
**Solution:** (1) Start with 2-3 MCOs (prioritize MCOs strongest in YOUR geography), (2) Master those 2-3, then expand, (3) Geographic focus (Boston agencies → BMC HealthNet + NHP; Worcester → Fallon + BMC; Springfield → HNE + BMC), (4) Don't feel obligated to serve all 6 (many successful agencies focus on 2-4 MCOs).
